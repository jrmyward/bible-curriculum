# Worldview — Pacing Priority Guide

**School Year:** 2026–2027
**Total Instructional Days:** 154 (T1: 51 · T2: 53 · T3: 50)
**Buffer Days Built In:** 36 total (T1: 8 · T2: 13 · T3: 15)

---

## Purpose

This guide answers a different question than [teaching-map.md](teaching-map.md). The teaching map is the plan. This guide is the triage framework for real-time decisions when you're running behind. Consult at the end of each trimester; consult immediately if a unit runs over by 2+ days.

The short version: the course has 36 built-in buffer days. Most pacing problems dissolve by using them. Beyond that, this guide tells you what to cut and in what order.

---

## Priority Levels

- **CORE** — Non-negotiable. Every worldview unit is CORE; each exists for a specific pedagogical reason. You may condense, you may not skip.
- **IMPORTANT** — Should cover, but can compress. Usually individual days within a unit (e.g., Day 7 Contemporary Resource) rather than whole units.
- **FLEX** — Buffer days built into each trimester. Use these first when you fall behind.

## Flex Day Strategy

Buffer days are intentionally located at trimester boundaries, not spread thin across units. Use them in this order:

**T1 Buffer (8 days, Oct 29 – Nov 10)** — consolidated review + T1 Capstone presentations. If running behind in T1: pull from these days first.

**T2 Buffer (13 days, Feb 5 – Feb 24)** — largest buffer. Absorbs: Thanksgiving transition, Christmas transition, T2 Capstone presentations, T2 end assessment. If running behind: condense the T2 Capstone presentations to 2 days instead of 3.

**T3 Buffer (15 days, Apr 30 – May 21)** — finals week + course wrap + final farewells. Last 2 weeks (May 10–21) are explicitly planned as wrap-up; they can absorb up to 7–8 additional days of overflow content from earlier units.

**Rule of thumb:** If you're on track, use flex days for depth — extend a unit's Socratic seminar, add a guest speaker, watch a full film and discuss. If you're behind, use flex days to catch up. If you're way ahead, slow down — nobody benefits from rushing.

## Emergency Condensing Plan

If you are 3+ days behind, apply these cuts in order. Cuts earlier in the list have the lowest educational cost.

### First cuts (minimal impact)

1. **Absorb T1/T2/T3 buffer days into the preceding unit.** Each trimester has buffer that can be consumed before cutting actual content.
2. **Cut Day 7 (Contemporary Resource) from any unit after Unit 1.** This is the first per-unit day to drop. Assign the contemporary resource as homework instead of in-class viewing.
3. **Combine Days 5+6 (Case Study + vs. Christianity) in any unit after Unit 1.** Merge the case study debrief directly into the worldview-vs.-Christianity comparison.

### Second cuts (moderate impact)

4. **Compress Unit 2 (Judaism) from 10 to 7 days.** Preserve the Four-Stages theological arc; cut Days 5, 7, and combine 9+10.
5. **Compress Unit 3 (Mormonism) from 10 to 7 days.** Preserve the vocabulary-difference framing and the Islam-parallel lesson; cut Days 5, 7, and combine 9+10.
6. **Compress Unit 9 (Revolutionary Lineage) from 15 to 12 days.** Combine Marxism (Movement 1) and Progressivism (Movement 2) into 5 days; keep Postmodernism (Movement 3) at 5 days; reduce review days.

### Third cuts (higher impact)

7. **Compress Unit 5 (Hinduism) or Unit 6 (Buddhism) from 10 to 7 days.** If time is very tight, compress both. Do NOT combine them — that reverts to Myers' "New Spirituality" category this course explicitly rejects.
8. **Compress Unit 10 Capstone prep from 5 prep days to 2.** Preserve all student presentations (Days 7–10 of the unit). Students write the Final Brief at home.

### Last resort (significant impact)

9. **Compress Unit 0 (Foundations) from 10 to 8 days.** Cut Day 7 (contemporary resource) and Day 8 (cohort-tools walkthrough); fold the walkthrough into Day 6.
10. **Cut one Worldview Brief (make it oral only) from the unit running longest.** The Brief is the summative artifact for each unit; losing one weakens the end-of-year reference library students build, but preserves class time.

### Do not cut

- Unit 1 (Christianity) below 10 days — the baseline breaks the course if compressed.
- Unit 4 (Islam) below 10 days — 1.8B adherents; inadequate coverage fails the stated course goal.
- Unit 7 (Secularism) below 10 days — the worldview most students will live inside for the next decade.
- Unit 8 (Expressive Individualism / MTD) below 10 days — this is the most formative unit for their own spiritual trajectory.
- The Capstone presentations in Unit 10 — every student presents, no exceptions.
- The Gospel Conversation day (Day 8) in any unit — this is what the course is for.

## Decision Checkpoints

- **End of October** (just before T1 ends): Are Units 0–3 complete? If behind by 2+ days, absorb T1 buffer; if behind 5+ days, apply First Cuts.
- **Mid-January** (return from Christmas, start of Unit 6): Are Units 4–5 complete? If behind, cut in Unit 6 or Unit 7 preemptively rather than letting Unit 7 (hard floor) get squeezed.
- **Mid-February** (T2 end): Are Units 4–7 complete? T2 ends Feb 25. Do NOT carry Unit 7 into T3 — the worldview students need the summer to sit with Unit 8 and Unit 9 material, and T3 cannot absorb T2 overflow.
- **End of March** (just before Spring Break): Is Unit 9 Movement 1 (Marxism) complete? Spring Break falls mid-unit. If behind, use the week after break to compress Movements 2+3.

## Philosophy of This Guide

The bias of this guide is toward **depth, honesty, and preservation of the Gospel-conversation days**. If forced to choose between covering more worldviews superficially or fewer worldviews well, choose fewer. Students are not graded on completeness of coverage. They are graded — and more importantly, formed — by the quality of engagement with each worldview they *do* encounter.

A student who leaves this course able to represent 7 worldviews fairly and present the Gospel meaningfully to adherents of each is better prepared for adult life than a student who has been hurried through all 9 and cannot do either well.
