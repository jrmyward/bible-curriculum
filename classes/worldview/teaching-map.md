# Worldview — Teaching Map (Revised)

**School Year:** 2026–2027  
**Grade Level:** 11–12  
**Textbook:** *Understanding the Times: A Survey of Competing Worldviews* by Jeff Myers  
**Supplemental Coverage:** Buddhism, Hinduism, Mormonism (LDS), American Progressivism, Frankfurt School (fills gaps in textbook's Marxism → Postmodernism connection)  
**Total Instructional Days:** 147 (T1: 49 days | T2: 53 days | T3: 45 days)

---

## Unit Overview

| Unit | Weeks | Worldview(s) | Type | Key Question | Capstone |
|------|-------|--------------|------|--------------|----------|
| **1** | 1–3 | Christianity (Biblical Theism) | Core | What is the biblical worldview across all disciplines? | No |
| **2** | 4 | Mormonism (LDS) | Core (SE Idaho) | Is Mormonism Christian, or a distinct religion? | No |
| **3** | 5–7 | Islam | Core | How does Islamic theology shape law, politics, and culture? | Yes (T1) |
| **4** | 8–9 | Buddhism & Hinduism | Core | How do Eastern religions differ from Western monotheism? | No |
| **5** | 10–12 | Secularism (Naturalism) | Core | Can secularism provide a coherent foundation for ethics and meaning? | Yes (T2) |
| **6** | 13–16 | Marxism → Postmodernism | Core | How did Marxism evolve into critical theory? | Yes (T2/T3) |
| **7** | 17–19 | Cross-Worldview Synthesis | Application | How do all 7 worldviews answer life's ultimate questions? | No |
| **8** | 20 | Contemporary Issues | Application | How does worldview shape responses to bioethics, justice, and technology? | No |
| **9** | 21–26 | Final Capstones & Review | Assessment | How has this course shaped your worldview? | Yes (Final) |

**Total Worldviews Covered:** 7 (Christianity, Mormonism, Islam, Buddhism/Hinduism, Secularism, Marxism → Postmodernism combined, Cross-Worldview Synthesis)

### Week 16 — Dec 7–11, 2026
**Unit 6: Marxism → Postmodernism (Week 3 of 4)**  
**Topic:** The Frankfurt School & Postmodernism (The Bridge & The Mask Change)

**Activities:**
- **Mon (Dec 7):** The Frankfurt School: Antonio Gramsci (cultural hegemony), Herbert Marcuse (*Eros and Civilization*, sexual revolution as liberation).
- **Tue (Dec 8):** Critical theory as method: expose and dismantle power structures. Pair & Defend #6: "Capitalism exploits workers (Marxism) / Capitalism creates prosperity but needs biblical ethics (Christianity)."
- **Wed (Dec 9):** Introduce postmodernism: rejection of metanarratives. Michel Foucault: power/knowledge framework, sexuality as performative.
- **Thu (Dec 10):** Jacques Derrida: deconstruction. The tension: postmodernism's inconsistency (rejects metanarratives but smuggles in Marxist liberation narrative).
- **Fri (Dec 11):** Discussion Brief #8 ("Can postmodernism's rejection of objective truth be coherently defended?"). Class discussion.

**Due:** Discussion Brief #8 (Fri)

### Week 17 — Dec 14–18, 2026
**Unit 6: Marxism → Postmodernism (Week 4 of 4)**  
**Topic:** Modern Applications — Critical Theory in Practice

**Activities:**
- **Mon (Dec 14):** Critical race theory (CRT): class struggle → race struggle. Oppressor (white) vs. oppressed (BIPOC). Origins: Derrick Bell, Kimberlé Crenshaw.
- **Tue (Dec 15):** Intersectionality: multidimensional oppression (race + gender + sexuality + class). Privilege = inverse of oppression.
- **Wed (Dec 16):** Gender ideology: Foucault's legacy. Gender as social construct (not biological reality). Rejection of binary as oppressive.
- **Thu (Dec 17):** Equity vs. equality. Biblical justice vs. critical theory justice. Pair & Defend #7: "Systemic injustice requires systemic solutions (equity) / Individual sin requires individual repentance (equality)."
- **Fri (Dec 18):** Debrief Pair & Defend. Case Study: Analyze a "decolonize the curriculum" or "anti-racist" proposal.

**Due:** Case Study write-up (Fri)

## Notes for the Teacher

1. **Pacing:** This map accounts for **147 instructional days** vs. the original plan's 162 days. The corrected calendar includes all 15 teacher work days and 7 teacher absences for HS golf in April-May.

2. **Shortened weeks:** Many weeks in September, January-February, and April-May are 3-4 days instead of 5 due to teacher work days, holidays, and teacher absences.

3. **Unit 4 expanded to 2 weeks:** Hinduism (Week 8) and Buddhism (Week 9) each receive dedicated time. Buddhism and Hinduism represent ~2 billion people globally and offer fundamentally different metaphysics from Western monotheism.

4. **Unit 6 is the centerpiece:** The combined Marxism → Postmodernism unit (4 weeks, condensed from 5) fills critical gaps left by the *Understanding the Times* textbook. It traces the genealogy: Marx → American Progressivism (Wilson, Sanger) → Frankfurt School (Marcuse) → Postmodernism (Foucault, Derrida) → Critical Theory (CRT, intersectionality, gender ideology). **See [unit-06-marxism-to-postmodernism-outline.md](unit-06-marxism-to-postmodernism-outline.md) for detailed week-by-week content.**

5. **7 worldviews total:** Christianity, Mormonism (SE Idaho context, 1 week), Islam, Buddhism & Hinduism (2 weeks), Secularism, Marxism → Postmodernism (combined, 4 weeks), Cross-Worldview Synthesis. Unit 8 (Contemporary Issues) condensed to 1 week.

6. **Capstones:** Four capstones total. The first two focus on Islam and Secularism; Capstone #3 focuses on Marxism → Postmodernism (critical theory); the final capstone is cumulative and application-focused.

7. **Debates & Panels:** Students defend assigned worldviews (not their own) to build empathy and sharpen rhetorical skill.

8. **Worldview Chart:** Week 23 chart project is a major synthesis tool — students see all 7 worldviews side-by-side.

9. **Discussion Briefs:** Collect 9 briefs across the year. These are low-stakes but high-value — they prepare students for discussions.

10. **Contemporary Issues:** Unit 8 (Week 25) condensed to 1 week, focusing on bioethics and social justice panel discussion.

11. **Final Exam:** Cumulative exam covers all 7 worldviews. Balance recall (key concepts) with analysis (compare/contrast).

12. **April-May pacing:** The final 6 weeks include 7 teacher absences for HS golf plus 3 teacher work days, resulting in many 3-day weeks. The final capstone presentations are spread across Weeks 28-32, and final exam is in Week 34. Weeks 35-39 are buffer/flex weeks.

13. **Supplemental materials required:** Unit 6 requires supplemental readings (Wilson, Sanger, Marcuse, Foucault, Crenshaw) not fully covered in *Understanding the Times*. See resource list in [unit-06-marxism-to-postmodernism-outline.md](unit-06-marxism-to-postmodernism-outline.md).
