# Unit 1 — Christianity: Student Learning Outline

**Course:** Understanding the Times — A Survey of Competing Worldviews
**Unit:** 1 — Christianity (the baseline)
**Dates:** Sep 9 – Sep 28, 2026 (13 instructional days)
**Framing question:** *What is the Christian answer to each of the 10 disciplines, and why?*

---

## What This Document Is

This is the **learning spine** for Unit 1 — what you should walk away knowing, understanding, and able to do by the Day-13 assessment. Read it before the unit begins. Return to it as you study. By Day 12, every claim and term in this outline should be something you can articulate in your own words with a Scripture anchor.

The reading packet is the *content*. The lectures are the *teaching*. This outline is the *target*.

---

## By the End of Unit 1, You Should Be Able To…

1. **Summarize the Christian worldview in one sentence** — using the publisher's definition: *Christianity is a theistic worldview centered on the person of Jesus Christ that derives its understanding of the world through the teachings of the Holy Bible.*
2. **Tell the four-act story** — Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration — and place at least one Scripture in each act.
3. **Name the eight theological anchors** of Christianity (listed below) and explain each in your own words.
4. **State Christianity's one-sentence answer to each of the 10 disciplines** — with at least one Scripture anchor per lens.
5. **Apply Myers's four tests (Reason, Outer World, Inner World, Real-World Consequences)** to Christianity itself — name two pressure points and how a thoughtful Christian responds to each.
6. **Distinguish Trinitarian Christianity from non-Trinitarian traditions** that use Christian vocabulary differently (e.g., Mormonism, Jehovah's Witnesses) — without dismissing the genuine differences inside the Christian family (Catholic / Orthodox / Protestant).
7. **Write a 1–2 page Doug Letter** (Day 13 morning) that addresses general/special revelation, sin, redemption, and the rationality of biblical faith — using publisher vocabulary (general revelation, special revelation, agape, redemptive narrative, etc.).
8. **Defend the Christianity column** of the worldview grid from memory by Day 12.

## The Eight Theological Anchors

These eight claims recur in every lens of the unit. Each is something a Christian holds as foundational; each will be tested.

| # | Anchor | One-sentence claim | Scripture |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Trinitarian Monotheism** | One God eternally exists as three coequal, coeternal persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. | Matt. 28:19 |
| 2 | **Imago Dei** | Every human being bears God's image and has inviolable dignity. | Gen. 1:26–27 |
| 3 | **Special Creation** | The world and life are deliberately created by God, not self-generated. | Gen. 1:1 |
| 4 | **Natural Law** | Moral reality is built into creation and knowable, in part, by reason. | Rom. 2:14–15 |
| 5 | **Redemptive Narrative** | History unfolds as Creation → Fall → Redemption → Restoration. | Rev. 21:5 |
| 6 | **Sphere Sovereignty** | God ordained distinct spheres (family, church, state), each with its own authority. | Rom. 13:1–7 |
| 7 | **Agape Love** | Self-giving love is the moral foundation, grounded in God's own character. | Luke 10:27; John 13:34 |
| 8 | **Biblical Stewardship** | We steward what God owns; we do not own outright. | Ps. 24:1; Gen. 1:26–30 |

## Christianity Through the 10 Disciplines

Each lens is structured the same way:

- **The discipline asks:** the central question
- **Christianity answers:** the one-sentence claim
- **Publisher vocabulary:** the exact terms the test will use
- **Scripture anchor:** at least one verse you should be able to cite
- **Where this view will be tested:** which later worldview pushes hardest on this answer

### 1. Theology

- **The discipline asks:** Who is God?
- **Christianity answers:** **Trinitarian Monotheism** — one God eternally existing as three coequal, coeternal persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit).
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Theism* [8.1] · *Monotheism* [8.6] · *Trinitarian Monotheism* [8.6] · *General revelation* [2.4] · *Special revelation* [2.4] · *Holy* [8.9c]
- **Scripture anchor:** Isa. 44:6 — *"I am the first, and I am the last; besides me there is no god."*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Secularism (Unit 8) and Revolutionary Lineage (Unit 9) deny God's existence; Hinduism (Unit 5) affirms an impersonal divinity; Islam (Unit 3) rejects the Trinity; Mormonism (Unit 4) reframes God as an exalted man.

### 2. Philosophy

- **The discipline asks:** What is real, and how do we know?
- **Christianity answers:** **Realism + Mind/Body Dualism** — reality exists independent of human perception; humans have both a material body and an immaterial mind; mind preceded matter.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Philosophy* [9.1] · *Metaphysics* [9.1] · *Epistemology* [9.1] · *Realism* [9.7] · *Dualism* [9.7] · *Mind/body dualism* [9.7c]
- **Scripture anchor:** John 1:1 — *"In the beginning was the Word…"*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Buddhism (Unit 6) denies an enduring self; Postmodernism (Unit 9) denies stable meaning; Secularism (Unit 8) reduces mind to brain.

### 3. Ethics

- **The discipline asks:** What is good, on what basis?
- **Christianity answers:** **Agape / Natural Law** — moral truth is grounded in God's character; the summary command is to love God with everything you are and your neighbor as yourself.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Ethics* [10.2] · *Agape* [10.8] · *Sermon on the Mount* [10.8] · *Greatest commandment* [10.8] · *Universal lawgiver* [10.1]
- **Scripture anchors:** Luke 10:27; Rom. 12:9 — *"Let love be genuine. Abhor what is evil; hold fast to what is good."*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Secularism (Unit 8) attempts ethics without God; Postmodernism (Unit 9) denies universal moral standards; Hinduism (Unit 5) replaces sin with karma; Buddhism (Unit 6) replaces sin with attachment.

### 4. Biology

- **The discipline asks:** What is life, and where did it come from?
- **Christianity answers:** **Special Creation in the image of God** — life is the deliberate work of a Creator; humans are categorically different because they are image-bearers. Christians disagree on the *timing and mechanism* (young-age, progressive, theistic-evolution) but unite on the claim that humans bear God's image.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Biology* [11.2] · *Special creation* [11.8] · *Young-age creationism* [11.8] · *Progressive creationism* [11.8] · *Theistic evolution* [11.8] · *Intelligent design movement* [11.7] · *Irreducible complexity* [11.8]
- **Scripture anchor:** Gen. 1:27.
- **Where this view will be tested:** Secularism (Unit 8) and Revolutionary Lineage (Unit 9) hold neo-Darwinian materialism; New Spirituality (Unit 7) blurs human and divine; Hinduism (Unit 5) and Buddhism (Unit 6) hold cyclical-rebirth views.

### 5. Psychology

- **The discipline asks:** What is the self?
- **Christianity answers:** **Mind/Body Dualism (Fallen but Redeemable)** — humans are a unity of body and immaterial soul; the soul accounts for identity, free will, and our proclivity to do wrong; we are bent inward by sin but not beyond redemption.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Psychology* [12.1] · *Mind/body dualism* [12.8] · *Imago Dei* · *Three sources of temptation: world, flesh, devil* [12.8] · *Positive psychology* [12.8]
- **Scripture anchor:** Matt. 10:28 — *"Do not fear those who kill the body but cannot kill the soul."*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Buddhism (Unit 6) teaches *anatta* (no self); Hinduism (Unit 5) identifies self with Brahman; Secularism (Unit 8) reduces self to brain; New Spirituality (Unit 7) divinizes the self.

### 6. Sociology

- **The discipline asks:** How should people live together?
- **Christianity answers:** **Sphere Sovereignty** — God ordained family, church, and state as distinct spheres, each with its own authority; the family is what holds society together; sin (rebellion against God's design) is the root cause of social breakdown.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Sociology* [13.1] · *Sphere sovereignty* [13.8] · *Subsidiarity* [13.8 / 15.9b]
- **Scripture anchor:** Gen. 2:18, 24 (foundational anthropology of family); Acts 2:42–47 (church as community).
- **Where this view will be tested:** Islam (Unit 3) sees the *umma* as primary above family; Marxism (Unit 9) replaces family with class; Secularism (Unit 8) elevates personal autonomy; Mormonism (Unit 4) extends family into eternal hierarchy.

### 7. Law

- **The discipline asks:** What is justice, and where does authority come from?
- **Christianity answers:** **Natural Law grounded in God's character** — divine moral order is knowable by reason and revealed clearly in Scripture; laws are *discovered*, not *invented*.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Law* [14.3] · *Natural law* [14.3] · *Source of divine law: the character and nature of God* [14.9] · *Order vs. justice* [14.3]
- **Scripture anchor:** Rom. 2:14–15.
- **Where this view will be tested:** Islam (Unit 3) holds Sharia as direct divine command; Secularism (Unit 8) holds *legal positivism*; Postmodernism (Unit 9) holds *Critical Legal Studies*.

### 8. Politics

- **The discipline asks:** What is the role of the state?
- **Christianity answers:** **Subsidiarity / Limited Government** — government's primary mission in a fallen world is to preserve justice and punish evil; rights are *protected*, not *created*, by the state; Christians are involved in politics with humility, knowing politics is real but penultimate.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Politics* [15.1] · *Subsidiarity* [15.9b] · *Sphere sovereignty* [15.9b]
- **Scripture anchors:** Rom. 13:1–7; Acts 5:29 — *"We must obey God rather than men."*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Islam (Unit 3) integrates religion and state in caliphate models; Marxism (Unit 9) sees the state as agent of class liberation; Secularism (Unit 8) holds *progressivism*.

### 9. Economics

- **The discipline asks:** What is wealth, and how should it be stewarded?
- **Christianity answers:** **Biblical Stewardship** — God owns it all; we are accountable to Him for how we manage what we are given; the right to property stems from the duty to work; private property is presupposed by the commandments against stealing and coveting.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *Economics* [16.1] · *Biblical stewardship* [16.10]
- **Scripture anchors:** Ps. 24:1; Prov. 10:4; Prov. 14:23; Prov. 16:8.
- **Where this view will be tested:** Marxism (Unit 9) holds private property is the root of injustice; Secularism (Unit 8) endorses interventionist redistribution; Islam (Unit 3) prohibits *riba* (interest).

### 10. History

- **The discipline asks:** Where is history going? What is its meaning?
- **Christianity answers:** **Redemptive Narrative** — the metanarrative of Creation → Fall → Redemption → Restoration; history follows a *linear* (not cyclical) pattern, anchored in real events; God works *through* human action to direct history toward his ultimate end.
- **Publisher vocabulary:** *History* [17.1] · *Redemptive narrative* [17.9] · *Linear conception of history* [17.9b] · *Cyclical conception of history* [17.9b] (rejected by Christianity)
- **Scripture anchors:** Gal. 4:4; Rev. 21:5; 1 Cor. 15:14 — *"If Christ has not been raised, [our] preaching is in vain and your faith is in vain."*
- **Where this view will be tested:** Marxism (Unit 9) holds *historical materialism*; Postmodernism (Unit 9) denies history has meaningful direction; Hinduism (Unit 5) and Buddhism (Unit 6) hold cyclical views; Secularism (Unit 8) holds *social progress*.

## The Four Tests (Day 10)

Myers's four-test framework lets us evaluate any worldview, including our own. On Day 10 (Wed Sep 23) you will apply these to Christianity itself in a small-group exercise. By the unit's end, you should be able to apply two of the four to Christianity and articulate one strength and one pressure point for each.

1. **Reason** — Is the worldview internally coherent? Does it contradict itself?
2. **Outer World** — Does the worldview match what we observe in nature, history, and human experience?
3. **Inner World** — Does the worldview match our deepest moral, aesthetic, and relational intuitions?
4. **Real-World Consequences** — When the worldview is lived at scale, what fruit does it bear?

You will use this framework against every other worldview in the course. Day 10 is the model exercise.

## The Christian Family (Day 9)

Christianity is taught in this course as a single column on the worldview grid because the three main traditions — **Roman Catholic**, **Eastern Orthodox**, and **Protestant** — share the Trinitarian, Christ-centered, scripturally-grounded confession of the Nicene Creed (AD 325/381). The differences between them are real, but they operate at a different level than the differences between Christianity and Islam, or Christianity and Buddhism.

You should be able to:

- Recite the heart of the Nicene Creed (or recognize it when you read it)
- Distinguish where the three traditions agree (Theology, Biology, History) from where they diverge (authority, salvation, sacraments)
- Explain why traditions like Mormonism and Jehovah's Witnesses, despite using Christian vocabulary, sit on the worldview grid as separate columns

## What You Will Be Asked to Demonstrate (Day 13)

The Day-13 blended chapter test (~50 minutes, 51 questions, 83 points) covers:

- **Multiple choice** — recognize publisher claims about Christianity
- **True / false with correction** — alter a misstatement to make it accurate
- **Fill-in-the-blank** — produce the publisher's exact vocabulary (every term in the lists above)
- **Short answer** — define key terms in one sentence each (Trinitarian monotheism, agape, special creation, biblical stewardship, redemptive narrative, etc.)
- **Essay** — apply two of the four tests to Christianity, naming both strengths and pressure points

Plus: hand in your **Doug Letter** (1–2 pages) at the start of class.
