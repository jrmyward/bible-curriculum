# Day 11 — Thu Sep 24, 2026 · THE FOUNDATIONAL EXERCISE

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 11 of 13)
**Topic:** Gospel Conversation practice — articulating the Gospel to someone who doesn't know it
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Why This Day Matters

This is **the foundational exercise** of the course's "defend and present the faith" arc. Every subsequent worldview unit (Units 2–9) has a Day 8 Gospel Conversation that **adapts** today's structure for that worldview's adherents. Skeptic cards will change — a Muslim friend in Unit 3, an LDS friend in Unit 4, a Hindu friend in Unit 5 — but the four words (God · Sin · Christ · Redemption) and the listen-first posture stay.

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. State the four words of the Gospel (**God · Sin · Christ · Redemption**) and define each in a single non-Christianese sentence
2. Engage a Skeptic in conversation by *listening first* — finding out what the person already thinks before talking *about* the Gospel
3. Articulate at least two of the four words in response to a specific Skeptic's questions, without using insider language
4. Identify (in debrief) one thing they'd do differently next time

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening + ground rules

Two rules: **No Christianese** + **Listen first**. The most important sentence in a Gospel Conversation is *"tell me more about that."*

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Four things the Gospel says — God · Sin · Christ · Redemption*

See [lecture companion / facilitation guide §Day 11 — Opening](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md).

### 0:05–0:13 · The four words (quick teacher review) [8 min]

For each word, a one-line student-facing version:

- **GOD** — There is a God. He is personal, loving, transcendent, and immanent. He made you on purpose, and you matter to him.
- **SIN** — Something has gone wrong with the human heart — and with mine. We are bent inward. We hurt each other. We can't fix it.
- **CHRIST** — God didn't leave us in the mess. He came in person, in Jesus, lived among us, died for our sins, and rose from the dead. The resurrection is the central historical claim.
- **REDEMPTION** — The invitation is to trust Jesus. Not to clean yourself up first. To trust that Jesus has done for us what we couldn't do for ourselves.

*"Those four words are not the whole Christian story. They are the spine. Memorize them. You can hang the rest on them in any conversation."*

### 0:13–0:16 · Roleplay setup [3 min]

Pair up. One partner is **Christian**, one is **Skeptic**. The Skeptic gets a card with their persona + 3 questions; the Christian has no card and has to respond live. After 8 minutes, swap roles.

### 0:16–0:24 · Round 1 [8 min]

Hand out the four Skeptic cards (Sarah / Marcus / Priya / Daniel — distribute as evenly as group count allows). Skeptic asks their first question; Christian responds using the four words, listening first, no Christianese.

### 0:24–0:32 · Round 2 — swap [8 min]

Previous Skeptic becomes Christian; previous Christian becomes Skeptic and gets a new card.

### 0:32–0:48 · Debrief [16 min — but conversational, not strictly timed]

Three full-class questions:

1. *"What was the hardest question you faced as the Christian, and how did you handle it?"* (3 answers)
2. *"What did your Skeptic partner say that you weren't expecting?"* (3 answers)
3. *"What would you do differently next time?"* (3 answers)

See [facilitation guide §Day 11 — Debrief](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md).

### 0:48–0:50 · Closing + bridge

*"Every later worldview unit has a Day 8 Gospel Conversation. We will adapt the Skeptic card for the worldview we're studying. The four words don't change. What changes is the entry point — what part of the Gospel best meets that person where they are. Today you practiced the baseline. From here on, you adapt."*

Distribute 3×5 cards. *"One sentence: what did you learn today?"* Collect on the way out.

## Teacher Notes

- **The Skeptic cards do not let students off the hook.** Each Skeptic's questions are real questions real people ask — Daniel's "where was God in 1944?" is the question every Jewish skeptic eventually asks. If a Christian partner deflects with "you should just believe," coach: *"That's not an answer. Try again."*
- **Christianese vocabulary that needs to be banned today:** *"washed in the blood," "born again," "saved," "let Jesus into your heart," "personal Lord and Savior."* These are fine in church; they don't work in a coffee shop with a non-Christian. Make this rule explicit at the start.
- **If a student gets defensive,** pause the pair, coach: *"Take a breath. The Skeptic is asking what real people ask. Don't argue — listen, then respond using one of the four words."*
- **If a student tells you they had a real conversation like this last week,** welcome it: *"What worked? What didn't?"* Use it as a class example.
- **Daniel's card on the Holocaust** is genuinely hard. Don't model the Christian response yourself unless you're confident. If a pair flounders, name it: *"This is the hardest question in this class. Don't pretend to have an answer if you don't. 'I don't know but I'd want to think about it with you' is a Christian answer."*
