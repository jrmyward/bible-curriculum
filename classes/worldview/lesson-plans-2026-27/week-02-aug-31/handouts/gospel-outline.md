# The Gospel — An Outline

**A reference document for the year-long Gospel thread in this course.**

---

## The Gospel in Four Words

1. **God**
2. **Sin**
3. **Christ**
4. **Redemption**

These four words are the skeleton. Every Gospel conversation this year will put flesh on them in a way specific to the worldview of the person we're speaking to.

---

## The Gospel in One Paragraph

> God created a good world and placed people in it to know Him, love Him, and reflect His character. People rebelled — this is what the Bible calls sin — and the relationship with God was broken. God did not abandon us; He came Himself, in the person of Jesus, fully God and fully human. Jesus lived the faithful human life we couldn't, died a death we deserved, and rose again, defeating sin and death. Because of Jesus, anyone who trusts Him is reconciled to God, given new life now, and promised a renewed creation to come. This is not earned by our performance — it is received as a gift.

Memorize the structure. Internalize the substance. Say it in your own words.

## The Four Parts, Unpacked

### 1. GOD

- God exists. God is holy, loving, just, and personal. God is not a force or a vague presence — God is a *someone* who can be known.
- God is the Creator. Everything that exists comes from Him and owes its existence to Him.
- **Key Scripture:** *"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."* (Gen 1:1)

### 2. SIN

- Sin is not just "doing bad things." It's the deeper problem: humanity has turned away from God. Every specific wrong action flows from this root break.
- Sin is universal. No exceptions. *"All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."* (Rom 3:23)
- Sin's consequence is real: separation from God, brokenness in ourselves and our relationships, and ultimately death. *"The wages of sin is death."* (Rom 6:23)
- Sin cannot be fixed by more effort. You cannot un-break the relationship by trying harder to be good.

### 3. CHRIST

- God did not abandon us to the consequences of sin. He came Himself — in Jesus.
- Jesus is fully God and fully human. He lived the faithful human life we were supposed to live but couldn't.
- Jesus died on the cross — not as a tragic victim, but as a substitute. He took the consequence of sin that was ours.
- Jesus rose from the dead on the third day, defeating death itself. The resurrection is the linchpin — if Jesus stayed dead, Christianity falls apart. *"If Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile."* (1 Cor 15:17)
- **Key Scripture:** *"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."* (John 3:16)

### 4. REDEMPTION

- Redemption is what Jesus's death and resurrection accomplished: restored relationship with God for everyone who trusts Him.
- This is not earned. It is received. *"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God."* (Eph 2:8)
- Redemption is not just about "going to heaven when you die." It begins now — a new identity, a new community, a new direction.
- Redemption is not complete yet. We still live in a broken world. But it will be. One day God will make all things new. *"Behold, I am making all things new."* (Rev 21:5)

## What the Gospel Is NOT

These can sound Christian but are not the Gospel. Watch for them in yourself, in Christian culture, and in presentations you'll encounter this year.

- **"Be a good person."** — This reverses the Gospel. The Gospel says we can't be good enough, and that's why we need Christ.
- **"God wants you happy."** — God wants you reconciled and transformed. Sometimes that includes happiness; sometimes it doesn't.
- **"Jesus is your life coach / cheerleader / therapist."** — Jesus is Lord, not a performance-enhancer for the life you already wanted.
- **"America is a Christian nation."** — Maybe true historically in some sense, but the Gospel is a universal claim, not a national one. The Kingdom is larger than any country.
- **"Go to church, vote this way, avoid these sins."** — These are downstream. The Gospel is the upstream reality that makes them make sense. Putting them first inverts the order.
- **"Follow your heart."** — Nearly the opposite of the Gospel, which says our hearts are part of what needs redeeming (Jer 17:9).

The Gospel is a person (Jesus) and what He did (died and rose). Everything else flows from that.

## How the Gospel Conversation Changes by Worldview

You will adapt the Gospel to nine different listeners this year. The four-word structure (God, Sin, Christ, Redemption) doesn't change. The entry point, the objections, and the language that lands all do.

| Worldview | Entry Point | Primary Objection to Expect |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Judaism** | The Temple and atonement | "Jesus isn't the Messiah." |
| **Mormonism** | "When you say 'Jesus,' who do you mean?" | "We believe in Jesus too." |
| **Islam** | Jesus is honored as prophet in Islam | "Jesus didn't die on the cross." |
| **Hinduism** | The question of the self | "All paths lead to the same place." |
| **Buddhism** | Suffering and desire | "Christianity makes people attached." |
| **Secularism / New Atheism** | Meaning, morality, death | "There's no evidence for God." |
| **Expressive Individualism / MTD** | "What if you are not basically fine?" | "Don't tell me what to believe." |
| **Revolutionary Lineage** | Truth and metanarrative | "Christianity is a power move." |

You'll practice each of these. The final exam includes a live Gospel conversation with a randomly-assigned worldview partner.
