# Day 3 — Fri Sep 11, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 3 of 13)
**Topic:** Deep Dive Part 2 — Biology · Psychology · Sociology (three lenses)
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. State Christianity's answer to **Biology** using *Special Creation in the image of God* (with the three Christian positions on timing/mechanism)
2. State Christianity's answer to **Psychology** using *Mind/Body Dualism, Fallen but Redeemable*
3. State Christianity's answer to **Sociology** using *Sphere Sovereignty* — family · church · state, each with its own God-given authority
4. Articulate the through-line across all three lenses: *humans bear God's image — body and soul, individually and in community*
5. Fill in lenses 4, 5, and 6 of their student learning outline

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening review

*"Yesterday we said God's character is the source. Today we trace what that means for what WE are. One sentence: what does Christianity say it means to be human?"* Land on: *image of God (Imago Dei), fallen but redeemable*. See [lecture companion §Day 3 — Opening review](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:05–0:17 · Lens 4 — Biology [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Special Creation in the image of God.*

Tell the Francis Collins story (atheist scientist → Christian via Lewis's *Mere Christianity*). Three Christian positions: young-age creationism, progressive creationism, theistic evolution. **Local note:** SE Idaho has a young-earth majority; don't condescend; the categorical claim (image-bearers) holds across all three positions. Vocabulary: Special creation (Ch 11.8), all three positions, intelligent design movement (Ch 11.7), irreducible complexity (Ch 11.8). Scripture anchor: Genesis 1:27. See [lecture companion §Day 3 — Lens 4 Biology](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:17–0:29 · Lens 5 — Psychology [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Mind/Body Dualism, Fallen but Redeemable.*

Tell the Henri Nouwen / L'Arche story. Vocabulary: Mind/body dualism (Ch 12.8), what the soul accounts for (Ch 12.8), three sources of temptation (Ch 12.8), positive psychology (Ch 12.8). Scripture anchor: Matthew 10:28. See [lecture companion §Day 3 — Lens 5 Psychology](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:29–0:41 · Lens 6 — Sociology [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Sphere Sovereignty — family · church · state.*

Read Kuyper's "not one square inch" quote aloud. Vocabulary: Sphere sovereignty (Ch 13.8 / 15.9b), Subsidiarity (Ch 13.8 / 15.9b), government's responsibility — secure rights not create them (Ch 13.8), source of social problems = sin (Ch 13.8). Scripture: Gen 2:18, 24 + Acts 2:42–47. See [lecture companion §Day 3 — Lens 6 Sociology](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:41–0:46 · Synthesis check

*"What's the through-line across Biology, Psychology, Sociology?"* Land on: *humans bear God's image — body and soul, individually and in community*.

### 0:46–0:50 · Bridge to Day 4

*"Monday: the last four lenses — Law, Politics, Economics, History. Then Tuesday is your Socratic seminar — YOUR day. Bring two or three questions from this week's reading and lectures, written down."*

## Teacher Notes

- **The local note on Day 3 Biology matters at Watersprings.** Many students come from young-earth-creationist families. The unit's claim is *not* that one position is correct — the claim is that the *categorical* image-bearer doctrine holds across all three Christian positions. Make space for that distinction. If a student asks "what do YOU believe?" — that's a question for office hours, not a board moment in week 1.
- **Friday energy:** students will be more restless than Thursday. Use the Kuyper quote and the Nouwen story to break up the vocabulary density.
- Weekend reading is heavier than weeknight (full 4 lenses). Mention this so students don't underestimate.
