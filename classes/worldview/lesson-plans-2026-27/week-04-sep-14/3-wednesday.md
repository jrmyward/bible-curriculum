# Day 6 — Wed Sep 16, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 6 of 13) · Pair & Defend (full version)
**Topic:** Two-round simultaneous pair debate — every student argues both sides of two contested claims
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Articulate the strongest case *for* AND *against* Christianity's claim to be the one true worldview
2. Articulate the strongest case for AND against the claim that human beings have inherent dignity regardless of whether God exists
3. Identify (in the debrief) the strongest argument heard from the side they started disagreeing with

---

## Materials

- Scratch paper (for the 5-min brainstorm before Round 1)
- Two propositions written on the board (see below)
- Timer (visible to the room)

---

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:03 · Opening + frame

*"Today every one of you will argue against Christianity for part of the period, and for it for the other part. This is not a trick. The goal is not to convert anyone to anything. The goal is to make sure you have heard the strongest version of the position you disagree with, and that you can make the strongest version of the position you agree with."*

Pair up. Assign starting sides (alternate down the row).

### 0:03–0:08 · Brainstorm both sides [5 min]

Each student silently brainstorms arguments for BOTH propositions on scratch paper before anyone speaks. (See [facilitation guide §Day 6 — Anticipated student arguments to listen for](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for what good brainstorms surface.)

### 0:08–0:28 · Prompt 1 — *"Christianity's claim to be the one true worldview is reasonable" vs. "...is arrogant"* [20 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** the two propositions.

Standard two-round protocol: 8 min Round 1 (each student argues assigned side, partner asks one probing question) → 6 min Round 2 (sides swap; each student strengthens what the partner just said) → brief 1-min class debrief.

### 0:28–0:48 · Prompt 2 — *"If naturalism is true, human beings have no inherent dignity" vs. "Human beings have inherent dignity regardless of whether God exists"* [20 min]

Same protocol. Reassign sides so partners argue something new.

### 0:48–0:50 · Whole-class debrief

*"Which prompt was harder to argue against? Why?"* Take 3–4 answers. Don't resolve. *"Both prompts will return in later units."*

## Assessments Collected

---

## Teacher Notes

- **The brainstorm phase is critical.** Don't let students skip it. Without 5 min of brainstorming, Round 1 is just talking off the top of one's head.
- **Round 2 is the whole point.** "Strengthen what your partner just said" is where charitable engagement happens. Watch for pairs who try to skip it.
- **If a student refuses to argue their side:** *"You agreed to take that side for 8 minutes. After that you can disagree. For now, make the strongest case you can think of."*
- **If a Christian student gets defensive on Prompt 1:** coach gently — they're not denying their faith; they're representing an opposing argument. *"Jesus's brother James said be 'quick to hear, slow to speak.' Today's exercise is making sure you've actually heard."*
