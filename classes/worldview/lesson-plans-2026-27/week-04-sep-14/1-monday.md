# Day 4 — Mon Sep 14, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 4 of 13)
**Topic:** Deep Dive Part 3 — Law · Politics · Economics · History (last four lenses)
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. State Christianity's answer to **Law** using *Natural Law grounded in God's character* — laws are *discovered*, not *invented*
2. State Christianity's answer to **Politics** using *Subsidiarity / Limited Government* — politics is real but penultimate
3. State Christianity's answer to **Economics** using *Biblical Stewardship* — God owns it all; right to property stems from duty to work
4. State Christianity's answer to **History** using *Redemptive Narrative* — Creation → Fall → Redemption → Restoration; Christ is the center
5. Fill in lenses 7, 8, 9, 10 of their student learning outline; have the Christianity column of the worldview grid filled in by end of class

---

- Board: pre-write the four lens headings

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening review

*"In two sentences: what has Christianity claimed about God so far, and about us?"* Land on: *God is one being in three persons, eternal, personal, the source of truth and love. We are image-bearers — body and soul, made for relationship, fallen but redeemable.* See [lecture companion §Day 4 — Opening review](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:05–0:15 · Lens 7 — Law [10 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Natural Law grounded in God's character — laws are discovered, not invented.*

Carl F. H. Henry quote. Blackstone story — natural-law framework underlying the American founding. Vocabulary: Natural law (Ch 14.3), source of divine law (Ch 14.9), order vs. justice (Ch 14.3), Christian sources of law (Ch 14.9 — both general AND special revelation). Scripture: Romans 2:14–15. See [lecture companion §Day 4 — Lens 7 Law](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:15–0:25 · Lens 8 — Politics [10 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Subsidiarity / Limited Government — protect justice, punish evil, secure rights. Politics is real but penultimate.*

Contemporary example: Christians-and-politics in 2026. The five problematic positions → the sixth view (penultimate). Vocabulary: Subsidiarity (Ch 15.9b), sphere sovereignty (Ch 15.9b), government's primary mission (Ch 15.8), Acts 5:29 limit. Scripture: Romans 13:1–7; Acts 5:29. See [lecture companion §Day 4 — Lens 8 Politics](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:25–0:35 · Lens 9 — Economics [10 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Biblical Stewardship — God owns it all.*

Read Proverbs 10:4 / 14:23 / 16:8 aloud back-to-back. Vocabulary: Biblical stewardship (Ch 16.10), right to property (Ch 16.10), accountability (Ch 16.10), private property (Ch 16.10). Scripture: Psalm 24:1; Prov 10:4. See [lecture companion §Day 4 — Lens 9 Economics](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:35–0:45 · Lens 10 — History [10 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Redemptive Narrative — Christ is the center.*

Tell the N. T. Wright story — Christian hope is renewal, not escape. Revelation 21:5 — *"all things new"* not *"all new things."* Vocabulary: Redemptive narrative (Ch 17.9), linear conception of history (Ch 17.9b), cyclical conception of history (Ch 17.9b — rejected by Christianity), Christianity is rooted in history (Ch 17.9a). Scripture: Galatians 4:4; Revelation 21:5; 1 Cor 15:14. See [lecture companion §Day 4 — Lens 10 History](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:45–0:50 · Synthesis + bridge to Day 5

*"All ten lenses, three days. By Day 12 you should be able to write the Christianity column from memory. Tomorrow is Socratic seminar — YOUR day. Bring two or three questions from this week's reading and lectures, written down. The discussion goes where you take it."*

## Teacher Notes

- **Lens density:** Day 4 covers 4 lenses (vs. 3 on Days 2 and 3). The 10-min-per-lens pace is tighter. Don't go over.
