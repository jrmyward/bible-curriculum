# Day 5 — Tue Sep 15, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 5 of 13) · Socratic Seminar
**Topic:** Students drive discussion from their reading and from questions they brought
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should:

1. Have engaged at least two substantive contested questions about Christianity at depth
2. Have cited evidence (reading packet, Scripture, lecture content, personal experience) when making claims
3. Have practiced **steel-manning** — restating opposing positions before pushing back
4. Have heard at least one peer make a stronger argument for a position the student initially disagreed with

---

## Materials

- Each student brings 2–3 written questions (homework from Day 4)
- Chairs in a circle (or two concentric circles for fishbowl variant)
- A timer
- A clipboard for tracking participation (don't let it show; just take notes)

---

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening + ground rules

Three rules: cite evidence · steel-man before you object · make space, take space. See [facilitation guide §Day 5 — Opening](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for the verbatim framing script.

### 0:05–0:43 · Socratic discussion

Anchor prompt (write on board):

> *"Christianity claims to be true — not just personally meaningful, but objectively, historically, universally true. Is that claim defensible in 2026?"*

Let it sit. Resist filling silence. Cold-call if needed. Track three likely directions: historical (resurrection), moral (utility vs. truth), personal (inherited vs. owned belief). See [facilitation guide §Day 5 — Anticipated discussion paths + Backup prompts + Cold-call menu](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for the full set of materials.

### 0:43–0:48 · Closing debrief

Two questions:

1. *"What was the strongest argument you heard from the side you started disagreeing with?"* (Hand-raise; 3 answers.)
2. *"What is one question you are now carrying that you weren't carrying when class started?"* (Write on back of reading packet; don't collect.)

### 0:48–0:50 · Bridge to Day 6

*"Tomorrow is Pair & Defend. You'll be assigned a side. You may not refuse it. Same rule we set in Unit 0. Read the propositions on the board tomorrow before you sit down."*

## Teacher Notes

- **This is the first day in this class where you say almost nothing.** That feels wrong. It is right. The discussion only goes deep if students do the work.
- **Silence is not failure.** Adolescents will speak; they just need longer than you think. If 30 seconds passes with no one volunteering, cold-call.
- **If discussion turns into Sunday-school agreement,** push for friction: *"What is the* hardest *version of this objection? Don't soften it."*
- **If a student attacks Christianity sincerely,** welcome it. That's the pressure the unit was designed to produce.
- **Sub-friendly note:** if you needed a sub for today, this day is do-able — the prompts are scripted in the facilitation guide. The sub's job is to enforce the rules and run the clock, not to teach theology.
