# Understanding the Times — A Survey of Competing Worldviews

**Grade Level:** 11–12
**School:** Watersprings School
**School Year:** 2026–2027
**Source Text:** *Understanding the Times* by Jeff Myers & David Noebel
**Instructor:** Jeremy Ward

---

## Course Description

A year-long comparative worldview course for high school juniors and seniors. The course uses Myers & Noebel's 10-discipline analytical framework as its spine but restructures delivery from **discipline-first** (the textbook's order) to **worldview-first** — each worldview receives its own two-week unit examined through all 10 disciplines, compared directly with Christianity, and culminating in a practiced Gospel conversation specific to that worldview.

### Stated Goal

By May, every student should be able to sit across from a thoughtful proponent of any of these 9 worldviews, represent that person's view back to them in a way they would recognize as fair, and present the Gospel in a way that is meaningful and impactful to *that person specifically* — not a generic tract.

---

## Design Philosophy

**Worldview-first, not discipline-first.** Myers & Noebel introduce each worldview in isolation (Part 1) and then compare all worldviews on each discipline (Part 2). That structure is pedagogically elegant but weak for two reasons: it does not directly compare competing worldviews with the Christian worldview on the terms of the person who holds the competing view, and it does not equip students to present the Gospel to someone who holds that worldview in a way meaningful to *them*. This course reorders the material around those two goals.

**Charitable engagement is the curricular commitment.** A student who cannot represent a worldview fairly cannot engage it faithfully. Every unit includes a "framing" day where the worldview is presented in its best, most charitable form with no strawmen — and the Capstone rubric weights charitable representation as the first scoring category.

**The Gospel is a conversation, not a script.** Students practice 9 different Gospel conversations across the year, one tailored to the language, longings, and objections of each worldview's adherents. The final exam includes a live Gospel-conversation oral assessment.

## The 10 Analytical Lenses

Every unit examines the worldview through all 10:
Theology · Philosophy · Ethics · Biology · Psychology · Sociology · Law · Politics · Economics · History
