Chapter 1
OVERVIEW
Talking with non-Christians about God
and Christian beliefs can be challenging.
Christians may face apathy, skepticism,
or antagonism from non-Christians and
be unable to answer the questions others
have about Christianity. Apologetics
complements evangelism as it helps
Christians answer questions and challenges
to the Christian worldview. Apologetics
can also strengthen Christians' faith. A
worldview is like a map to guide us through
life, and the Christian worldview provides
reasonable answers to hard questions.
The Christian worldview and the
mission of God are grounded in the biblical
metanarrative, which is summarized in
six acts and an interlude. God's mission is
something that he does and invites us to
participate in. We carry out God's mission
by doing acts of compassionate service,
telling the truth of the gospel, and being a
community of love, forgiveness, and grace.
All Christians are called to carry out the
Great Commission and can be confident
doing so because of the power and presence
of the Holy Spirit.
Christians can balance grace and truth
as they spread the message of salvation.
Evangelism and apologetics are not
about forcing a conversation or winning
arguments. Yet, we can expose false beliefs
and move people toward a relationship with
Jesus. In this battle, the Christian's chief
enemy is the spiritual powers of evil, and
the chief weapon is prayer. Since humans
are relational, spiritual, emotional, and
experiential beings, doubt is part of the
Christian experience. Some things can be
proved beyond a reasonable doubt but not
beyond all possible doubt. People who
become Christians can see that Christianity
is reasonable but above all because they
experience Jesus' love and grace and place
their trust in him.

helps us make sense of the world as it really is so that we can live in
it with meaning and purpose.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. What difficulties, questions, and concerns have you
encountered when reading the Bible? What role does the Bible
play in your life? [1.3]
Answers will vary.
2. In what ways do you see God on a mission throughout the six
acts of the Bible's story? [1.3]
God's mission to bless, redeem, and restore creation is found
throughout the Bible. From the beginning, God displayed his
mission through his amazing creation as he created humans in his
image so they could rule with him. After the fall, God continued
to reach out to human beings. Jesus most fully embodied God's
mission by announcing God's kingdom and giving his life so that all
might be saved through faith in him. Today, God's Spirit empowers
the church to spread the good news about salvation and forgiveness.
Finally, God will complete his mission when he returns to defeat evil
and inaugurate the new heavens and new earth.
3. According to Matthew 28:18, how does knowing that "all
authority in heaven and on earth" belongs to Jesus strengthen us
for participation in God's mission? [1.4]
God provides us with our message. We announce that Jesus is Lord,
the true and rightful Sovereign of the universe. We draw strength
from the fact that Jesus has already won the battle against sin and
death and that he has complete control. We confidently share the
good news, knowing that the powers of evil cannot ultimately
overcome the gospel.
4. According to Matthew 28:19, what does it mean to "go
therefore and make disciples of all nations"? [1.4]
A disciple is a learner or pupil, one who follows a master, teacher, or
mentor and imitates his way of life. To make disciples is to do more
than simply convert people to belief in Jesus. It is also to disciple
them into a new way of life—a life lived loving, imitating, and
obeying Jesus.
5. How is Jesus always with us? [1.4]
Though Jesus is not physically present with us today, he is always
with us through the Holy Spirit, the third person of the Trinity.
The Holy Spirit lives inside each Christian and empowers them for
mission, encouraging them and giving them the right words to say.
The Spirit's presence gives us confidence in the face of criticism and
persecution.

6. Why shouldn't we leave participation in God's mission to the
professionals? [1.4]
Though some people may be specially gifted to announce the good news in a
more public way (e.g., pastors, missionaries, evangelists, and apologists), all of
us have been uniquely called to participate in God's mission regardless of our
job title. Ultimately, the results are not up to us because the Holy Spirit prepares
people's hearts to receive his message, and only he can bring about conversion.
We simply follow the Spirit's lead and get the joy of participating in his mission.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. What is evangelism? Describe the three tasks of evangelism.
1.5]
Evangelism is sharing the good news about Jesus and inviting
people to respond with faith. Evangelism includes three parts.
The first is a clear presentation of the gospel message-
-we
communicate the good news that Jesus is Lord and that salvation
and forgiveness are available to all through him. The second is an
active demonstration—we live our lives in ways that reveal to others
the love, justice, and compassion of Jesus. The last part is a generous
invitation—we invite people to respond to the gospel message with
further investigation and consideration or simply with trust in Jesus.
2. What is apologetics? Describe the three tasks of apologetics.
[1.5]
Apologetics is the art of defending, commending, and translating the
Christian faith. Defending the faith includes dealing with peoples
criticism and objections to Christianity or simply addressing difficult
questions. Commending the faith is showing Christianity to be
good, true, and beautiful. It is offering it up to others so they can see
for themselves. Translating the faith involves learning to speak the
language of our audience and sharing the gospel in such a way that it
connects with their deepest needs and desires.
3. Apologetics has as much to do with careful listening as it does
with actual arguments. What does this sentence means? [1.5]
There are no one-size-fits-all answers to hard questions about
Christianity. Some questioners may seek a purely reason-based
response, while others may ask a question because of a difficult
personal experience that has caused frustration or doubt. Providing
a strictly logical answer to an emotional question (and vice versa)
fails to address the person's real reason for asking the question.
That is why we listen carefully to what the person is saying and ask
clarifying questions before we respond.
4. How have you seen evangelism or apologetics done
ineffectively? How have you seen them done well? [1.6]
Answers will vary.
5. What can we learn from Jesus and Paul about effective
evangelism? [1.6]
Jesus desires all people to be saved, but he doesn't force anyone to
accept him (2 Peter 3:9). He came in humility and left people free
to reject him. His witness wasn't flashy or forceful. He wasn't soft on
sin, but neither did he condemn sinners. Instead, he invited them
into a relationship. Paul reasoned with people, seeking to do more
APOLOGETICS S
than simply tear down false beliefs. He wanted to put something better in the
place of false beliefs. He knew his various audiences and tailored his arguments
to resonate with whomever he was speaking. He urged speaking the truth in love
and living at peace as much as possible.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Why must the Lord's work be done in the Lord's way? [1.7]
Suppose we adopt worldly methods manipulation,
misrepresentation, or minimization—in our zeal for others to
know Jesus. In that case, we fail to imitate Jesus and will end up
counteracting the gospel we are trying to proclaim. If our message
speaks of love and grace, but our method is dishonest, forceful, or
disrespectful, our message may be perceived as the opposite of what
we intend. In fact, we will no longer be doing the Lord's work.
2. Who is the true enemy in the spiritual battle? How can this
enemy be defeated? [1.7]
The true enemy is the powers of evil that have enslaved the world
to sin. They cannot be defeated with their own weapons, such as
intimidation, dishonesty, or violence. For Christians, such weapons
are off-limits. Ultimately, this enemy can only be conquered by
Christ. We participate in the battle through prayer, asking God to
break sin's power over non-Christians. We also pray that God opens
their hearts, convicts them of sin, and gives us the words to say when
we share the gospel with them.
3. What is the role of reason in Christian apologetics? [1.7]
Christians are commanded to love God with their minds. Using
our reason well is one way of showing love and respect for God and
those we share with. In an often-unreasonable culture, Christians
have a responsibility to listen well, clarify their terms, and make
reasonable and logical arguments for Christianity (even if these
arguments do not convince everyone). Engaging our reason helps
us have rational conversations with non-Christians and demonstrate
that Christianity is reasonable to believe.
4. Is it possible to achieve absolute certainty about Christianity?
Why or why not? [1.8]
While much evidence supports the truth of Christianity, there is no
way of proving beyond doubt that Christianity is true. Ultimately,
every belief we hold rests on some other previous belief that cannot
be proven, meaning that we must place our faith in something or
someone. Yet, this faith need not be blind. We use our minds to
investigate all available evidence to discern what or who to place our
faith in. For Christians, truth is ultimately found in a person-Jesus.
To say that we cannot achieve absolute certainty about Christianity
acknowledges our limitations but does not deny the truth.
5. How might doubt stimulate Christian growth and maturity?
[1.8]
Our doubts can motivate us to investigate what we believe and
why we believe it. They can also drive us to prayer, study, and wise
counsel, helping us to grow deeper in our relationship with God.

What Is the Role of Apologetics in Christianity?
1. According to the speaker, what do many people in the church feel about
apologetics?
They are confused about apologetics and do not know how it relates to
Scripture.
2. How did Jesus show that evidence matters?
Jesus repeatedly pointed his followers to evidence through miracles he performed
or proofs he gave after his resurrection.
3. Why was it so important for Jesus to emphasize evidence to his disciples?
It was important because the disciples would be his eyewitnesses. He would
commission them to share the gospel.
4. How did Jesus respond when John the Baptist had doubts and sent his
disciples to him?
He worked miracles in front of John's disciples and told them to go back and tell
John what they saw. This showed Jesus' high regard for making a case for faith.
5. Why is apologetics needed if God's Word has power and God calls whom
he wills?
People can build walls to keep themselves from hearing the gospel clearly. Those
walls can be like speaking a foreign language. To reach them, you have to speak
their language.
6. What does the speaker say about the relationship between evangelism and
apologetics?
He says evangelism and apologetics are closely connected. Apologetics is a way
to do evangelism, having learned the language of the culture you want to reach.

Activity
VENTURING INTO EVANGELISM
OBJECTIVE:
Students will diagram causes for resistance or
receptiveness to the gospel.
PREPARATION:
• Materials needed: TR 1.7 and pen
• Time required: 20-25 minutes
PROCEDURE:
1. Read Jesus' words from Matthew 4:19 to the class.
2. Have students work in pairs. Distribute TR 1.7
Venturing into Evangelism to each pair.
3. Direct students to examine the Effect description
on the Rough Seas Diagram, and then brainstorm
possible causes categorized under each of the four
Influences.
4. Have students write possible influences under the
Emotions, Reason, Relationships, and Desires/Loves
headings on the diagram and answer the question
at the bottom of the page.
5. Discuss students' answers as a class.
6. Repeat the process for the Calm Seas Diagram.
7. Discuss this question with the class: "How can these
four influences help you engage more graciously the
next time you speak with a non-Christian about the
gospel?"
