• OBJECTIVE: You will be able to explain the ways technology and
postmodernism have influenced the rise of sexual brokenness and gender

isn't. This confusion is just a symptom of a deeper problem—confusion about the physical
body and what it means for who we are, how we relate, and how we live. Two significant
factors behind this confusion are shifting worldviews and the body's gradual displacement
in modern life and society.6
In the past two centuries, technological advancements have transformed human life.
We have become less limited by our physical bodies and less dependent on other people.
Medical advances have made lives longer and cosmetic changes to our bodies possible.

3. Created as Embodied Souls
• OBJECTIVE: You will be able to defend the truth about God's good design and
purpose for human beings.
Why start a discussion about the body before talking about sexuality and gender? Why not
just cut to the chase? Because misunderstanding God's design and purpose for our physical
bodies is a root cause of confusion about sexuality and gender. We need clarity about our
God-given purpose to flourish in life.
So, what do we need to know about the body? The physical body is created, limited,
valuable, and personal. Human beings have souls, which means we are "incarnate spirits,"
a "marriage of body and sou!." We are neither all-material nor all-soul or all-mind, as
some worldviews teach.

Because of the fall, all creation-including our bodies—became cursed (Romans

To review, Scripture teaches that our bodies are created, limited, valuable, personal,
and sexed. Though our bodies are broken and tainted by the fall, as Christians, we look
with hope toward the day our bodies will be resurrected and restored. In this, we see that
the metanarrative of Scripture tells not just the story of God's mission but also of our
bodies and what it means to be human. We are made male and female in the image of
God, body and soul. This story informs how we think about and treat our physical bodies
as well as how we understand sexuality and gender.
But in this postmodern world, we ve lost the plot of that story. We've attempted to
shape sex and gender according to our desires instead of our design. Often, we embrace
aspects of the fall as good or try to
overcome the effects of sin, shame,
pain and suffering, dysphoria, or death
on our own. But God, not us, is the
One who created us and made a way
for our redemption. Sexual brokenness
was overcome at the cross of Jesus!

struggle with gender dysphoria
or experience temptations toward
same-sex or opposite-sex sins, Jesus
empowers us with a new identity and
union with Christ day by day. The
Holy Spirit gives us the power to live
in obedience to Jesus' commands. As we cooperate with him, we can resist temptation, flee
from sin, stand up under suffering, receive comfort, and persevere through confusion.
Ultimately, hope for sexual brokenness comes through Christ and his church, which
serves as his hands and feet until he returns. As we pursue a deeper relationship with Jesus
and minister to others struggling with sexual brokenness, we become more like the people
God created us to be. Gradually, the more we become transformed into the image of
Christ, the more we become the person God created us to be.
By serving and walking faithfully alongside those who are struggling, the church
demonstrates God's love to the world and to each other. As we will discuss more in
Chapter 17, love is key to prayerful and effective outreach to those who do not yet know
