1. Examine the **nine questions** commonly asked about the Christian worldview. `[1.9]`

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:06 · Do Now & attendance
- Quick read of the room — where students place themselves (unprepared / confident / doubting / unconcerned). No judgment; it's a starting line.

### 0:06–0:26 · Discuss §§1.9–1.10 (manual p.13, model answers)
- *Which of the nine apologetics questions makes you curious? Which challenges you?* `[1.9]`
- *How does God empower us to be part of his mission?* (Jesus sent under-trained disciples out **in the Spirit's power**) `[1.10]`
- *What matters most in sharing — what we say, how we say it, or the balance?* `[1.10]`
- *What do you still need to articulate the Christian worldview clearly?* (sets up the whole year)

### 0:26–0:52 · Activity — *Servanthood Bible Study* (SM 15–16; Bibles; TR 1.5)
- Students work the three-step study on **1 Timothy 1:1–2, 18–20; 4:6–16; Titus 1:1–5; 2:1–15; 3:1–7**: canonical context (NT; Act 5 — the church), book context (Paul's letters to mentees), immediate context (marks of a good servant of God).
- Discuss: *how do these character marks shape how we share the gospel and disciple others?*

### 0:52–0:60 · Bridge to the test
- Hand out / point to the **Study Guide (TR 1.SG)**. Preview: tomorrow is a review day; the test is **Wed**.
