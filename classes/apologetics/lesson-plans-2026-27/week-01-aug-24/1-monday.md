## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:07 · Welcome & attendance
- Greet at the door; confirm seating; norms (phones, bathroom, water) in one breath.

### 0:07–0:18 · Course orientation
- What Apologetics **is:** the **second** of Summit's four-book *Understanding the Times* series — this year we learn to **share, defend, and commend** the Christian faith, and to answer the hard questions people actually ask.
- What it is **not:** winning arguments or memorizing comebacks. It's *"giving a reason for the hope that you have"* — with **gentleness and respect** (1 Peter 3:15).
- How you're graded: chapter tests, in-class activities, discussion, a few **debate days** later in the year.

### 0:18–0:30 · Hook — "Ever been there?" (ST 4–5)
- Read/tell the chapter's opening scenarios (Tom & Jin on suffering; the friend who says "that's true for you"). Ask: **has anything like this happened to you?** (Do Now cards seed this.)
- Name the tension: it's *good* to want to share the joy of knowing Jesus — and *normal* to hit walls. This class is training for those walls.

### 0:30–0:45 · Read & discuss §§1.1–1.2
- Students read 1.1–1.2 (silently or aloud), then discuss. Use manual Qs (**manual p.5**, model answers there):
  - *Which of the five stories in 1.1 do you relate to most?* `[1.1]`
  - *What difficulties — inside and outside us — come up when we share faith?* `[1.2]`
  - *What is a worldview? How is it like a map or GPS? Why does getting the right map matter?* `[1.2]`
- **Land the definition:** **a worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions.** *A wrong map gets you more lost.*

### 0:45–0:60 · Activity — *Christ's Ambassadors* (SM 6; groups; computers/concordances)
- Representing Christ takes **knowledge, wisdom, and character** (define each — manual p.5).
- Three groups, one attribute each; use a computer/concordance to find **two Bible verses** that describe it. Fill in the **Representing Christ Study** (SM 6): what's new that you learned, and how growing in this attribute helps you share the gospel.
- Each group reports out.
