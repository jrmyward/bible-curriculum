## Learning Objectives (teacher)

By the end of class, students can:

1. Describe the appropriate **weapons** Christians use in the spiritual battle against evil — chiefly **prayer**. `[1.7]`
2. Explain the roles of **reason** and **doubt** in apologetics. `[1.8]`

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:06 · Do Now & attendance
- Take a hand-vote on the Do Now; hold the resolution for the discussion.

### 0:06–0:30 · Discuss §§1.7–1.8 (manual pp.10–11, model answers)
- *Why must the Lord's work be done the Lord's way?* (worldly methods counteract the gospel) `[1.7]`
- *Who is the real enemy, and how is it defeated?* (the powers of evil — by Christ, through **prayer**) `[1.7]`
- *What's the role of reason?* (love God with your mind; make Christianity **reasonable to believe**) `[1.7]`
- *Can we be absolutely certain? Should we want to be?* (evidence, not proof beyond all doubt; **faith isn't blind**) `[1.8]`
- *How can doubt actually grow us?* (drives us to study, prayer, wise counsel) `[1.8]`

### 0:30–0:54 · Activity — *Ready for Battle* (SM 12–13; individual → share)
