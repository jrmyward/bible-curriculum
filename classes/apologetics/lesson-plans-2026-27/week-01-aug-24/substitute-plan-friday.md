## Dear Substitute — the whole day in one paragraph

Today students watch **five short videos** (about 12 minutes of footage total) and answer questions in a **Google Classroom worksheet**. Have students open their Chromebooks and go to Google Classroom → the assignment **"Video Questions — Chapter 1"** → open their own copy. Before each video, students read that video's questions so they know what to listen for. Play the video (replay once if needed), then give students 2–3 minutes to **type** their answers. Move to the next video. After the fifth video, students write a **short reflection** at the end of the worksheet. Students **turn in the worksheet in Classroom** at the end of class — this is the first graded work of the year, so make sure they click **Turn In** (not just close the tab).

## If the technology fails (no-tech fallback)

If Chromebooks or Wi-Fi are down, hand out the **printed worksheet copies** and have students write by hand; collect on paper. If the *videos* won't play at all, have students **reread §§1.1–1.8** in their textbook and write a paragraph answering: *"What is apologetics, and why does it matter?"* — collect at the end. No videos required.
