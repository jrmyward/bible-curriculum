## Learning Objectives (teacher)

By the end of class, students can:

1. Analyze how the **mission of God** is grounded in the biblical **metanarrative** (six acts + an interlude). `[1.3]`
2. Explain **three aspects of God's mission** given in the Great Commission (Matt 28:18–20). `[1.4]`

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:06 · Do Now & attendance
- Students list the acts of the story. Reveal the six acts + interlude; leave them on the board all block.

### 0:06–0:12 · Frame — God is on a mission
- The Christian worldview and God's mission are grounded in **the Bible's one story**. God's mission: to **bless, redeem, and restore** creation (manual p.6).

### 0:12–0:34 · Discuss §§1.3–1.4 (manual pp.6–7, model answers)
- *Where do you see God "on mission" across the six acts?* `[1.3]`
- *Matt 28:18 — how does "all authority… belongs to Jesus" strengthen us for mission?* `[1.4]`
- *Matt 28:19 — what does "go… make disciples of all nations" actually mean?* (more than converts — a **new way of life**) `[1.4]`
- *How is Jesus "always with us"?* (the Holy Spirit) · *Why not leave mission to the professionals?* `[1.4]`

### 0:34–0:56 · Activity — *Mission and Metanarrative* (SM 8–9; groups of 3–4)
- Each group gets one category: **(a)** the acts + interlude of the metanarrative, or **(b)** the **three lessons from the Great Commission** (authority is Jesus' · we go/baptize/teach · Jesus is with us).
- Groups design **memory aids** — symbols, acronyms, rhymes, visual cues — on the poster.

### 0:56–0:60 · Gallery walk & report
- Volunteers present their memory aids; post the best ones for the rest of the chapter.
