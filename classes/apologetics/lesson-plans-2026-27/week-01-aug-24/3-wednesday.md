## Learning Objectives (teacher)

By the end of class, students can:

1. Differentiate **evangelism** (three tasks) from **apologetics** (three tasks). `[1.5]`
2. Contrast ineffective approaches with **Jesus' and Paul's model** for reaching sinners. `[1.6]`

## Teaching Outline (~45 min · short day)

### 0:00–0:05 · Do Now & attendance
- Collect one or two guesses aloud; hold the definitions until after the reading.

### 0:05–0:20 · Discuss §§1.5–1.6 (manual pp.8–9, model answers)
- **Evangelism** = sharing the good news and inviting a response — three parts: **clear presentation · active demonstration · generous invitation.** `[1.5]`
- **Apologetics** = **defending · commending · translating** the faith. `[1.5]`
- *"Apologetics is as much careful listening as arguments" — what does that mean?* (a logical answer to an emotional question misses the person) `[1.5]`
- *What do Jesus and Paul teach us about doing this well?* (invitation not force; truth **in love**) `[1.6]`

### 0:20–0:42 · Activity — *Community Outreach Plan* (SM 10; groups of 3–4)
- Each group takes one demographic and builds a plan: *What might keep these people from saying yes to Jesus? What's your action plan? Who helps you build bridges? What prep do you need? How do you prepare spiritually?*
- Groups share quickly (short day — keep it moving).

### 0:42–0:45 · Close
- One-line takeaway: *evangelism tells the good news; apologetics clears the road to it.*
