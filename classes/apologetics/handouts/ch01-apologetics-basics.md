## Evangelism vs. Apologetics

| | What it is | The three tasks |
|---|---|---|
| **Evangelism** | Sharing the good news about Jesus and inviting a response | **Clear presentation** · **active demonstration** · **generous invitation** |
| **Apologetics** | Defending, commending, and translating the Christian faith | **Defending** (answer objections) · **commending** (show it's good, true, beautiful) · **translating** (speak the hearer's language) |

They work together: evangelism tells the good news; apologetics clears the road to it. Apologetics
is as much **careful listening** as argument — a logical answer to an emotional question misses the
person.

## The Mission of God

God's mission is his plan to **bless, redeem, and restore** creation — running through the whole
Bible, from Creation to New Creation. It's something God does and **invites us to participate in**.
The work of conversion belongs to the **Holy Spirit**; we get the joy of taking part.

## The Great Commission — Matthew 28:18–20 (three lessons)

1. **All authority** in heaven and on earth belongs to Jesus.
2. We **"go"** to make disciples — baptizing and teaching them to obey.
3. Jesus is **always with us**, by his Spirit.

A **disciple** is a *learner or pupil* — making disciples means teaching a whole new way of life,
not just gaining converts.

## The Bible's Story in Six Acts (plus an interlude)

1. **Creation** — God makes a good world and image-bearers.
2. **The Fall** — sin enters; "what's wrong with the world?"
3. **Redemption Initiated — Israel** — God chooses Abraham's family to bless the world.
   *Interlude: the Second Temple Period* — ~400 years waiting for a savior.
4. **Redemption Accomplished — Jesus** — his sinless life, death, and resurrection.
5. **Restoration Initiated — The Church** — the Spirit sends Jesus' followers, until he returns.
6. **Restoration Accomplished — New Creation** — new heavens and new earth.

## Reason, Doubt, and the Spiritual Battle

- **Reason** has a place: we love God with our minds and show Christianity is *reasonable to believe*
  — but we can't prove it beyond all possible doubt. **Faith is not blind.**
- **Doubt** is normal and can drive us to study, prayer, and wise counsel — it can *grow* our faith.
- The real **enemy** is the spiritual powers of evil (not people); the chief **weapon** is **prayer**.

## Key Verses

- **Isaiah 1:18** — "Come now, let us reason together." (Reason has a place; God invites it.)
- **Matthew 28:18–20** — the Great Commission.
- **John 20:21** — "As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you." (Every believer is sent.)
- **Ephesians 6:12** — the struggle is against spiritual powers of evil, not flesh and blood.
