# Chapter 1 Study Guide
## Introduction — Why Apologetics, and the Mission of God

**Apologetics · Bible 10/11**

Use this to get ready for the Chapter 1 Test (73 points). Answer everything **from memory first**,
then check the [Apologetics Basics reference](ch01-apologetics-basics.md) and your textbook. If you
can teach it to someone else, you know it.

---

## Key Terms — be able to define each
- **Apologetics** (and its three tasks: defending, commending, translating)
- **Evangelism** (and its three parts: clear presentation, active demonstration, generous invitation)
- **Worldview**
- **Mission of God**
- **Metanarrative**
- **Disciple**

## Key Verses — know the reference and the point
- **Isaiah 1:18** — "Come now, let us reason together."
- **Matthew 28:18–20** — the Great Commission (Jesus' command to share the gospel).
- **John 20:21** — "As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you."
- **Ephesians 6:12** — the struggle is against the spiritual powers of evil.

## Big ideas the test asks about
1. **Why be prepared to explain your faith**, and what apologetics is *for* (it complements
   evangelism and can strengthen your own faith).
2. **The mission of God** — his plan to **bless, redeem, and restore** creation — and that the work
   of conversion belongs to the **Holy Spirit**.
3. **The Great Commission's three lessons** (all authority is Jesus' · we go and make disciples ·
   Jesus is with us).
4. **Evangelism vs. apologetics** — be able to tell them apart and give an example of each.
5. **The six acts of the Bible's story** — especially which act answers *"What's wrong with the
   world?"* (**Act 2: The Fall**) and what happens in **Act 4: Redemption Accomplished** (Jesus).
6. **Reason and doubt** — reason shows the faith is reasonable but can't prove it beyond all doubt;
   doubt is normal and can grow your faith.
7. **The spiritual battle** — the true enemy is the powers of evil; the chief weapon is **prayer**.
8. **The nine commonly asked questions** the course will take up (be able to list three).

## Practice — write these out
- In one sentence each, define **evangelism** and **apologetics**, then give a real example of each.
- List the **three lessons** of the Great Commission.
- Put the **six acts** in order and write one line for **Act 2** and **Act 4**.
- Explain the roles of **reason** and **doubt** in the Christian life (essay-length).
- Who is the true **enemy**, and what are the **weapons** for the battle?
