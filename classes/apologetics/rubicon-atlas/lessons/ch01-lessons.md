## Day 1 - Why Be Prepared; What Apologetics Is For (1.1-1.2)

**Anticipatory Set:** On an index card, students finish the line: the last time someone asked me a hard question about God or faith, I ___. Then tell the chapter's opening scenarios (ST 4 to 5): Tom and Jin on suffering, the friend who says "that is true for you." Everyone hits these walls.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can explain why it matters to be prepared to explain the faith and describe the purpose of apologetics (1.1 to 1.2). First day also covers course orientation: what Apologetics is and how it is graded.
**Input:** Apologetics is the work of defending, commending, and translating the faith; it complements evangelism and can strengthen the believer's own faith. A worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that makes sense of the world and guides our actions; the wrong map gets you more lost. Key terms: apologetics, worldview. Key verse: Isaiah 1:18.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.5): which of the five stories do you relate to, what difficulties come up when sharing faith, what is a worldview and why does the right one matter. Reflective, so honest uncertainty is welcome on day one.
**Guided Practice:** Christ's Ambassadors (SM 6): three groups take knowledge, wisdom, or character, use a Bible or concordance to find two verses each, and complete the Representing Christ Study.
**Independent Practice:** Homework: read 1.3 to 1.4 (ST 7 to 10).
**Closure:** One line: apologetics is for giving a reason for the hope we have, with gentleness and respect.

## Day 2 - God's Mission and the Metanarrative (1.3-1.4)

**Anticipatory Set:** From memory, students list as many acts of the Bible's story as they can. Reveal the six acts and interlude and leave them on the board through the block.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can analyze how God's mission runs through the biblical metanarrative and explain three aspects of the mission given in the Great Commission (1.3 to 1.4).
**Input:** God's mission is to bless, redeem, and restore creation, running from Creation to New Creation. The Great Commission (Matthew 28:18–20) grounds our part: all authority is Jesus', we go and make disciples, and Jesus is with us by the Spirit. Key terms: mission of God, metanarrative. Key verse: Matthew 28:18–20.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.6 to 7): where do you see God on mission across the six acts, what does making disciples mean, how is Jesus always with us, why not leave mission to the professionals.
**Guided Practice:** Mission and Metanarrative (SM 8 to 9): groups of three or four build a memory aid (symbol, acronym, rhyme) for either the acts of the story or the three lessons of the Great Commission, then present.
**Independent Practice:** Homework: read 1.5 to 1.6 (ST 10 to 13).
**Closure:** Exit note: one act of the story and one thing Jesus told us to do in the Great Commission.

## Day 3 - Evangelism vs Apologetics; Jesus' Model (1.5-1.6)

**Objective and Purpose:** Students can differentiate evangelism from apologetics and contrast ineffective approaches with the model of Jesus and Paul (1.5 to 1.6). Short Wednesday period.
**Input:** Evangelism is sharing the good news and inviting a response, in three parts: a clear presentation, an active demonstration, and a generous invitation. Apologetics is defending, commending, and translating the faith. Apologetics is as much careful listening as argument, since a logical answer to an emotional question misses the person. Key verse: John 20:21.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.8 to 9): the three tasks of evangelism and of apologetics, what listening well means, what Jesus and Paul teach about doing this in love.
**Guided Practice:** Community Outreach Plan (SM 10): groups take one demographic (soup kitchen guests, refugee center residents, a domestic violence shelter, a retirement center) and design a plan to reach it with the gospel, then share.
**Independent Practice:** Homework: read 1.7 to 1.8 (ST 13 to 15).
**Closure:** One line: evangelism tells the good news, apologetics clears the road to it.

## Day 4 - Spiritual Battle; Reason and Doubt (1.7-1.8)

**Anticipatory Set:** True or false, and why: if I have enough evidence, I can prove Christianity beyond all possible doubt. Take a hand vote and hold the answer.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can describe the weapons of the spiritual battle and explain the roles of reason and doubt (1.7 to 1.8).
**Input:** The Lord's work must be done the Lord's way; worldly methods counteract the gospel. The real enemy is the powers of evil (Ephesians 6:12), conquered by Christ, and our chief weapon is prayer. Reason has a place, we make Christianity reasonable to believe, but we cannot prove it beyond all doubt; faith is not blind, and doubt can drive growth. Key verse: Ephesians 6:12.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.10 to 11): why the Lord's work must be done the Lord's way, who the true enemy is and how it is defeated, the role of reason, whether we can be absolutely certain, how doubt can grow us.
**Guided Practice:** Ready for Battle (SM 12 to 13): each student takes Romans 8:31–39, 2 Corinthians 10:1–5, or Ephesians 6:10–20, completes the self assessment, then reads the passage and answers on the spiritual battle, prayer, and endurance.
**Independent Practice:** Homework: read 1.9 to 1.10 (ST 16 to 19) for Monday. Note that tomorrow is a sub day with videos.
**Closure:** Chief enemy, the powers of evil; chief weapon, prayer.

## Day 5 - SUB DAY: Apologetics Video Set

**Objective and Purpose:** Students reinforce the chapter's ideas through five short expert videos (teacher absence; self-contained).
**Input:** Five videos on apologetics featuring Mittelberg, Sharp, McDowell, and Wallace (teacher manual p.15 to 17). Model answers for grading are in the teacher manual.
**Independent Practice:** For each video, students read the questions, watch, and type answers in the worksheet; after the fifth, they write a short reflection. Collected as the first graded work of the year.
**Closure:** Collect the worksheets. Fallback if streaming fails: reread 1.1 to 1.8 and write a paragraph on what apologetics is and why it matters.

## Day 6 - The Nine Questions; Empowered for Mission (1.9-1.10)

**Anticipatory Set:** Students pick the word that fits them now (unprepared, confident, doubting, or unconcerned) and say why in a sentence.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can examine the nine questions apologetics commonly answers and explain how God empowers believers for his mission (1.9 to 1.10).
**Input:** The chapter names nine common worldview questions the course will take up. God empowers ordinary believers for mission by the Holy Spirit, as Jesus sent under-trained disciples out in the Spirit's power.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.13): which of the nine questions draws you, how God empowers us, what matters most in sharing the gospel.
**Guided Practice:** Servanthood Bible Study (SM 15 to 16, TR 1.5): using 1 Timothy and Titus, students work the canonical, book, and immediate context to name the marks of a good servant of God and apply them to sharing the faith.
**Independent Practice:** Study for the Chapter 1 Test (Wednesday) using the Study Guide (TR 1.SG).
**Closure:** Name one of the nine questions you most want answered this year.

## Day 7 - Chapter Review

**Anticipatory Set:** Students define worldview, mission of God, evangelism, and apologetics from memory, then check them together.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students consolidate Chapter 1 and prepare for the test.
**Input:** Chapter 1 Review (PS 1.7): revisit the mission of God, the three lessons of the Great Commission, the three parts each of evangelism and apologetics, and the four key verses. These carry the most weight on the test.
**Guided Practice:** Venturing into Evangelism (TR 1.7, pairs): after reading Matthew 4:19, students diagram causes of resistance and receptiveness to the gospel under emotions, reason, relationships, and desires, then discuss engaging more graciously.
**Independent Practice:** Students work the Study Guide (TR 1.SG) and finish it as homework. The test is tomorrow.
**Closure:** Give one of the four key verses, by reference or gist, on the way out.
