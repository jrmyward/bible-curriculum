Chapter titles verified against the chapter-opener pages; key terms/verses from each teacher-manual chapter.*

## Course Overview

Foundations is the first of four volumes in Summit's *Understanding the Times* high-school series (Foundations → Apologetics → Worldviews → Culture). It introduces the building blocks of the Christian worldview: what a worldview is, the nature and authority of Scripture, the Bible's grand story (metanarrative), the character of God, and what it means to follow Christ personally and in community.

**Publisher design:** each chapter is built as **10 lessons** — roughly 9 content lessons (Key Terms & Verses are gathered on Lesson 9, the review) plus a **Chapter Test on Lesson 10** (teacher-manual test codes: `TR <ch>.10A`). At 18 chapters × 10 lessons that is a **180-day, full-year daily-lesson course.**

**Our adaptation (grade 9, 2026–27):** the usable calendar is **145 instructional days** (after no-school dates and the two light weeks). We keep the publisher's daily-lesson spine and chapter tests but **compress to ~8 days per chapter**, merging the lighter lessons. Delivery is **lecture-seminar** (teacher-led content with guided notes + regular discussion/application), not the cohort/debate model used in the 11–12 courses — a better fit for freshmen meeting this material for the first time. See [../teaching-map.md](../teaching-map.md) for the day-level pacing.

## Three-Part Structure (aligned to trimesters)

| Part | Chapters | Theme | Trimester |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Worldview & the Word** | 1–4 | What a worldview is; what the Bible is, its authority, and how to read it | T1 |
| **2 — The Story of the Bible** | 5–12 | The six-act biblical metanarrative, Creation → New Creation | T1 tail → T2 |
| **3 — Knowing & Following God** | 13–18 | The character of God; discipleship, spiritual life, community, love; conclusion | T3 |

The book's Part 2 is an explicit **six-act metanarrative** (the chapters are titled "Act 1," "Act 2," etc.), with Ch 5 introducing the Bible-as-story and Ch 9 a "Second Temple" interlude between Israel (Act 3) and Jesus (Act 4).

## Per-Chapter Rhythm (compressed, ~8 days)

Adapted from the publisher's 10-lesson chapter to an 8-day block:

1. **Days 1–5/6 — Content** (lecture + guided notes): the chapter's lessons, merged where the publisher splits thin ones.
2. **Day 6/7 — Seminar / application**: teacher-manual discussion questions (provided *with answers*), key-term and key-verse review (publisher gathers these on "Lesson 9").
3. **Day 8 — Chapter Test** (publisher `TR <ch>.10A`), with the student-manual Study Guide as the review tool.

Sub days (teacher absences) are slotted onto test days or self-contained review/video/application days wherever they fall — see the teaching map.

---

## Assessment

- **Chapter tests** — publisher-provided (`TR <ch>.10A`), one per chapter, on the block's final day. Reused as-is; not regenerated.
- **Student Study Guide** — publisher-provided in the student manual; the standing review tool before each test.
- **Trimester grades** — each trimester closes on a chapter test (Ch 6 / Ch 12 / Ch 18), so trimester assessment falls naturally on the publisher's own tests plus in-class discussion/application work.
