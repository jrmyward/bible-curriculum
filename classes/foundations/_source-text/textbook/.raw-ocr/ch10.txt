For many people, the resurrection is the last great event in the life

Forty days after his resurrection, Jesus was with his disciples on

This is not just a footnote to the story of Jesus. The ascension

Jesus announced, "All authority in heaven and earth has been
given to me" (Matthew 28:18). Jesus is now sitting at the right
hand of the Father where he rules and reigns over the whole earth
(Ephesians 1:20-23). Of course, not everyone recognizes that Jesus
is the King. That is why Jesus commissioned his disciples to spread
the message of the gospel throughout all the earth. He commissioned them to "make
disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of
the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you" (Matthew

As Christians, we have the task of acknowledging Jesus as Lord and submitting to him.
We are called to pick up our cross and follow him (Matthew 16:24-26). Yet Jesus said,
"My yoke is easy, and my burden is light" (Matthew 11:30). Obedience to Christ is for our
good. It enables us to be all that we were intended to be. We have the job of announcing

God, and new life are available to all who will give him their allegiance.
The second major implication of the ascension is that "Jesus is available, accessible,

longer need to go to the tabernacle or temple to experience the presence of God. As
God, Jesus is always present with us, just as he promised his disciples (Matthew 28:20).
Furthermore, Jesus is our great High Priest. Having been made human himself, Jesus
understands our weaknesses and struggles. He stands before the Father, allowing us to
approach God with confidence, knowing that our sins have been forgiven (Hebrews
4:14-16). Jesus constantly makes intercession for us. Even as we wrestle with sin in
our own lives, we can have confidence that nothing will ever separate us from his love
(Romans 8:31-39).

10. Conclusion
The good news of the gospel is set out clearly in 1 Corinthians 15:3-4:
For I delivered to you as of first importance what I also received: that Christ died for
our sins in accordance with the Scriptures, that he was buried, that he was raised on
the third day in accordance with the Scriptures...
Paul continues his letter assuming that Jesus Christ ascended to heaven, adding that
the resurrected Jesus will return and reign in glory "that God may be all in all"
(1 Corinthians 15:22-28).
The death, buria, resurrection, and ascension of Jesus were all part of God's rescue
operation for the world. This is the meaning of redemption— through Jesus, the sin and
death that enslaved us have been defeated once and for all, our sins have been paid for,
and new life is available to all who will put their trust in him. Through Jesus, we are made
new and commissioned to be part of God's rescuing work in the world.
In the next chapter, we'll explore what it looks like to live for Jesus as we await his
return. In the meantime, what does all of this mean for you now? How can you receive
forgiveness for sin? How can you become part

the redemption that Jesus offers?
