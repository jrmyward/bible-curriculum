OVERVIEW
The Christian life is lived in community.
Any Christian spiritual practice that
encourages us to permanently withdraw
from others or keeps us from becoming the
people Christ called us to be is dangerous
and short-sighted. Being part of the life of
a local church is essential. Christians may
be tempted to avoid church because it is
filled with sinful people. But church is the
training ground where we are sanctified and
formed to be like Christ. Church be a place
where we give and receive encouragement,
comfort, and support to one another as we
seek to follow Jesus.
Historically, Christians have met
together regularly for worship. Community
worship typically includes Bible teaching,
singing, and Bible study. Other practices
essential to the church are prayer and
exercising spiritual gifts. All Christians
receive at least one spiritual gift. Spiritual
gifts are for encouraging the church, not for
self-promotion. All gifts are to be used in
an appropriate, loving, and orderly fashion.
The church also administers the sacraments
of baptism and communion. Confession,
accountability, and mentoring are also
essential practices for Christian growth.
The church equips Christians to serve
others and to share the gospel with the
world. It is essential that Christians share
the gospel with both words and actions.
Throughout history, the church's impact on
society has extended to caring for the sick
and impoverished, honoring and protecting
women and children, pursuing racial unity,
and promoting culture and education
through artwork, music, architecture,
and founding the university system. As
Christians have followed Jesus together,
shared their testimonies, and brought
positive impact to their communities, the
power of the gospel has been made evident.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Should we imitate Simeon Stylites? Why or why not? [16.1]
Simeon Stylites' devotion to God was admirable, but his
methods were misguided. Christians should never inflict physical
punishments on their bodies or nearly starve themselves to death as
he did. Denying yourself certain foods or pleasures for a time is a
good spiritual practice, but self-harm has no place in the Christian
life. Simeon's pursuit of an isolated life would have made it difficult
for him to practice love and forgiveness since he wasn't living in
community. While solitude can be good in moderation, God forms
us in community with other believers.
2. What was the typical form of an ancient church service? [16.2]
Christians in ancient times gathered on Sundays to read the
Scriptures, hear instruction and exhortation from a pastor, pray, take
communion, and give to those in need.
3. Think of the symbols, banners, images, and artwork you
have seen in churches. What are they meant to communicate?
How could artists use their talents in the church to encourage
others to worship God? [16.2]
Possible answer: The art in church communicates love for God.
Representations of the cross remind us of what Jesus did for us.
Artists can use their skills to draw other people's attention to who
God is and what he has done. In a sense, creating art that glorifies
God can be an act of worship.
4. What is the purpose of singing in church? [16.2]
Worship through song is about responding to what the Spirit is
doing in us and offering praise, thanksgiving, or lament to God.
Worshipping in community helps us remember and reflect on who
God is and what he has done for us. Singing together is unifying,
and it helps us grow together as a community of gratitude and
thankfulness.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Why do we need to read and study Scripture together? [16.3]
No one is an infallible interpreter of Scripture. We are all prone to
making mistakes or reading our own ideas into the text. Studying
together with others provides the opportunity for our interpretations
to be corrected or enriched. It also keeps our faith from becoming
a private affair—just you, Jesus, and the Bible. Studying together
can grow us in humility, patience, and charity as we learn from one
another.
2. Why are creeds, confessions, catechisms, and statements of
faith important? [16.3]
Creeds, confessions, catechisms, and statements of faith represent
the effort of Christian communities to articulate the fruits of their
study of Scripture. They can lay out the fundamentals of the faith,
clarify the beliefs of a particular group, or provide easy-to-remember
questions and answers about Christian theology. Studying these
expressions of the faith puts us in conversation with Christians who
have gone before us and aids our study of Scripture.
3. What are four guidelines Scripture provides for exercising
spiritual gifts? [16.4)
First, spiritual gifts are gifts from God; they are not abilities we
develop through our own strength. Second, spiritual gifts are
for building up the church, not for self-promotion or private
indulgence. Third, every Christian has at least one spiritual gift. No
spiritual gift is more important than another. Finally, spiritual gifts
must be exercised in an appropriate, loving, and orderly way.
4. Use the spiritual gifts listed in the student textbook for
reference. List and describe how five of those spiritual gifts
could be used in the church. [16.4]
Answers will vary but should include five of the following gifts:
• Prophecy: identifying a particular temptation in the community
and lovingly challenging the church to remain faithful to Jesus
• Service: stacking chairs after a service or running the sound system
• Teaching: teaching a Sunday school class
• Encouragement: intentionally speaking words of encouragement to
friends and acquaintances
• Generosity: freely giving out of your abundance to those in need
• Leadership: leading a small group or a church program
• Mercy: organizing a soup kitchen for the community
• Wisdom: mentoring or counseling a younger Christian
• Knowledge: speaking a word of blessing into someone's life
• Faith: encouraging others to trust God by recalling his faithfulness
in the past
• Healing: anointing the sick and asking God to eradicate sickness and disease
• Miracles: being used by God to accomplish the seemingly impossible
• Discernment: prayerfully discerning the direction of your church and humbly
making any concerns known to leadership
• Speaking in tongues: praying in a different language as the Spirit leads
• Interpreting tongues: listening to a person speaking in tongues and translating
for the congregation
• Apostleship: being sent out by a church community to proclaim the gospel
Note: Some hold that this gift was limited to the 12 disciples of Jesus and Paul.
• Evangelism: intentionally sharing the gospel in your workplace, neighborhood,
or a foreign nation
• Shepherding: providing loving oversight and servant leadership for a
congregation or a group of people within a congregation

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Why is it important to regularly confess your sins to other
believers? [16.5]
Turning from sin is difficult, especially when sinful habits have been
ingrained in us over time. When we keep our sins hidden, we can
become hardened against change, or we may despair in our guilt and
shame. As long as sins are hidden, we cannot receive help in shedding
them. We need the encouragement and support of other believers.
When we bring our sins into the light, repentance, accountability,
and healing from the consequences of sin becomes possible.
Note: To confess your sins to other Christians, you do not need to
find someone with the same sin struggle as you. It is important to
confess to those who are wise and trustworthy, who will not take
advantage of your struggles or give poor advice that might lead you
to sin. Surround yourself with people who speak the truth with love.
2. What is a sacrament? [16.6]
According to Augustine, a sacrament is a "visible form of an invisible
grace." In the sacraments, we experience God's grace in real and
tangible ways, though the actions themselves have important
symbolic dimensions. Sacraments also help us remember and
celebrate what God has done, is doing, and will do.
3. Explain what baptism is. [16.6]
Baptism with water does not itself save us or forgive our sins. Jesus
does that. Baptism is a requirement for those who have trusted in
Christ and symbolizes dying to sin and rising with Christ. Baptism
visually demonstrates what happens when we receive Jesus: we receive
new life in Christ. It retells the salvation story and publicly declares
our identity as God's children and our allegiance to Jesus. Baptism is
a defining moment in our spiritual life.
Note: Historically, evangelical branches of the church affirm that
faith and repentance are necessary for salvation. Other traditions
include the necessity for baptism to accompany faith and repentance.
The Reformed tradition affirms that both faith and repentance
are gifts of God that result in regeneration. Emphasize your own
tradition as appropriate in your school or church.
4. What is the purpose of communion? Why does confession of
sin often come before communion? [16.6]
Communion is both a commemoration of Christ's sacrifice on the
cross and a celebration of his grace to sinners. The bread and wine
symbolize the body and blood of Christ given for us. Communion is
also a solemn remembrance, and it assumes that we are in fellowship
with God and other believers, so Christians must take it seriously.
That is why there is often space for confession before communion.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Where does the concept of the tithe come from? [16.7]
The concept of the tithe is rooted in the Old Testament story of
Abraham giving 10 percent of the spoils he won in battle to a priest
named Melchizedek. The Mosaic law also instructed the Israelites
to give tithes for priests, feasts, and the poor. Giving 10 percent
is not mandated in the New Testament, though many Christian
communities consider it a good goal.
2. How should Christians give today? [16.7]
Christians today should give freely and cheerfully, recognizing that
every good gift comes from God.
3. Think about your local church and community. What are
some ways that you and your friends could work together to
serve others? [16.7]
Possible answers: Take meals to people who have been ill. Do yard
work for the elderly. Visit people in the hospital. Volunteer for
community service projects. Clean up after an event at your church.
4. What do you think about this statement: "Preach the gospel,
and when necessary, use words"? Is it a good framework for
evangelism? Explain. [16.8]
Possible answer: This statement could encourage Christians not
to preach a loveless message or live in a way that undermines the
message. The way Christians love, serve, and forgive one another,
bless their enemies, and do good works in their communities can
serve as a powerful testimony to outsiders. The statement's weakness
is that it could leave the impression that words are secondary or
unnecessary to sharing the gospel. Jesus gave the Great Commission,
the command to "go therefore and make disciples... teaching them
to observe all that I have commanded you" (Matthew 28:19, 20). It's
clear that sharing the gospel means using words to tell people about
Jesus and inviting them to follow him.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. How have Christians throughout history cared for the sick and
honored women and children? [16.9]
In ancient Rome, Christians risked their lives to care for their sick
neighbors, both Christian and otherwise. Christians also led the
way in founding hospitals and shelters for the poor. Christians also
rejected abortion and infanticide. When babies were abandoned,
Christians took them into their own homes to raise them. In contrast
to ancient Roman culture, which allowed men to sleep around freely,
the church required husbands to love and be faithful to their wives.
2. Why should Christians care about promoting culture and
education? (16.9]
Possible answer: Christians are called to love God with all their heart,
soul, mind, and strength. Christians care about culture and education
because God's world can enrich human life and bring God glory.
3. What can Christians today learn from Rwanda and Charleston,
South Carolina, about racial unity and healing? [16.9]
Possible answer: These horrifying incidents of racial prejudice and
violence were followed by forgiveness and attempts to heal the divide.
Those affected have demonstrated the power of forgiveness to bring
healing and hope even in the darkest situations. In Ephesians 2, Paul
makes clear that Christians have already been reconciled to God and
each other through Christ. Sadly, we don't always live in this truth.
Ethnic prejudices have no place in the church, but because the church
is full of sinners, racial partiality is one sin that can emerge. Our
responsibilities as Christians include cooperating with the Holy Spirit
to put away all prejudice and partiality he exposes within our hearts,
gently correct partiality we encounter in our relationships, and forgive
those who have shown prejudice against us. The church is to model
unity, reconciliation, and forgiveness to a spiritually lost world.
4. Why do you think Christians have made such a positive impact
on society? [16.9]
Possible answer: First, Christians believe all people are made in God's
image, have inherent dignity, and deserve respect regardless of their
gender, size, ethnicity, age, or situation in life. Second, Christians are
motivated by love. Because they have received God's love, Christians
can be dedicated to extending love to their neighbors.
5. What is the universal church? [16.10]
The universal church consists of all people from every nation and
tongue who confess and follow Jesus as Lord, with many different
denominations, variations on doctrine, and cultural expressions. There
are people from all different ethnicities and walks of life. In the universal
church, all people are called to unity as brothers and sisters in Christ.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
1. Critique Simeon Stylites' model of spiritual solitude in relation
to the Christian's need to be in community.
Stylites' model of spiritual solitude included extreme asceticism
and a rejection of Christian community. The Bible calls us to grow
spiritually in community. We are to avoid any form of spirituality that
includes physical self-harm or causes us to permanently withdraw from
community. Being part of the local church matters.
2. What are the elements of Christian worship in community?
Christian worship includes reading and studying Scripture, listening
to a sermon or homily, prayer, communion, singing, giving thanks to
God, and giving generously to care for the needs of others.
3. What is the value of worship services and small groups?
The Christian life is meant to be lived in community. Worship services
and small groups bring us together to read and study Scripture. These
gatherings encourage and support us as we seek to understand and
apply God's Word and help us stay true to the faith fundamentals.
4. Explain the importance of prayer and the use of spiritual gifts.
Prayer together is a powerful expression of our faith in God. It helps
us think about needs besides our own, helps us grow in unity with
one another, and teaches us to depend on God and notice how he is
at work. Spiritual gifts are given to each Christian by the Holy Spirit
so we can encourage and build up the church. Spiritual gifts must be
exercised in an appropriate, loving, and orderly manner.
5. What is the significance of confession, accountability partners,
and spiritual mentors?
Confession is acknowledging our sin and being honest about ways
we have failed to love God and others. Accountability partners and
spiritual mentors help us acknowledge our sin instead of hiding it.
We can be trapped in sins that we hide in darkness. An accountability
partner helps us admit the sins we struggle with and helps us to
fight the battle against our sin. A spiritual mentor helps us grow and
improve in areas of Christian growth where we need greater maturity.
6. What is the purpose of the church?
The church is a training ground where Christians grow to be like
Christ and give and receive encouragement as we seek to follow Christ.
7. Explain the purpose of baptism and communion.
Baptism and communion proclaim our faith and tell a story about
what God has done, is doing, and will do. Baptism symbolizes dying to
sin and rising with Christ. It retells the salvation story. Communion
celebrates the body and blood of Jesus given for the forgiveness of sin.
8. How do giving and serving meet the needs of our community?
Generosity is a distinctive mark of Christian living. Christians give tithes and
offerings to the church to support the staff, care for the property, provide for
missionaries and church planting, and care for the poor. Christians also serve
together through giving of our time and talents to care for those in need.
9. Give examples of how to share the gospel.
To share the gospel, we need words and a life that reflects Christ. Our actions
and service can help raise the curiosity of unbelievers so that we can share the
gospel with them. Supporting children in impoverished countries and serving the
homeless by providing food and shelter are examples of this. Our lives should be
attractive to unbelievers, even if they don't understand us.
10. What are four specific ways the church has positively impacted society?
Caring for the sick and impoverished, honoring and protecting women and
children, promoting culture and education, and pursuing racial unity are specific
ways the church has positively impacted society.
11. What are historical examples of the church's positive impact on society?
Christians cared for the sick when plagues hit Rome in the second and third
centuries, even though pagan priests and doctors fled. Churches led racial unity
and healing efforts after killings in Rwanda and South Carolina. Schools were
established to promote learning. Christians rescued and adopted babies.
12. What are creeds, confessions, and catechisms?
Creeds help Christians grasp fundamental aspects of the faith; confessions clarity
various biblical doctrines; and catechisms provide short, memorable questions and
answers about Christian truth accompanied by Scripture references.
13. What is the difference between confession and repentance?
To confess is to acknowledge our sin and to be honest about the ways that we
have failed to love God and others. To repent is to turn from our sin and follow
a different way. Confession acknowledges sin and brings it out into the light to
be dealt with. Repentance is a change of heart and life to abstain from sin and to
reorient us toward following Jesus in that area.
14. What is the Holy Spirit's role in sharing the gospel?
The Holy Spirit empowers us to share the gospel with others.
15. What does the word catholic in the Nicene Creed mean?
This comes from the line: "I believe in one holy catholic and apostolic church."
In the Nicene Creed, the word catholic means universal. This refers to the church
throughout all times in history, in all geographic locations.
16. List the spiritual gifts discussed in Chapter 16.
The Bible includes the following spiritual gifts: prophecy, service, teaching,
encouragement, generosity, leadership, mercy, wisdom, knowledge, faith, healing,
miracles, discernment, tongues, apostleship, evangelism, and shepherding.
