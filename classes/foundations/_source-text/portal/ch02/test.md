## Short Answer — *3 points each (9 pts); complete sentences* — **hand-graded**

36. **How is the Bible like a map?** — A map gives perspective and orients you; it shows the way to your destination. The Bible shows you how to get your bearings and live well in God's world.
37. **Explain the analogy that the Bible is like a compass.** — A compass gives a fixed point of reference/direction; like a compass, the Bible points to truth.
38. **Give two examples each of general and special revelation.** — *General:* anything from creation or conscience. *Special:* God revealing himself through Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Scriptures, or miraculous events.

## Essay — *5 points each (10 pts); at least one paragraph* — **hand-graded**

39. **Why is "Truth cannot be known" self-refuting? Then explain why truth matters.** — If truth cannot be known, we couldn't know *that* claim — it contradicts itself. Truth can be known and it matters: it's the foundation for a life, telling us who we are, where we are, and who made us.
40. **Describe your own journey among the four ways of reading the Bible (skeptic, seeker, scholar, saint) — refer to at least two.** — Answers vary; must reference at least two of skeptic / seeker / scholar / saint.
