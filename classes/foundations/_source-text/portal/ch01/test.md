## Short Answer — *3 points each (18 pts); complete sentences* — **hand-graded**

34. **What is a worldview? Explain how it can be compared to a map.** — A worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions. Like a map, it is only useful if it corresponds with reality.
35. **What happens in the first act?** — God creates the universe and all that is in it, including human beings, and tells them to fill and rule the earth.
36. **What happens in the third act?** — God chooses Abraham, through whom he will bless the earth; some of Abraham's descendants become the nation of Israel.
37. **What were God's people hoping for during the interlude?** — More revelation from God (after ~400 years of silence) and a savior who would defeat their conquerors.
38. **What happens in the fourth act?** — The story of Jesus' life, death, and resurrection.
39. **What happens in the last act?** — Jesus returns to judge everyone; sin, death, and evil are destroyed; God makes a new heaven and earth and reigns forever.

## Essay — *5 points each (15 pts); at least one paragraph* — **hand-graded**

40. **Define *Christian* and describe three common misconceptions.** — A Christian is someone who follows Jesus. Three misconceptions: that it means following lots of rules; that you must be born into a Christian family or country; that it is only about heaven.
41. **Summarize what Christians believe about Jesus.** — Jesus is God (part of the Trinity) who became human and lived a sinless life; he taught and healed, died for our sins, rose again, and returned to heaven; he will come back to judge everyone. He is the Messiah, and we must trust him for forgiveness; his followers seek to imitate him.
42. **Describe what the main characters of the fifth act are supposed to do, and how long it takes.** — The main characters are Jesus' followers, the church. They are to love God and others, imitate Christ, and proclaim God's kingdom — for their whole lives, until Jesus returns.
