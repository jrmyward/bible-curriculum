## How this block works

This is the **opener of Trimester 3 and Part 3 of the course** — "Knowing & Following God." For two
trimesters we told the whole story of the Bible, Creation to New Creation. Now we turn from the
story to the God of the story: who he is (Ch 13), who he wants us to be (Ch 14), and how to walk
with him (Ch 15–18). Day 1 frames that turn before the first content lands.

The chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 7 days** — by *merging*, not cutting. The
calendar gives one short Wednesday (Day 3, ~45 min) and no sub day, so the block leans on merges
rather than resequencing around an absence:

| Day | Date | Publisher lesson(s) | Textbook | Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mon Mar 1 | Lesson 1 (13.1–13.2) + **Part 3 opener + Ch 12 test return** | ST 178–180 | Content + discussion + **Are All Believers in God Christians?** (groups) |
| 2 | Tue Mar 2 | Lesson 2 (13.3–13.4) | ST 181–183 | Content + **Random or Design?** (evidence activity) |
| 3 | **Wed Mar 3 · short** | Lesson 7 (Videos) | — | Six expert videos + questions |
| 4 | Thu Mar 4 | Lesson 3 (13.5–13.6) | ST 183–185 | Content + **Revealing God's Nature** (verse game) |
| 5 | Fri Mar 5 | Lessons 4 + 5 (13.7–13.10) *merged* | ST 185–187 | Content + **Defending the Trinity** + **Why Worship God?** |
| 6 | Mon Mar 8 | Lessons 6 + 8 + 9 (Who God Is game + Review + Study) *merged* | SM 261–263 | Synthesis game + review + Study Guide |
| 7 | Tue Mar 9 | Lesson 10 | — | **Chapter 13 Test** |

**Three merges make the compression:** the four closing Trinity subsections **13.7–13.10** combine
on Day 5 (publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the Trinity in the Old and New Testaments, why it matters, and
the conclusion); the publisher's separate Review (L8) and Study-Guide (L9) days combine on Day 6 —
the same review/study merge used all year; and the capstone **Who God Is** synthesis game (L6) folds
onto that same Day 6, where it does double duty as the review activity — it is built to make students
pull the chapter's key terms back together, which is exactly what a pre-test review needs. The
**Videos** lesson (L7) takes the short Wednesday, where a light, self-contained lesson fits the
45-minute period and videos 3–4 preview the Trinity before we teach it on Days 4–5.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 13 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies every
activity (`PS 13.2A`, `PS 13.6A`, `TR 13.4A`, `TR 13.6A`) plus the discussion questions with answers,
the video questions with answers, and the Study Guide. This plan sequences and times them; it does
not reproduce them. Discussion questions are cited by their teacher-manual location; open
[teaching-manual/ch13.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch13.md) for the full list with model

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 13.10A` (/63) is **matching (10 pts) · multiple choice (16 pts) · true/false (9 pts, the
"change the underlined word" style) · fill-in-the-blank (5 × 2 = 10 pts) · short answer (4 × 3 =
12 pts) · one essay (6 pts).** Its weight lands squarely on two things. First, the **twelve
attributes of God** — self-existent (**aseity**), spirit/incarnate, personal, eternal, omniscient,
omnipotent, omnipresent, immutable, holy, wise, righteous, loving — which the matching, most of the
multiple choice, several true/false, two fill-ins, and a short-answer question that asks students to
*list all twelve* all draw from. Second, the **Trinity** — the doctrine (one Being, three Persons),
and why **tritheism** (the egg analogy) and **modalism** (the three-states-of-water analogy) are
false — which anchors the matching, the closing multiple choice, three true/false items, a fill-in,
a short answer, and the entire **6-point essay** ("Why does the Trinity matter? Give three reasons").
Also present: **perfect being theology**, that God **reveals** himself through creation, the Bible,
and Jesus, the **Muslim view of the Trinity** (from `TR 13.4A`), and **Isaiah** as the prophet of
God the Son. Day 4 (the last attributes + the Trinity) and Day 5 (the Trinity in the Testaments +
why it matters) carry the most weight, with Day 2 (the first attributes) close behind. Day 6 review
pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Mon Mar 1 · Part 3 opener; four views of God; can we know an infinite God? (13.1–13.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) categorize the four ways people who believe in God view who he is;
(b) conclude that although we cannot know God exhaustively, we can know him truly because he reveals
himself in creation, Scripture, and Jesus. `[13.1, 13.2]`

**Materials.** The graded Ch 12 tests (to return); student textbooks; Bibles, paper, and pen for the
group activity.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:08 · Return the Chapter 12 test + open Part 3.** Hand back Ch 12 tests; walk the two or
  three items most students missed, briefly. Then frame the turn: *for two trimesters we told the
  whole story — Creation, Fall, Israel, Jesus, the Church, New Creation. Now we spend Trimester 3 on
  the God of that story: who he is, who he wants us to be, and how to follow him.* This chapter asks
  the first question: **who is God, really?**
- **0:08–0:20 · Hook — America's Four Gods (ST 178).** About 95% of Americans say they believe in
  God, but not the same God. Put the four categories on the board and keep them there: an
  **authoritative** God (engaged + judgmental), a **benevolent** God (engaged + nonjudgmental), a
  **critical** God (disengaged + judgmental), and a **distant** God (disengaged + nonjudgmental).
  Land Tozer's line: *"What comes into our minds when we think about God is the most important thing
  about us."* Our view of God both shapes and is shaped by how we live — and everyone, even atheists,
  is in that sense a theologian.
- **0:20–0:34 · Read & discuss 13.1–13.2 (manual p.270–271).** Move to the harder question: can a
  finite mind know an infinite God? Introduce **perfect being theology** — Anselm's definition of God
  as *"that than which none greater can be conceived,"* the greatest possible being. The trap to name:
  because we can't know God *exhaustively* doesn't mean we can't know him *truly* — he **reveals**
  himself through creation, the Scriptures, and most fully through Jesus (Heb 1:1–2). Use manual
  Qs 1–4: *What comes to mind when you think about God? How would believing each of the four views
  change how a person lives? What is perfect being theology — and can we know God at all? Why do you
  think God chose to reveal himself?*
- **0:34–0:56 · Activity: Are All Believers in God Christians? (four groups; manual p.271).** Groups
  first discuss *is there a difference between a Christian and someone who just believes in God?*,
  taking notes. Then each group reads an assigned passage — **James 2:19; Luke 18:9–14; John 3:1–5;
  1 John 5:3** — and reports what it says about mere belief. Close by reading **1 John 3:23–24** to
  the class and returning to the opening question. *Purpose:* the chapter is about knowing God, and
  the entry point is that knowing *about* God is not the same as knowing him.
- **0:56–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **13.3–13.4** (ST 181–183) — a dozen of God's attributes.
**Assessments.** Are-All-Believers responses (participation).
**Notes.** Culture/turn-setting day for the new trimester. Leave the four views of God and the phrase
*"know God truly, not exhaustively"* on the board through the block — the test's true/false item on
"exhaustively" keys off it.

## Day 2 — Tue Mar 2 · Who God reveals himself to be: the first attributes (13.3–13.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) determine that God is self-existent (**aseity**), personal, spirit,
and incarnate in Jesus; (b) discuss God's eternality, omniscience, omnipotence, omnipresence, and
immutability. `[13.3, 13.4]` *(High test weight — these attributes fill the matching, multiple
choice, and true/false sections.)*

**Materials.** `PS 13.2A Random or Design?` (display); the online video you previewed (ATP synthase,
DNA replication, *or* the bacterial flagellar motor); pen; student manuals.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 13.3–13.4 (manual p.272–273).** Walk the first eight attributes,
  writing the key terms on the board. **Aseity** — God is self-existent and self-sufficient; he does
  not depend on anything to be who he is, and everything depends on him (so a designer must lie behind
  the design). **Spirit and incarnate** — God is not a physical being but pure spirit (John 4:24),
  yet Jesus, who is God, became human to make him known. **Personal** — God is *a person to be known,
  not a force to be used* (contrast the Star Wars "force"). Then **eternal, omniscient, omnipotent,
  omnipresent,** and **immutable** (unchanging — the ground of trust: he won't go back on his
  promises). Use manual Qs 1–4, favoring *why aseity matters*, *how God can be both spirit and Jesus*,
  and *why an unchanging God is a trustworthy one*.
- **0:26–0:54 · Activity: Random or Design? (`PS 13.2A`; manual p.272–273).** Display nine images
  (Mars landscape, Wave Rock, Blue Horsehead Nebula, a nautilus shell, Easter Island statues…);
  students score each 1–5 for design vs. chance, then stand and sit as you count off their scores so
  the class *sees* how confidence rises toward the obviously designed images. Draw out the criteria
  they used (improbability, purposeful intent, patterns nature can't generate). Then play the molecular
  video and ask them to apply those same criteria. *Land it:* design in life is self-evident — we are
  the work of a Designer, which is exactly what aseity says about God as the source of everything.
- **0:54–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** *Which criterion convinced you most — and does design prove
  it's the God of the Bible, or only that there's a designer?*

**Homework.** Read **13.5–13.6** (ST 183–185) — the last attributes and the Trinity. *(Tomorrow's
short Wednesday is the video set — no new reading due for it.)*
**Assessments.** Random or Design? scores + discussion (participation).
**Notes.** The twelve attributes are the test's largest single target (short answer 44 asks students
to list all twelve). Keep the running list on the board and add to it Thursday. If the discussion
runs long, the video clip is the flex item — the nine-image round is the part that must survive.

## Day 4 — Thu Mar 4 · The last attributes; the Triune God (13.5–13.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) explain that God is holy, wise, righteous, and loving; (b) summarize
the **doctrine of the Trinity** — one Being in three Persons — and argue that **tritheism** and
**modalism** are false beliefs. `[13.5, 13.6]` *(Highest test weight with Day 5 — the Trinity and its
two false analogies run through matching, true/false, fill-in, short answer, and the essay.)*

**Materials.** Bibles and a pen per student (`Revealing God's Nature`).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 13.5–13.6 (manual p.274–275).** Finish the attribute list: **holy**
  (set apart, absolutely pure), **wise** (knows every implication of his actions — no unintended
  consequences), **righteous** (just and morally pure; can't be bribed, won't lie — and on the cross
  gives us his righteousness), and **loving** (*"God is love,"* 1 John 4:8 — to misunderstand this is
  to not know him). Then the chapter's hinge: the **doctrine of the Trinity** — one divine Being in
  three distinct, equal Persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit). Kill the two popular analogies carefully:
  the **egg** (shell/white/yolk are separate *parts* — that's **tritheism**, three gods) and the
  **three states of water** (one thing appearing as solid/liquid/gas over time — that's **modalism**,
  one God in changing modes). Neither fits: the three Persons are distinct, equal, and simultaneous
  (Matt 3:16–17, all three present at Jesus' baptism). Note the word *Trinity* is not in the Bible
  (Tertullian coined it), but the teaching is. Use manual Qs 1–6, spending most time on Qs 4–6.
- **0:26–0:46 · Activity: Revealing God's Nature (individual, then class; manual p.274–275).** A
  silent competition: students read eight verses (Ps 147:5; 1 Thess 5:23; Eph 2:5; Hab 1:13; Rom 8:33;
  1 Pet 5:7; Job 8:3; Isa 40:28) and label each as **holiness, wisdom, righteousness,** or **love**.
  Score together; ties break on Jeremiah 29:11 (love) and Isaiah 50:8 (righteousness). *Purpose:*
  students practice matching real Scripture to the four character attributes the test asks them to
  define.
- **0:46–0:60 · Anchor the Trinity + homework.** Before they leave, run a fast cold-call: *egg —
  which error? Water — which error? How many Beings, how many Persons?* This is the single most-tested
  distinction in the chapter; drill it now.

**Homework.** Finish reading **13.7–13.10** (ST 185–187) if you haven't, for tomorrow.
**Assessments.** Revealing God's Nature (informal — matching Scripture to attributes).
**Notes.** Tritheism and modalism are near-certain test items (matching 6/10, true/false 33/34, fill
39, short 41, essay 45). If a class needs it, the flex time is the tail of the attribute discussion —
protect the Trinity teaching and the analogy drill.

## Day 5 — Fri Mar 5 · The Trinity in both Testaments; why it matters; conclusion (13.7–13.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) use Old Testament verses to support that God is three Persons in one;
(b) identify New Testament verses that confirm God is Father, Son, and Holy Spirit; (c) give reasons
the Trinity is important to the Christian faith; (d) conclude that good theology leads to worship, not
just knowledge. `[13.7, 13.8, 13.9, 13.10]` *(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four closing
subsections. High test weight: the three reasons the Trinity matters are the essay.)*

**Materials.** `TR 13.4A Muslim View of the Trinity` — 1 per student; Bibles, textbooks, paper, and
pen.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 13.7–13.10 (manual p.276–279).** Move through the four sections,
  favoring the Trinity's biblical footing and the "so what." *Old Testament (13.7):* God speaks of
  himself as plural (Gen 1:26; Isa 6:8); the **Father** is named (Jer 3:19; Isa 63:16), the **Son** is
  prophesied by **Isaiah** as Immanuel and Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God (Isa 7:14; 9:6), and the
  **Spirit** hovers at creation and rests on the Messiah (Gen 1:2; Isa 11:2; 61:1). *New Testament
  (13.8):* all three appear together (Matt 28:19; the baptism, Matt 3:16–17), and each is called God
  (John 5:17; John 1:1 and Thomas' *"my Lord and my God,"* John 20:28; Acts 5:3–4). *Why it matters
  (13.9) — the three reasons that are the essay:* (1) a solitary God could be powerful but not
  inherently **loving** — the Father, Son, and Spirit shared love before creation, so God made us out
  of overflow, not need; (2) the **Son** shares the life of the Trinity with us — through Jesus alone
  we know God and are saved; (3) the Trinity is the **model for Christian community** — unity and
  diversity together. *Conclusion (13.10):* good theology is not just thinking more accurately about
  God; it is meant to end in **worship**. Use manual Qs from p.276–279.
- **0:26–0:48 · Activity: Defending the Trinity (`TR 13.4A`; manual p.276–277).** Students read
  `TR 13.4A Muslim View of the Trinity` together and name the difference between the Christian Trinity
  and the Muslim rejection of it (that it means three gods). Then each student writes a **150–200 word
  paragraph defending the Trinity** — one God in three Persons — using their Bible, textbook, and
  `TR 13.4A`. A few read theirs aloud. *Purpose:* this is the essay in miniature, plus the multiple-
  choice item on the Muslim view.
- **0:48–0:58 · Activity: Why Worship God? (groups; manual p.278–279).** A quick race — groups list
  as many reasons to worship God as they can (God's attributes are the seed), then cross off any
  reason another group also has; most unique reasons wins. Ties the chapter's head-knowledge back to
  its point: worship.
- **0:58–0:60 · Close & homework.** *Which of the three reasons the Trinity matters lands hardest for
  you?*

**Homework.** Begin studying for the Chapter 13 Test (Study Guide, SM 261–263; Key Terms & Verses).
**Assessments.** Defending-the-Trinity paragraph (completion — maps to the essay); Why Worship God?
(participation).
**Notes.** The block's fullest day — two sections and two activities. If a class runs slow, **Why
Worship God?** is the flex item; protect the discussion of the three reasons and the defense
paragraph, which are the essay and a short-answer target. Have students keep the paragraph — it is a
ready-made essay outline for Tuesday.

## Day 6 — Mon Mar 8 · Who God Is synthesis game + Review + Study Guide (Lessons 6 + 8 + 9, merged)

**Objectives.** Students synthesize the chapter's key terms into a single picture of who God is,
consolidate the chapter, and self-assess against the Study Guide; lock in the **Key Terms** (perfect
being theology, aseity, doctrine of the Trinity, tritheism, modalism) and **Key Verses** (Ps 90:2;
Isa 46:9–10; Jer 23:23–24; Matt 3:16–17; 1 John 4:8). *(High test weight — every Key Term is a
matching or fill-in item.)*

**Materials.** `TR 13.6A Chart and Word Sneak Tracker` — 1 per group of 4–5; `PS 13.6A Who God Is`
(display); `PS 13.8A Chapter 13 Review` (display); student manuals (Study Guide, SM 261–263);
textbooks. *(Computers only if you run the optional Names of God research.)*

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:30 · Activity: Who God Is (`TR 13.6A` + `PS 13.6A`; manual p.280–281).** Groups of 4–5 fill
  a three-column chart — **Perfect Being Theology · Aseity · Doctrine of the Trinity** — with a
  definition, characteristics, examples, and false ideas for each, working the terms **modalism** and
  **tritheism** in appropriately. A "Word Sneak" secretary tallies each time the group lands a target
  term in an answer (any form counts). Give ~22 minutes to build, then display `PS 13.6A` and have
  groups count how many bolded terms they matched; most words used wins. This is the review activity:
  it forces students to reassemble the exact vocabulary the matching and fill-in sections test. *(In a
  full 45–50-min slot the publisher runs a longer version; here it is timed to leave room for the
  Study Guide.)*
- **0:30–0:42 · Chapter Review (`PS 13.8A`).** Walk the review slides as a class; let students read
  them aloud. Hit the heavy-weight items: the **twelve attributes**, the **Trinity** (one Being, three
  Persons), and why **tritheism** (egg) and **modalism** (water) fail.
- **0:42–0:56 · Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses (manual p.285–287).** Students work the Study Guide;
  confirm the five Key Terms and five Key Verses. The manual's 18 study-guide questions with answers
  are the answer key.
- **0:56–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: 63 points — matching, multiple choice, true/false, fill-in,
  short answer, and a three-reason essay on the Trinity. *"If you can list the twelve attributes,
  define the Trinity, and say why the egg and the water analogies both fail, you're most of the way
  there."*

**Homework.** Study for the test.
**Assessments.** Who God Is chart (synthesis) + Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** *Optional — Names of God (publisher L8; manual p.284):* if a class finishes the chart
early or you want a devotional close, pairs research the meanings of twelve names of God
(Jehovah-Jireh, Jehovah-Rapha, El Shaddai…) and a verse for each, and consider how a name can shape a
prayer. It's enrichment, not tested — use it only if time allows. If Day 4 (attributes + Trinity) or
Day 5 (the Testaments) felt rushed, reteach here; they are the test's center of gravity.

## Day 7 — Tue Mar 9 · Chapter 13 Test (Lesson 10)

**Objective.** Demonstrate mastery of Chapter 13.

**Materials.** `TR 13.10A Chapter 13 Test` — 1 per student (from [portal/ch13](../_source-text/portal/ch13/));
answer key for grading.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:04 · Settle & distribute.** Clear desks; hand out the test (63 pts).
- **0:04–0:52 · Test.** Students complete independently. Early finishers begin reading **14.1–14.2**
  (Chapter 14).
- **0:52–0:60 · Collect & bridge.** Collect tests. Preview Ch 14: *"Who God Wants Us to Be" — we've

**Homework.** Read **14.1–14.2** (ST 188–191) — start of Chapter 14.
**Assessments.** **Chapter 13 Test — collected and graded** (`TR 13.10A`, /63). Trimester 3 grade.
**Notes.** Return and review the test early in the Ch 14 block so misconceptions (especially
tritheism vs. modalism, and the twelve attributes) don't harden.
