## Dear Substitute — the whole day in one paragraph

First, students take a quick **"What Do You Know?"** Bible quiz on paper (three questions) and self-check as you read the answers — the class tallies how it did against the national numbers on the board. ## Part 1 — "What Do You Know?" quiz (self-check)

Students answer on paper, then self-check as you read the answers:

1. **Name the first five books of the Bible, in order.** → Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy
2. **Name the four Gospels.** → Matthew, Mark, Luke, John
3. **Is the Bible the literal and inspired Word of God?** (opinion — no wrong answer)

Then compare to the national numbers on the board: only **43%** of Americans can name the first five books; **45%** can name all four Gospels; (Gallup 2022) **20%** call the Bible the literal Word of God, **49%** inspired, **29%** a book of fables. *Point: this chapter builds the case that the Bible is the inspired Word of God.*
