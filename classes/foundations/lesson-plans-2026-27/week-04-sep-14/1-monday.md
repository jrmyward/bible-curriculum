## Supplies & Prep

- One **handheld whiteboard** (or paper pad), **marker**, and **eraser** per group of 3–5

## Learning Objectives (teacher)

1. Differentiate truth from opinion.
2. Name the real-life cost of treating an opinion as truth. *(Reinforces the chapter's most-tested idea — truth vs. relativism — through play.)*

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:08 · Set up (manual p.36)

- Groups of 3–5; each names a **secretary** (writes answers) and a **spokesperson**. Display `PS 2.6A`; read the definitions of truth and opinion (slides 2–3).

### 0:08–0:48 · Play

- Each slide shows three statements — **two truths and one opinion**. Groups get 60 seconds to label them A/B/C with **T** or **O**; answers concealed until called.
- Reveal, score a point per correct group, and — the real work — for each slide **discuss:** *How would believing this truth strengthen someone's character? What could go wrong if someone treated this opinion as truth? How do you even know it's true?* Keep it moving through the slides.

### 0:48–0:58 · Debrief

- The payoff question: *where in your life is it costly to confuse an opinion with a truth?* Tie back to relativism from Day 5.

### 0:58–0:60 · Reward + homework

- Recognize the top group; assign study.
