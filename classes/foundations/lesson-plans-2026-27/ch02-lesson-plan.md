## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 8 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
The calendar deals the hard card up front: the block opens on a **sub day** (Sep 3, teacher
absence), and then two no-school days (Sep 4, Labor Day Sep 7) fall before Day 2. So Day 1 has
to be fully self-contained — which the publisher's Videos lesson is built for. We move it to the
front:

| Day | Date | Publisher lesson(s) | Textbook | Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Thu Sep 3 · SUB** | Lesson 1 activity + **Lesson 7 (Videos)** | ST 20–22 | Independent quiz + video + questions |
| 2 | Tue Sep 8 | Lessons 1 + 2 (2.1–2.4) *discussion* | ST 20–25 | Content + **Not Just a Book of Instructions** (pairs) |
| 3 | Wed Sep 9 | Lesson 3 (2.5–2.6) | ST 25–28 | Content + **God's Revelation** (A–Z game) |
| 4 | Thu Sep 10 | Lesson 4 (2.7–2.8) | ST 28–30 | Content + **Bible Is Rooted in History** (research) |
| 5 | Fri Sep 11 | Lesson 5 (2.9–2.10) | ST 30–31 | Content + **Why Truth Matters** (role groups) |
| 6 | Mon Sep 14 | Lesson 6 | — | **Two Truths and One Opinion** (full-period game) |
| 7 | Tue Sep 15 | Lessons 8 + 9 (Review + Study) *merged* | SM 41–43 | Defense activity + review + Study Guide |
| 8 | Wed Sep 16 | Lesson 10 | — | **Chapter 2 Test** |

**Two merges make the compression:** the publisher's Videos day (L7) folds onto the front sub
day alongside Lesson 1's "What Do You Know?" quiz, and the separate Review (L8) and Study-Guide
(L9) days combine on Day 7 — the same review/study merge used in Chapter 1. The Lesson-1 and
Lesson-2 *discussions* then share Day 2, since Day 1 already carried Lesson 1's activity.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 2 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies
every activity (`PS 2.2A`, `PS 2.6A`, and the four in-manual activities) plus the discussion
questions with answers, the video questions with answers, and the Study Guide. This plan
sequences and times them; it does not reproduce them. Discussion questions are cited by their
teacher-manual location; open [teaching-manual/ch02.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch02.md)
for the full list with model answers.

### Print / prep checklist for the block (from the teacher manual)

  famous actor · a liberal woman in NYC · a teen in a Muslim country · a teen in a Christian school*
- **Day 6:** `PS 2.6A Two Truths and One Opinion`; one handheld whiteboard (or paper pad), marker,
  and eraser per group of 3–5
- **Day 7:** display `PS 2.8A Chapter 2 Review`; student manuals (Study Guide, SM 41–43); textbooks
- **Day 8:** `TR 2.10A Chapter 2 Test` — 1 per student

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 2.10A` (≈/60) is multiple choice + true/false + fill-in-the-blank + short answer + two essays.
Its weight lands on: **general vs. special revelation** (definitions + examples); the **canon** and
the **three NT criteria** (apostolicity, orthodoxy, pedigree); **relativism, self-refuting claims,
and the correspondence theory of truth** (why truth matters); the Bible's **organization** (66
books = 39 OT + 27 NT, the Torah, the Gospels); the **four ways people read** the Bible (skeptic,
scholar, seeker, saint); **inspiration vs. inerrancy**; the **map and compass** analogies; and the
**Key Verses** (Ps 19:1–4, Rom 1:19–20, Rom 2:14–15, 2 Tim 3:16). Day 3 (revelation + canon) and
Day 5 (truth/relativism) carry the most test weight, and Day 7 review pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Thu Sep 3 · **SUB DAY** · Bible-knowledge check + video set (Lesson 1 activity + Lesson 7)

*Teacher absence (personal). Fully self-contained — no content expertise needed. Falls the day
after the Chapter 1 test and just before two no-school days, so it stands alone cleanly. A
standalone `/generate-substitute-plan` version can be produced; this section is sub-ready as written.*

**Objective.** Students gauge what they already know about the Bible, then expand the chapter's
ideas through four expert videos. `[2.1]`

**Materials.** Paper and pen per student; the four videos queued (see list); student manuals
(video questions, SM 37–39) or a printed video-question sheet; something to play video + audio.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:05 · Settle & set up.** Sub takes attendance; students read the objective on p.17 of
  the student textbook.
- **0:05–0:25 · Activity: What Do You Know? (manual p.27).** On paper, students answer three
  questions cold: *name the first five books of the Bible in order* (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus,
  Numbers, Deuteronomy); *name the four Gospels* (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John); *is the Bible the
  literal and inspired Word of God?* The sub reads the answers; students self-check and the class
  tallies percentages, then compares to the national numbers on the board: only **43%** of
  Americans can name the first five books, **45%** can name all four Gospels, and (2022 Gallup)
  **20%** call the Bible the literal Word of God vs. **49%** inspired, **29%** fiction. *Point:
  this chapter builds the case that the Bible is the inspired Word of God.*
- **0:25–0:53 · Videos + questions (Lesson 7; manual p.37–39).** Before each clip, students read
  that video's questions so they know what to listen for; play it (replay once if needed); give
  2–3 minutes to write answers; move on. The four clips total ~13.5 min of footage:
  1. *How Did We Get the Bible?* (Wallace, 3:37)
  2. *Is the Bible Applicable?* (Shlemon, 4:34)
  3. *Is the Bible's Impact Important?* (Shlemon, 1:59)
  4. *Isn't Christianity a Bunch of Rules and Regulations?* (Shlemon, 3:28)
- **0:53–0:60 · Collect.** Gather the quiz papers and video-question sheets.

**Sub instructions (verbatim-ready).** *"Have students answer the three Bible questions on paper,
then self-check as you read the answers and tally the class percentages against the national
numbers on the board. Then, before each video, have students read that video's questions so they
know what to listen for. Play the video (replay once if needed), give 2–3 minutes to write
answers, and move to the next. Collect the quiz papers and the video answers at the end."* Model
answers for the videos are in [teaching-manual/ch02.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch02.md)
(**manual p.37–39**) for grading.

**Homework.** Read **2.3–2.4** (ST 23–25) for Tuesday.
**Assessments.** Video question sheet — **collected** (first graded work of the chapter).
**Notes.** Streaming instructions are on the inside back cover of the teacher manual. If streaming
fails, the sub should have students read 2.1–2.2 and write a paragraph: *"What is one way the
Bible has shaped the world, and one way people misread it?"* — a safe fallback needing no tech.

## Day 2 — Tue Sep 8 · The Bible's influence, the four readers & how it's arranged (2.1–2.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) name four ways the Bible has influenced the world; (b) identify
and explain the four ways people read the Bible (skeptic, scholar, seeker, saint); (c) describe how
the Bible is arranged and explain the map and compass analogies. `[2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.4]`
*(Carries Lessons 1 and 2 — Day 1 already ran Lesson 1's activity.)*

**Materials.** `PS 2.2A Books of the Bible` (display); student manuals (Not Just a Book of
Instructions activity).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Welcome back & debrief.** Two-minute recap of the sub-day quiz results and the
  videos: *what surprised you about how few people can name the first five books?*
- **0:06–0:24 · Discuss 2.1–2.2 (manual p.26–27).** Four ways the Bible has influenced the world
  (world literature; our nation; Western civilization; the mark of an educated person). Then the
  **four readers** — *skeptic* (unsure it's true or relevant), *scholar* (studies it deeply — as
  literature or to know God), *seeker* (reads for life's big questions), *saint* (reads to know and
  obey God). *Ask:* which are you right now? Use manual Qs 1–4.
- **0:24–0:44 · Read & discuss 2.3–2.4 (manual p.28–29).** Display `PS 2.2A`: the Bible is 66
  books — **39 OT + 27 NT** — divided into Law (the **Torah**, first five books), history, poetry,
  prophecy; the NT holds the **Gospels**, Acts, letters, and Revelation. Name the **Apocrypha** and
  why it isn't in all Bibles. Then the two pictures that reframe the whole book: the Bible as a
  **map** (perspective — it orients you and shows the way) and a **compass** (a fixed reference
  point outside yourself; drift a little and you end up lost). Use manual Qs 1–7.
- **0:44–0:58 · Activity: Not Just a Book of Instructions (pairs; manual p.29).** Pairs write a
  short *how-to guide* for using the Bible in daily life — including how to use the index and a
  concordance. A few pairs share; note common themes.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **2.5–2.6** (ST 25–28) — revelation and the canon.
**Assessments.** How-to guide (participation).
**Notes.** Keep `PS 2.2A` up through the block if you can — students refer back to the OT/NT split
all year.

## Day 3 — Wed Sep 9 · Revelation & the canon (2.5–2.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) distinguish **general** from **special** revelation with examples;
(b) explain how the **canon** was formed; (c) name the **three criteria** used to select the NT books.
`[2.5, 2.6]` *(High test weight — revelation and the three criteria are all over the test.)*

**Materials.** One sheet of paper and a pen per student.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:14 · Content: revelation & canon (mini-lecture, guided notes; manual p.30–31).**
  God is **personal** (mind, will, emotions), so he *reveals* himself. Two kinds of revelation:
  **general** — what everyone can know of God from **creation and conscience** (Ps 19; Rom 1–2); and
  **special** — God's unique self-disclosure through **Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Scriptures, and
  miraculous events**. Then the **canon** (the collection of writings acknowledged as genuine and
  authoritative): the OT canon was settled by Jesus' day; the NT was recognized by the second
  century using **three criteria** — write these on the board and keep them there:
  1. **Apostolicity** — written by an apostle or an apostle's associate?
  2. **Orthodoxy** — does it match the apostles' teaching?
  3. **Pedigree** — was it accepted early and widely across the churches?
- **0:14–0:20 · Quick check.** *Is a sunset general or special revelation? A verse of Scripture?
  Your conscience nagging you? Jesus?* Fast cold-call to lock the distinction.
- **0:20–0:43 · Activity: God's Revelation A–Z (manual p.31).** Students write the alphabet down
  the page and race (10 min) to name one thing in creation for each letter that reveals God's
  nature (A: atom, B: baboon…). Then go A→Z: one point for each item **no one else** wrote; students
  may defend an unusual answer. High scorer wins. *Debrief:* what did the whole list say about God?
- **0:43–0:45 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **2.7–2.8** (ST 28–30) — inspiration, inerrancy, and truth.
**Assessments.** A–Z game (informal — who can connect creation to God's character).
**Notes.** The three criteria are a near-certain test item. If the mini-lecture runs long, the A–Z
game is the flex item — it can shrink to 12 minutes.

## Day 4 — Thu Sep 10 · Is God's Word vs. contains God's words; inspiration & inerrancy (2.7–2.8)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) explain why saying the Bible *contains* God's words is false;
(b) give three reasons to trust the whole Bible as God's Word; (c) state the **correspondence theory
of truth** and how it fits the Bible. `[2.7, 2.8]`

**Materials.** Computers/tablets — one per student, pair, or group depending on how many are available.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:22 · Read & discuss 2.7–2.8 (manual p.32–33).** The key distinction: the Bible *is*
  God's Word (his revelation), not merely *contains* God's words — because "contains" puts a human
  in charge of deciding which parts count. Then **inspiration** (God enabled people to write
  Scripture — the writers claim messages from the Spirit 3,000+ times) and **inerrancy** (the Bible
  is without error in the original writings; totally trustworthy in all it teaches). Three reasons
  for confidence: it is **inspired**, **rooted in history**, and **inerrant**. Close with the
  **correspondence theory of truth** — a statement is true when it *corresponds to reality*; the
  Bible holds up a mirror to the world. Use manual Qs 1–5.
- **0:22–0:52 · Activity: The Bible Is Rooted in History (research; manual p.33).** Individually,
  in pairs, or in groups, students find **two OT** and **two NT** historical facts that have been
  corroborated — recording each fact, how it was confirmed, and the source. Seed with the manual's
  examples if they stall: the **Tel Dan** "House of David" inscription; the walls of **Jericho**;
  **Tacitus** on Jesus; the five-colonnaded **Pool of Bethesda** (John 5). Share a few.
- **0:52–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** *Does historical confirmation prove the Bible's spiritual
  claims? What does it establish, and what does it not?*

**Homework.** Read **2.9–2.10** (ST 30–31) — relativism and why truth matters.
**Assessments.** Rooted-in-History research (completion — fact, proof, source for each).
**Notes.** If devices are short, run this as small groups sharing screens — two or three good facts
per group beats four rushed ones.

## Day 5 — Fri Sep 11 · Relativism, self-refuting claims & why truth matters (2.9–2.10)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) define **relativism** and explain why it's false; (b) explain why
*"there is no truth"* is a **self-refuting** claim; (c) say why truth matters and what the Bible's
purpose is. `[2.9, 2.10]` *(High test weight — relativism, self-refutation, and truth run through
the fill-in-the-blank and essay sections.)*

**Materials.** The five prepared role cards (see checklist).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:07 · Warm-up.** Put on the board: *"That's true for you, but not for me."* — is that a
  statement everyone can live by? Surface a few takes; name **relativism**.
- **0:07–0:28 · Read & discuss 2.9–2.10 (manual p.34–35).** **Relativism** — truth/morality is
  relative to the person, society, or era — collapses on itself: *"There is no truth"* would itself
  have to be a truth, so it's **self-refuting**. Why truth matters: without it we're lost; truth
  tells us who we are, where we came from, and whose we are. Then the Bible's **purpose** — God's
  revelation, calling us to examine our lives before our Creator. Distinguish an **objective** truth
  claim (holds regardless of belief; based on facts) from a **subjective** one (changes with feeling
  or opinion). Use manual Qs 1–4.
- **0:28–0:53 · Activity: Why Truth Matters (role groups; manual p.35).** Five groups each get a
  role card and a topic (cheating, stealing, drugs). Each group role-plays how their character would
  apply relativism to the topic; the class then names the Bible's **objective** truth claim on that
  topic and explains why the relativist version fails. Work through all five.
- **0:53–0:60 · Close & homework.** *One place you've heard relativism this week — an ad, a show, a
  conversation?*

**Homework.** Begin studying (Study Guide, SM 41–43). Optional: skim 2.9–2.10 again.
**Assessments.** Why Truth Matters (participation — objective vs. subjective claims).
**Notes.** Freshmen will conflate "I have a right to my opinion" with "all truth is opinion." Draw
that line explicitly — it's exactly what the essay question tests.

## Day 6 — Mon Sep 14 · Two Truths and One Opinion (Lesson 6)

**Objective.** Students differentiate truth from opinion and name the real-life cost of treating an
opinion as truth. *(Reinforces the chapter's most-tested idea — truth vs. relativism — through play.)*

**Materials.** `PS 2.6A Two Truths and One Opinion` (slides); one handheld whiteboard (or paper pad),
marker, and eraser per group of 3–5.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:08 · Set up (manual p.36).** Groups of 3–5; each names a **secretary** (writes answers)
  and a **spokesperson**. Display `PS 2.6A`; read the definitions of truth and opinion (slides 2–3).
- **0:08–0:48 · Play.** Each slide shows three statements — two truths and one opinion. Groups get
  60 seconds to label them A/B/C with **T** or **O**, answers concealed until called. Reveal, score a
  point per correct group, and — this is the real work — for each slide **discuss**: *How would
  believing this truth strengthen someone's character? What could go wrong if someone treated this
  opinion as truth? How do you even know it's true?* Keep the discussion moving through the slides.
- **0:48–0:58 · Debrief.** The payoff question: *where in your life is it costly to confuse an
  opinion with a truth?* Tie back to relativism from Day 5.
- **0:58–0:60 · Reward + homework.** Recognize the top group; assign study.

**Homework.** Study for the test (Study Guide, SM 41–43; Key Terms & Verses).
**Assessments.** Game participation (informal).
**Notes.** The discussion between slides matters more than the score — don't let it become a pure
quiz-show. If a group defends a wrong label well, mine it; that's where relativism gets exposed.

## Day 7 — Tue Sep 15 · Defense activity + review + Study Guide (Lessons 8 + 9, merged)

**Objectives.** Students consolidate the chapter and self-assess against the Study Guide; lock in
the **Key Terms** (Apocrypha, general/special revelation, canon, inspiration, inerrancy, relativism)
and **Key Verses** (Ps 19:1–4, Rom 1:19–20, Rom 2:14–15, 2 Tim 3:16). *(High test weight.)*

**Materials.** Display `PS 2.8A Chapter 2 Review`; student manuals (Study Guide, SM 41–43); textbooks.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:22 · Activity: The Bible Is the Word of God (five groups; manual p.40).** Each group
  takes one textbook topic — *the Bible in history · not merely a book of instructions · what
  "revelation" means · how we got the Bible · what "the Word of God" means* — and writes **five
  bullet points** they'd use to show a skeptic the Bible is God's true Word. Each group presents;
  others flag the most compelling points. *Purpose:* students rehearse the chapter as an argument,
  not a list.
- **0:22–0:40 · Chapter Review (`PS 2.8A`).** Walk the review slides. Hit the heavy-weight items
  hard: **general vs. special revelation**, the **three NT criteria**, **relativism / self-refuting
  / correspondence**, and the **66-book** organization.
- **0:40–0:55 · Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses (manual p.41–43).** Students work the Study Guide;
  confirm the seven Key Terms and four Key Verses. The manual's study-guide questions with answers
  are the answer key.
- **0:55–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: multiple choice + true/false + fill-in + short answer +
  two essays. *"If you can define general and special revelation, name the three criteria, and
  explain why 'there is no truth' refutes itself, you're most of the way there."*

**Homework.** Study for the test.
**Assessments.** Defense bullets + Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** If Day 3 (revelation/canon) or Day 5 (truth) felt rushed, this is the place to reteach —
they are the test's center of gravity.
