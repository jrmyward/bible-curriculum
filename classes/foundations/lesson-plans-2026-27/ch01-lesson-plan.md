## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 8 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
The publisher's Chapter 1 is already activity-rich (only about half the lessons are content
delivery), so the compression preserves the rhythm:

| Day | Date | Publisher lesson(s) | Textbook | Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mon Aug 24 | Lesson 1 (1.1–1.2) + **first-day orientation** | ST 4–7 | Content + discussion + index-card activity |
| 2 | Tue Aug 25 | Lesson 2 (1.3–1.4) | ST 7–9 | Content + **Bible Scavenger Hunt** (game) |
| 3 | Wed Aug 26 | Lesson 3 (1.5–1.6) | ST 9–11 | Content + **Producer's List** (die game) |
| 4 | Thu Aug 27 | Lessons 4 + 5 (1.7–1.10) *merged* | ST 12–16 | Discussion + **Being a Christian** (pairs) |
| 5 | **Fri Aug 28 · SUB** | Lesson 7 (Videos) | — | Independent video + questions |
| 6 | Mon Aug 31 | Lesson 6 (Mapmaking) | — | Full hands-on group activity |
| 7 | Tue Sep 1 | Lessons 8 + 9 (Review + Study) *merged* | SM 21–26 | Review games + Study Guide |
| 8 | Wed Sep 2 | Lesson 10 | — | **Chapter 1 Test** |

**Two merges make the compression:** the four short preview subsections 1.7–1.10 combine on
Day 4, and the publisher's separate Review (L8) and Study-Guide (L9) days combine on Day 7.
The Beliefs & Behavior Brainstorm (publisher L4 activity) is the one thing set aside — offered
as an optional Day 4 warm-up below.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 1 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies
every activity (`TR 1.2A`, `TR 1.5A`, `PS 1.6A`, `PS 1.8A`) and the discussion questions with
answers. This plan sequences and times them; it does not reproduce them. Discussion questions
are cited by their teacher-manual location; open [teaching-manual/ch01.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch01.md)
for the full list with model answers.

### Print / prep checklist for the block (from the teacher manual)

- **Day 2:** `TR 1.2A Bible Scavenger Hunt` — 1 per group of 4–5 (+ answer key for you); class Bibles
- **Day 3:** one die (2–3 dice if >8 students)

- **Day 7:** three index cards per student; display `PS 1.8A Chapter 1 Review`

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 1.10A` (/87) weights: **matching the six acts + interlude** to their names (Creation, Fall,
Redemption Initiated/Israel, Second Temple, Redemption Accomplished/Jesus, Restoration
Initiated/Church, Restoration Accomplished/New Creation); worldview definition & examples; the
conditions (and non-conditions) of becoming a Christian; who Jesus is; why the Bible matters.
Day 3 (the acts) and Day 7 (review) carry the most test weight — spend time there.

## Day 1 — Mon Aug 24 · First day + worldview & being a Christian (1.1–1.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) define *worldview* and give examples; (b) explain what it means
to be a Christian and name three common misconceptions. `[1.1, 1.2]`

**Materials.** Syllabus handout; one index card per student; the opening question on the board:
*"How do you find direction when you're lost?"*

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Welcome & attendance.** Greet at the door; seating; bathroom/water norms.
- **0:06–0:20 · Course orientation.** What Foundations *is*: the first of four Summit books —
  this year we lay the foundation (what a worldview is, what the Bible is and why we trust it,
  the whole story of the Bible, and how to follow God). What it is *not*: a memorize-and-forget
  class. How you're graded: chapter tests, in-class activities, participation. One line to leave
  them with: *"By June you'll be able to tell the whole story of the Bible and say why it's yours."*
- **0:20–0:29 · Hook — the San Francisco map (ST 4).** Read/tell the textbook's opening: lost in
  a city, a dead phone, and the wrong map (Detroit) won't help. *A worldview is your map for life.*
  Land the definition: **a worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of
  the world and guides our decisions and actions.**
- **0:29–0:45 · Read & discuss 1.1–1.2.** Students read 1.1–1.2 silently or aloud. Then discuss —
  use teacher-manual Qs 1–6 (manual p.4–5): *What is a worldview? How would you describe yours?
  What did you think being a Christian meant?* Name the **three misconceptions** (born into it /
  keeping rules / only about heaven) and the true center: **a Christian is a follower of Jesus.**
  *Teacher move:* these are reflective, not right-answer, questions — "I don't know yet" is fine
  on Day 1. Your job is to get them talking.
- **0:45–0:60 · Activity: Curious About… (index cards; manual p.5).** Front of card: *"What from today stirred
  your curiosity?"* Back: *"What do you hope to learn in this class?"* Pairs share their cards, then
  a few share with the room. Collect or let them keep (revisit in May).

**Homework.** Read **1.3–1.4** (ST 7–9) for tomorrow.
**Assessments.** None (index cards optional review).
**Notes.** Culture-setting day — don't rush content. Watch who engages vs. sits silent for later
pairings.

## Day 2 — Tue Aug 25 · The Bible & how to read it (1.3–1.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) articulate why the Bible is foundational for Christians; (b) explain
two common mistakes in reading the Bible (proof-texting; making the Bible itself the goal). `[1.3, 1.4]`

**Materials.** `TR 1.2A Bible Scavenger Hunt` (1 per group of 4–5) + answer key; class Bibles.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:07 · Warm-up.** *"Should an idea be dismissed just because it's old?"* — surface a few
  answers; name **chronological snobbery** (manual Q2, p.6).
- **0:07–0:28 · Read & discuss 1.3–1.4.** The Bible as God's written revelation — 66 books, many
  authors, one story; we place ourselves *under* its authority. Then the two mistakes: **proof
  texting** (ripping verses from context) and **making the Bible the end** rather than God. Use
  manual Qs 1–5 (p.6–7).
- **0:28–0:54 · Activity: Bible Scavenger Hunt (`TR 1.2A`; manual p.7).** Groups of 4–5; name your group;
  race to answer the 25 Bible-navigation questions using a physical Bible. Check with the answer
  key; fastest correct group wins. *Purpose:* students get their hands in the actual book and learn
  its layout (Old/New Testament, book order) — the literacy the rest of the year assumes.
- **0:54–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** What surprised you about how the Bible is arranged? Which
  book was hardest to find?

**Homework.** Read **1.5–1.6** (ST 9–11) — the six acts of the story.
**Assessments.** Scavenger Hunt (participation/completion).
**Notes.** If Bibles are short, groups can share or use a phone Bible app — but a physical Bible is
the point of the activity.

## Day 3 — Wed Aug 26 · The story of the Bible in six acts (1.5–1.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) describe the first three acts + interlude; (b) summarize the final
three acts. `[1.5, 1.6]` *(Highest test weight — the matching section keys off this.)*

**Materials.** One die (or 2–3 for large classes); the six acts written on the board.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:12 · The six acts (guided notes / mini-lecture).** Walk the arc, writing each on the
  board as you go — this is the spine of the whole course and the test's matching section:
  1. **Creation** · 2. **The Fall** · 3. **Redemption Initiated — Israel** · *Interlude: Second
  Temple* · 4. **Redemption Accomplished — Jesus** · 5. **Restoration Initiated — The Church** ·
  6. **Restoration Accomplished — New Creation.** Keep it to a sentence each (manual Qs 2–6, p.8).
- **0:12–0:20 · Discuss.** *In which act do we live now?* (Act 5 — "already, not yet.") *How does
  the story end?* (Act 6 — Jesus returns, all things new.)
- **0:20–0:42 · Activity: Producer's List (die game).** Class in a circle. A student rolls, says
  *"I'm the producer of a Bible drama. In my show, for Act ___ I need ___,"* naming an object/person
  tied to that act (e.g., Act 2 → an apple). Next student repeats the chain and adds one; a wrong
  act or item is out. Reinforces the act numbering through play (manual p.9).
- **0:42–0:45 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **1.7–1.10** (ST 12–16) — God, self, becoming like Christ, love. *(Two sections
tomorrow — it's a lighter, reflective read.)*
**Assessments.** Producer's List (informal — who can hold the six acts).
**Notes.** Leave the six acts on the board through the block if you can; students will see them
again every chapter of T1–T2.

## Day 4 — Thu Aug 27 · Knowing God & self; becoming like Christ; love (1.7–1.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) discuss why we must know God and ourselves; (b) conclude that
becoming like Christ is a lifelong process; (c) express why love is a Christian's primary calling.
`[1.7, 1.8, 1.9]` *(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four short preview subsections.)*

**Materials.** `TR 1.5A Being a Christian` (1 per pair).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Warm-up: Beliefs & Behavior Brainstorm (publisher L4 activity; manual p.11).** T-chart on the
  board — *Beliefs Affect Behavior* / *Behavior Affects Beliefs*. Seed each side (trust fall;
  gratitude journal), then have students add examples. Sets up why knowing *and living* the faith
  both matter.
- **0:10–0:32 · Read & discuss 1.7–1.10.** Move through the four reflective previews: knowing God
  (why *"the fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom,"* Prov 9:10) and knowing ourselves
  (identity — image-bearers); becoming like Christ as a **lifelong process** helped by spiritual
  disciplines; **love as the Christian's primary calling**; and the conclusion's four invitations
  (locate yourself re: the faith; stay open; discuss with classmates; pray). Use manual Qs from
  p.11–12 — pick 5–6, favor 1.8 (Christlikeness) and 1.9 (love).
- **0:32–0:55 · Activity: Being a Christian (`TR 1.5A`, pairs; manual p.13).** Pairs read 10 quotations on what
  it means to be a Christian (Metaxas, Corrie ten Boom, Billy Graham, Hitchens, Wilberforce…) and
  relate each to the misconceptions / definition from 1.2. Share out the ones that provoked debate.
  *Purpose:* students hear a range of voices — believers and skeptics — and sharpen the real
  definition against the caricatures.
- **0:55–0:60 · Homework + sub-day briefing.** Tell students tomorrow is a **sub day**: they'll
  watch six short videos and answer questions independently — it counts like any class day.

**Homework.** None required; optional — skim the Chapter 1 Study Guide (SM 21–23).
**Assessments.** Being a Christian responses (participation).
**Notes.** This day merges two publisher lessons (1.7–1.10). The 60 minutes now hold both the
Beliefs & Behavior warm-up *and* the Being a Christian discussion — if a given class runs slow,
the warm-up is the flex item.

## Day 5 — Fri Aug 28 · **SUB DAY** · Worldview video set (Lesson 7)

*Teacher absence (personal). Fully self-contained — no content expertise needed. A standalone
`/generate-substitute-plan` version can be produced; this section is sub-ready as written.*

**Objective.** Students expand and reinforce the chapter's ideas through six expert videos.

**Materials.** The six videos queued (see list); student manuals (video questions) or a printed
video-question sheet; something to play video + audio.

**Schedule (60 min).** Play each video, then give students time to read the next set of questions
and write answers before playing the next video (the six clips total ~13 min of footage):

1. *What Is a Worldview, and Why Does It Matter?* (Myers, 1:38)
2. *How Can Christians Become More Intentional about Their Worldview?* (Stonestreet, 1:28)
3. *How Should We Begin Studying Worldview?* (Sherrard, 2:02)
4. *Is Christianity Just a List of Rules to Follow?* (Myers, 1:59)
5. *How Can We Overcome Apathy toward Christianity?* (Wallace, 3:07)
6. *How Can Christians Portray the Fulfilling Life Jesus Gives?* (Heard, 3:15)

Budget ~0:00–0:48 for the six videos + question writing. Then **0:48–0:58 · written reflection**:
*"Of everything you heard today, which idea do you most want to be true, and why?"* (half page).
**0:58–0:60 · collect.**

**Sub instructions (verbatim-ready).** *"Before each video, have students read that video's
questions so they know what to listen for. Play the video (replay once if needed). Give 2–3
minutes to write answers. Move to the next. After the sixth video, have students write a
half-page reflection on the question on the board. Collect the answer sheets and reflections at
the end."* Model answers are in [teaching-manual/ch01.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch01.md)
(**manual pp.15–17** — the full Videos lesson) for grading.

**Assessments.** Video question sheet + reflection — **collected** (first graded work of the year).
**Notes.** Streaming instructions are on the inside back cover of the teacher manual. If streaming
fails, the sub should have students read 1.1–1.6 again and write a paragraph: *"What is the story
the Bible tells?"* — a safe fallback needing no tech.

## Day 6 — Mon Aug 31 · Mapmaking synthesis (Lesson 6)

**Objective.** Students organize the chapter's concepts by building a visual map of the Christian
worldview.

**Materials.** `PS 1.6A Mapmaking` (preview beforehand); poster board, sticky notes, markers per
group; scratch paper.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Set up (`PS 1.6A`; manual p.14).** Display the slides; explain: in groups of 4–5, build a map of
  the Christian worldview — either a **geographical map** (title, direction, legend, scale, labels)
  or a **mind map** (title, labels, showing relative importance and relationships of ideas).
- **0:10–0:38 · Build.** Groups brainstorm on scratch paper, then design their poster. Circulate —
  push them to include the six acts, the definition of a worldview, who Jesus is, why the Bible
  matters (i.e., the chapter's big ideas become map features).
- **0:38–0:56 · Present.** Each group presents and explains their map. Class notes similarities/
  differences.
- **0:56–0:60 · Close.** *"You just drew the map for the whole year — every chapter fills in more
  of it."*

**Homework.** Begin studying for the Chapter 1 Test (Study Guide, SM 21–23).
**Assessments.** Group map (participation/synthesis).
**Notes.** This is a synthesis checkpoint — listen for misconceptions in the presentations and note
what to reteach in tomorrow's review.

## Day 7 — Tue Sep 1 · Review games + Study Guide (Lessons 8 + 9, merged)

**Objectives.** Students consolidate the chapter and self-assess against the Study Guide; lock in
**Key Terms** (worldview, Christian) and **Key Verses** (Luke 18:22; Prov 9:10). *(High test weight.)*

**Materials.** Three index cards per student; display `PS 1.8A Chapter 1 Review`; student-manual
Study Guide (SM 21–26).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:22 · Activity: Quiz Cards (`Lesson 8`; manual p.18).** Each student writes **9 questions** — one per
  section 1.1–1.9 — three per card, answers on the back (textbook allowed; may not copy the manual's
  questions). Then pairs quiz each other; swap partners and repeat.
- **0:22–0:40 · Chapter Review (`PS 1.8A`).** Walk the review slides as a class; field the questions
  students missed on their maps yesterday. Hit the **six-acts matching** hard — it's the test's
  biggest section.
- **0:40–0:55 · Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses.** Students work the Study Guide (SM 21–23); confirm
  the two Key Terms and two Key Verses. The manual's 17 study-guide questions with answers (manual
  p.19–21) are the answer key.
- **0:55–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: 87 points — matching the six acts, multiple choice on
  worldviews / becoming a Christian / Jesus / the Bible. *"If you can tell the six-act story and say
  what a worldview is and what makes someone a Christian, you're ready."*

**Homework.** Study for the test.
**Assessments.** Quiz Cards + Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** If Day 4 felt rushed, reteach 1.7–1.10 briefly here — this is the flex point.
