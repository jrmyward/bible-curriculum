## Teaching Outline (~45 min · short day)

### 0:00–0:12 · The six acts (guided notes / mini-lecture)
Walk the arc, writing each on the board — this is the **spine of the whole course** and the test's matching section. One sentence each (manual Qs 2–6, **pp.8–9**):

1. **Creation** — God makes the world and humans, and rests.
2. **The Fall** — humans disobey; relationships break; violence spreads.
3. **Redemption Initiated — Israel** — God chooses Abraham's family to bless the world.
   - *Interlude: the Second Temple period* — ~400 years of silence, waiting for the Savior.
4. **Redemption Accomplished — Jesus** — God in the flesh; life, death, resurrection.
5. **Restoration Initiated — The Church** — the Spirit empowers Jesus' followers to spread the good news.
6. **Restoration Accomplished — New Creation** — Jesus returns to judge; all things made new.

### 0:12–0:20 · Discuss
- *Which act do we live in now?* → **Act 5** ("already, not yet").
- *How does the story end?* → **Act 6** — Jesus returns, all things new.

### 0:20–0:42 · Activity — Producer's List (die game; manual p.9)
- Class in a circle. A student rolls and says: *"I'm the producer of a Bible drama. In my show, for Act ___ I need ___,"* naming an object/person tied to that act (e.g., **Act 2 → an apple**).
- Next student **repeats the chain and adds one**; a wrong act or item is out. Reinforces the act numbering through play.

### 0:42–0:45 · Homework
- Assign §§1.7–1.10. *"Two sections tomorrow, but it's a lighter, reflective read."*
