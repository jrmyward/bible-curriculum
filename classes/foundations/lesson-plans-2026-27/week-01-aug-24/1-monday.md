## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:06 · Welcome & attendance
- Greet at the door; assign/confirm seating; bathroom & water norms in one breath.

### 0:06–0:20 · Course orientation
- What Foundations **is:** the first of a four-book series — this year we lay the foundation (what a worldview is, what the Bible is and why we trust it, the whole story of the Bible, and how to follow God).
- What it is **not:** a memorize-and-forget class.
- How you're graded: chapter tests, in-class activities, participation.
- Leave-them-with line: *"By June you'll be able to tell the whole story of the Bible and say why it's yours."*

### 0:20–0:29 · Hook — the San Francisco map (ST 4)
- Tell the textbook's opener: lost in a city, dead phone, and the **wrong map** (Detroit) is worse than none.
- Land the definition: **a worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions.** *A worldview is your map for life.*

### 0:29–0:45 · Read & discuss §§1.1–1.2
- Students read 1.1–1.2 (silently or aloud), then discuss. Use manual Qs 1–6 (**manual pp.4–5**):
  - *How do people find directions for life's biggest questions?* (parents, teachers, friends, experience, culture)
  - *What is a worldview? How would you describe yours?*
  - *What did you think being a Christian meant?* → name the **three misconceptions** (born into it / keep the rules / only about heaven) and the true center: **a Christian is a follower of Jesus.**
- **Teacher move:** these are reflective, not right-answer, questions. "I don't know yet" is fine on Day 1 — your job is to get them talking.

### 0:45–0:60 · Activity — *Curious About…* (index cards; manual p.5)
- **Front:** *"What from today stirred your curiosity?"* (Jesus, Christianity, the Bible, or worldview).
- **Back:** *"What do you hope to learn in this class?"*
- Pairs share; a few share with the room. Students keep the card (revisit in May) or turn it in.
