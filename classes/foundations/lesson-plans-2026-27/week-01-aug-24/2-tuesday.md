## Learning Objectives (teacher)

1. Articulate why the Bible is foundational for Christians. `[1.3]`
2. Explain two common mistakes in reading the Bible — **proof-texting** and making the Bible itself the goal. `[1.4]`

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:07 · Warm-up
- *"Should an idea be dismissed just because it's old?"* Surface a few answers; name **chronological snobbery** — assuming we're smarter than everyone before us (manual Q2, **p.6**).

### 0:07–0:28 · Read & discuss §§1.3–1.4
- The Bible as **God's written revelation** — 66 books, many authors, one story; we place ourselves **under** its authority because in it God reveals himself.
- The **two mistakes**: **proof-texting** (ripping verses from context to prove your point) and **making the Bible the end** rather than God (it becomes a weapon or a rule book).
- Use manual Qs 1–5 (**pp.6–7**). Land: *the Bible is a true **story** — many genres, one story about God and the world.*

### 0:28–0:54 · Activity — Bible Scavenger Hunt (`TR 1.2A`; manual p.7)
- Groups of 4–5; name your group at the top of the sheet.
- Race to answer the Bible-navigation questions using a **physical Bible**. Check with the answer key; **most correct wins** (ties broken by who finished first).
- **Purpose:** students get their hands in the actual book and learn its layout (OT/NT, book order) — the literacy the rest of the year assumes.

### 0:54–0:60 · Debrief & homework
- *What surprised you about how the Bible is arranged? Which book was hardest to find?* Assign the reading.
