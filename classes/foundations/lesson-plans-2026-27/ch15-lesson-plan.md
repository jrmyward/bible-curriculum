## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 7 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
The block opens the Monday after spring break (Mar 22–26), so Day 1 also closes out the last
chapter by returning the Chapter 14 test. The calendar then shapes the rest: **Day 3 (Wed Mar 31)
is a short ~45-min period**, **Day 5 (Mon Apr 5) is a sub day** (teacher at HS golf), and the
**test lands on the second short Wednesday (Day 7, Apr 7)** — so it's timed to 45 minutes.
Good Friday (Apr 2) is already off the calendar.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mon Mar 29 | Lesson 1 (15.1–15.2) + **Ch 14 test return** | ST 204–206 | Content + discussion + **Right Turn** (Psalm 51 groups) |
| 2 | Tue Mar 30 | Lesson 2 (15.3–15.4) | ST 207–209 | Content + **Strategy for Growth** (chart) |

| 5 | **Mon Apr 5 · SUB** | Lesson 7 (Videos) | — | Independent video + questions |

| 7 | **Wed Apr 7 · short** | Lesson 10 | — | **Chapter 15 Test** |

**Three merges make the compression:** the four closing subsections **15.7–15.10** combine on
Day 4 (publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — fasting/abstinence, giving/stewardship/simplicity, the saints,
and the conclusion; 15.10 is a short reflective close, so this is a genuine thin merge); the
publisher's separate Review (L8) and Study-Guide (L9) days combine on Day 6, the same review/study
merge used every chapter; and the **Category Clues** game (L6) folds onto that Day 6 review, where
its eight-category matching does double duty as the review activity — the Review lesson's own
*Discipline Your Memory* game is held as the flex option. Two more lessons land on the calendar's
awkward days rather than getting their own slot: the **Videos** lesson (L7) moves to the sub day
(Day 5), where a sub only presses play, and the **Test** (L10) sits on the short Wednesday (Day 7),
timed to 45 minutes.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 15 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 15.10A` (/60) is **multiple choice (14 pts, 1 each — some items have more than one answer) ·
true/false (8 pts, 1 each — correct the underlined word if false) · fill-in-the-blank (7 × 2 = 14
pts) · short answer (3 × 3 = 9 pts) · essay (3 × 5 = 15 pts).** Its weight lands on **fasting** —
what fasting is and isn't, and the **three fasts** (Defining Moment, Turning from Sinful Choices,
Tragic Calamity — a short-answer *and* multiple items); the **purpose and definition of spiritual
disciplines** (they don't earn salvation or favor; they put us where God works); **silence, solitude,
and self-examination** and how they differ from **Eastern transcendence** (two of the three essays);
**prayer** and the **Lord's Prayer** as a model (a short answer); **Christian meditation vs.
contemplation** and the four steps of **Lectio Divina** (read, meditate, contemplate, pray);
**reading widely**, including authors we disagree with (an essay); **simplicity, stewardship, and
generosity**; **worship** as a response to God; **asceticism**; and the four **Key Verses** (Gen
2:15, 1 Thess 5:17, Eph 4:28, Heb 12:1). Day 4 (fasting + giving) and Day 1 (silence/Eastern
contrast + the definition — both essays live here) carry the most weight, with Day 2 (prayer) and
Day 3 (meditation/Lectio/reading widely) close behind. Day 6 review pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Mon Mar 29 · Purpose of the disciplines; solitude, silence & self-examination (15.1–15.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) explain the purpose of spiritual disciplines — and why they don't
earn salvation or God's favor; (b) differentiate the Christian disciplines of solitude and silence
from Eastern-religion transcendence practices; (c) say why regular self-examination matters. `[15.1, 15.2]`

**Materials.** The graded Ch 14 tests (to return); student textbooks; Bibles, paper, and pen for the
Right Turn activity.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Welcome back & return the Chapter 14 test.** Quick reset after spring break, then
  hand back Ch 14 tests and walk the two or three items most students missed. Keep it brief — close
  last chapter's gaps before they harden.
- **0:10–0:22 · Hook & content 15.1 (guided notes; ST 204–205).** Set the frame: spiritual
  disciplines are *not* a measuring stick for how "good" a Christian you are, and they don't win
  God's love or favor — salvation is a free gift (Eph 2:8–9; the test's very first item). They are
  the practices that put us *in a place where God can work on our hearts.* Land the **Key Term**:
  **spiritual disciplines are habits and practices that help form us to be more like Jesus.**
- **0:22–0:40 · Read & discuss 15.1–15.2 (manual p.316–317).** Solitude and silence force us to
  confront the inner self; listening (reflecting on how God leads through Scripture, counsel, and
  circumstances) and self-examination (confession without self-condemnation) grow out of them. Draw
  the line the test's essay asks for: unlike Eastern transcendence, Christian silence isn't emptying
  the mind or transcending the self — it's *filling* the mind with God and putting sinful tendencies
  to death. Use manual Qs 1–5.
- **0:40–0:58 · Activity: Right Turn (5 groups; manual p.316–317).** Divide into five groups; assign
  each a stretch of **Psalm 51** (vv. 1–2, 3–4, 7–9, 10–12, 16–17). Each group answers: *What does
  this teach about God? about sin? about self-examination, confession, or repentance? How do we
  follow the psalmist's example?* Groups present. *Purpose:* the quiet disciplines exist to bring us
  to confession and repentance — David's psalm is the model.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **15.3–15.4** (ST 207–209) — prayer and worship.
**Assessments.** Right Turn responses (participation).
**Notes.** The silence/Eastern-transcendence contrast is one of three essay prompts — say it out loud
and leave it on the board. If time's short, the mini-lecture is the anchor; the Psalm 51 group
presentations can be trimmed to three groups reporting.

## Day 2 — Tue Mar 30 · Prayer & worship (15.3–15.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) give examples of various approaches to prayer; (b) explain the
Lord's Prayer as a model for prayer; (c) describe various forms of Christian worship. `[15.3, 15.4]`

**Materials.** A pen per student; student manuals (Strategy for Growth chart).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Warm-up.** *"What makes prayer hard?"* — surface a few honest answers (can't see God,
  no immediate reward, unanswered prayers). Name the reframe: prayer isn't mainly something we do to
  get results; it's being in relationship with God, and something God uses to shape us.
- **0:06–0:30 · Read & discuss 15.3–15.4 (manual p.318–319).** *Prayer:* approaches (short prayers
  through the day, written prayers — the Psalms, *The Book of Common Prayer*, *The Valley of Vision*;
  set times; praying the hours; involving the body), and **"pray without ceasing"** (1 Thess 5:17) as
  a posture of dependence rather than nonstop muttering. Walk the **Lord's Prayer** as the model Jesus
  gave — address God as a loving parent while honoring his holiness, seek his will, trust him for
  daily needs, seek and extend forgiveness, ask to be brought safely through trials (the test's
  short-answer question). *Worship:* reverence and adoration for God — through praise, through
  adoration, and through our whole lives, since even work (Hebrew *abad*, Gen 2:15) can be an act of
  worship. Worship is our **response to who God is, what he has done, and what he is doing.** Use
  manual Qs 1–6.
- **0:30–0:52 · Activity: Strategy for Growth (student manual; manual p.318–319).** Students complete
  the Strategy for Growth chart — setting a reachable goal in **prayer, worship, Bible reading, and
  memorization**, one small change to move toward each, and a plan for when they fall short. Share a
  few strategies. *Purpose:* the disciplines only form us if they become habits; small, actionable
  goals are how habits stick.
- **0:52–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** *Which of your four goals feels most doable — and which most
  intimidating?*

**Homework.** Read **15.5–15.6** (ST 209–212) — reading, memorizing, and meditating on Scripture,
and reading widely.
**Assessments.** Strategy for Growth chart (completion).
**Notes.** The Lord's Prayer is a near-certain short-answer item (three things it teaches) — make
sure every student can name at least three before they leave.

## Day 3 — Wed Mar 31 · Scripture, meditation & contemplation; reading widely (15.5–15.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) incorporate **Christian meditation** and **Christian contemplation**
into personal Bible study, and name the four steps of **Lectio Divina**; (b) evaluate the merits of
reading from a variety of authors, including those they disagree with. `[15.5, 15.6]`

**Materials.** `TR 15.3A Method of Prayer` (1 per student); a pen; student manuals.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:16 · Content & discuss 15.5–15.6 (guided notes; manual p.320–321).** Reading and
  memorizing Scripture (read regularly, not to check a box; memorize in paragraphs, not stray
  verses). Then the two **Key Terms**, kept on the board: **Christian meditation** — reflecting on
  God's Word — and **Christian contemplation** — resting quietly in the presence of God and his
  infinite love; unlike Eastern practice, meditation *fills* the mind rather than empties it. Name the
  four steps of **Lectio Divina — read, meditate, contemplate, pray** (a fill-in and true/false
  target). Then **reading widely:** why we read authors we disagree with and even non-Christians (all
  truth is God's; C. S. Lewis's "one old book between every new book"), always measuring what we read
  against Scripture. Use manual Qs — pick 3–4, favoring the meditation/contemplation distinction and
  the case for reading widely (both essay-relevant).
- **0:16–0:40 · Activity: Method of Prayer (`TR 15.3A`; manual p.320–321).** Students practice
  **Lectio Divina** as a method for Bible study and prayer, working the four steps on a short passage,
  then discuss their experience. If time allows, ask what other Bible-study and prayer methods they
  already use. *Purpose:* the activity *is* the lesson — students don't just define meditation and
  contemplation, they do them.
- **0:40–0:45 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **15.7–15.10** (ST 212–215) — fasting and abstinence, giving and stewardship, the
saints, and the conclusion. *(Tomorrow merges two lessons, so it's a fuller reading day.)*
**Assessments.** Method of Prayer / Lectio Divina worksheet (completion).
**Notes.** Short period — keep the mini-lecture tight so the Lectio Divina practice gets its full
time; the four steps and the meditation-vs.-contemplation pair are the tested pieces. If the room
runs slow, shorten the closing discussion of Method of Prayer rather than the practice itself.

## Day 4 — Thu Apr 1 · Fasting, giving & stewardship, the saints, and the conclusion (15.7–15.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) distinguish the **three types of fasts** and describe appropriate
fasting (and define **asceticism**); (b) articulate the biblical view of **simplicity and
stewardship** and what a **steward** is; (c) explain how the stories of faithful believers encourage
spiritual growth, and why dry seasons are a normal part of walking with God. `[15.7, 15.8, 15.9, 15.10]`
*(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four closing subsections. Highest test weight: fasting and
giving run through every section of the test.)*

**Materials.** One ruler per pair, white printer paper, pencil, and Bibles (Counting the Cost);
computers/tablets, paper, and pen (Models for Living).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:24 · Read & discuss 15.7–15.10 (manual p.322–325), favoring fasting and giving.**
  *Fasting:* what it is (intentionally abstaining from food/drink for a set time to give God more
  attention) and what it is **not** (dieting; a display of holiness; an attempt to earn God's favor or
  forgiveness — the test contrasts these directly). The **three fasts** — write them on the board:
  **Defining Moment, Turning from Sinful Choices, Tragic Calamity.** Then **asceticism** (avoiding all
  forms of indulgence) and modern abstinence (a week off social media or video games). *Giving,
  stewardship, simplicity:* a Christian earns "so that he may have something to share with anyone in
  need" (Eph 4:28); a **steward** cares for and manages what belongs to another; **generosity** is
  more than money (time, talent), and giving money can sometimes make things worse; **simplicity** is
  living simply so we can seek God's kingdom first. *The saints & conclusion (lighter):* faithful
  believers (Monica, Bonhoeffer, Susanna Wesley, Luis Palau…) are the "great cloud of witnesses"
  (Heb 12:1); dry seasons are normal — God works when we can't see it; the disciplines are an
  invitation, not a to-do list. Use manual Qs from p.322–325.
- **0:24–0:44 · Activity: Counting the Cost (pairs; manual p.322–323).** Pairs read **Mark 12:41–44**
  (the widow's mite) and **John 12:1–8** (Mary's ointment) and build a **bar graph** comparing the
  widow's offering, a day laborer's daily wage (1 denarius), Mary's ointment (~300 denarii), and a
  laborer's yearly wage (~300 denarii). Debrief with the manual's questions: both women gave their
  best; Jesus valued the *heart* of the giver over the amount. *Purpose:* the graph makes the point
  land — God wants us to give generously and sacrificially, not to give a certain amount.
- **0:44–0:58 · Activity: Models for Living (pairs; manual p.324–325).** Assign each pair a figure
  from Christian history (Amy Carmichael, Augustine, George Müller, Corrie ten Boom, Brother
  Lawrence…). Pairs research basic biography and how the person practiced the disciplines, then write
  **three principles** for following Jesus (an acronym helps) and give a brief presentation.
  *Purpose:* the saints aren't for comparison — they show that ordinary people, through the Spirit,
  can walk faithfully with God.
- **0:58–0:60 · Close & sub-day briefing.** Tell students Monday is a **sub day** — five short videos
  with questions, and it counts like any class day.

**Homework.** None new. Optional: begin the Chapter 15 Study Guide (student manual) — the content is
now complete.
**Assessments.** Counting the Cost bar graph (completion); Models for Living principles (participation).
**Notes.** The block's fullest day — two sections' worth of content plus two activities. **Models for
Living is the flex item:** if a class runs slow, assign the research as prep and keep the saint
presentations to Day 6, but don't cut Counting the Cost or shortchange the fasting discussion — both
map straight onto the test's short-answer and multiple-choice items.

## Day 5 — Mon Apr 5 · **SUB DAY** · Walking-with-God video set (Lesson 7)

*Teacher absence (HS golf). Fully self-contained — no content expertise needed. It falls after all
of the chapter's content is taught, so the videos reinforce and expand rather than introduce. A
standalone `/generate-substitute-plan` version can be produced; this section is sub-ready as written.*

**Objective.** Students expand and reinforce the chapter's ideas — feeling distant from God, prayer,
where to begin reading, engaging culture, and knowing what we believe — through five expert
interviews.

**Materials.** The five videos queued (see list); student manuals (video questions, SM 305–307) or a
printed video-question sheet; something to play video + audio.

**Schedule (60 min).** Before each clip, students read that video's questions so they know what to
listen for; play it (replay once if needed); give 2–3 minutes to write answers; move on. The five
clips total about **14 minutes** of footage:

1. *Why Does God Feel Far Away?* (Wood, 4:30)
2. *Why Should We Pray?* (Sherrard, 1:31)
3. *Where Should We Begin Reading?* (Bywater, 3:07)
4. *How Should Christians Encounter Culture?* (Gould, 2:27)
5. *Why Do We Need to Know What We Believe?* (McDowell, 2:08)

Budget ~0:00–0:48 for the five videos + question writing. Then **0:48–0:58 · written reflection:**
*"You've spent this chapter on the disciplines that draw us close to God. When God feels far away,
which of these practices would you most want to lean on, and why?"* (half page). **0:58–0:60 ·
collect.**

**Sub instructions (verbatim-ready).** *"Before each video, have students read that video's
questions in their student manual (or on the question sheet) so they know what to listen for. Play
the video (replay once if needed), give 2–3 minutes to write answers, and move to the next. After the
fifth video, have students write a half-page reflection on the question on the board. Collect the
video answers and the reflections at the end."* Model answers are in
[teaching-manual/ch15.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch15.md) (**manual p.327–329** — the full
Videos lesson) for grading.

**Homework.** Begin studying for the Chapter 15 Test (Study Guide, student manual; Key Terms &
Verses).
**Assessments.** Video question sheet + reflection — **collected and graded.**
**Notes.** Streaming instructions are on the inside back cover of the teacher manual. If streaming
fails, the sub should have students reread 15.7–15.8 and write a paragraph: *"What are the three
biblical fasts, and how is fasting different from dieting?"* — a safe fallback needing no tech that
also drills a heavily tested item.

## Day 6 — Tue Apr 6 · Category Clues game + review + Study Guide (Lessons 6 + 8 + 9, merged)

**Objectives.** Students sort the chapter's ideas into the **eight discipline families**, consolidate
the chapter, and self-assess against the Study Guide; lock in the **Key Terms** (spiritual
disciplines, Christian meditation, Christian contemplation, simplicity) and **Key Verses** (Gen 2:15,
1 Thess 5:17, Eph 4:28, Heb 12:1). *(High test weight.)*

**Materials.** `PS 15.6A Category Clues` (preview beforehand); one handheld whiteboard, marker, and
eraser (or a paper pad) per group of 3–4; `TR 15.8A Discipline Your Memory` (cut into cards, bagged,
1 set per 4 students) if you run the flex game; display `PS 15.8A Chapter 15 Review`; student manuals
(Study Guide); textbooks.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:30 · Activity: Category Clues (`PS 15.6A`, teams; manual p.326).** Teams of 3–4 write
  answers on a whiteboard. Display each clue; teams get 30 seconds to place it in one of the **eight
  categories** — *Solitude and Silence · Prayer · Worship · Reading and Memorizing Scripture · Further
  Reading · Fasting and Abstinence · Giving, Stewardship, and Simplicity · Examples of the Saints* —
  then reveal and score. Work through the slides as time allows. *Purpose:* this is the chapter in one
  game — every family of disciplines gets rehearsed, which is exactly how the multiple-choice and
  fill-in sections are built.
- **0:30–0:42 · Chapter Review (`PS 15.8A`).** Walk the review slides as a class; let students read
  them aloud. Hit the heavy-weight items hard: **the three fasts** and what fasting is/isn't, the
  **definition and purpose** of the disciplines, **silence/solitude vs. Eastern transcendence**, the
  **Lord's Prayer**, **meditation vs. contemplation** and **Lectio Divina**, **reading widely**, and
  **simplicity/stewardship**.
- **0:42–0:55 · Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses (manual p.330–331).** Students work the Study Guide;
  confirm the four Key Terms and four Key Verses. The manual's study-guide questions with answers are
  the answer key.
- **0:55–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: 60 points — multiple choice, true/false, fill-in, short
  answer, and three essays. *"If you can name the three fasts, define a spiritual discipline, explain
  the Lord's Prayer, and say how Christian silence differs from Eastern practice, you're most of the
  way there."*

**Homework.** Study for the test.
**Assessments.** Category Clues (informal — the eight discipline families) + Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** **Discipline Your Memory (`TR 15.8A`) is the flex game:** if Category Clues runs short or
a class needs a change of pace, run the verse-to-discipline matching game with the bagged cards. If
Day 3 (meditation/Lectio) or Day 4 (fasting/giving) felt rushed, this is the place to reteach — they
are the test's center of gravity.

## Day 7 — Wed Apr 7 · Chapter 15 Test (Lesson 10)

**Objective.** Demonstrate mastery of Chapter 15.

**Materials.** `TR 15.10A Chapter 15 Test` — 1 per student (from [portal/ch15](../_source-text/portal/ch15/));

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:03 · Settle & distribute.** Clear desks; hand out the test (60 pts). Note that some
  multiple-choice items have more than one correct answer, and false true/false items ask them to

- **0:03–0:40 · Test.** Students complete independently. Early finishers begin reading **16.1–16.2**
  (Chapter 16).
- **0:40–0:45 · Collect & bridge.** Collect tests. Preview Ch 16: *"Walking with God in Community" —
  we've practiced the disciplines on our own; next we learn to practice them together, because no one
  was meant to walk with God alone.*

**Homework.** Read **16.1–16.2** (ST 216–219) — start of Chapter 16.
**Assessments.** **Chapter 15 Test — collected and graded** (`TR 15.10A`, /60). Trimester 3 grade.
**Notes.** Return and review the test early in the Ch 16 block so misconceptions (especially the
three fasts and the meditation-vs.-contemplation distinction) don't harden.
