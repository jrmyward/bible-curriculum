## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 8 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
This is the act we live in now: **"already, but not yet."** Jesus' work is finished and he reigns,
but the kingdom isn't fully realized, so the church waits, grows, and witnesses. The block keeps
the publisher's whole sequence and both of its no-new-content lessons (Videos, Ideal Church) by
putting one on the short Wednesday and giving the other its own day.

| Day | Date | Publisher lesson(s) | Textbook | Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mon Feb 1 | Lesson 1 (11.1–11.2) + **Ch 10 test return** | ST 152–154 | Content + discussion + **Church Comparisons** (groups) |
| 2 | Tue Feb 2 | Lesson 2 (11.3–11.4) | ST 154–157 | Content + **Extraordinary Epistles** (write your own) |
| 3 | **Wed Feb 3 · short** | Lesson 7 (Videos) | — | Five expert videos + questions |
| 4 | Thu Feb 4 | Lesson 3 (11.5–11.6) | ST 157–159 | Content + **Find Your Match** (verse halves) |
| 5 | Fri Feb 5 | Lessons 4 + 5 (11.7–11.10) *merged* | ST 159–161 | Content + **Restoration Roundup** + **Green Glossary** |
| 6 | Mon Feb 8 | Lesson 6 | — | **The Ideal Church** (profile + groups) |
| 7 | Tue Feb 9 | Lessons 8 + 9 (Review + Study) *merged* | SM 224 | How Christianity Grew + review + Study Guide |
| 8 | **Wed Feb 10 · short** | Lesson 10 | — | **Chapter 11 Test** |

**Two merges make the compression:** the four closing subsections **11.7–11.10** combine on Day 5
(publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — restoration of self, others, creation, and the conclusion), and the
publisher's separate Review (L8) and Study-Guide (L9) days combine on Day 7 — the same review/study
merge used in every prior chapter. Two lessons are also *resequenced* onto the short Wednesday and
its neighbor rather than dropped: the **Videos** lesson (L7) moves up to the short Wednesday (Day 3),
where five self-contained clips fit a 45-minute period, and **The Ideal Church** (L6) keeps its own
full day (Day 6), since the profile-building activity runs 45–50 minutes. Neither carries new tested
content, so the short Wednesday costs nothing on the test.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 11 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies every
activity (`PS 11.6A`, `PS 11.8A`, `TR 11.3A`, `TR 11.5A`, `TR 11.6A`) plus the discussion questions
with answers, the video questions with answers, and the Study Guide. This plan sequences and times
them; it does not reproduce them. Discussion questions are cited by their teacher-manual location;
open [teaching-manual/ch11.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch11.md) for the full list with model
answers.

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 11.10A` (/63) is **matching (6 × 1 = 6 pts) · multiple choice (9 × 1 = 9 pts, some with more than
one answer) · true/false with correction (9 × 1 = 9 pts) · fill-in-the-blank (6 × 2 = 12 pts) · short
answer (4 × 3 = 12 pts) · essay (3 × 5 = 15 pts).** Its weight lands on: the **early-church people**
matched to what they did (Stephen stoned, John/Peter arrested and warned, Philip in Samaria, Paul's
conversion, Thomas to India); the definition of the **church**, the **Great Commission**, and
**Pentecost**, plus how the early Christians lived; the **church metaphors** (temple, holy nation,
royal priesthood) and what an **epistle** is; the **church-history milestones** — Constantine's 313
legal status, the 1054 split, **Luther** and the 1517 Reformation, Protestants seeking freedom of
worship, and Christianity now the largest religion; **justification vs. sanctification** (a state vs.
a lifelong *process*); the **four restored relationships** (God, self, others, creation) and
**reconciliation**; "redemption saves us for life on Earth, not from it"; and the **Key Verse**
John 13:34–35 (*love one another*). Weight is spread evenly — Day 1 and Day 2 feed the matching and
the church/epistle items, Day 4 carries church history plus justification, and Day 5 carries
sanctification and the four relationships. Day 7 review pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Mon Feb 1 · Already–not-yet, the church, and Pentecost (11.1–11.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) express that the kingdom of God is *already here but not yet fully
realized*; (b) describe the relationship between the **church**, the **Great Commission**, and
**Pentecost**. `[11.1, 11.2]`

**Materials.** The graded Ch 10 tests (to return); student textbooks; class Bibles for the five
Church Comparisons groups.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Return & review the Chapter 10 test.** Hand back Ch 10 tests. Walk the two or three
  items most students missed — expect the cross/atonement material to need a quick reteach. Keep it
  brief; close last chapter's gaps before they harden, then pivot: *last chapter Jesus finished the
  work — this chapter, what do his followers do while they wait for him to return?*
- **0:10–0:24 · Content: already, but not yet (guided notes; manual p.226).** Jesus accomplished our
  redemption — he proclaimed the kingdom, died on the cross, began the new covenant in his blood,
  rose, and ascended. So the kingdom of God is **already** here: Jesus is the world's rightful Ruler
  right now. But it is **not yet** fully realized — evil, death, and broken relationships remain, and
  not everyone acknowledges him as Lord. When he returns, the kingdom will be consummated: sin
  banished, all things made new, every knee bowed. *We live in the in-between.* Put **"already / not
  yet"** on the board and leave it up.
- **0:24–0:44 · Read & discuss 11.1–11.2 (manual p.226–227).** The **church** is the followers of
  Christ in all places and times (Greek *ekklesia* — "called out"). Its mission is the **Great
  Commission** (Matt 28:18–20): make disciples, baptize, teach to obey. **Pentecost** — 50 days after
  Passover — is the day the promised Holy Spirit filled the disciples (wind, flames of fire, tongues);
  Peter preached and 3,000 believed. Then how the early church *lived* (Acts 2:42–47): teaching,
  homes, shared meals and prayer, selling possessions to help the needy, a good reputation with
  neighbors. Use manual Qs 1–5. *(The capitalization note on p.227 — *Church* for a specific
  congregation, *church* for all believers — is worth one sentence.)*
- **0:44–0:58 · Activity: Church Comparisons (five groups; manual p.227).** Each group takes one set
  of verses and works out what the biblical **metaphor** teaches about the church — body (Rom 12,
  1 Cor 12, Eph 1), temple (1 Cor 3, 2 Cor 6, Eph 2), bride (Eph 5, Rev 19, 21), royal priesthood /
  chosen nation (1 Pet 2, Rev 1, 5), family (Matt 12, John 1, Gal 3). Groups summarize for the class.
  *Purpose:* the test asks students to pick real church metaphors (temple, holy nation, royal
  priesthood) out of a list — this puts those images in their hands from Scripture.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **11.3–11.4** (ST 154–157) — how the church grew, and the letters to the churches.
**Assessments.** Church Comparisons summaries (participation).
**Notes.** Keep "already / not yet" on the board through the block — it's the frame for the whole
chapter and the correction the true/false section (Q16) is looking for.

## Day 2 — Tue Feb 2 · Challenges, growth, and the letters to the churches (11.3–11.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) relate how the early church grew *despite* the challenges it faced;
(b) discuss how the New Testament **epistles** addressed the concerns of the early church. `[11.3, 11.4]`

**Materials.** Bibles, paper, and pen for Extraordinary Epistles.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Warm-up.** *"Why would anyone risk their life to keep telling a story the authorities
  told them to stop telling?"* Surface a few answers; that's the early church's situation.
- **0:06–0:30 · Read & discuss 11.3–11.4 (manual p.228–229).** *Challenges and growth:* Jewish leaders
  persecuted the apostles — Peter and John were arrested and released with a warning, **Stephen** was
  stoned, and **Saul** (a Pharisee) drove believers to prison until a vision of the risen Christ on
  the road to Damascus turned him into the apostle **Paul**. The gospel still spread — **Philip** in
  Samaria, Paul's missionary journeys through Asia Minor, traditions of **Thomas** to India and others
  farther — helped by Roman **roads** and the common **Greek** language. *(These people and deeds are
  the test's whole matching section — name them clearly.)* *Letters to the churches:* an **epistle**
  is a short letter to a community or individual (21 in the NT). Reading them is "like reading someone
  else's mail" — Scripture *for* us, but written *to* specific churches, so we interpret how they
  apply. The big one: God always intended, through the family of **Abraham**, to bless **all** people
  — so Jews and Gentiles are united in Christ; Gentiles are **grafted into** Israel, not the reverse.
  The epistles also address family, master/slave, and government relationships, and Christian ethics
  (love, forgiveness, mercy, sexual integrity, blessing enemies). Use manual Qs 1–7 — favor Qs 1–5.
- **0:30–0:50 · Activity: Extraordinary Epistles (individual; manual p.229).** Each student writes
  their own epistle to a present-day church or Christian (no naming a real church or person): a
  **greeting, a prayer, a body that names one pressing issue and gives advice, and a farewell.** They
  may model the NT letters but not copy them. Work 10–15 minutes, then survey which issues came up
  most.
- **0:50–0:58 · Debrief.** Read two or three volunteer epistles; name the issues the class kept
  reaching for — the same categories the real epistles address.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **11.5–11.6** (ST 157–159) — the church through history, and our restored
relationship with God — for Thursday. *(Tomorrow is a short Wednesday: a video set, no new reading due.)*
**Assessments.** Extraordinary Epistles (completion — all four letter parts present).
**Notes.** The matching section rewards students who can attach a *deed* to each name. If you keep a
running list on the board — *Stephen → stoned, Paul → blinded then converted, Philip → Samaria,
Thomas → India* — leave it up; it's free review for Day 7.

## Day 4 — Thu Feb 4 · The church through history; restored relationship with God (11.5–11.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) summarize major events in the history of the church from the first
century to today; (b) articulate how **sin** affects our relationship with God and how it is restored
through Christ — **justification**. `[11.5, 11.6]` *(High test weight — the history milestones and
justification are all over the test.)*

**Materials.** `TR 11.3A Find Your Match` prepared into bags A and B (see checklist); class Bibles.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:22 · Content: 2,000 years in one timeline (mini-lecture, guided notes; manual p.230).**
  Walk the milestones and write the dates on the board — the test asks these directly:
  1. **AD 80–100** — Revelation written; the NT nears completion.
  2. **313** — Emperor **Constantine** ends persecution, grants Christianity legal status.
  3. **1054** — the **Roman Catholic / Eastern Orthodox split** over doctrine and Crusades tension.
  4. **1517** — **Martin Luther** kicks off the **Protestant Reformation**; recovers **justification**
     and renewed emphasis on Scripture; new denominations form.
  5. **18th–19th c.** — Protestant **missionary** movements carry the gospel worldwide; Protestants
     migrate to America seeking **freedom of worship**.
  6. **20th–21st c.** — Pentecostal and evangelical revival; the majority of Christians now live
     **outside the West**, and Christianity is the **largest religion on Earth.**
  *Why study church history?* If you're a Christian, it's your family story — encouragement from the
  faithful, warning from the failures.
- **0:22–0:36 · Content: restored relationship with God (manual p.231).** Sin **warped** our view of
  God — some hide in fear (Adam), some grow disrespectful (Cain), some deny him, some swap in idols.
  Only God can restore it, and he did through Christ: **justification** is *the state of being declared
  righteous before God through the work of Jesus Christ.* We can't earn it (Eph 2:8–9) — it's grace,
  through faith. And it's more than pardon: we're **adopted as children and heirs**, so we come to God
  without fear. Put **justification = declared righteous** on the board.
- **0:36–0:56 · Activity: Find Your Match (`TR 11.3A`; manual p.231).** Group A draws A-halves, Group B
  draws B-halves; each student finds the partner holding the matching half of their Bible verse. Pairs
  line up A–B, read their verse aloud in turn, and a volunteer names how that verse informs
  **justification by faith.** Work through all the pairs. *Purpose:* students hear justification stated
  across many verses, not just defined once — exactly what essay Q35 and true/false Q21 test.
- **0:56–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **11.7–11.10** (ST 159–161) — restored relationships with self, others, and
creation, and the conclusion — for tomorrow. *(Tomorrow merges two sections, so it's a fuller reading.)*
**Assessments.** Find Your Match observations (participation).
**Notes.** The date milestones (Constantine 313, Luther 1517) are near-certain test items (MC 13, 14;
TF 20). If the mini-lecture runs long, Find Your Match can shrink — read fewer pairs — but don't cut
it; the justification reps matter.

## Day 5 — Fri Feb 5 · Restored relationships: self, others, creation; conclusion (11.7–11.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) explain how God restores our relationship with *ourselves* and keeps
transforming us — **sanctification**; (b) describe how Christ's redemption opens restored
relationships with *others* — forgiveness and **reconciliation**; (c) associate redemption with the
restoration of *all creation* and our call to stewardship; (d) explain why living out the gospel
happens in **community**. `[11.7, 11.8, 11.9, 11.10]` *(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four
closing subsections. High test weight: sanctification, reconciliation, and the four relationships run
through the true/false, short-answer, and essay sections.)*

**Materials.** Two containers and one paper slip per student (`Restoration Roundup`); `TR 11.5A Green
Glossary` (1 per pair) + computers.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 11.7–11.10 (manual p.232–235).** Move through the four sections,
  favoring self and others. *Self (11.7):* even Paul did "the evil I do not want" (Rom 7). Christ sets
  us free from sin's **power**, but change is a **process** — **sanctification**, *becoming like Jesus*,
  is a lifelong journey the Holy Spirit drives while we cooperate. Contrast it sharply with
  justification: justification is a *state* declared **instantly**; sanctification is a *process* over
  time. *(True/false Q22 hinges on exactly this word — "process," not "instant.")* *Others (11.8):* the
  cross has a vertical beam (God) and a horizontal one (others); we're made **ambassadors of
  reconciliation** (2 Cor 5), which requires **forgiveness** — Jesus tied our being forgiven to our
  forgiving (Matt 6:14). *Creation (11.9):* Christ will reconcile "all things" (Col 1:20); caring for
  creation isn't a distraction from the gospel — **"redemption saves us for life on Earth, not from
  it."** *Conclusion (11.10):* the kingdom is already/not yet, there's no perfect church (every one is
  "broken people awaiting full restoration"), and the church's greatest witness is believers who
  **love one another** (John 13:35 — the Key Verse). Use manual Qs from p.232–235.
- **0:26–0:44 · Activity: Restoration Roundup (two groups; manual p.233).** Each student writes their
  name on a slip into their group's container. On the board: *How to Have Restored Relationships.*
  Students take turns offering a tip (you write it up) and drawing a name from the other group's
  container. Then each student writes an encouraging word for the classmate whose name they drew and
  delivers it. If time allows, close in prayer (manual step 5). *Purpose:* the chapter's "restored
  relationships with others" becomes something students actually *do* to a real person.
- **0:44–0:56 · Activity: Green Glossary (pairs; `TR 11.5A`; manual p.235).** Pairs read the
  environmental terms, mark which they know, research the rest, and write definitions; then a quick
  class pass on how the terms connect to a **restored relationship with creation.** *(This is the flex
  item — if the room runs slow, assign the research half as homework rather than cut Restoration
  Roundup, which maps to the reconciliation items on the test.)*
- **0:56–0:60 · Close & homework.** *Which of the four relationships — God, self, others, creation —
  is hardest for you to picture being fully restored, and why?*

**Homework.** Begin studying for the Chapter 11 Test (Study Guide, SM 224; Key Terms & Verses).
**Assessments.** Restoration Roundup (participation) + Green Glossary (completion).
**Notes.** This is the block's fullest day — four subsections and two activities. The line students
must not blur is **justification (a state, at once) vs. sanctification (a process, over time)** — it's
the essay (Q35) and a true/false correction (Q22). If a class runs slow, Green Glossary is the flex
item.

## Day 6 — Mon Feb 8 · The Ideal Church (Lesson 6)

**Objective.** Students create a profile of their idea of an ideal church, weighing what a healthy
church community actually needs.

**Materials.** `PS 11.6A The Ideal Church` (preview beforehand); three copies of `TR 11.6A Ideal
Church Profile` (one per group); paper and pen; student manuals (individual Ideal Church Profile).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:12 · Set up (`PS 11.6A`; manual p.236).** Students take turns reading the slides. Frame it:
  the chapter says there's *no perfect* church, but that doesn't mean every church is the same — what
  would a genuinely *healthy* one look like?
- **0:12–0:20 · Individual profile.** Students complete the Ideal Church Profile in their student
  manual — no more than five minutes — jotting what matters most to them in a church.
- **0:20–0:44 · Group profiles (three groups; `TR 11.6A`).** Groups compare their individual answers
  and build one shared ideal-church profile, including the **staff and volunteer positions** their
  church would need and each position's requirements and responsibilities. Circulate.
- **0:44–0:56 · Present & discuss.** Each group presents its profile. Then discuss as a class, using
  the manual's prompts (p.236): large vs. small churches (money, staffing); how much worship style,
  aesthetics, and architecture should matter next to ministry; and how churches relate to parachurch
  organizations.
- **0:56–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Study for the test (Study Guide, SM 224; Key Terms & Verses).
**Assessments.** Ideal Church profiles (participation/synthesis).
**Notes.** This is a synthesis-and-application day, not new content — listen in the presentations for
misconceptions about what the church *is* (the definition and Great Commission are test items) and
note anything to reteach tomorrow. Church community returns as a full chapter later (Ch 16), so this
is a first pass, not the last word.

## Day 7 — Tue Feb 9 · How Christianity Grew + review + Study Guide (Lessons 8 + 9, merged)

## Day 8 — Wed Feb 10 · Chapter 11 Test (Lesson 10)

**Objective.** Demonstrate mastery of Chapter 11.

**Materials.** `TR 11.10A Chapter 11 Test` — 1 per student (from [portal/ch11](../_source-text/portal/ch11/));
answer key for grading.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:03 · Settle & distribute.** Clear desks; hand out the test (63 pts). Remind students the
  true/false items ask them to *correct* the underlined word when a statement is false.
- **0:03–0:40 · Test.** Students complete independently. Early finishers begin reading **12.1–12.2**
  (Chapter 12).
- **0:40–0:45 · Collect & bridge.** Collect tests. Preview Ch 12: *"Act 6: Restoration Accomplished —
  New Creation" — we've lived in the already-but-not-yet; next comes the "not yet," how the whole
  story ends.*

**Homework.** Read **12.1–12.2** (ST 162–165) — start of Chapter 12.
**Assessments.** **Chapter 11 Test — collected and graded** (`TR 11.10A`, /63). Trimester 2 grade.
**Notes.** Because the period is short, keep setup tight so students get the full ~37 minutes to work.
Return and review the test early in the Ch 12 block so misconceptions (especially justification vs.
sanctification) don't harden.
