## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 8 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
The calendar is the hard part here: this block sits in the **April golf season**, so **two of the
eight days are sub days** — Day 2 (Mon Apr 12) and Day 7 (Mon Apr 19) — and Day 4 (Wed Apr 14) is a
short ~45-min period. We build around all three. Day 1 opens the chapter with the teacher in the
room; the first sub day carries the publisher's fully self-contained **Videos** lesson (the sub just
presses play); the short Wednesday carries the **In This Together Prep** slide-build, a single
focused work period that adds no new tested content; and the second sub day carries the
student-run **presentations + review + Study Guide**, which students can work largely on their own
the day before the test.

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 16.10A` (≈/77) is **multiple choice (14 items, scored per correct answer — several take more than
one answer, ~31 pts) · true/false (8 pts, correct-the-underlined-word) · fill-in-the-blank (7 × 2 =
14 pts) · short answer (3 × 3 = 9 pts) · three essays (3 × 5 = 15 pts).** Its weight lands on the
**spiritual gifts** (the four things Scripture emphasizes — a gift from God, for building up the
church not self-promotion, every Christian has at least one, exercised in an appropriate/loving/
orderly way — plus naming gifts); the **sacraments of baptism and communion** (definitions + why they
matter to the community, one full essay); **confession, repentance, accountability partners, and
spiritual mentors** (most of the fill-in-the-blank Key Terms live here); **worship in community**
(Justin Martyr's elements, the purpose of art and symbols — one full essay); **creeds, confessions,
and catechisms** (which does which); **prayer in community**; the **four ways the church has
positively impacted society** (both short-answer targets, including Rwanda); **sharing the gospel in
word and deed** (one full essay); and the **universal church** (*catholic* = universal). The Key
Terms (spiritual gift, accountability partner, spiritual mentor, baptism, communion) are the fill-in
section almost verbatim. Day 3 (spiritual gifts + prayer + creeds) and Day 5 (confession +
sacraments) carry the most test weight, with Day 1 (worship) and Day 6 (gospel + church impact) close
behind. Day 7 review pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Thu Apr 8 · The Christian life in community; worship in community (16.1–16.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) critique Simeon Stylites' model of spiritual solitude against the
Christian's need for community; (b) describe the elements of Christian worship in community. `[16.1, 16.2]`

**Materials.** The graded Ch 15 tests (to return); student textbooks; paper and pen for the Worship
Composition groups; a computer to play hymn tunes if needed.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Return & review the Chapter 15 test.** Hand back Ch 15 tests. Walk the two or three
  items most students missed. Keep it brief — the point is to close last chapter's gaps before they
  harden.
- **0:10–0:22 · Hook — Simeon Stylites (ST 216–217).** Tell the story: a fifth-century Syrian who
  said, in effect, *"I love Jesus, but not the church"* — he punished his body, nearly starved
  himself, then spent 37 years living atop a 50-foot pillar to escape people (who came anyway). Land
  the chapter's thesis: Simeon's devotion was real, but any spirituality that makes us **permanently
  withdraw from others** or **harm ourselves** is dangerous and short-sighted. *It is only with other
  people that we become who Christ called us to be* (John 13:34–35). The church is filled with sinners,
  but it is the **training ground** where we're formed to be like Christ.
- **0:22–0:38 · Read & discuss 16.1–16.2 (manual p.336–337).** *Should we imitate Simeon? Why not?*
  Then the elements of **worship in community**: the second-century apologist **Justin Martyr**
  described a typical Sunday service — reading the gospels or prophets, a sermon of instruction and
  exhortation, prayer together, bread and wine (communion), and a collection for those in need. Add
  **singing** (Pliny noted Christians reciting a hymn) and the **symbols, art, and architecture** that
  draw our attention to God's character, beauty, and majesty. Use manual Qs 1–4.
- **0:38–0:57 · Activity: Worship Composition (groups; manual p.337).** Groups of 3–4 get a
  public-domain hymn (*"Amazing Grace," "Rock of Ages," "How Great Thou Art," "It Is Well with My
  Soul"*) — play it if they don't know the tune. Each group writes **new worship lyrics on the theme
  of salvation through Christ** set to that tune, then reads or sings them to the class. *Purpose:*
  students feel from the inside that composing worship is itself an act of worship, not just the
  singing of it.
- **0:57–0:60 · Homework + sub-day briefing.** Tell students **Monday is a sub day** — five short
  videos with questions, and it counts like any class day.

**Homework.** Read **16.3–16.4** (ST 219–221) — studying Scripture together, prayer, and spiritual gifts.
**Assessments.** Worship Composition (participation).
**Notes.** Leave *"the church is the training ground"* on the board through the block. Watch the clock
in the activity — if a group can't land a tune, let them speak their lyrics rather than sing.

## Day 3 — Tue Apr 13 · Studying Scripture together; prayer & spiritual gifts (16.3–16.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) discuss the value of worship services and small groups, and explain
why creeds, confessions, and catechisms matter; (b) explain the importance of prayer in community and
the four guidelines for the appropriate use of spiritual gifts. `[16.3, 16.4]` *(High test weight —
spiritual gifts, prayer, and the creeds/confessions/catechisms distinction run through the multiple
choice and fill-in.)*

**Materials.** `TR 16.2A Loving Church Bible Study` (1 per student) + answer key; class Bibles; pens.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Welcome back & debrief the videos.** Quick link from Monday: *what did the experts say
  the church gives us that solitude can't?* (love, care, correction — being called out when we
  misbehave).
- **0:06–0:30 · Read & discuss 16.3–16.4 (manual p.338–339).** Why study Scripture **together**: no one
  is an infallible interpreter, so a private "just me, Jesus, and the Bible" faith goes wrong — small
  groups and the **creeds, confessions, and catechisms** correct and enrich our reading (keep the
  three straight: creeds → grasp the fundamentals; confessions → clarify what a group teaches;
  catechisms → short Q-and-A about the truth). Then **prayer in community** (grows unity, puts others'
  needs first, teaches dependence on God) and the **four guidelines for spiritual gifts** — write
  these on the board and keep them there:
  1. A gift is a **gift** — from God, not developed by our own strength.
  2. Gifts are **for building up the church**, not self-promotion or private indulgence.
  3. **Every Christian has at least one**, and no gift outranks another.
  4. Gifts must be exercised in an **appropriate, loving, and orderly** way.
  Read the list of gifts from the textbook (prophecy, service, teaching, encouragement, generosity,
  leadership, mercy, wisdom, knowledge, faith, healing, miracles, discernment, tongues, apostleship,
  evangelism, shepherding). Use manual Qs 1–4.
- **0:30–0:56 · Activity: Loving Church Bible Study (`TR 16.2A`, individual then share; manual p.339).**
  Distribute the worksheet; students read the assigned verses about love and explain how each relates
  to life in the church, then share answers with the class. Grade against the `TR 16.2A` answer key.
  *Purpose:* the chapter's "one another" commands become concrete before the abstractions harden.
- **0:56–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **16.5–16.6** (ST 221–224) — confession, accountability, mentoring, and the
sacraments. *(Reminder: tomorrow is a short Wednesday — a slide-build work period, no new reading due.)*
**Assessments.** Loving Church Bible Study (completion).
**Notes.** The four spiritual-gift guidelines are a near-certain test item (multiple choice 13). If the
mini-lecture runs long, the Bible Study can start as a shorter in-class chunk and finish as homework.

## Day 5 — Thu Apr 15 · Confession, accountability, mentoring; baptism & communion (16.5–16.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) relate the significance of confession, accountability partners, and
spiritual mentors; (b) explain the purpose of baptism and communion. `[16.5, 16.6]` *(High test weight
— these are the fill-in-the-blank Key Terms and a full essay.)*

**Materials.** Student textbooks; a pen per student (the Find the Differences Chart is in the student
manual, SM 318).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 16.5–16.6 (manual p.340–341).** Because we're new creations still being
  renewed (2 Cor 5:17; 4:16), we need **confession** (acknowledging sin honestly) and **repentance**
  (turning from it) — and we can't do it hidden and alone. An **accountability partner** (same gender,
  trustworthy, asks *inviting* questions rather than shaming ones) and a **spiritual mentor** (an older
  companion with wisdom in a specific area) are the relationships that help. Then the two
  **sacraments** ("visible form of an invisible grace," per Augustine): **baptism** symbolizes dying to
  sin and rising with Christ, a public declaration of new identity; **communion** commemorates Christ's
  body and blood given for the forgiveness of sins, and it assumes we're in fellowship — which is why
  confession often comes first. Use manual Qs 1–4.
- **0:26–0:52 · Activity: What's the Difference? (chart, individual; manual p.341).** Students use the
  textbook to complete the **Find the Differences Chart** (SM 318): confession vs. repentance;
  accountability partner vs. spiritual mentor; communion vs. baptism — noting the **similarities** and
  the **differences** for each pair. Then discuss the manual's personal questions (*Do you have an
  accountability partner? Have you been baptized — can you tell the story?*). *Purpose:* the test asks
  students to distinguish exactly these paired terms; the chart builds the distinction they'll need.
- **0:52–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** *Which of these three pairs is easiest to confuse, and what's the
  one word that keeps them apart?*

**Homework.** Read **16.7–16.10** (ST 224–230) — giving, sharing the gospel, the church's impact, and
the conclusion. *(Tomorrow merges two lessons, so it's a fuller reading day.)*
**Assessments.** Find the Differences Chart (completion — similarities + differences for all three pairs).
**Notes.** The five Key Terms (spiritual gift, accountability partner, spiritual mentor, baptism,
communion) are the fill-in section almost verbatim — say each one out loud and have students point to
where it lives on their chart.

## Day 6 — Fri Apr 16 · Giving & serving; sharing the gospel; the church's impact; conclusion (16.7–16.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) express how giving and serving meet the needs of the community;
(b) give examples of how to share the gospel in word and deed; (c) identify four specific ways the
church has positively impacted society; (d) explain what the universal church is. `[16.7, 16.8, 16.9, 16.10]`
*(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four closing subsections. High test weight: the four ways the
church impacted society are both short-answer targets, and sharing the gospel is a full essay.)*

**Materials.** `TR 16.5A World-Changing Words` (cut into sections; "1" sections clipped together, "2"
sections clipped together); computers for Go and Make Disciples; a pen per student.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:24 · Read & discuss 16.7–16.10 (manual p.342–345).** Move through the four sections, favoring
  the tested ones. **Giving & serving:** the **tithe** traces to Abraham and Melchizedek (Gen 14) and
  the Mosaic law, but the New Testament asks for **cheerful, free generosity** more than a fixed
  percentage — and money is only one way to give (time, skills, advocacy). **Sharing the gospel:**
  weigh *"Preach the gospel, and when necessary, use words"* — actions give the message credibility,
  but faith comes by **hearing**, so words are necessary too; both are required (the Great Commission,
  Matt 28:19–20). The **Holy Spirit** does the converting; he empowers us to share. **Four ways the
  church has impacted society** — write these on the board: (1) **caring for the sick and
  impoverished** (Christians stayed to nurse plague victims when others fled), (2) **honoring and
  protecting women and children** (rejecting abortion and infanticide, adopting abandoned infants),
  (3) **promoting culture and education** (preserving writings, founding schools and universities),
  (4) **pursuing racial unity** (Rwanda; the Charleston forgiveness). **Conclusion:** the **universal
  church** — *catholic* in the Nicene Creed means universal — all who confess Jesus as Lord, of every
  nation and tongue. Use manual Qs from p.342–345.
- **0:24–0:42 · Activity: World-Changing Words (`TR 16.5A`, two teams; manual p.345).** Team 1 gets the
  "1" verses, Team 2 the "2" verses. Call a category (*caring for the sick · honoring women and
  children · promoting culture and education · pursuing racial unity*); students whose verse fits grab
  their pen and raise it; the first up reads the verse and has 30 seconds to explain the fit. One point
  for a correct match, a second for a correct explanation. *Purpose:* this is the recall hook for the
  short-answer question that asks students to *name* the four ways.
- **0:42–0:56 · Activity: Go and Make Disciples (pairs; manual p.343).** Pairs briefly research a
  gospel-sharing method (Good News/Bad News, Life in 6 Words, or Romans Road). One plays the
  **Christian**, one the **unbeliever**; the Christian walks the gospel (God made everything good →
  people rejected God → all have broken the moral law → God is just → God is merciful, paying our debt
  in Christ → anyone who trusts Jesus is forgiven) while showing care and respect. Debrief: *what was
  easy, what was hard?* *(Flex item — if the room ran slow on the discussion, do one round instead of
  swapping roles.)*
- **0:56–0:60 · Close & homework.** *One person in your life you could love toward Jesus in both word
  and deed — what's one action, and what's one thing you'd want to say?*

**Homework.** Begin studying for the Chapter 16 Test (Study Guide, SM 332; Key Terms & Verses). Send
your slide deck to me tonight if you haven't. *(Reminder: Monday is a sub day — you'll present your
decks and review for the test.)*
**Assessments.** World-Changing Words (informal) + Go and Make Disciples (participation).
**Notes.** This is the block's fullest day. If a class runs slow, **Go and Make Disciples** is the flex
item — cut to one round rather than trim the discussion of the four ways the church impacted society,
which is the day's tested core. Remind pairs one more time to email their decks for Monday.
