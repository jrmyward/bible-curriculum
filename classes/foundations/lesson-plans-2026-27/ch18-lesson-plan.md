**Course:** Foundations (grade 9) · **Trimester:** 3 · **Block:** Ch 18 of 18 — **the final chapter**
**Dates:** Tue May 4 – Fri May 14, 2027 · **8 instructional days**
**Periods:** ~60 min most days; **~45 min on Wednesdays** (Day 2 May 5 and Day 6 May 12 are the short days). No sub day this block.
**Test:** Fri May 14 — `TR 18.10A` **Cumulative Test** (from [portal/ch18](../_source-text/portal/ch18/))
**Course completion:** The May 14 test closes **Trimester 3 and the whole course.** There is no next chapter — the block bridges into the **May 17–21 light week** (course wrap / celebration; May 21 is the last day, a half-day).

## How this block works

The conclusion is built differently from the seventeen chapters before it. The publisher's
Chapter 18 is not ten content lessons with a chapter test — it is **eight lessons that review the
whole year and a cumulative final.** The first three lessons re-walk the course (reading the Bible
well and the six-act story; who God is and how we grow; the ten big questions Christianity answers;
evangelism and apologetics), and the back half turns the year into a personal project: each student
writes and presents a **Statement of Faith**, then everyone reviews for the cumulative test. So this
plan keeps the publisher's arc as-is and compresses only where it must.

| 1 | Tue May 4 | Lesson 1 (18.1–18.2) + **Ch 17 test return** | ST 245–248 | Content + discussion + **A Look at Jonah** (groups) |
| 2 | **Wed May 5 · short** | Lesson 2 (18.3–18.4) | ST 248–250 | Content + **How Should I Live?** (charades game) |
| 3 | Thu May 6 | Lessons 3 + 4 (18.5) *merged* | ST 250–252 | Content + **Writing FAQs** + **Statement of Faith** launch |
| 4 | Mon May 10 | Lesson 5 | — | **Write** the personal statement of faith (in class) |
| 5 | Tue May 11 | Lesson 6 (Videos) | — | Five videos + **course reflection**; finalize statements |
| 6 | **Wed May 12 · short** | Lesson 7 | — | **Statement of Faith presentations** (part 1) |
| 7 | Thu May 13 | Lesson 8 (Presentations + Review) | SM (Cumulative Study Guide) | Finish presentations + **Cumulative Review** + Study Guide |
| 8 | Fri May 14 | Test | — | **Cumulative Test** |

**One merge makes the compression.** The publisher already folded the review lesson and the
study-guide work together into **Lesson 8** (presentations, then `PS 18.8A Cumulative Review`, then
the Cumulative Study Guide), so the usual Review+Study merge is done for us. That leaves eight
lessons for the seven pre-test days, so **Lesson 3 (18.5 + Writing FAQs) and Lesson 4 (the Statement
of Faith launch) share Day 3** — 18.5 is a single short section, and launching the project only needs
enough class time to write the outline before students finish it at home. Nothing is cut: the Jonah
activity, the charades game, the FAQs activity, the full statement-of-faith project, the videos, and
the cumulative review all keep their place.

**Lean-layer note:** Chapter 18 needs **no newly generated handouts** — the publisher supplies every
piece (`PS 18.1A`, `PS 18.8A`, `TR 18.1A–B`, `TR 18.2A`, the discussion questions with model answers,
the five video questions with answers, the Statement of Faith project directions, and the 40-question
Cumulative Study Guide with answers). This plan sequences and times them; it does not reproduce them.
Discussion questions are cited by their teacher-manual location; open
[teaching-manual/ch18.md](../_source-text/teaching-manual/ch18.md) for the full list with model answers.

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 18.10A` — the **Cumulative Test (/75)** — is the one all-objective final of the year: **Matching
(30 pts) · True/False (15 pts) · Multiple Choice (30 pts),** one point each. It does not test Chapter
18 alone; it **sweeps the whole course (Ch 1–18).** Its weight lands here:

- **Matching, items 1–5 (5 pts):** the five **Key Verses** to their references — Matt 3:16–17
  (Jesus' baptism), Matt 28:18–20 (Great Commission), John 13:34–35 (love one another), 2 Tim 3:16–17
  (all Scripture is profitable), 1 Peter 2:9 (a royal priesthood, a holy nation).
- **Matching, items 6–30 (25 pts) — the single heaviest block on the test:** twenty-five people,
  events, and images matched to the **six acts + the interlude** (e.g., *Six days* → Act 1; *Tree of
  Knowledge / ground cursed* → Act 2; *Abraham, Moses, Passover, naming animals* → Acts 1 and 3;
  *Maccabees, Apocrypha* → Interlude; *crucifixion, Beatitudes, parables, Martha, John the Baptist* →
  Act 4; *Pentecost, Paul, epistles, Martin Luther* → Act 5; *New Jerusalem, second coming, Lake of
  fire, apocalypse* → Act 6). The whole-course metanarrative is one-third of the test.
- **True/False, 31–45 (15 pts):** the kings of Israel in order (Saul, David, Solomon, Rehoboam), the
  Great Commission, Jesus treating **all** of Scripture as authoritative, the **Trinity** (one God in
  three Persons), hellenization, **love as the mark of a Christian**, the church's future glorification.
- **Multiple Choice, 46–75 (30 pts):** Bible reliability and **textual variants** (<1% meaningful),
  mistakes in Bible reading, why the metanarrative matters, creation myths, covenants, the groups that
  threatened OT Israel, the **kingdom of God**, God's attributes (holy, eternal), **hermeneutics**,
  spiritual disciplines, early-church worship, the two greatest commandments, **original sin**, the
  Sinai covenant, the exile, Daniel's four beasts, the four symbols of Jewish life, **relativism**,
  external evidence, **gnostic books and the canon criteria**, spiritual mentor, **imago Dei**,
  baptism, **justification/sanctification**, imitating Jesus, resurrection theories, the **Incarnation**.

The **30 Key Terms** and **5 Key Verses** at the top of the Cumulative Study Guide map straight onto
the True/False and Multiple Choice items — reviewing those is reviewing most of the test. **Day 1**
(the six-act story + who God is) and **Day 7** (the Cumulative Review + Study Guide) carry the most
weight; because the test is cumulative, the year's earlier work is being reviewed here too.

## Day 1 — Tue May 4 · Reading Scripture well, the six-act story, and who God is (18.1–18.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) list the seven steps to reading the Bible well and summarize the
six-act metanarrative; (b) name at least 15 attributes of God; (c) explain how to grow in
relationship with God and become more like Christ. `[18.1, 18.2]` *(Highest test weight — the six-act
matching keys off 18.1, and the attributes of God run through the True/False and Multiple Choice.)*

**Materials.** The graded **Ch 17 tests** (to return); display `PS 18.1A`; `TR 18.1A A Look at Jonah`
(cut into four sections) + `TR 18.1B` answer key; class Bibles; a pen per student.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:08 · Return & review the Chapter 17 test.** Hand back the **Ch 17 "Loving God and Others"**
  tests from yesterday. Walk the two or three items most students missed. Keep it brief — the point is
  to close the last chapter before the cumulative review begins.
- **0:08–0:14 · Frame the last chapter.** Read the objectives on ST 245. Tell them plainly: *"This is
  the final chapter. For seventeen chapters we've built the Christian worldview; now we review the
  whole thing, ask what it answers, and you put it in your own words."* Reach back to Day 1 of the
  year: **"On the first day I said that by June you'd be able to tell the whole story of the Bible and
  say why it's yours. This is that week."**
- **0:14–0:34 · Review 18.1–18.2 (guided notes / mini-lecture; manual p.376, model answers).** Move
  briskly — this is recall, not new content. **Reading the Bible well, seven steps:** (1) choose a
  good translation, (2) attend to the content, (3) its historical context and (4) its literary
  context, (5) look for relationships, (6) use Bible helps, (7) courageously pursue a response. Then
  re-walk the **six acts + interlude** (write them on the board — this is the test's biggest section):
  Creation · The Fall · Redemption Initiated (Israel) · *Interlude: Second Temple* · Redemption
  Accomplished (Jesus) · Restoration Initiated (the Church) · Restoration Accomplished (New Creation).
  Then **who God is** — name 15 attributes (self-existent, Creator, personal, spirit, incarnate in
  Jesus, eternal, omniscient, omnipotent, omnipresent, unchanging, holy, wise, righteous, loving,
  triune) — and **how we grow** (trusting Christ, imitating him, spiritual disciplines, community, the
  two greatest commandments). Use manual Discussion Qs 1–4 (p.376).
- **0:34–0:58 · Activity: A Look at Jonah (`PS 18.1A`, `TR 18.1A–B`; groups; manual p.377).** Four
  groups, one chapter of Jonah each; distribute the matching `TR 18.1A` section. Display `PS 18.1A`;
  groups take turns reading the slides, then read their assigned Jonah chapter plus the additional
  clue verses and answer the questions (15 min to work). Groups present; check against `TR 18.1B`.
  *Purpose:* students apply the whole year's tools — metanarrative, genre, God's character — to one
  short book, proving the framework actually reads Scripture.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **18.3–18.4** (ST 248–250) — the ten big questions Christianity answers.
**Assessments.** A Look at Jonah (participation / application).
**Notes.** Leave the six acts on the board all block — it's a quarter of the test and every review day
points back to it.

## Day 2 — Wed May 5 · The ten big questions Christianity answers (18.3–18.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) articulate how Christianity answers five of life's biggest questions
(where I came from, who I am, where I am, is there a God and what is he like, is there truth); (b)
describe how it answers five more (whom can I trust, why am I here, how should I live, how do I know
right from wrong, what happens after I die). `[18.3, 18.4]`

**Materials.** `TR 18.2A How Should I Live?` (cut into the seven strips, in a bag); two pads of chart
paper; two markers; two timers.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:04 · Warm-up.** On the board: *"You have your truth, I have mine, and both of us are equally
  right."* — one or two reactions. Name it: this is the question the chapter answers head-on.
- **0:04–0:24 · Review 18.3–18.4 (brisk discussion; manual p.378–379, model answers).** Walk the ten
  questions as a rapid round — these are answers students already own from the year, so keep it moving:
  origins (created in love, not accident), identity (image-bearers, God's beloved), the world
  (purposeful, corrupted, to be restored), God (the uncreated Creator, personal and near), truth (it
  exists and is knowable in Christ), trust (Jesus never fails), purpose (participating in God's
  mission), how to live (self-giving love, not pleasure-chasing), right from wrong (God is the standard),
  and life after death (new creation for those in Christ). Use manual Discussion Qs 1–7 — favor Q4
  (answering the "everyone has their own truth" claim) and Q6 (how to know right from wrong).
- **0:24–0:42 · Activity: How Should I Live? (`TR 18.2A`; two teams; manual p.379).** Drawing charades.
  Seven verses in the bag, each with an underlined word about how believers should live. A sketcher
  from each team draws clues (no words, letters, or symbols) for two minutes while the team guesses;
  a point per correct word. Play until all seven verses are guessed, then **read the winning verses
  aloud and discuss what they teach about how to live.**
- **0:42–0:45 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **18.5** (ST 250–252) — evangelism and apologetics.
**Assessments.** How Should I Live? (participation).
**Notes.** The ten answers are the year in miniature — if a class is thin on one (truth and morality
are the usual soft spots), that's the one to slow down on. The game is the flex item if the discussion
runs long on the short day.

## Day 3 — Thu May 6 · Sharing the good news; launching the Statement of Faith (18.5, Lessons 3 + 4 merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) explain that **evangelism** and **apologetics** are part of living
out the Christian faith and distinguish the two; (b) begin a **personal Statement of Faith** that
synthesizes the whole course. `[18.5]` *(Merges publisher Lessons 3 and 4 — a single short section
plus the launch of the capstone project.)*

**Materials.** Pen and paper (Writing FAQs); student manuals (Statement of Faith Project directions);
Bibles; a computer per student is optional for the outline.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:20 · Review 18.5 + activity: Writing FAQs (pairs; manual p.380–381, model answers).**
  **Evangelism** — sharing the good news and inviting people to trust Jesus, backed up by good works
  and a changed life. **Apologetics** — defending, commending, and translating the faith; clearing
  the way for evangelism by answering honest questions. Use manual Discussion Qs 1–3. Then run
  **Writing FAQs:** each student writes one question a visitor to a Christian-worldview website would
  ask; partners trade papers and write a clear, friendly answer. Share a few.
- **0:20–0:28 · Launch the Statement of Faith Project (manual p.382–383).** Read the directions from
  the student manual. Each student writes a **350–500 word personal statement of faith** that must
  cover: (a) what the Bible is (Ch 2–5); (b) sin, redemption, and who Jesus is (Ch 6–12); (c) who God
  is (Ch 13); (d) who we are (Ch 14); (e) actions to grow in Christ (Ch 15–17); (f) their own response
  to the course (Ch 1 and 18). Order is up to them; all six must appear. Presentation option: read
  from paper, or a slide deck of **no more than six slides** (emailed ahead if using slides).
- **0:28–0:56 · Write the outline (in class).** Students build the outline of their statement — one
  or two lines per required topic, pulling from their textbook and notes. Circulate; the common
  struggle is topic (f), so push them: *"How did this class change what you actually believe?"*
- **0:56–0:60 · Homework + set expectations.** Tell them: outline finished tonight; **Monday you write
  the full statement in class**, and presentations begin Wednesday.

**Homework.** Finish the **Statement of Faith outline** — all six required topics represented.
**Assessments.** Writing FAQs (participation); statement-of-faith outline started (completion).
**Notes.** This is the merge day. If 18.5 and FAQs run long, the outline can start with just topics
(a)–(c) in class and (d)–(f) at home — but every student should leave with an outline underway, since
Monday assumes it.

## Day 4 — Mon May 10 · Writing the personal Statement of Faith (Lesson 5)

**Objective.** Students write a 350–500 word personal statement of faith that synthesizes what the
Bible is, sin and redemption and who Jesus is, who God is, who we are, how they will grow in Christ,
and how the course has affected them.

**Materials.** The students' outlines from Day 3; computers optional (draft on device or paper);
Bibles.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Set the task (manual p.383).** Restate the requirements: 350–500 words, all six topics,
  written in the student's own voice. Remind slide-users to keep it to six slides and email it ahead.
- **0:06–0:52 · Write.** Students draft the full statement from their outlines. Circulate and coach —
  this is the one place all year they say the whole worldview in their own words, so the coaching is
  about honesty and clarity, not length. Point them back to specific chapters for anything thin.
- **0:52–0:60 · Wrap + presentation logistics.** Explain how presentation time is set: divide the
  class into the roughly one and a half periods available (Days 6–7), which gives each student a
  fixed few minutes. Tell them to **practice at home and stay within the limit.**

**Homework.** **Finalize the personal statement** (polish to 350–500 words); **practice presenting**
within the time limit. Slide-users email the deck to the teacher.
**Assessments.** Personal statement of faith — drafted in class, finalized at home (this is the
chapter's major graded work).
**Notes.** Whoever finishes early can begin reviewing the Cumulative Study Guide. If the room needs
more writing time, Tuesday's videos day has slack to finish.

## Day 5 — Tue May 11 · Worldview & apologetics video set; year-end reflection (Lesson 6)

**Objective.** Students expand the chapter's ideas through five expert videos and reflect on the year,
while finalizing their statements for presentation.

**Materials.** The five videos queued (see list); student manuals (video questions) or a printed
video-question sheet; something to play video + audio; computers optional for finalizing statements.

**Schedule (60 min).** Before each clip, students read that video's questions so they know what to
listen for; play it (replay once if needed); give 2–3 minutes to write answers; discuss; move on. The
five clips total about **10 minutes** of footage:

1. *Does God Mostly Just Want Us to Be Happy?* (Koch, 1:56)
2. *What Is Wisdom, and How Do I Get It?* (Myers, 2:05)
3. *How Can We Better Understand the Bible?* (Paauw, 1:40)
4. *What Is the Relationship between Apologetics and the Bible?* (Sharp, 1:44)
5. *What Is One Way to Reach the Next Generation with Truth?* (Jimenez, 2:44)

- **0:00–0:33 · Videos + questions (manual p.384–386, model answers).** Work the five clips as above.
- **0:33–0:45 · Year-end reflection (manual note, p.386).** Ask the manual's closing question: *"Which
  video — or which idea — from the whole year was your favorite or most memorable, and why?"* Let
  several students answer aloud. This is the first of the course's closing beats.
- **0:45–0:60 · Finalize statements + rehearse.** Students do a last polish and a quiet run-through of
  their statement, checking they're under the time limit. Circulate for last questions before
  tomorrow's presentations.

**Homework.** Last polish + practice presenting. Begin studying the **Cumulative Study Guide** (30 Key
Terms, 5 Key Verses).
**Assessments.** Video question sheet (collected); statements presentation-ready (check).
**Notes.** Streaming instructions are on the inside back cover of the teacher manual. If streaming
fails, use the full time for finalizing and rehearsing statements and move the reflection question to
the start of Day 6 — no content is lost, since the videos add no new tested material.

## Day 6 — Wed May 12 · Statement of Faith presentations, part 1 (Lesson 7)

**Objective.** Students present their personal statements of faith, articulating the Christian
worldview in their own words.

**Materials.** Students' finished statements (and slide decks, ≤6 slides, emailed ahead); a computer +
display for slide presentations.

**Schedule (45 min · Wednesday — short day).**

- **0:00–0:04 · Set the norm (manual p.387).** Remind presenters of the time limit; remind the class
  to **listen respectfully** while others present. Confirm the order.
- **0:04–0:43 · Presentations.** Students present in turn, keeping to the allotted minutes. After each,
  a brief affirming comment or one appreciative question from the class — this is a capstone, not a
  critique. Work through as many as the short period allows (roughly the first half to two-thirds of
  the class, depending on size); the rest present tomorrow.
- **0:43–0:45 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Study for the **Cumulative Test** (Study Guide, 30 Key Terms, 5 Key Verses). Any
students not yet presented: be ready for tomorrow.
**Assessments.** Statement of Faith presentation (for those who present today) — graded on coverage of
the six required topics and clarity.
**Notes.** Keep the room warm and unhurried — for many freshmen this is the first time they've said the
whole Christian worldview out loud in their own voice. Note who still needs to present so Day 7 opens
without a scramble.

## Day 7 — Thu May 13 · Finish presentations + Cumulative Review + Study Guide (Lesson 8)

**Objectives.** Students finish presentations, then consolidate the whole course against the
**Cumulative Study Guide** and lock in the **30 Key Terms** and **5 Key Verses**. *(High test weight —
this is the last review before the cumulative final.)*

**Materials.** The remaining students' statements; display `PS 18.8A Cumulative Review`; student
manuals (Cumulative Study Guide).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:18 · Finish presentations (manual p.388).** Any students who did not present Wednesday go
  now, same norms and time limit.
- **0:18–0:44 · Cumulative Review (`PS 18.8A`).** Walk the review slides as a class. Aim at the test's
  center of gravity: the **six acts + interlude** (the 25-item matching — drill placing people and
  events into acts), the **five Key Verses** to their references, and the heavy Key Terms
  (metanarrative, textual variants, relativism, hermeneutics, imago Dei, Trinity, Incarnation,
  justification/sanctification, gnosticism, hellenization). Cold-call across the year, not just this
  chapter.
- **0:44–0:57 · Cumulative Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses (manual p.389–395).** Students work the
  40-question Cumulative Study Guide; confirm the 30 Key Terms and 5 Key Verses. The manual's answers
  are the answer key — spot-check the ones students most often miss (the kings in order; the four
  factions of the Second Temple period; Daniel's four beasts).
- **0:57–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: 75 points, all objective — matching, true/false, multiple
  choice, across the whole year. *"If you can place the six acts, match the five Key Verses, and define
  the 30 Key Terms, you're most of the way there."*

**Homework.** Study for the Cumulative Test.
**Assessments.** Remaining presentations; Cumulative Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** This is the last teaching day of the year — the review pulls all eighteen chapters together.
If any early chapter (revelation, external evidence, the Second Temple period) looks shaky in the
cold-calls, spend the review minutes there; it's cumulative.

## Day 8 — Fri May 14 · Cumulative Test (Trimester 3 & whole-course close)

**Objective.** Demonstrate cumulative mastery of the Foundations course.

**Materials.** `TR 18.10A Cumulative Test` — 1 per student (from [portal/ch18](../_source-text/portal/ch18/));
answer key for grading. *(Point out the second column of items, 24–30, on the first page.)*

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:05 · Settle & distribute.** Clear desks; hand out the test (75 pts). Note the two-column
  layout on the first page so no one misses items 24–30.
- **0:05–0:55 · Test.** Students complete independently. Early finishers may quietly reread their own
  Statement of Faith or the Study Guide.
- **0:55–0:60 · Collect & close the course.** Collect tests. No new reading is assigned — **the course
  is complete.** Close by naming what next week is: **May 17–21 is the course-wrap / celebration week**
  (May 21, the last day, is a half-day), and Chapter 18's review carries into it. Land the callback:
  *"On the first day I said that by June you'd be able to tell the whole story of the Bible and say why
  it's yours. You just did — on this test and in your statement of faith. Now it's yours to keep."*

**Homework.** **None — the course is finished.** Next: the **May 17–21 light week** (course wrap /
celebration), not new reading.
**Assessments.** **Cumulative Test — collected and graded** (`TR 18.10A`, /75). Trimester 3 grade and
the capstone assessment of the year.
**Notes.** Return and review the Cumulative Test during the May 17–21 wrap week — it's the natural
centerpiece of the celebration, and reviewing it closes the year on what students got right rather than
letting the last test go unseen. This is the final block of Foundations; there is no next chapter to
bridge to.
