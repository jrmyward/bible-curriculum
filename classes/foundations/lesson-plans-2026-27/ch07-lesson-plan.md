## How this block works

This chapter compresses the publisher's **10 lessons into 8 days** — by *merging*, not cutting.
The calendar shapes the block twice: **Day 4 (Wed Nov 18) is a short ~45-min period**, and
**Thanksgiving break (Nov 23–27) falls inside the chapter**, between Day 6 (Fri Nov 20) and Day 7
(Mon Nov 30). So the content runs front-loaded before the break, the short Wednesday carries the
self-contained Videos lesson, and the fun, low-stakes Charades game lands on the last day before
the week off.

**Two merges make the compression:** the four closing subsections **7.7–7.10** combine on Day 5
(publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — God's grace and the protoevangelium, the way of Cain and the flood,
the covenant with Noah, the tower of Babel, and the conclusion), and the publisher's separate
Review (L8) and Study-Guide (L9) days combine on Day 7 — the same review/study merge used in
Chapters 1–6. Two lessons are also *resequenced* onto the awkward calendar days rather than
dropped: the **Videos** lesson (L7) moves up to the short Wednesday (Day 4), where four short clips
fit a 45-minute period cleanly, and **The Fall Charades** game (L6) moves to Day 6, the last day
before Thanksgiving — by then the whole chapter has been taught, so the game reviews all of it and
sends the class into break on a light note. Neither the videos nor the game carries new tested
content, so the short period costs nothing on the test.

**A note on the break gap:** there is no class Nov 23–27. Day 6 (Fri Nov 20) is the last meeting
before the week off; Day 7 (Mon Nov 30) is the first meeting after. Homework over the break is kept
to nothing — the review and the test both wait until after students are back.

### What the test asks (so the review aims true)

`TR 7.10A` (/61) is **matching (4 pts) · multiple choice (8 pts, some items with more than one
answer) · true/false (8 pts, correct the false ones) · fill-in-the-blank (Q21 = 4 pts + five × 2 =
14 pts) · short answer (4 × 3 = 12 pts) · essay (3 × 5 = 15 pts).** Its weight lands on **the four
relationships broken by the fall** — God, self, others, nature (essay 31, the single biggest item);
on **God's gracious response and the protoevangelium** — Genesis 3:15, "he shall bruise your head"
(matching 4, the 4-point fill-in 21, and both essays 32 and 33); on the **three words for sin**
(sin, transgression, iniquity) and **shalom** (matching 1–3, MC 10); on the **serpent and the first
sin** (MC 5, 7, 8, 9 — Adam and Eve ate from the Tree of Knowledge of Good and Evil, the serpent
was Satan and misrepresented God); on the **doctrine of original sin** and what **secularists
assume** (short 27, MC 6/11/12, TF 13); on **covenant vs. contract** and the covenant with Noah
(short 30, TF 14/16/18); on **Cain, Abel, Enoch, and Lamech** and why faith pleases God (short 29,
TF 15/19); on **death as unnatural** (TF 20); and on the memory verses **Romans 3:23** and
**Romans 5:12** (fill-in 23, TF 17). Day 5 (God's grace, the protoevangelium, the covenant) and
Days 2–3 (the fall and the four broken relationships) carry the most weight; Day 1 (the sin words
and shalom) is discrete recall for the matching and MC. Day 7 review pulls it together.

## Day 1 — Fri Nov 13 · Shalom, what sin is, and the return of the Ch 6 test (7.1–7.2)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) compare the harmony of the original creation with the state of the
world today; (b) explain the three words the Bible uses for sin — *sin*, *transgression*, and
*iniquity*. `[7.1, 7.2]`

**Materials.** The graded Ch 6 tests (to return); student textbooks; computers (one per group) for
News Analyses; paper and pen.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:10 · Return & review the Chapter 6 test.** Hand back Ch 6 tests. Walk the two or three
  items most students missed. Keep it brief; the point is to close last chapter's gaps before they
  harden, then pivot into the new act of the story.
- **0:10–0:20 · Hook — "All was well," then it wasn't (ST 94).** Read or tell the opening: at the
  end of Genesis 2 the world was in **shalom** — the Hebrew word for **peace, harmony, wholeness,
  prosperity, welfare, and tranquility**, everything good and in order. Then look around: we clearly
  aren't in Eden anymore. Nearly every worldview admits something has gone wrong; they just disagree
  about what. Land the chapter's thesis: **the Bible says the problem with the world is sin — an
  intruder in God's good creation, not part of it.**
- **0:20–0:40 · Read & discuss 7.1–7.2 (manual p.140–141; SM 130–131).** Define sin properly against
  the caricature (an angry God spoiling our fun). Walk the **three words**: **sin** — to *miss the
  goal*, to fail to love God and others; **transgression** — the act of *rebelling or breaking
  trust* with another person; **iniquity** — *crooked, wicked, perverse* behavior. Use manual Qs 1–4:
  *What is shalom? What's wrong with the world now? What comes to mind when you hear "sin"? Describe
  the three words.* Draw out and dispel the false notions (Q3) before landing the biblical picture.
- **0:40–0:58 · Activity: News Analyses (groups; manual p.141).** Four groups, each assigned one of
  the **four relationships** — God, ourselves, other people, creation. Groups search online for a
  current news story showing sin, transgression, or iniquity that damages *their* relationship, then
  summarize how the sin affects it (10–12 min) and present. *Purpose:* the abstract words for sin get
  attached to real, current wreckage — and it previews the four broken relationships the essay tests.
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **7.3–7.4** (ST 96–99) — the fall and its consequences for our relationship with
God and nature.
**Assessments.** News Analyses summaries (participation).
**Notes.** Leave *shalom* and the three sin words on the board through the block; the matching
section keys off them. If the test return runs long, News Analyses can shrink to three groups.

## Day 2 — Mon Nov 16 · The fall, and its consequences for God and nature (7.3–7.4)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) describe what the fall is and how it happened; (b) identify the
physical and spiritual consequences of the fall in people's relationship with God and with nature.
`[7.3, 7.4]` *(High test weight — the serpent, the first sin, death, and the broken relationships
run through the MC and the biggest essay.)*

**Materials.** Student textbooks; student manuals (Breaking News Story — no other materials needed).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:24 · Read & discuss 7.3–7.4 (manual p.142–143; SM 132–133).** The **fall** is *the belief
  that sin entered the world when Adam and Eve chose to disobey God* — the second act of the
  metanarrative. Walk Genesis 3: the serpent (identified elsewhere as **Satan**) first *questioned*
  God's command, then *rejected* it ("You will not surely die"), then attacked God's *goodness*
  (God is holding out on you). The first sin was **more than eating fruit** — Adam and Eve failed to
  love God, rebelled against his authority, and wanted to be gods on their own terms. Then the
  consequences: **death is unnatural** — the great enemy, an invader (why Jesus wept at Lazarus'
  tomb); **spiritual death** is the fracturing of our relationship with God (they hid, they blamed);
  and **nature** was cursed — the ground resists, work turns frustrating, creation "groans" (Rom
  8:19–22). Use manual Qs 1–7: favor Q1 (the first sin), Q2 (how the serpent tempts), Q4 (is death
  natural?), and Q6–7 (the fractured relationships).
- **0:24–0:44 · Activity: Breaking News Story (groups of 4–5; manual p.143).** Groups brainstorm how
  to present the fall as *breaking news*, build a short newscast skit, rehearse, and perform. After
  each, the class names the most effective point. *Purpose:* students have to retell Genesis 3
  accurately and out loud — which is exactly what the MC and essay ask them to reproduce.
- **0:44–0:58 · Debrief.** *Which relationship did the fall damage most visibly in the skits — God,
  nature, or both? Where do you see "the ground fighting back" (frustrating work, decay) today?*
- **0:58–0:60 · Homework.**

**Homework.** Read **7.5–7.6** (ST 99–101) — the fall's effect on self and others, and the doctrine
of original sin.
**Assessments.** Breaking News Story skits (participation).
**Notes.** Freshmen will want the serpent to be "just a snake." Note early that Scripture identifies
it as Satan (Rom 16:20; Rev 12:9; 20:2) — MC 9 asks it directly. *Death is unnatural* is a near-
certain true/false item (TF 20).

## Day 3 — Tue Nov 17 · Consequences for self and others; the doctrine of original sin (7.5–7.6)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) infer the consequences of the fall in people's relationships with
themselves and with others; (b) discuss the doctrine of original sin and whether God is to blame for
people's sin. `[7.5, 7.6]` *(High test weight — original sin and the secularists' assumptions are
all over the short-answer, MC, and true/false.)*

**Materials.** Bibles and pens (the Bible Verses about Original Sin Study in the student manual).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:24 · Read & discuss 7.5–7.6 (manual p.144–145; SM 135–136).** *Self:* after the fruit,
  Adam and Eve were ashamed and "no longer comfortable in their own skin" — internal brokenness that
  leads us to hunt for identity in what we know, own, or achieve, and never be satisfied. *Others:*
  the intimacy and trust between them broke into blame and division, and every kind of relationship
  since bears it. Then **original sin** — *the belief that Adam and Eve's first sin corrupted the
  nature of their descendants*; we're born with a bent toward rebellion and can't fix ourselves. Work
  the **who-is-to-blame** question hard: secularists say society/culture is to blame and deny any
  natural bent to evil (Hitchens: "created sick and commanded to be well"); the Bible answers that
  **God is not the author of evil** (Gen 1:31; James 1:13; 1 John 1:5) — evil is the result of
  *rebellion*. Use manual Qs 1–5: favor Q3 (respond to Hitchens), Q4 (define original sin), Q5 (are
  humans basically good or evil?).
- **0:24–0:50 · Activity: Original Sin Scriptures (pairs; manual p.145).** Pairs work the *Bible
  Verses about Original Sin Study* in the student manual — reading and making observations on **Job
  14:4; Psalm 51:5; Isaiah 53:6, 64:6; Mark 7:14–23; Ephesians 2:1–3; 1 John 1:8–10**, ~2–3 minutes
  each. Pairs share observations. *Purpose:* students see original sin isn't one proof-text but the
  Bible's consistent verdict — which is what short-answer 27 asks them to state.
- **0:50–0:60 · Debrief & homework.** *If everyone is born bent toward sin, is that hopeless — or does
  it explain something you already knew was true?* Point ahead: Thursday we reach God's answer.

**Homework.** Read **7.7–7.10** (ST 101–104) — God's grace after the fall, the way of Cain and the
flood, the covenant with Noah, and Babel. *(Tomorrow is a short video day — no new reading due for
it; this reading is for Thursday's fuller merged day.)*
**Assessments.** Original Sin Scriptures observations (participation).
**Notes.** Draw the line freshmen blur: "humans were created *very good*" and "humans are born with
a sin nature" are both true — the problem isn't being human, it's being *fallen*. That distinction
is exactly what short-answer 28 (did they become like God?) and MC 6 test.

## Day 5 — Thu Nov 19 · God's grace, the way of Cain, the covenant, and Babel (7.7–7.10, merged)

**Objectives.** Students can (a) summarize how God responded to Adam and Eve's sin and showed grace
by promising redemption; (b) give examples of sin in the lives of Adam and Eve's descendants;
(c) articulate what a covenant is, how God made one with Noah, and what happened after the tower of
Babel. `[7.7, 7.8, 7.9]` *(Merges publisher Lessons 4 and 5 — the four closing subsections. High test
weight: the protoevangelium, the covenant, and Cain/Abel carry the matching, the 4-point fill-in, and
both grace essays.)*

**Materials.** `TR 7.4A Genesis 3:15 Study` (1 per student) + answer key; `PS 7.5A Student Covenant`
(preview beforehand); Bibles, paper, and pen.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:26 · Read & discuss 7.7–7.10 (manual p.146–149; SM 137–140).** Move through the four
  sections, favoring **7.7** — the highest-weight material. *God's grace in judgment:* even in Genesis
  3, God is gracious — he calls "Where are you?" to give them a chance to confess; he speaks the
  **protoevangelium**, the *first announcement of the gospel*, in Genesis 3:15 ("he shall bruise your
  head, and you shall bruise his heel"), pointing ahead to Jesus crushing Satan at the cross; and even
  banishing them from the Tree of Life is mercy, so they won't live forever in a broken world. *7.8 —
  the way of Cain:* Cain's sacrifice was rejected (Abel's was offered in **faith**, Heb 11:4, 6), Cain
  murdered Abel, and humanity split into two paths — those who "walked with God" (**Enoch**) and those
  who followed "the way of Cain" (**Lamech**), until the flood. *7.9 — covenant and Babel:* a
  **covenant** is *an agreement between two parties involving both rights and responsibilities* —
  other-centered, unlike a self-centered **contract** (marriage is the model); God's covenant with
  Noah, signed by the **rainbow**, promised never to flood the earth again *even though he knew they'd
  fail*; at **Babel**, human pride tried to "make a name," so God confused their language. *7.10 —
  conclusion:* God never abandons his people. Use the manual Qs across p.146–149 — favor 7.7 Q2 (the
  protoevangelium) and Q3 (how death is gracious), and 7.9 Q1 (covenant vs. contract).
- **0:26–0:48 · Activity: Genesis 3:15 Study (`TR 7.4A`, individual; manual p.146–147).** Students
  use their Bibles to complete the worksheet tracing Genesis 3:15 to the rest of Scripture, then share
  answers. Grade against the `TR 7.4A` key. *Purpose:* the protoevangelium is the chapter's single
  most-tested idea (matching 4, the 4-point fill-in 21, essays 32 and 33) — this is where students
  lock it in.
- **0:48–0:58 · Activity: Student Covenant (`PS 7.5A`, groups; manual p.148–149).** Display the
  slides; groups draft a few items for a one-page *student covenant* (an other-centered agreement with
  rights and responsibilities), then share one item each. *Purpose:* students build the concept of
  covenant by making one — reinforcing short-answer 30 (marriage as covenant) and TF 14 (covenant vs.
  contract).
- **0:58–0:60 · Close & homework.**

**Homework.** Optional — begin skimming the Chapter 7 Study Guide (SM 143–145). *(Tomorrow is a
game day; no reading due.)*
**Assessments.** Genesis 3:15 Study worksheet (completion, `TR 7.4A` key); Student Covenant draft
(participation).
**Notes.** This is the block's fullest day — four sections and two activities. Protect the discussion
of **7.7** and the **Genesis 3:15 Study**; if the class runs slow, the **Student Covenant** is the
flex item — shorten it to a quick brainstorm-and-share, since the covenant concept is also carried by
the Day 4 video and the Day 7 review.

## Day 6 — Fri Nov 20 · The Fall Charades (Lesson 6) — last day before break

*The publisher's Charades game reinforces the whole chapter's vocabulary and concepts. Placed here,
on the last day before Thanksgiving, it reviews everything just taught (7.1–7.10) and sends the class
into the week off on a light note. It adds no new tested content. Tell students today that the week
off is coming and that the review and test wait until they're back.*

**Objective.** Students reinforce the chapter's key terms and concepts through a full-period team game.

**Materials.** `TR 7.6A Act 2: The Fall Charades` — printed, cut apart, and placed in a bag (prep
beforehand).

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:06 · Set up (manual p.150).** Divide into three or four teams. Read the rules: the
  performer acts the word/phrase silently while teammates guess; speaking forfeits the turn (the slip
  goes back in the bag); the ear-pull means "sounds like," fingers give the syllable count, and
  **elbows together forming a "V"** flag an underlined **vocabulary term**. A correct guess scores a
  point.
- **0:06–0:52 · Play.** Run through as many turns as time allows, teammates taking turns performing.
  As terms come up — *shalom*, *the fall*, *protoevangelium*, *covenant*, *original sin*,
  *transgression*, *iniquity*, Babel, the way of Cain — call the class back to their meaning for a
  beat before the next draw. The team with the most points wins.
- **0:52–0:58 · Debrief & break briefing.** Quick recap of the terms that gave teams the most
  trouble — those are the ones to look at when they get back. Remind students: **no class Nov 23–27**;
  Day 7 (Mon Nov 30) is review, and the test is Tue Dec 1.
- **0:58–0:60 · Reward.** Recognize the winning team.

**Homework.** **None over Thanksgiving break.** (Optional only: glance back at the Key Terms if you
want a head start on the review.)
**Assessments.** Game participation (informal — which terms students can act and read).
**Notes.** If Day 5 felt rushed, weave the covenant and protoevangelium terms into the debrief here —
this game is free review for the block's heaviest-weighted vocabulary. Keep the energy up; it's the
last thing before a week away.

## Day 7 — Mon Nov 30 · Fall in Literature + review + Study Guide (Lessons 8 + 9, merged)

**Objectives.** Students consolidate the chapter and self-assess against the Study Guide; lock in the
**Key Terms** (shalom, sin, transgression, iniquity, the fall, doctrine of original sin, covenant)
and **Key Verses** (Genesis 3:6; Genesis 3:14–19; Genesis 9:7–13; Romans 3:23; Romans 5:12).
*(High test weight — first day back from break, so re-anchor before Tuesday's test.)*

**Materials.** Computers (one per group) for The Fall in Literature; display `PS 7.8A Chapter 7
Review`; student manuals (Study Guide, SM 143–145); textbooks.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:05 · Welcome back & reset.** One minute to re-anchor after the break: *the fall is Act 2 —
  sin entered, four relationships broke, and God responded with grace.* Then straight into review.
- **0:05–0:24 · Activity: The Fall in Literature (five groups; manual p.154).** Assign each group one
  work the fall has inspired — *The Life of Adam and Eve* (apocryphal), *Le Jeu d'Adam* (medieval
  drama), Milton's *Paradise Lost*, Twain's *The Diaries of Adam and Eve*, and Lewis's *Perelandra*.
  Groups research (12 min) the origin or author plus the work's plot and purpose, then present.
  *Purpose:* students see how far the story of the fall reaches — and rehearse the chapter's shape by
  retelling it in someone else's words.
- **0:24–0:42 · Chapter Review (`PS 7.8A`).** Walk the review slides as a class. Hit the heavy-weight
  items hard: the **four broken relationships** (essay 31), the **protoevangelium** / Genesis 3:15
  (matching 4, fill-in 21, essays 32–33), the **three words for sin** and **shalom** (matching 1–3),
  **original sin** (short 27), **covenant vs. contract** (short 30, TF 14), and **Cain/Abel/Enoch/
  Lamech** (short 29, TF 15).
- **0:42–0:55 · Study Guide + Key Terms/Verses (manual p.155–157).** Students work the Study Guide;
  confirm the seven Key Terms and five Key Verses. The manual's 17 study-guide questions with answers
  are the answer key.
- **0:55–0:60 · Test preview.** Tomorrow: 61 points — matching, multiple choice, true/false, fill-in,
  short answer, and three essays. *"If you can name the four relationships the fall broke, explain the
  protoevangelium, and define original sin and covenant, you're most of the way there."*

**Homework.** Study for the test.
**Assessments.** Fall in Literature research (participation) + Study Guide completion.
**Notes.** Coming off a week away, spend the first review beat re-warming memory, not introducing
anything new. If Day 5 (grace/protoevangelium/covenant) felt rushed before the break, this is the
place to reteach — it is the test's center of gravity.

## Day 8 — Tue Dec 1 · Chapter 7 Test (Lesson 10)

**Objective.** Demonstrate mastery of Chapter 7.

**Schedule (60 min).**

- **0:00–0:04 · Settle & distribute.** Clear desks; hand out the test (61 pts).
- **0:04–0:52 · Test.** Students complete independently. Early finishers begin reading **8.1–8.2**
  (Chapter 8).
- **0:52–0:60 · Collect & bridge.** Collect tests. Preview Ch 8: *"Act 3: Redemption Initiated —
  Israel" — we've seen sin break the world in Act 2; next, God chooses one man and one nation to begin

**Notes.** Return and review the test early in the Ch 8 block so misconceptions (especially the
protoevangelium and covenant vs. contract) don't harden.
