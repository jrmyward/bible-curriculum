## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:07 · Warm-up

- On the board: *"That's true for you, but not for me."* — is that a statement everyone can live by? Surface a few takes; name **relativism**.

### 0:07–0:28 · Read & discuss 2.9–2.10 (manual pp.34–35)

- **Relativism** — truth/morality is relative to the person, society, or era — collapses on itself: *"There is no truth"* would itself have to be a truth, so it's **self-refuting**.
- **Why truth matters:** without it we're lost; truth tells us who we are, where we came from, and whose we are.
- The Bible's **purpose** — God's revelation, calling us to examine our lives before our Creator.
- Distinguish an **objective** truth claim (holds regardless of belief; based on facts) from a **subjective** one (changes with feeling or opinion). Use manual Qs 1–4.

### 0:28–0:53 · Activity: Why Truth Matters (role groups; manual p.35)

- Five groups each get a role card and a topic (cheating, stealing, drugs). Each group role-plays how their character would apply relativism to the topic; the class names the Bible's **objective** truth claim on that topic and explains why the relativist version fails. Work through all five.

### 0:53–0:60 · Close & homework

- *One place you've heard relativism this week — an ad, a show, a conversation?*
