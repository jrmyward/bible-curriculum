## Teaching Outline (~45 min · short day)

### 0:00–0:14 · Content: revelation & canon (mini-lecture, guided notes; manual pp.30–31)

- God is **personal** (mind, will, emotions), so he **reveals** himself. Two kinds:
  - **General revelation** — what everyone can know of God from **creation and conscience** (Ps 19; Rom 1–2).
  - **Special revelation** — God's unique self-disclosure through **Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Scriptures, and miraculous events**.
- The **canon** (writings acknowledged as genuine and authoritative): the OT canon was settled by Jesus' day; the NT was recognized by the second century using **three criteria** (keep on the board):
  1. **Apostolicity** — written by an apostle or an apostle's associate?
  2. **Orthodoxy** — does it match the apostles' teaching?
  3. **Pedigree** — accepted early and widely across the churches?

### 0:14–0:20 · Quick check

- Fast cold-call: *sunset? a verse of Scripture? your conscience nagging you? Jesus?* — general or special? Lock the distinction.

### 0:20–0:43 · Activity: God's Revelation A–Z (manual p.31)

- Students write the alphabet down the page and race (10 min) to name one thing in creation for each letter that reveals God's nature (A: atom, B: baboon…). Then go A→Z: one point for each item **no one else** wrote; a student may defend an unusual answer. High scorer wins. *Debrief:* what did the whole list say about God?
