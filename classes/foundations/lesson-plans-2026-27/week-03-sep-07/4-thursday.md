## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:22 · Read & discuss 2.7–2.8 (manual pp.32–33)

- The key distinction: the Bible **is** God's Word (his revelation), not merely **contains** God's words — "contains" puts a human in charge of deciding which parts count.
- **Inspiration** — God enabled people to write Scripture (the writers claim Spirit-given messages 3,000+ times). **Inerrancy** — the Bible is without error in the original writings; trustworthy in all it teaches.
- Three reasons for confidence: it is **inspired**, **rooted in history**, and **inerrant**.
- **Correspondence theory of truth** — a statement is true when it **corresponds to reality**; the Bible holds up a mirror to the world. Use manual Qs 1–5.

### 0:22–0:52 · Activity: The Bible Is Rooted in History (research; manual p.33)

- Students find **two OT** and **two NT** historical facts that have been corroborated — recording each **fact**, **how it was confirmed**, and the **source**.
- Seed if they stall: the **Tel Dan** "House of David" inscription; the walls of **Jericho**; **Tacitus** on Jesus; the five-colonnaded **Pool of Bethesda** (John 5). Share a few.

### 0:52–0:60 · Debrief & homework

- *Does historical confirmation prove the Bible's spiritual claims? What does it establish, and what does it not?*
