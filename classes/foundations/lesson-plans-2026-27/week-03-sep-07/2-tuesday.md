## Learning Objectives (teacher)

1. Name four ways the Bible has influenced the world. `[2.1]`
2. Identify and explain the four ways people read the Bible — skeptic, scholar, seeker, saint. `[2.2]`

## Teaching Outline (~60 min)

### 0:00–0:06 · Welcome back & debrief

- Two-minute recap of the sub-day quiz + videos: *what surprised you about how few people can name the first five books?*

### 0:06–0:24 · Discuss 2.1–2.2 (manual pp.26–27)

- **Four ways the Bible has influenced the world:** world literature; our nation; Western civilization; the mark of an educated person.
- **The four readers:** *skeptic* (unsure it's true/relevant), *scholar* (studies it deeply), *seeker* (reads for life's big questions), *saint* (reads to know and obey God). *Ask:* which are you right now? Use manual Qs 1–4.

### 0:24–0:44 · Read & discuss 2.3–2.4 (manual pp.28–29)

- Display `PS 2.2A`: the Bible is **66 books — 39 OT + 27 NT** — Law (the **Torah**, first five books), history, poetry, prophecy; the NT holds the **Gospels**, Acts, letters, Revelation. Name the **Apocrypha** and why it isn't in all Bibles.
- The two pictures: the Bible as a **map** (perspective — orients you, shows the way) and a **compass** (a fixed reference point outside yourself; drift a little and you end up lost). Use manual Qs 1–7.

### 0:44–0:58 · Activity: Not Just a Book of Instructions (pairs; manual p.29)

- Pairs write a short **how-to guide** for using the Bible in daily life — including how to use the index and a concordance. A few pairs share; note common themes.
