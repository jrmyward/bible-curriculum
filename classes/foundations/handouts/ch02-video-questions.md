# Chapter 2 — Video Questions

**Foundations · Bible 9 · Chapter 2, Day 1 (video day)**

Name: ________________________

Before each video, read its questions so you know what to listen for. Answer in complete sentences
after each clip.

---

## Video 1 — How Did We Get the Bible? *(Wallace)*

1. Explain the first criterion the speaker used for deciding whether a document was included in the New Testament.
2. Who were two writers of New Testament books that were **not** eyewitnesses?
3. Early followers of Jesus (Ignatius, Papias, Polycarp, Irenaeus) quoted from the Gospels. How does this help prove the reliability of the New Testament?
4. The speaker used to be a skeptic but became a Christian because of reliable evidence. Would eyewitness testimony make you more confident about an event's authenticity? Why or why not?

## Video 2 — Is the Bible Applicable? *(Shlemon)*

1. What question do people generally ask when they read the Bible?
2. Is that the first question we should ask when we read the Bible? Why or why not?
3. What two steps does the speaker recommend for reading the Bible?
4. Explain why Exodus 14:14 does not apply to us the same way it did for the Israelites.
5. How is Exodus 14:14 still relevant to us?

## Video 3 — Is the Bible's Impact Important? *(Shlemon)*

1. Is the Bible the only religious book that has had a major impact on the world? Is a book's impact a good final measure of its value? Why or why not?
2. What is another way to assess a book's value?
3. Compare the Quran and the Book of Mormon to the Bible. What makes the Bible unique?

## Video 4 — Isn't Christianity a Bunch of Rules and Regulations? *(Shlemon)*

1. What is a common concern people have about Christianity?
2. What is the problem with this concern?
3. Do your parents have rules and expectations for you? What are some, and why do you think they have them?
4. How is the relationship between children and their parents far more than following a bunch of rules?
5. How would you explain to someone that Christianity, God, and the Bible are not just a bunch of rules to follow?
