# Chapter 2 — Key Concepts at a Glance

**Foundations · Bible 9 · Chapter 2 reference**

The test leans hard on these four clusters. Learn them cold.

---

## 1. How the Bible is arranged

- **66 books = 39 Old Testament + 27 New Testament.**
- **Torah** — the first five books of the OT (Law).
- OT also has **history, poetry, prophecy**; the OT runs Genesis → Malachi.
- **Gospels** — the first four books of the NT. The NT also has the history of the early church (Acts), letters, and Revelation.
- **Apocrypha** — extra writings included in some Bibles but not all; **not** part of the Protestant canon.
- Two pictures: the Bible as a **map** (gives perspective, shows the way) and a **compass** (a fixed point of reference outside yourself).

## 2. Revelation — how God makes himself known

| | **General revelation** | **Special revelation** |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | What anyone can know of God | God's unique self-disclosure |
| **Through** | **Creation and conscience** | **Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Scriptures, miraculous events** |
| **Verses** | Ps 19:1–4; Rom 1:19–20; Rom 2:14–15 | 2 Tim 3:16 |

*Quick test:* a sunset or your conscience → **general**; a verse of Scripture or Jesus → **special**.

## 3. The canon & the three NT criteria

- **Canon** — the collection of writings acknowledged as **genuine and authoritative** (God's inspired Word).
- The OT canon was settled by Jesus' day; the NT was recognized by the second century using **three criteria**:
  1. **Apostolicity** — written by an apostle or an apostle's associate?
  2. **Orthodoxy** — does it match the apostles' teaching?
  3. **Pedigree** — accepted early and widely across the churches?

## 4. Why we can trust the Bible — and why truth matters

- The Bible **is** God's Word (not merely *contains* God's words).
- **Inspiration** — God enabled people to write Scripture (a prompting of the Holy Spirit).
- **Inerrancy** — the Bible is without error in the **original writings**.
- Three reasons for confidence: it is **inspired**, **rooted in history**, and **inerrant**.
- **Correspondence theory of truth** — a statement is true when it **corresponds to reality**.
- **Relativism** — the belief that truth/morality is relative to the person, society, or era. It's **self-refuting**: *"there is no truth"* would itself have to be true. Truth matters because it tells us who we are, where we are, and whose we are.

## Key Verses — know the reference and the point
- **Ps 19:1–4** — creation declares God's glory (general revelation).
- **Rom 1:19–20** — God's nature is seen in what he made.
- **Rom 2:14–15** — the law is written on the conscience.
- **2 Tim 3:16** — "All Scripture is God-breathed" (inspiration).
