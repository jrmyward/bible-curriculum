# The Story of the Bible in Six Acts

**Foundations · Bible 9 · Chapter 1 reference**

The whole Bible tells **one story** in six acts (plus an interlude). This is the spine of the
entire course — you'll come back to it in almost every chapter this year. Learn the order, and
one sentence for each.

---

| # | Act | In one sentence | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Creation** | God makes the universe and everything in it, including human beings made in his image, and tells them to fill and rule the earth. | Old Testament |
| 2 | **The Fall** | Human beings disobey God; sin enters the world, relationships break, and violence spreads. | Old Testament |
| 3 | **Redemption Initiated — Israel** | God chooses Abraham's family to bless the whole world; his descendants become the nation of Israel. | Old Testament |
| — | *Interlude: the Second Temple Period* | About **400 years of silence** — no new revelation — while God's people wait for a savior. | Between the Testaments |
| 4 | **Redemption Accomplished — Jesus** | Jesus, God become human, lives a sinless life, dies for our sins, and rises again. | New Testament |
| 5 | **Restoration Initiated — The Church** | The Holy Spirit empowers Jesus' followers to love God and others, imitate Christ, and proclaim God's kingdom — **until Jesus returns**. | New Testament |
| 6 | **Restoration Accomplished — New Creation** | Jesus returns to judge; sin, death, and evil are destroyed; God makes a new heaven and earth and reigns forever. | New Testament |

---

## Two ideas to know with the story

- **"Already and not yet."** We live in **Act 5**. God's kingdom has truly *begun* (already) but is
  not yet *fully here* (not yet). The church lives in the in-between.
- **The story has a shape.** Creation → Fall → Redemption → Restoration. Things are made good,
  break, get rescued, and are finally made new.

## Quick self-check

1. Can you name all six acts **in order** from memory?
2. Which act are we living in right now? *(Act 5 — the Church.)*
3. What happens in the **interlude**? *(≈400 years of silence; waiting for a savior.)*
4. How does the story **end**? *(Act 6 — Jesus returns; all things new.)*

> On the test, you'll **match each act to its name in order** and describe what happens in several
> of them. If you can tell the six-act story, you're most of the way to a good grade.
