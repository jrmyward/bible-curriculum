# Chapter 1 — Video Questions & Reflection

**Foundations · Bible 9 · Chapter 1, Day 5 (video day)**

Name: ________________________

Before each video, read its questions so you know what to listen for. Answer in complete sentences
after each clip. Finish with the reflection at the end.

---

## Video 1 — What Is a Worldview, and Why Does It Matter? *(Myers)*

1. How does the speaker describe what a worldview is?
2. How do ideas about God and reality impact morals and behaviors?
3. What is one of the most important things that Christians can do with worldview patterns?

## Video 2 — How Can Christians Become More Intentional about Their Worldview? *(Stonestreet)*

1. How is secularism most powerful in its challenges to faith?
2. In what ways have Christians adopted a worldview of secularism?
3. How should we view and talk about faith?

## Video 3 — How Should We Begin Studying Worldview? *(Sherrard)*

1. How can you decide what apologetics resources to read?
2. What is the best way to learn worldview?
3. How can you avoid being overwhelmed by the resources out there?

## Video 4 — Is Christianity Just a List of Rules to Follow? *(Myers)*

1. What suspicion did the speaker say he had when he was younger? Do you agree or disagree? Why?
2. What does the speaker say about whether Christianity is a religion or a relationship?
3. What does the speaker say makes Christianity different from other religions and philosophies?
4. What problems and questions are answered in or through Jesus?

## Video 5 — How Can We Overcome Apathy toward Christianity? *(Wallace)*

1. What worldviews does the speaker say competed with Christianity where he grew up? How did he describe them?
2. To counter apathy, the speaker recommended two "why" questions for every "what" in a conversation. Explain what he meant.
3. What two things did the speaker say people are interested in that Christianity provides answers to?

## Video 6 — How Can Christians Portray the Fulfilling Life Jesus Gives? *(Heard)*

1. What misconception do people often have about the gospel? Why is it wrong?
2. The speaker said the gospel "summons us to participate in a story where we are no longer the star of our own story but find significance outside of ourselves." What is your response?
3. Why does the speaker mention morality, culture, beauty, truth, goodness, and justice?
