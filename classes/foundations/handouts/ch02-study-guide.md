**Foundations · Bible 9**

## Review Questions

**The Bible's influence & the four readers**
1. Name three ways the Bible has influenced the world.
2. Name and explain the **four ways** people read the Bible.

**How it's arranged**
3. How many books are in the Bible? How many in each Testament?
4. What is the **Torah**? What are the **Gospels**? What is the **Apocrypha**?
5. Explain the Bible as a **map** and as a **compass**.

**Revelation & the canon**
6. What is the difference between **general** and **special** revelation? Give two examples of each.
7. What is the **canon**?
8. Name and explain the **three criteria** used to choose the New Testament books.

**Trust & truth**
9. Why is it wrong to say the Bible merely *contains* God's words?
10. Define **inspiration** and **inerrancy**. What three reasons give us confidence the Bible is God's Word?
11. What is **relativism**, and why is *"there is no truth"* self-refuting?
12. What is the **correspondence theory of truth**? Why does truth matter?

## Answer Key

1. World literature; our nation; Western civilization; knowledge of it is the mark of an educated person. *(any three)*
2. **Skeptic** (unsure it's true/relevant), **scholar** (studies it deeply), **seeker** (reads for life's big questions), **saint** (reads to know and obey God).
3. **66 books** — **39 Old Testament + 27 New Testament.**
4. **Torah** — the first five books of the OT (Law). **Gospels** — the first four books of the NT. **Apocrypha** — extra writings in some Bibles, not part of the Protestant canon.
5. **Map** — gives perspective and shows the way to live in God's world. **Compass** — a fixed point of reference outside yourself that always points to truth.
6. **General revelation** — what everyone can know of God through **creation and conscience** (Ps 19; Rom 1–2). **Special revelation** — God's unique self-disclosure through **Jesus, the Holy Spirit, the Scriptures, and miraculous events.** *(examples: a sunset/conscience = general; a Bible verse/Jesus = special.)*
7. The **canon** is the collection of biblical writings acknowledged as **genuine and authoritative** — God's inspired Word.
8. **Apostolicity** (written by an apostle or associate), **orthodoxy** (matches the apostles' teaching), **pedigree** (accepted early and widely).
9. "Contains" puts a human in charge of deciding which parts count. The Bible **is** God's Word — his revelation.
10. **Inspiration** — God enabled people to write Scripture. **Inerrancy** — the Bible is without error in the original writings. Confidence: it is **inspired**, **rooted in history**, and **inerrant**.
11. **Relativism** — truth/morality is relative to the person, society, or era. *"There is no truth"* would itself have to be a truth, so it **refutes itself**.
12. **Correspondence theory of truth** — a statement is true when it **corresponds to reality**. Truth matters because it tells us who we are, where we are, and whose we are — the only foundation for a life.
