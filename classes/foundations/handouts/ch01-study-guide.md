## The Story of the Bible in Six Acts
The test asks you to **match each act to its name in order**, and to say **what happens** in several of them. Learn the spine and one line for each:

1. **Creation**
2. **The Fall**
3. **Redemption Initiated — Israel**
   - *Interlude: the Second Temple period*
4. **Redemption Accomplished — Jesus**
5. **Restoration Initiated — The Church**
6. **Restoration Accomplished — New Creation**

## Review Questions

**Worldview**
1. What is a **worldview**? Give two or three examples of worldviews people hold today.
2. A worldview is like a **map**. Explain the comparison.
3. Do **all** worldviews accurately explain the world? Why does it matter which one you have?

**Being a Christian**
4. What does it mean to be a **Christian**? Name the **three common misconceptions** and why each is wrong.
5. How does someone actually **become** a Christian?
6. What do Christians believe about **Jesus** — who is he, and what did he do?
7. Is **"Christ"** Jesus' last name? What does **Messiah** mean, and where were Jesus' followers first called "Christians"?

**The Christian life**
8. What is a Christian's **primary calling**, and what does that kind of **love** actually look like?
9. What are **spiritual disciplines**, and what are they for? Is becoming like Christ quick or lifelong?
10. What is the difference between **anthropology** and **theology**?

**The Bible**
11. Why is the **Bible** important and authoritative for Christians, even though it is such an old book?
12. How is the Bible **organized**?
13. What are **two common mistakes** people make when reading the Bible?

**The story**
14. Tell the story of the Bible in **six acts**, in order.
15. What happens in **Act 1 (Creation)**, **Act 3 (Israel)**, **Act 4 (Jesus)**, and the **last act**?
16. What were God's people hoping for during the **interlude**?
17. In **Act 5 (the Church)**, what are Jesus' followers supposed to do, and for how long? What does **"already and not yet"** mean?

## Answer Key

**Key Terms**
- **Worldview** — a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions.
- **Christian** — a person who follows Jesus.
- **Spiritual disciplines** — practices such as prayer, Bible reading, and giving that help us grow in our faith and become more like Christ.
- **Already and not yet** — the kingdom of God has truly begun but is not yet fully here; the church lives in the time between.
- **Anthropology / theology** — anthropology is the study of *people*; theology is the study of *God*.
- **Messiah** — a title of Jesus (not a last name); it means the promised, anointed Savior.

**Key Verses**
- **Luke 18:22** — Jesus says, "Come, follow me." Being a Christian means following Jesus, not being born into a Christian family, keeping a set of rules, or only caring about heaven.
- **Proverbs 9:10** — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom." Knowing God is where real understanding starts.

**Review Questions**
1. A pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions. Examples: Christianity, Islam, secularism, Marxism, new spirituality, postmodernism.
2. A good map gives you perspective and shows you the way to go; a worldview does the same for life. A map is only useful if it matches reality, and so is a worldview.
3. No — **not all** worldviews are accurate. The wrong map, like the wrong worldview, leaves you lost, so it matters which one you actually live by.
4. To be a Christian is to follow Christ. The three misconceptions: (a) you must be **born into** a Christian family — but anyone can become a Christian by choosing to follow Jesus; (b) it is about **keeping a set of rules** — Christians try to obey God but do not stop being Christians when they fail; (c) it is **only about heaven** — Christians are also called to love and serve people and to care about earthly problems like poverty and injustice.
5. By deciding to **follow Jesus** and trusting in him — not by family background, rule-keeping, church attendance, or cultural heritage.
6. Jesus was more than a good teacher. He is **God** (part of the Trinity), yet became **fully human** and lived without sin. He taught and healed, died on the cross for our sins, rose from the dead, and returned to heaven; he will come back to judge everyone. He is the **Messiah** and Lord, and we must trust him to be saved.
7. No — **"Christ" is a title, not a surname**; it means **Messiah** (the promised Savior). Jesus' followers were **first called "Christians" in Antioch**, in the first century.
8. A Christian's primary calling is **love**. Christian love means **giving of ourselves for others** and **honoring people as God's image-bearers** — not just loving those close to us, not merely a private feeling between you and God, and not accepting anything a person does as automatically right.
9. **Spiritual disciplines** are practices like **prayer, Bible reading, and giving** that help us grow in faith. Becoming like Christ is a **lifelong** process, not something that happens all at once.
10. **Anthropology** is the study of **people**; **theology** is the study of **God**.
11. In the Bible, God **reveals himself** so people can know him and follow his ways, and it tells us how to live to please him. Christians place themselves **under its authority** because they believe it is God's Word. An old book is not automatically wrong — assuming we are smarter than everyone before us is *chronological snobbery* (a point C. S. Lewis made).
12. The Bible is made up of 66 books, and each book is divided into **chapters** and then into shorter portions called **verses**.
13. (a) **Proof-texting** — pulling a verse out of its context to make it say what we want. (b) Making the **Bible itself the goal** (bibliolatry) instead of letting it point us to God.
14. Creation → The Fall → Redemption Initiated (Israel) → *[Interlude: Second Temple]* → Redemption Accomplished (Jesus) → Restoration Initiated (The Church) → Restoration Accomplished (New Creation).
15. **Act 1 (Creation):** God creates the universe and everything in it, including humans, and tells them to fill and rule the earth. **Act 3 (Israel):** God chooses **Abraham**, and some of his descendants become the nation of Israel. **Act 4 (Jesus):** Jesus' life, death, and resurrection. **Last act:** Jesus returns to judge everyone; sin, death, and evil are destroyed; God makes a new heaven and earth and his kingdom lasts forever.
16. During the **interlude** there were about **400 years of silence** (no new revelation). God's people were hoping for more revelation and for a **savior** who would rescue them from their conquerors.
17. In **Act 5**, Jesus' followers (the church) are to **love God and others, imitate Christ, and proclaim God's kingdom** — for their **whole lives, until Jesus returns**. **"Already and not yet"** means God's kingdom has truly begun but is not yet fully here.
