## Answer Key

**Key Terms**
- **Worldview** — a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions.
- **Christian** — a person who follows Jesus.

**Key Verses**
- **Luke 18:22** — Jesus says, "Come, follow me." Being a Christian means following Jesus — not being born into a Christian family, keeping a set of rules, or only caring about heaven.
- **Proverbs 9:10** — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom." Knowing God is where real understanding starts.

**Review Questions**
1. A worldview is a pattern of ideas and beliefs that helps us make sense of the world and guides our decisions and actions. Examples: Christianity, Islam, secularism, Marxism, new spirituality, postmodernism.
2. A good map gives you perspective and shows you the way to go. A worldview works the same way: it helps you understand reality and find your way in life. The wrong map — or the wrong worldview — leaves you lost.
3. To be a Christian is to follow Christ. The three misconceptions: (a) that you must be *born into* a Christian family — but anyone can become a Christian by choosing to follow Jesus; (b) that it is about *keeping a set of rules* — Christians try to obey God, but they do not stop being Christians when they fail; (c) that it is *only about heaven* — Christians are also called to love and serve the people around them.
4. A person becomes a Christian by deciding to follow Jesus and trusting in him — not by family background, rule-keeping, or focusing only on heaven.
5. Christians believe Jesus was more than a good teacher. He was fully human, lived without sin, and was also God himself. He healed and taught, was crucified, died, and rose from the dead three days later. He is the Messiah and Lord; he ascended to heaven and will return one day to judge everyone. We must trust in him to be saved.
6. In the Bible, God reveals himself so people can know him and follow his ways, and Christians place themselves under its authority. An old book is not automatically wrong or unreliable — assuming we are smarter than everyone who came before us is called *chronological snobbery*.
7. (a) **Proof-texting** — pulling a verse out of its context to make it say what we want. (b) Making the **Bible itself the goal** instead of letting it point us to God.
8. Creation → The Fall → Redemption Initiated (Israel) → *[Interlude: Second Temple]* → Redemption Accomplished (Jesus) → Restoration Initiated (The Church) → Restoration Accomplished (New Creation).
