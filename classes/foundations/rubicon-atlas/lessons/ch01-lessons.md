## Day 1 - Worldview & Being a Christian (1.1-1.2)

**Anticipatory Set:** Put the question on the board: how do you find your way when you're lost? Then tell the textbook's San Francisco opening (ST 4). A map of the wrong city does not help. A worldview is your map for life.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can define a worldview and give examples, and explain what it means to be a Christian and three common misconceptions (1.1 to 1.2). First day also covers course orientation: what Foundations is and how it is graded.
**Input:** Worldview: a pattern of ideas and beliefs that makes sense of the world and guides our choices. A Christian is a follower of Jesus, not simply someone born into a Christian family, someone who keeps rules, or someone concerned only with heaven. Key term: worldview. Key verse: Luke 18:22.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion (teacher manual p.4 to 5): What is a worldview? How would you describe your own? What did you think being a Christian meant? Reflective, so "I don't know yet" is fine on day one.
**Independent Practice:** Curious About activity (manual p.5): on an index card, students write what stirred their curiosity and what they hope to learn this year, then share with a partner. Homework: read 1.3 to 1.4 (ST 7 to 9).
**Closure:** Everyone has a worldview, and the question is whether yours has been examined. Preview where the course is headed.

## Day 2 - The Bible & How to Read It (1.3-1.4)

**Anticipatory Set:** Warm-up question: should an idea be dismissed just because it is old? Surface a few answers, then name chronological snobbery (manual p.6).
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can explain why the Bible is foundational for Christians and identify two common mistakes people make when reading it (1.3 to 1.4).
**Input:** The Bible is God's written revelation: 66 books, many authors, one story, and Christians place themselves under its authority. Two mistakes: proof-texting (pulling verses out of context) and treating the Bible itself, rather than God, as the goal.
**Guided Practice:** Bible Scavenger Hunt (TR 1.2A, manual p.7): in groups of 4 to 5, students race to answer 25 Bible-navigation questions using a physical Bible. Check answers with the key; the fastest correct group wins. Gets students into the book and learning its layout.
**Independent Practice:** Homework: read 1.5 to 1.6 (ST 9 to 11), the six acts of the story.
**Closure:** Quick debrief: what surprised you about how the Bible is arranged, and which book was hardest to find?

## Day 3 - The Story of the Bible in Six Acts (1.5-1.6)

**Objective and Purpose:** Students can describe the first three acts and the interlude and summarize the final three acts of the Bible's story (1.5 to 1.6). This carries the most weight on the chapter test.
**Input:** The six-act story: Creation, the Fall, Redemption Initiated (Israel), the Second Temple interlude, Redemption Accomplished (Jesus), Restoration Initiated (the Church), and Restoration Accomplished (New Creation).
**Modeling:** Walk the six acts on the board, writing each one as you go and keeping it to a sentence each (manual p.8). This board map is the spine of the whole course and the test's matching section; leave it up through the block.
**Guided Practice:** Producer's List die game (manual p.9): in a circle, each student rolls, says "for Act ___ I need ___," names an object tied to that act, then repeats the growing chain. A wrong act or item is out. Reinforces the act order through play.
**Closure:** Ask where we live in the story now (Act 5, already and not yet) and how it ends (Act 6). Homework: read 1.7 to 1.10 (ST 12 to 16).

## Day 4 - Knowing God & Self; Christlikeness; Love (1.7-1.10)

**Anticipatory Set:** Beliefs and Behavior Brainstorm (manual p.11): a T-chart, beliefs affect behavior and behavior affects beliefs, with a few quick student examples.
**Objective and Purpose:** Students can explain why knowing God and ourselves matters, that becoming like Christ takes a lifetime, and why love is a Christian's primary calling (1.7 to 1.10).
**Input:** Knowing God (Prov 9:10, the fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom) and knowing ourselves as image-bearers; becoming like Christ as a lifelong process helped by spiritual disciplines; love as the Christian's primary calling.
**Check for Understanding:** Discussion from manual p.11 to 12, favoring 1.8 (Christlikeness) and 1.9 (love).
**Guided Practice:** Being a Christian (TR 1.5A, manual p.13): in pairs, students read 10 quotations about being a Christian, from believers and skeptics, and relate each to the misconceptions from 1.2, then share the ones that provoked debate.
**Closure:** Tell the class tomorrow is a sub day with videos that counts like any class day. Optional homework: skim the Study Guide (SM 21 to 23).

## Day 5 - SUB DAY: Worldview Video Set

**Objective and Purpose:** Students reinforce the chapter's ideas through six short expert videos (teacher absence; self-contained).
**Input:** Six videos on worldview and apologetics featuring Myers, Stonestreet, Sherrard, Wallace, and Heard (manual p.15 to 17). Model answers for grading are in the teacher manual.
**Independent Practice:** For each video, students read the questions, watch, and write answers; after the sixth, they write a half-page reflection. Collected as the first graded work of the year.
**Closure:** Collect the answer sheets and reflections. Fallback if streaming fails: reread 1.1 to 1.6 and write a paragraph on the Bible's story.

## Day 6 - Mapmaking Synthesis

**Objective and Purpose:** Students organize the chapter's ideas by building a visual map of the Christian worldview.
**Modeling:** Display PS 1.6A (manual p.14) and explain the two options: a geographical map (title, direction, legend, scale, labels) or a mind map (title, labels, and the relationships among ideas).
**Guided Practice:** In groups of 4 to 5, students build a poster map, then present and explain it. Push them to include the six acts, the definition of a worldview, who Jesus is, and why the Bible matters.
**Closure:** Note the similarities and differences across the maps. Land the point: you just drew the map for the whole year. Homework: begin studying the Study Guide (SM 21 to 23).

---

## Day 7 - Review Games & Study Guide

**Objective and Purpose:** Students consolidate the chapter and lock in the key terms (worldview, Christian) and key verses (Luke 18:22, Prov 9:10). High test weight.
**Guided Practice:** Quiz Cards (manual p.18): each student writes nine questions, one per section 1.1 to 1.9, and pairs quiz each other. Then walk the PS 1.8A review slides as a class, hitting the six-acts matching hard.
**Independent Practice:** Students work the Study Guide (SM 21 to 23); the manual's study-guide answers are on p.19 to 21.
**Closure:** Preview the test (87 points): matching the six acts, plus multiple choice on worldviews, becoming a Christian, Jesus, and the Bible. Homework: study.

---

## Day 8 - Chapter 1 Test

**Objective and Purpose:** Students demonstrate mastery of Chapter 1.
**Independent Practice:** Chapter 1 Test (TR 1.10A, 87 points), completed independently. Early finishers begin reading 2.1 to 2.2.
**Closure:** Collect the tests. Preview Chapter 2: where did the Bible come from, and can we trust it? Homework: read 2.1 to 2.2.
