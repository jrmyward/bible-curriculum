*Source: Understanding the Faith: A Survey of Christian Apologetics by Jeff Myers (Summit). Rebuilt 2026-05-08 from Apple Vision OCR of the publisher's syllabus pages and the textbook's Contents page.*

## Course Overview

A 180-day (36-week, 5 days/week) survey of Christian apologetics for high school. Students engage 18 chapters covering the Bible, God, the Christian story, ethics, and difficult cultural questions. Each chapter occupies two weeks (one week of reading + discussion + activities, one week of supplemental quizzes/videos + chapter test).

## Standard Weekly Pattern

The publisher's syllabus follows a near-identical rhythm for every chapter. Recognizing the pattern makes the daily schedule below easier to read.

**Odd-numbered week (intro week, e.g. Week 1, 3, 5, …):**

- **Day 1 of week:** REVIEW prior chapter test + assignment, ASSIGN new chapter assignment, VIEW new chapter objectives, READ new chapter (in class + at home)
- **Days 2–5:** DISCUSS chapter questions + EXPLORE activities

**Even-numbered week (test week, e.g. Week 2, 4, 6, …):**

- **Day 1 of week:** DISCUSS questions + EXPLORE activities
- **Day 2:** READ supplemental article + GIVE supplemental quiz + DISCUSS supplemental questions
- **Day 3:** WATCH supplemental video + GIVE video quiz + DISCUSS video questions
- **Day 4:** STUDY for chapter test + COLLECT chapter assignment
- **Day 5:** GIVE chapter test + READ next chapter (at home)

Week 1 omits the prior-test review (no prior chapter). Weeks 35–36 (Ch 18 / Conclusion) drop the supplemental media and use the extra time for review and the final test.

## Pacing Notes

- **Total duration:** 180 instructional days (36 weeks × 5 days). Local school calendars will require compression — the publisher's rhythm assumes no holidays, breaks, or teacher absences.
- **Per-chapter rhythm:** Two weeks per chapter for chapters 1–17. Chapter 18 (Conclusion) gets two weeks but no supplemental media — extra time is used for final review and the test.
- **Assessment cadence:** Each chapter ends with a test (administered on the 10th day of its two-week block) and an assignment that is collected the day before the test.
- **Supplemental media:** Most chapters integrate one short reading + one video (each with its own quiz and discussion). These are sourced from the curriculum's supplementary CD/DVD materials (marked "CD" in the original page-number column).

## Materials

The publisher's syllabus references the following recurring item types:

- **Chapter Assignment:** Per-chapter written workbook assignment (each is given a starting page number).
- **Chapter Objectives:** Learning objectives list (in the teacher's manual).
- **Chapter Questions:** End-of-chapter discussion questions in the textbook.
- **Activities:** Per-chapter "EXPLORE UTF NN Activities" — typically extension exercises in the workbook.
- **Quizzes:** Per supplemental reading and per supplemental video.
- **Chapter Test:** End-of-chapter assessment.
- **Supplemental Readings:** Articles/excerpts paired with most chapters (e.g., "Misquoting Jesus", "Why Work?", "Isn't Religion Based on Faith?").
- **Supplemental Videos:** Video segments paired with most chapters (e.g., "The Case for Truth", "Dealing with Doubt", "Naturalism" Parts 1–2).
