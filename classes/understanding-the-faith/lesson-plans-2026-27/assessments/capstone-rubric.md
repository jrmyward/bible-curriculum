# Capstone Project Rubric

**Format:** Individual (or pairs, at teacher discretion)
**Presentation:** 5–7 minutes + 2–3 minutes Q&A from classmates
**Visual Aid:** Slides, poster, or handout — student's choice
**Final Brief:** Written reflection submitted after all presentations

**Total: _____ / 100 points**

---

## Rubric

| Category | Points | What You're Looking For |
|----------|--------|-------------------------|
| **Thesis Clarity** | 25 | Clear, specific, and defensible. Directly answers the prompt. |
| **Evidence Quality** | 30 | Uses evidence from the textbook, class discussions, and primary sources. Evidence is relevant and well-explained. |
| **Counterargument** | 25 | Addresses a *real* objection (not a straw man). Response is thoughtful and substantive. |
| **Presentation** | 10 | Clear, organized, within time limits. Visual aid supports the argument. |
| **Final Brief** | 10 | Shows depth of reflection and evidence of listening to peers during Q&A. |

---

## Scoring Guide

### Thesis Clarity (25 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **22–25** | Thesis is sharp, specific, and clearly defensible. Reader/listener knows exactly what the student is arguing. |
| **17–21** | Thesis is present and reasonable but could be more specific or focused. |
| **12–16** | Thesis is vague, overly broad, or only implied. |
| **0–11** | No clear thesis, or thesis doesn't address the prompt. |

### Evidence Quality (30 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **26–30** | Multiple sources (Scripture, textbook, class discussion, outside sources). Evidence is specific, relevant, and clearly connected to the thesis. |
| **20–25** | Evidence is present and relevant but thin — relies on one source or lacks specificity. |
| **13–19** | Evidence is weak, generic, or poorly connected to the argument. |
| **0–12** | Little to no evidence, or evidence doesn't support the thesis. |

### Counterargument (25 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **22–25** | Identifies a genuine, strong objection. Response is substantive and shows real engagement with the opposing view. |
| **17–21** | Counterargument is present but could be stronger or more specific. Response is adequate. |
| **12–16** | Counterargument is a straw man or the response is superficial. |
| **0–11** | No counterargument addressed, or counterargument is dismissed without engagement. |

### Presentation (10 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **9–10** | Clear, organized, good pacing. Visual aid supports the argument without replacing it. Within time limits. |
| **7–8** | Mostly clear but some disorganization or pacing issues. Visual aid present but underutilized. |
| **5–6** | Hard to follow, significantly over/under time, or visual aid is distracting/absent. |
| **0–4** | Unprepared, incoherent, or refused to present. |

### Final Brief (10 pts)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **9–10** | Genuine reflection showing how thinking evolved. References specific peer presentations or Q&A moments. |
| **7–8** | Adequate reflection but surface-level. Some evidence of engagement with peers' ideas. |
| **5–6** | Brief is generic or doesn't show engagement beyond the student's own presentation. |
| **0–4** | Missing, incomplete, or shows no reflection. |

---

## Presentation Requirements

1. State a thesis related to the unit's central question
2. Support it with evidence from the text and class discussions
3. Address at least one counterargument
4. Visual aid supports the argument (slides, poster, handout — student's choice)
5. Stay within 5–7 minutes

## Final Brief

- Same format as a Discussion Brief (1 page, double-spaced)
- Prompt: *"How has your thinking changed since the beginning of this unit? What's the strongest idea you encountered, and why?"*
- Due after all presentations are complete

## Grading Notes

- Grade on clarity of thought and quality of evidence, not polish or presentation style
- The Final Brief is often the most revealing piece of student writing — read them carefully
- Print this rubric, mark scores in each category, and provide 1–2 sentences of feedback per category
