# Pair & Defend Rubric

**Format:** Paired debate — positions assigned, not chosen
**Duration:** ~15 minutes per pair (3 min opening each, 2 min follow-up each, 5 min class debrief)
**Grading:** Participation-based (pass/fail)

---

## How It Works

1. Students are paired (randomly or strategically)
2. Each partner is assigned a position related to the chapter's core question — one defends the thesis, one argues against it
3. Positions are **assigned, not chosen** — students must argue the side they're given
4. After all pairs present, the class debriefs: *"What was the strongest argument you heard from the side you disagreed with?"*

## What You're Grading

- **Did they show up prepared?**
- **Did they defend their assigned position (even if they disagreed with it)?**
- **Did they engage respectfully during Q&A?**

## Grading Scale

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **100%** | Participated fully. Defended assigned position for the full 3 min. Asked/answered follow-up questions. Engaged respectfully. |
| **50%** | Participated but unprepared. Didn't use the full 3 min, or struggled to articulate the assigned position. |
| **0%** | Absent or refused to participate. |

## Examples

### Full Credit (100%)

> Defended "culturally bound" position for 3 minutes. Argued: "The Bible regulated slavery in its cultural context, but the trajectory of Scripture is toward abolition. Paul tells Philemon to receive Onesimus 'no longer as a slave, but... as a dear brother' (Philemon 1:16)." Asked a follow-up question. Engaged respectfully even though they personally disagree with the position they defended.

### Partial Credit (50%)

> Defended their position for only 90 seconds. Struggled to articulate the argument: "Um... I guess slavery was different back then? I don't really know." Didn't ask a follow-up question. They showed up and tried, but weren't prepared.

### No Credit (0%)

> Refused to defend their assigned position: "I don't agree with this, so I'm not going to argue for it." The whole point of Pair & Defend is to argue a position you don't hold. Refusal to participate = 0.

## Grading Notes

- **Fail students who refuse to participate.** The pedagogical value is in defending a position you don't hold. If they won't do it, they miss the point.
- **Give partial credit for effort.** If a student tries but struggles, give 50%. The goal is to encourage participation, not punish weak arguments.
- **Watch for disrespectful engagement.** If a student argues by mocking the opposing view, dock points or give a warning.
- Remind students before every round: the goal is to **understand**, not to win.
