# Case Study Rubric

**Format:** Groups of 3–4 students
**Duration:** ~25–30 minutes (10 min group work, 5 min presentations, 5 min vote & debrief)
**Grading:** Participation-based (pass/fail)

---

## Scenario Types

- **Ethical dilemma:** A situation with no clean answer — two competing goods or an unavoidable trade-off
- **Cultural encounter:** A conversation or conflict between people with different worldviews
- **Historical parallel:** A past event that mirrors the chapter's themes, presented without the resolution

## Group Deliverables

Each group must produce all three:

1. **Summary of the dilemma** — What's the situation? What makes it hard?
2. **Recommended response** — What should the person/group do, and why?
3. **Strongest counterargument** — What's the best objection to your own recommendation?

## Grading Scale

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **100%** | Participated in group work. Group produced all 3 deliverables. Contributed to presentation. |
| **50%** | Participated but group deliverables were incomplete or rushed. |
| **0%** | Absent, refused to participate, or clearly did not contribute to the group. |

## Presentation & Vote

- Each group presents their recommendation (1–2 min per group)
- Class votes: *"Which response would you trust in a real situation?"*
- Teacher debrief follows the vote

## Grading Notes

- Grade on engagement, not on the "right" answer
- Use peer feedback: ask "Did everyone in your group contribute equally?"
- Rotate group composition regularly so students don't settle into comfortable roles
- Write scenarios that don't have an obvious Sunday School answer — the value is in the tension
