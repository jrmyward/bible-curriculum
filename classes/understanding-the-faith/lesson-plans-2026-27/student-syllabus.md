## Course Description

This course is about learning to think — clearly, honestly, and out loud. You'll explore the big questions that shape how people see the world: Does God exist? Can we know truth? Is the Bible reliable? Why do people walk away from faith? You won't just memorize answers. You'll read, write, argue, and defend positions — sometimes ones you disagree with — because understanding an idea is not the same as agreeing with it.

## How This Class Works

This is a **discussion-first** class. You won't sit through lectures. Instead, you'll read, write, debate, and defend ideas — often positions you don't personally hold. Here's what that looks like:

### Discussion Briefs
A short (1-page max) written response to a prompt, due before class discussion. You commit to a position in writing, then defend it in conversation. Graded on thoughtfulness, not on having the "right" answer. You'll write 18 of these throughout the year.

### Pair & Defend
You and a partner each get assigned a side of a debate — you don't get to pick. You prepare and present a 3-minute argument, then respond to each other's questions. The goal isn't to win; it's to make the strongest possible case for your assigned side.

### Case Studies
Small-group work where you apply what you've learned to real-world scenarios — ethical dilemmas, cultural encounters, and historical parallels. Your group presents a recommendation and defends it to the class.

### Capstone Projects
The big ones. Four times this year, you'll synthesize an entire unit into a thesis and present it to the class with a visual aid. You'll face questions from your classmates. These replace traditional tests as your major assessments.

## Expectations

1. **Come prepared.** Complete all reading before class. You can't discuss what you haven't read.
2. **Engage respectfully.** Disagree without being disagreeable. Attack ideas, not people.
3. **Take intellectual risks.** It's okay to be wrong. It's not okay to be silent.
4. **Do your own thinking.** Your opinions and arguments need to be yours, not borrowed from a screen (see Technology policy below).

## Policies

### Late Work

Late work is accepted up to **3 school days** after the due date with a 10% deduction per day. After 3 days, the assignment receives a zero. If you know in advance that you'll miss a deadline (illness, family obligation, extenuating circumstances, etc) talk to me *before* the due date. I'm reasonable when you communicate; I'm less reasonable when you don't.

Discussion Briefs have a hard deadline: they must be submitted before the class discussion they're tied to. A brief turned in after we've already discussed the topic defeats the purpose and will not be accepted. Plan ahead.

Capstone presentations cannot be made up outside the scheduled presentation window except in cases of documented absence. If you miss your presentation day, see me immediately to arrange an alternative.

### Academic Integrity

Your work must be your own. This means:

- **Discussion Briefs and essays:** Your words, your reasoning. You may reference Scripture, the textbook, and class discussions. You may not copy from classmates, websites, or any other source without citation.
- **Pair & Defend and Case Studies:** Collaboration within your assigned group is expected. Copying another group's work is not.
- **Capstones:** Your thesis and argument must be original. Cite all sources.

Plagiarism or cheating on any assignment results in a zero for that assignment and a conversation with me. Repeated violations will be referred to administration per the Watersprings Student Handbook.

### Technology

- **Phones:** Put them away. Phones are not permitted during class for any reason. If I see it, I'll collect it.
- **Laptops:** You may use a laptop for typing Discussion Briefs and taking notes. That's it. No browsing. No messaging. No multitasking.
- **AI and LLMs (ChatGPT, etc.):** Do not use AI tools to generate, draft, or refine your arguments. The entire point of this class is to form your own opinions and defend them with your own reasoning. If I suspect an argument isn't yours, I'll ask you to defend it on the spot. If you can't, it doesn't count.

### Accommodations

Students with documented accommodations should refer to the Watersprings Student Handbook and coordinate with the school office. I'm happy to discuss individual needs.
