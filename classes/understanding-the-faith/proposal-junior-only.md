# Proposal: Lock HS Bible Course Sequencing by Grade Starting 2026–2027

**To:** [Head of School] and [High School Principal]
**From:** Jeremy Ward, HS Bible
**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Re:** Replacing the year-by-year course alternation with a fixed Junior → Senior sequence

---

## The Proposal

Replace the current alternation between *Understanding the Faith* (apologetics) and *Understanding the Times* (worldview) with a permanent, grade-locked sequence:

- **Juniors:** *Understanding the Faith* — every year, no exceptions
- **Seniors:** *Understanding the Times* — every year, with *Understanding the Faith* as prerequisite

Implement beginning fall 2026, running two HS Bible sections this year (one per grade) instead of one mixed-grade section.

## The Problem with Year-by-Year Alternation

The current scheduling pattern offers Worldview to all juniors and seniors one year, then Apologetics to all juniors and seniors the next. On its face this looks like a fair rotation — every student takes both courses across their two years. But it produces *backwards sequencing for every other cohort*:

| Junior year is…                        | Junior course | Senior course | Sequence       |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------- | ------------- | -------------- |
| A "Worldview year" (e.g., 2026–27)     | Worldview     | Apologetics   | **Backwards**  |
| An "Apologetics year" (e.g., 2027–28)  | Apologetics   | Worldview     | Correct        |

Half of every two-year cycle gets the courses in the wrong order. The cohort entering 11th grade this fall is one of those halves.

## Why Apologetics Must Come First

*Understanding the Faith* answers *"What do Christians believe, and why is it defensible?"* It builds the student's own house: truth and relativism, the Bible's reliability and authority, the nature of God and the biblical metanarrative, the supernatural and resurrection, evil and science, hermeneutics, and ethical practice.

*Understanding the Times* takes that house and asks, *"How does it compare to the nine other houses your neighbors live in?"* Its stated goal is that every student leave able to fairly represent any of nine worldviews back to its adherents and present the Gospel in a way that is meaningful to *that person specifically*. That is comparative work. Comparative work fails — and slides toward caricature — when the student cannot first articulate the Christian baseline.

The worldview course's curricular commitment is *charitable engagement*: representing each worldview "in its best, most charitable form with no strawmen." A student who has not yet worked through *why* the Bible is reliable, *how* the problem of evil is answered, or *what* the resurrection evidences, will reach for caricature when comparing Christianity to Islam, secularism, or expressive individualism. Apologetics first prevents that.

There is also a developmental fit: juniors are starting to encounter serious challenges to their faith from peers, social media, and college-prep reading. They need *defensive* tools. Seniors are months from leaving for college or work and need *engagement* tools — the ability to hold a real conversation with someone whose worldview differs from theirs. Apologetics → Worldview maps cleanly onto that arc; the reverse does not.

## The Implementation Window

The current scheduling cycle creates a uniquely clean opportunity to lock the sequence in fall 2026 *without disrupting any current student*:

- **Rising seniors (class of 2027)** take *Understanding the Times* this year — exactly what they would have taken under the alternation plan. No change.
- **Rising juniors (class of 2028)** take *Understanding the Faith* this year, then *Understanding the Times* next year as seniors. They land on the correct sequence with no make-up work.

Every subsequent cohort follows the same path automatically. The fix is permanent after one decision.

## What Changes

- Two HS Bible sections in 2026–27 (one junior, one senior) instead of one mixed-grade section
- Both courses become permanent grade-locked offerings beginning this fall
- Course catalog and graduation-requirements language updated to reflect the prerequisite

## What Stays the Same

- All lesson plans, materials, and teaching maps already prepared for 2026–27 remain valid (the Worldview map is already designed for the senior course, and the Understanding the Faith map for the junior course)
- Total Bible credits required for graduation are unchanged
- Both courses retain their current scope, source texts, and discussion-first instructional model
- Total Bible teaching load remains within current staffing — two sections, two preps, both already prepared

## Anticipated Objections

**"Two sections is more work for the Bible department."**
The two preps already exist — both teaching maps are written, both sets of lesson plans are scaffolded. The marginal cost is running two sections during the same school year rather than one, which is a scheduling decision more than a content-creation cost. Going forward, both courses cycle through the same cohort every year, so the prep amortizes immediately rather than being shelved for a year between offerings.

**"What about students who transfer in or only attend for one of the two years?"**
A transfer student arriving as a senior without prior apologetics is a small case-by-case decision: either a one-trimester accelerated apologetics review embedded in the worldview course, or a waiver if their prior schooling covered equivalent material. The alternation pattern doesn't actually solve this problem — a transfer arriving in a "Worldview year" who hasn't done apologetics has the same gap. Locking the sequence at least makes the gap predictable and addressable.

**"We'd lose the flexibility of the alternating pattern."**
That flexibility is the source of the sequencing problem. The alternation was a reasonable single-section solution when staffing or scheduling required it, but it builds in a 50% chance of every cohort getting the courses in the wrong order. If two sections are feasible, the flexibility is not worth the pedagogical cost.

## Recommendation

Adopt the locked Junior → Senior sequence beginning fall 2026. The window to make this change without disrupting any current student closes when the school year starts; after August it becomes a 2027–28 implementation question with one cohort already mis-sequenced.

I am available to walk through the proposal at any leadership or department meeting, and to address any scheduling or staffing questions that come out of it.
