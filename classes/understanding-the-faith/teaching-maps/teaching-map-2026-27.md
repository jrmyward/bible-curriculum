# Understanding the Faith — Teaching Map

**School Year:** 2026–2027
**Grade Level:** 11–12
**Series:** Understanding the Faith: A Survey of Christian Apologetics (Jeff Myers, Summit)
**Total Instructional Days:** 152 (T1: 50 days | T2: 57 days | T3: 45 days)

## Course Context

A cohort-style, discussion-first survey of Christian apologetics. Students engage 18 chapters covering the Bible, God, the Christian story, and difficult cultural questions through structured dialogue, debate, case studies, and synthesis rather than lecture-heavy instruction.

This map paces the publisher's 180-day syllabus into the school's 152 actual instructional days (after holidays, teacher work days, and seven April–May golf absences). Chapter assignments and unit themes are anchored to the rebuilt official syllabus (see [`syllabus/syllabus.md`](../syllabus/syllabus.md)) and verified against the textbook source.

## Unit Overview

| Unit | Chapters | Theme | Weeks | Trimester |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ch 1–2 | Foundations: course intro + what the Bible is | 1–3 | T1 |
| 2 | Ch 3–4 | The Bible: authority + what it says about God | 4–7 | T1 |
| 3 | Ch 5–6 | God's Big Story (Part 1 + Part 2) | 8–10 | T1 |
| 4 | Ch 7–8 | How to read the Bible + loving God | 11–15 | T1/T2 |
| 5 | Ch 9–10 | Loving neighbors + Is God Christian? | 16–20 | T2 |
| 6 | Ch 11–12 | Supernatural + science | 21–27 | T2 |
| 7 | Ch 13–14 | Truth/tolerance + evil/suffering | 28–30 | T3 |
| 8 | Ch 15–18 | Hell, the OT God, walking away, and synthesis | 32–39 | T3 |

Capstones occur at the end of Units 2, 4, 6, and 8 (trimester-aligned). Spring Break sits between Units 7 and 8 (Wk 31).

## Cohort Tool Rhythm

Each unit follows this pattern:

1. **Introduce (1–2 days):** Reading + Discussion Brief
2. **Engage (1 day):** Pair & Defend
3. **Apply (1 day):** Case Study
4. **Synthesize (1–2 days, major units only):** Capstone

## Notes for the Teacher

1. **Pacing flexibility:** This map covers **152 instructional days** vs. the publisher's 180. The publisher paces 2 weeks per chapter; this map averages ~1.7 weeks (compressed via the cohort tools and Capstone synthesis weeks). Adjust as needed based on class engagement.

2. **Shortened weeks:** Many weeks are 3–4 days due to teacher work days, holidays, and seven April–May golf absences. Plan lighter activities for those weeks; use them for review, capstone work, or flex time.

3. **Capstone scheduling:** Capstones are placed before major breaks and trimester ends to allow natural stopping points and grade finalization. The Final Capstone runs across Weeks 36–38 because of the compressed April/May calendar.

4. **No-school adjustments:** All no-school dates from the school calendar are accounted for, including 15 teacher work days and 7 golf absences in April–May.

5. **Cohort tool integration:** Each unit follows Introduce → Engage → Apply → Synthesize. By Week 4 students should be familiar with the tools and increasingly independent.

6. **Discussion-first model:** Reading is completed *before* Discussion Briefs are due, so students engage the text and commit to a position in writing before class discussion.

7. **Student agency:** Scaffolded transfer of ownership across the year — Week 4 (accountable talk norms), Week 12 (mid-year peer feedback), Week 26 (student-led discussion), Week 38 (student-led final discussion).

8. **Final Capstone:** The culminating assessment. Students synthesize the entire course into a single thesis and presentation. Replaces a traditional final exam.

9. **Buffer and flex time:** Multiple flex days are scheduled for catch-up, deeper exploration of student questions, or extending Capstone presentations if class size requires it.

10. **April–May pacing:** Seven golf absences (Mondays Apr 5, 12, 19, 26 and May 3) plus three teacher work days result in many 3-day weeks. Final Capstone prep begins Week 35 (Apr 21) to spread the cognitive load. Presentations run Weeks 36–37 (Apr 27 – May 7), with work days available for makeups.

11. **T2 cognitive load management:** Weeks 21–25 cover heavy intellectual content (supernatural + science). Week 17 (Fri) includes an ungraded synthesis reflection and Week 20 (Tue) opens with a Socratic seminar to reconnect themes after Christmas break.

12. **Case Study variety:** Case Studies include ethical dilemmas, cultural encounters, historical parallels, and pastoral contexts — all anchored to actual chapter content (e.g., Wk 16 loving a hostile neighbor pairs with Ch 9; Wk 24 Christian student in biology class pairs with Ch 12; Wk 29 pastoral response to tragedy pairs with Ch 14).

13. **Substitute plans:** Days marked **SUB** indicate teacher absences requiring substitute plans (7 total: Aug 28, Sep 3, Apr 5, 12, 19, 26, May 3). These days use non-cohort activities a substitute can facilitate: individual work (reading, comprehension questions, reflection writing), video with guided notes, or work days. Cohort tools (Pair & Defend, Case Studies, presentations) are scheduled on days the teacher is present.
