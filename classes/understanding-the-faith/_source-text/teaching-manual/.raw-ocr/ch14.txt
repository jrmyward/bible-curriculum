"GOD AND EVIL" DEBATE CLIPS
Dr. William Lane Craig, Protessor of Philosophy at Talbot School of Theology, and D
Alex Rosenberg, Professor of Philosophy at Duke University, debated the Problem of
Evil at Biola University. Here are two excerpts having to do with the logical argument
of the problem of evil and the free will defense.
In part 1, Dr. Craig dismisses the logical argument of the problem of evil by asserting
logically impossible to make someone freely do something. The atheist would have to
prove there's a world of free creatures which God could create that has as much good
as this world but without as much evil, which is pure speculation.
In part 2, Dr. Rosenberg raises three arguments against the free will defense, the as-
sertion that evil and suffering are a necessary consequence of free will: 1) God could
have given us free will without giving us so much suffering. 2) God could have given
us free will without temptation being presented to us. 3) God gave some people free
will and they caused no suffering at all.

"THE PROBLEM OF EVIL" VIDEO
In tackling the problem of evil, Mark Mittelberg gently and thoroughly examines one
of the biggest obstacles to the Christian faith: Why does God allow evil and suffering?
He points out that the problem has more to do with the character of human beings
than with the character of God, since we are the cause of the vast majority of evil in
the world.
Mittelberg presents "seven points oflight" —truths about evil and suffering— that help
us understand why we suffer and how we can put it in perspective. His well-drawn
conclusion is that we can trust the God who came and suffered in our place to save us
from evil and suffering in the end.
