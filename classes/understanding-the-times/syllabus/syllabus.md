# Worldview — Official Syllabus

*Source: Understanding the Times: A Survey of Competing Worldviews by Jeff Myers & David Noebel (Summit, Teacher Manual). Rebuilt 2026-05-08 from Apple Vision OCR of the publisher's syllabus pages and the textbook's Contents page.*

---

## Course Overview

A 180-day (36-week, 5 days/week) survey of competing worldviews for high school. Students examine 7 worldviews in isolation (Ch 1–7) and then 10 academic disciplines compared across worldviews (Ch 8–17), concluding with a synthesis chapter (Ch 18). Each chapter occupies two weeks (one week of reading + discussion + activities, one week of supplemental reading + video + chapter test).

## Standard Weekly Pattern

The publisher's syllabus follows a near-identical rhythm for every chapter.

**Odd-numbered week (intro week):**

- **Day 1:** REVIEW prior chapter test + assignment, ASSIGN new chapter writing assignment, VIEW new chapter objectives, READ new chapter (in class + at home)
- **Days 2–5:** DISCUSS chapter questions + EXPLORE activities

**Even-numbered week (test week):**

- **Day 1:** DISCUSS questions + EXPLORE activities
- **Day 2:** READ supplemental primary-source text + GIVE supplemental quiz + DISCUSS supplemental questions
- **Day 3:** WATCH supplemental video + GIVE video quiz + DISCUSS video questions
- **Day 4:** STUDY for chapter test
- **Day 5:** COLLECT chapter writing assignment + GIVE chapter test + READ next chapter (at home)

Week 1 omits the prior-test review (no prior chapter). Ch 10 (Ethics) substitutes a second video for the supplemental reading. Ch 18 (Conclusion) drops most supplemental media.

## Pacing Notes

- **Total duration:** 180 instructional days (36 weeks × 5 days). Local school calendars will require compression — the publisher's rhythm assumes no holidays, breaks, or teacher absences.
- **Per-chapter rhythm:** Two weeks per chapter for chapters 1–17. Chapter 18 (Conclusion) gets two weeks but no supplemental media — extra time is used for final review and the test.
- **Assessment cadence:** Each chapter ends with a test (administered on the 10th day of its two-week block) and a writing assignment that is collected on the test day.
- **Supplemental media:** Most chapters integrate one primary-source reading + one video (each with its own quiz and discussion). Ch 10 (Ethics) substitutes a second video for the supplemental reading.

## Materials

The publisher's syllabus references the following recurring item types:

- **Chapter Writing Assignment:** Per-chapter written workbook assignment (each is given a starting page number).
- **Chapter Objectives:** Learning objectives list (in the teacher's manual).
- **Chapter Questions:** End-of-chapter discussion questions in the textbook.
- **Activities:** Per-chapter "EXPLORE UTT Chapter N Activities" — typically extension exercises in the workbook.
- **Quizzes:** Per supplemental reading and per supplemental video.
- **Chapter Test:** End-of-chapter assessment.
- **Supplemental Readings:** Primary-source texts paired with most chapters (e.g., *Plato's Cave*, *The Communist Manifesto*, *On the Origin of Species*, *Animal Farm*).
- **Supplemental Videos:** Worldview-specific video segments paired with most chapters (e.g., "The Christian Worldview", "The Marxist Worldview").
