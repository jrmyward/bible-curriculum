What are the three forces that combined to shape secular psychology? [12.3]
Today's secular psychology is often called third force psychology. The first force was B.
F. Skinner's behaviorism. The second force was Sigmund Freuds psychoanalysis. The
third force was based on Abraham Maslow's theory of self-actualization.
B. F. Skinner thought stimulus-response could explain everything. But behaviorism
fell out of favor because human actions can't be easily reduced to predictable animal
behavior. Behaviorism also doesn't allow for human free will.
Sigmund Freud thought conscious behavior was shaped by unconscious recollection
of past experiences. Through psychoanalysis he tried to uncover the unconscious mo-
ivations of his clients. Psychoanalysis is an attempt to resolve a patient's psychological
problems by uncovering and discussing unconscious desires.
Third force psychology attempts to address the limitations of behaviorism and psycho-
analysis. Its major theorists include Carl Rogers, Erich Fromm, and Abraham Maslow.

11. What assumptions shape postmodern psychology? [12.5]
The ideas of "soul" and human "essence" are obsolete to Postmodernists. Instead of an
identifiable, unique "self" they believe in a decentered self. Human beings are social
constructions without any singular essence, nature, or soul. Instead, we may actually have
multiple selves, an idea developed by French psychoanalyst Jacques Lacan and others.
Social psychologists have begun studying not only our "child selves," our "profes-
sional selves," our "friendship selves," and our "parent selves" but also what Hazel
Markus labels our "possible selves," our "feared possible selves," our "ideal selves,"
our "fleeting selves," our "tentative selves," and our "chronically accessible selves."
(Mitchell Stephens)
Postmodernists reject the idea of a coherent soul or a unified self. It's these old concepts
that keep us in the herd rather than expressing ourselves as we see fit. The truly healthy
person resists society's attempts to define him and rejects any attempt to diagnosis him
as insane merely for refusing to follow along.

A SUBSTANTIAL HEALING
by Francis Schaeffer
In Romans 8 Paul looks ahead to what is going to happen when Jesus Christ comes
back again. He writes: "For the earnest expectation of the creation waiteth for the
manifestation of the sons of God [the Christians]. For the creation was made subject

age of corruption into the glorious liberty of the children of God. For we know that

What Paul says there is that when our bodies are raised from the dead, at that

their animals.
As we stressed in the last chapter, the Bible has no place at all for Platonic distinc-
tions about nature. As Christ's death redeems men, including their bodies, from
the consequences of the Fall, so His death will redeem all nature from the Fall's evil
consequences at the time when we are raised from the dead.

Christ's death and resurrection not only redeems humans, body and soul, from the
