1. What do the Bhagavad Gita and New Spirituality share in common? [6.2]
Both believe in a universe that is eternal, non-personal, and all encompassing. We are all
part of this impersonal divine force or consciousness. This is in contrast to the illusion
of the individualistic, autonomous self at the heart of secular and religious worldviews.
2. Is there anything "new" in the worldview known as New Spirituality? [6.2]
There's not much new in New Spirituality, also referred to as the New Age Movement. It
is an old set of beliefs with roots in Hinduism and other Eastern religions that is dressed
up in pop psychology and pseudo science.
New Spirituality is a pantheistic worldview that teaches everything and everyone are con-
nected through divine consciousness. The physical world is an illusion, where nothing
is objective except our own mental states. Truth and enlightenment are found within.

7. What are the four basic teachings of New Spirituality? [6.5]
Everything is consciousness: Consciousness is the divine interconnected essence of
reality. It is an impersonal force characterized as silent and peaceful, self-sufficient,
awake, possessing infinite potential, self-organizing, spontaneous, dynamic, blissful,
knowing, and whole.
The whole is made up of existence and Being, the manifested and the unmanifested,
the whole and God. So when you become aligned with the whole, you become a
conscious part of the interconnectedness of the whole and its purpose: the emer-
gence of consciousness into this world. (Eckhart Tolle)
If everything is consciousness, then ultimately everything is God.
Everyone is God: Everything is consciousness, including humans. New Spiritualism
refers to this consciousness as "God." This god is not a separate person with identifiable
attributes. It is a force, a principle, a vibration, light and sound.
God is consciousness. And since we are all a part of this consciousness, we are all god.
The better we understand our connection to consciousness, the more godlike we become.
We are as much God as God is a part of us ... each of us is God. ... together we
are God ... this all-for-one-and-one-for-all ... makes us the whole of God. (Ruth
Montgomery)
A word used to describe this belief is "pantheism." Pan means "all" and theos means
"God." Pantheists believe "all is God."
Consciousness can be harnessed to achieve perfection: According to New Spiritual-
ity, everything is energy, including thoughts. Good thoughts produce good energy and
bad thoughts inhibit it. As Rhonda Byrne says in The Secret, "Your thoughts become the
things in your life," and "Your transmission creates your life and it creates the world."
This power of consciousness is sometimes called the "law of attraction."
According to Eckhart Tolle, the biblical prophecy of a new heaven and a new earth will
be fulfilled as more people harness the power of consciousness.

9.
What role does reincarnation play in New Spirituality? [6.5]
According to New Spirituality, reincarnation is part of the process we all go through.
Reincarnation is understood differently by Buddhists, Hindus, and New Age gurus. At
its simplest, it is the process by which the body dies but the soul continues its quest for
enlightenment.
Reincarnation is based on karma, which is a law of causality. Good is returned to those
who do good and evil is returned to those who do evil. In each successive lifetime, a
person must pay off past failings, suffering for and learning from past evils. Chief among
these failings is seeing ourselves as distinct individuals. This separates us from the con-
sciousness of the universe.

11. How does New Spirituality understand the universe and our place in it? [6.7]
New Spirituality teaches that the universe is eternal. It has no divine creator with no cause
or beginning. Furthermore, modern science has shown that classical physics doesn't go
deep enough in explaining the universe. For that we need quantum theory. Through
its lens we see the universe as chaotic, unpredictable, and mysterious wherein human
consciousness shapes physical reality.
What quantum physics has shown is that... when you break apart small aspects of
this energy ... the act of observation itself alters the results—as if these elementary
particles are influenced by what the experimenter expects... In other words, the
basic stuff of the universe, at its core, is looking like a kind of pure energy that is
malleable to human intention and expectation in a way that defies our old mecha-
nistic model of the universe. (James Redfield)
New Spirituality points to quantum theory to support its worldview.
12. What is the Gaia hypothesis? [6.7]
James E. Lovelock put forth the Gaia hypothesis in 1979. He believed that, "Taken as a
whole, the planet behaves not as an inanimate sphere of rock and soil ... but more as a
biological superorganism—a planetary body-that adjusts and regulates itself"
"Gaia" is a personification of the earth. The name comes from the Greek goddess of the
earth. Gaia is "Mother Earth," and we should pay close attention to her and our role as her
children. Though the Gaia hypothesis visualizes the universe as an orchestrated system
with a purpose, New Spiritualists do not acknowledge any kind of designer behind the
universe. It's a manifestation of the consciousness that existed before the universe itself.
There is no "who" behind the "what"
The Gaia hypothesis helped launch the modern ecological movement and some radical
offshoots like ecofeminism (the linking of feminism and ecology to fight male domina-
tion that exploits the planet), and deep ecology (a radical restructuring of society based
on the idea that all living things should have legal rights).

Trust the Universe. Trust and believe and have faith... The truth is that the Universe
has been answering you all of your life, but you cannot receive the answers unless
you are aware. (Rhonda Byrne)
We can become more self aware through disciplines like meditation and channel-
ing. Meditation, sometimes with crystals or mantras, is used to achieve a higher

18. How does New Spirituality relate to Christianity? [6.9]
New Spirituality is critical of religion, especially that which focuses on personal sin
and future salvation as Christianity does. Such an approach is "utopian," according to
Eckhart Tolle:
At the core of all utopian visions lies one of the main structural dysfunctions of the
old consciousness: looking to the future for salvation. The only existence the future
actually has is as a thought form in your mind, so when you look to the future for
salvation, you are unconsciously looking to your own mind for salvation. You are
trapped in form, and that is ego.
New Spirituality also rejects the ideas of a personal God, the unique deity of Christ, the
innate sinfulness of humans, the special revelation of the Bible, and most other core
Christian doctrines.
19. How does New Spirituality relate to Secularism? [6.9]
New Spirituality believes in consciousness as the ultimate reality. Therefore, it rejects
the materialism at the core of Secularism that says our spirit/soul/mind/consciousness
is simply a chemical process in our brains. Secularism is "demonic" according to the
Bhagavad Gita, and a dangerous superstition.
I am less concerned with attacks on God than I am with a far more insidious danger:
the superstition of materialism. (Deepak Chopra)
If materialism (only matter exists) and naturalism (everything has a natural explanation)
are correct, as Secularism insists, then New Spirituality is false, and vice versa.
20. How does New Spirituality relate to Marxism? [6.9]
The Marxist attempt to change the world won't succeed because it doesn't first change
people's state of consciousness.
The history of Communism, originally inspired by noble ideals, clearly illustrates

without any prior change in their inner reality, their state of consciousness. They
make plans without taking into account the blueprint for dysfunction that every
human being carries within: the ego. (Eckhart Tolle)

"BHAGAVAD GITA" DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
According to the Bhagavad Gita, what is yoga and what does it accomplish?
Yoga is a "state of serenity ... when you let go [of] attachment to sense objects and the
desire for the fruits of your efforts?" The essence of yoga is renunciation of all personal
desire. This leads to gaining control of the mind and getting in touch with your higher self.
Yoga practitioners should continue to concentrate their minds until they master
their minds and bodies, and thus experience a state of solitude wherever they may
be; then desires and possessiveness drop away. (6:10)
The detached mental state of yoga is dependent on physical disciplines.
It is impossible to practice Yoga effectively if you eat or sleep either too much or too
little. But if you are moderate in eating, playing, sleeping, staying awake and avoid-
ing extremes in everything you do, you will see that these Yoga practices eliminate
all your pain and suffering. (6:16-17)
2.
Can yoga be practiced by Christians as a spiritual discipline?
Yoga as it is understood by eastern religions such as Hinduism and Buddhism is contrary
to the Christian worldview in that it is based on non-Christian beliefs such as karma and
reincarnation. But meditation and self-control as ways to focus the mind on God and
to master physical drives are valid spiritual disciplines (Psalm 1:1-3; Romans 12:1-2; 1
Corinthians 9:27; Colossians 3:1-2).
In the Bhagavad Gita, yoga leads to an awareness of the oneness and godhood of all
things: pantheism. For the Christian, meditation can lead to a greater awareness of God
and our union with him through the indwelling Spirit (Philippians 4:8; 1 Timothy 4:15).
3. How does the concept of God in the Bhagavad Gita differ from the God of the Bible?
In the Bhagavad Gita, the idea and exact identity of God doesn't matter:
Devotees may select any name or form as the object of their worship. But if they
have sincere belief (shraddha), I make their faith strong and steady. Then when they
worship with steady faith the form they have chosen, they get what they want. But
actually, I am the one fulfilling their desires. (7:21-22)
In the Bible, there is one God who has revealed himself as the great I Am (Exodus 3:14).
To worship anything else as a god is idolatry (Exodus 20:3). This one God became flesh
in Jesus Christ (John 1:18).
For there is one God, and one mediator also between God and men, the man Christ
Jesus. (1 Timothy 2:5)

4. Why is reincarnation important to the followers of Krishna? How would you refute
reincarnation from the Bible?
Reincarnation —the return of the soul to another body after physical death-is impor-
tant for two reasons:
• It is necessary for the working out of karma—the full reward or punishment of a
person's deeds.
• Multiple lifetimes are necessary to reach enlightenment and complete awareness
of who Krishna is.
Those without sincere belief (shraddha) in this dharma will not realize who I am, Arjuna,
and must therefore return to the mortal world of death after death. (9:3)
On the other hand, the Bible says, "People are destined to die once, and after that to face
judgment" (Hebrews 9:27, NIV). Our souls don't go to another body upon death, nor do they
remain disembodied. There is a resurrection of the body and a reunion with the soul forever.
For we know that if the earthly tent we live in is destroyed, we have a building from
God, an eternal house in heaven, not built by human hands. Meanwhile we groan,
longing to be clothed instead with our heavenly dwelling, because when we are
clothed, we will not be found naked. (2 Corinthians 5:1-3, NIV)
5. What role does karma play in the salvation of the soul according to the Bhagavad
Gita? How would you refute the concept of karma from the Bible?
Karma is the outworking of a person's actions, good or bad, through several lifetimes
until he or she reaches enlightenment:
Iam the father and mother of the whole universe, and also its most ancient grandfather.
I am the one who gives you the results of your actions (karma). (9:17)
In this way you free yourself from the bondage of karma and its good or bad results.
Through this Yoga, you thus achieve true renunciation (sannyas) [of personal desires]
and come to me in a state of liberation. (9:28)
The Bible says we are accountable for our actions but we cannot save ourselves by good works.
For by grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift
of God; not as a result of works, so that no one may boast. For we are His workman-
ship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand so that
we would walk in them. (Ephesians 2:8-10, NASB)
As for our sinful deeds, we can't atone for them either. Jesus paid our sin debt in full and
offers salvation as a free gift.
God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become
the righteousness of God. (2 Corinthians 5:21, NIV)
He was delivered over to death for our sins and was raised to life for our justifica-
tion. (Romans 4:25, NIV)

1. What does the New Spiritualist mean by the word God?
To the New Spiritualist, the word God doesn't mean the God who revealed himself in
the Bible. God is an impersonal force like light or gravity, not a personal being in whose
image humans are made. Rather than there being a God of creation, God is the creation.
New Spirituality is pantheistic. It teaches that God is the transcendent reality of which
the material universe and human beings are only manifestations. This impersonal God
is seen in many guises and is synonymous with the universe itself.
2.
How do movie franchises like The Matrix and Star Wars promote New Spirituality?
In The Matrix trilogy, there is a spiritual reality that is true and a physical reality that is
an illusion. The key to enlightenment is becoming aware that what humans experience
with their senses isn't real (the Matrix) and choosing not to live in that delusion any
longer. This is a main tenant of New Spirituality.
In the Star Wars movies, the Force is the impersonal power source that animates the
universe. It has to be understood, harnessed, and used by humans who are trained and
become adept at it. The Force is neither good nor evil and can be used for both.
3.
How is the higher consciousness of New Spirituality different from the spiritual
maturity Christians are trying to attain?
Higher consciousness in New Spirituality involves becoming aware of our own existence,
our godhood, and the oneness of all things. Spiritual maturity for Christians involves
becoming aware of our dependence upon the indwelling Holy Spirit to conform us to
the image of Christ.
Christians don't become aware of their godhood; they become vessels through which
the indwelling God manifests himself (Galatians 2:20; Colossians 1:27).
4.
What are some things the New Spiritualist and Christian worldviews have in common?
Where do they differ?
New Spiritualists and Christians share a belief in the spiritual realm. They believe there is
more to life than the here and now. They believe there are consequences for our actions
and they believe that human beings can become better than they are now.
But these worldviews differ on some key points: pantheism versus a personal creator,
karma versus grace, reincarnation versus one physical life, godhood versus God in us,
cosmic evolution versus redemption.
