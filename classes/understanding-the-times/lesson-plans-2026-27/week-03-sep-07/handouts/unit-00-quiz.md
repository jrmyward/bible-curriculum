# ✂︎ Teacher-Facing Answer Key (detach before distributing)

## Scoring

- **Q1** (2 pts): "Worldview" = the set of answers (examined or unexamined) a person gives to ultimate questions; OR the framework / lens / mental map through which a person interprets the world. **Full credit:** captures "framework of answers" idea. **Partial credit:** mentions something related but vague (e.g., "how you see things"). **No credit:** totally off.

- **Q2** (3 pts, 0.3 per discipline): Theology, Philosophy, Ethics, Biology, Psychology, Sociology, Law, Politics, Economics, History. Any order. **Full credit for all 10.** Deduct 0.3 for each missing or wrong.

- **Q3** (3 pts): Steel-manning = representing an opposing view in its strongest, most charitable form. Straw-manning = misrepresenting an opposing view to make it easier to dismiss. **Full credit:** both defined + the contrast is clear. **2 pts:** one defined clearly, the other weakly. **1 pt:** confused but attempting.

- **Q4** (3 pts, 0.33 per worldview): Christianity, Judaism, Mormonism, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, Secularism (or "New Atheism"), Expressive Individualism (or "MTD"), Revolutionary Lineage (or "Marxism/Postmodernism"). Any order. Accept reasonable variants of names. **Full credit for all 9.**

- **Q5** (2 pts): Beliefs shape actions; actions shape culture; therefore worldviews (belief systems) have real consequences for how people live. **Full credit:** captures the "beliefs have consequences" logic. **Partial:** mentions consequences but doesn't explain why.

- **Q6** (2 pts): **TRUE.** Most people inherit their worldview from family, culture, or experience without consciously examining it. **Full credit:** correct answer + reasonable justification. **1 pt:** correct answer, weak justification. **0 pts:** wrong answer.

- **Q7** (3 pts): Student picks one discipline and explains (a) the question it asks and (b) why it matters for how someone lives. **Full credit:** both (a) and (b) present, with a concrete connection between the two. **Partial credit:** (a) present but (b) missing or vague.

- **Q8** (3 pts): The Gospel = God/sin/Christ/redemption; the specific news of who Jesus is and what He did. Christian culture = songs, behavior, church attendance, moral expectations — things that may SURROUND the Gospel but are not the Gospel itself. **Full credit:** distinction is clear. **2 pts:** one side understood, the other weakly. **1 pt:** mentions the distinction exists but doesn't articulate it well.

- **Q9** (3 pts): To force students to understand and articulate a position they may not hold, for the sake of intellectual charity, rhetorical skill, and empathy. **Full credit:** mentions the "assigned sides" structure and the learning goal (understand, not win). **Partial:** mentions debate but misses the "assigned, not chosen" point. **No credit:** "it's a debate" with no deeper explanation.

- **Q10** (1 pt): Completion credit. Any honest answer gets the point. Blank or joke answer gets 0.

**Total possible: 30 points.**

**Suggested grading weight:** this is a formative assessment — enter as a quiz grade but don't weight it heavily. It's a baseline, not a summative.

## If the Class Bombs This Quiz

Don't over-react. Common failure points to watch for:

- **Q2** (naming 10 disciplines): If <60% of students can name all 10, reinforce with a daily 30-second review for the first week of Unit 1. The 10 lenses are the vocabulary of the rest of the year.
- **Q4** (naming 9 worldviews): If <60% can name them, re-print the worldview grid handout and require students to carry it in their folders.
- **Q3** (steel-manning): Critical. If this is weak, steel-manning norms will not hold in Unit 1 Pair & Defend. Re-teach the concept Day 1 of Unit 1.
- **Q8** (Gospel vs. Christian culture): If this is weak, the Gospel Conversation days of each unit will be hollow. Revisit Day 7's Gospel outline in the first week of Unit 1.
