**Unit:** Unit 0 — Foundations (Day 10 of 10 — final)
**Topic:** Synthesis · Unit 0 quiz · bridge to Unit 1 (Christianity)

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Define "worldview" in their own words
2. Name all 10 analytical disciplines the course uses
3. Explain what steel-manning is and why it matters
4. Articulate the framing question for Unit 1: *"What is the Christian answer to each of the 10 disciplines, and why?"*

## Class Schedule (45 min)

### 0:00–0:10 · Return Worksheets + Discuss Thursday's Reading

- Hand back Thursday's worksheets with brief comments.
- Highlight 1–2 strong responses (with permission):
  - One that clearly summarized Myers Ch 1's argument
  - One that named a 10-discipline question the student found hardest and explained why
- Brief whole-class discussion (5 min):
  - *"What did you take from Myers Ch 1 overall?"*
  - *"Anything you're still unsure about?"*

### 0:10–0:25 · Unit 0 Quiz (15 min)

**10 questions · mix of short answer, fill-in, and short essay · closed-book.**

Suggested questions (adapt as needed):

1. Define "worldview" in one sentence.
2. List the 10 analytical disciplines the course uses to examine every worldview.
3. What is "steel-manning"? How is it different from "straw-manning"?
4. Name the 9 worldviews the course will study.
5. In Myers' view, why do worldviews matter? (1–2 sentences)
6. True/false: A person can hold a worldview without being aware of it. Briefly justify.
7. Short essay: Pick one of the 10 disciplines. Explain in 2–3 sentences what question that discipline asks and why it matters for how someone lives.
8. What is the difference between "the Gospel" and "Christian culture"?
9. What is the purpose of a Pair & Defend, in your own words?
10. Write one sentence of your own about why this course matters to you.

Students work silently for 15 minutes. No textbooks, no notes.

### 0:25–0:35 · Grade in Class (pair-swap)

- Students swap papers with a neighbor.
- Walk through each question, give the answer, students mark partner's paper.
- Questions 1, 2, 3, 4, 8, 9 are straightforward (name it / define it) — correct or not.
- Questions 5, 6, 7, 10 are short-answer — partial credit allowed.
- Collect papers for final scoring (you'll re-check before final grade goes in).

**Teacher move:** briefly clarify any misconceptions that surface. If half the class can't name all 10 disciplines, that's important information — address it directly.

### 0:35–0:45 · Bridge to Unit 1 (Christianity)

- Preview: *"We just finished the framework. Starting tomorrow, we fill in the first column of the grid — Christianity. Three weeks on this one unit, because it's the baseline we compare everything else to."*
- Framing question for Unit 1: *"What is the Christian answer to each of the 10 disciplines, and why?"*
- Assign reading: **Myers Ch 2 (Christianity), first half. Due Wednesday.**
- Remind about the Worldview Brief that will wrap Unit 1 — 1 page, collected at end of unit.
- Close with a preview of the tension point: *"Christianity makes a huge claim — that it's universally true for everyone, not just true for Christians. By the end of Unit 1 you should be able to articulate that claim charitably and honestly, even if you don't hold it yourself."*

## Teacher Notes

- **Grading philosophy for the Unit 0 quiz:** this is the first real grade of the year. It sets the tone. Don't over-weight it — it's a formative check, not a summative one. Some students will bomb it because they zoned out the first two weeks. Don't let one bad score define the year — private conversations with those students Wednesday.
- The pair-swap grading is a pedagogical move, not a time-saver — it forces students to see what a complete answer looks like. Watch for students who are dismissive of their partner's wrong answers; redirect them.
- The Myers Ch 2 assignment is the on-ramp to the real course. If the Ch 1 reading was rocky (many students didn't finish, or finished without understanding), spend extra time Wed framing what to look for in Ch 2.
- Unit 0 is now closed. Students have:
  - A personal artifact (the 10-discipline self-assessment from Day 2)
  - A graded reflection journal (Day 5 sub assignment)
  - A graded response worksheet (Day 9 sub assignment)
  - A quiz score (today)
  - A cohort-normed classroom
- **Next step in generation:** write Unit 1 (Christianity) lesson plans — Wed Sep 9 is Day 1 of Unit 1.
