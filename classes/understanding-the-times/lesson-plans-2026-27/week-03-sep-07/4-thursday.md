# Day 2 — Thu Sep 10, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 2 of 13)
**Topic:** Deep Dive Part 1 — Theology · Philosophy · Ethics (three lenses)
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. State Christianity's answer to **Theology** in one sentence using *Trinitarian Monotheism* (publisher Ch 8.6 wording) — one God in three coequal, coeternal persons
2. State Christianity's answer to **Philosophy** using *Realism + Mind/Body Dualism* — reality exists outside our heads; humans are body + soul
3. State Christianity's answer to **Ethics** using *Agape / Natural Law* — morality grounded in God's character; the summary command is love (Luke 10:27)
4. Fill in lenses 1, 2, and 3 of their student learning outline

- Board: pre-write the three lens headings as shown below

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening review

Cumulative recall question: *"What's the one claim Christianity makes that, if it's wrong, makes everything else collapse?"* Land on: the resurrection (1 Cor 15:14). See [lecture companion §Day 2 — Opening review](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:05–0:17 · Lens 1 — Theology [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Trinitarian Monotheism — one God, three persons, eternal relationship.*

Tell the story of Nicaea + Arius + Athanasius + *homoousios*. Hit vocabulary: Trinitarian Monotheism (Ch 8.6), general revelation (Ch 2.4), special revelation (Ch 2.4), personal God (Ch 2.3a). Scripture anchor: Isaiah 44:6. See [lecture companion §Day 2 — Lens 1 Theology](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:17–0:29 · Lens 2 — Philosophy [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Realism + Mind/Body Dualism.*

Tell the Acts 17 / Areopagus story — Paul engaged the philosophers on their ground. Hit vocabulary: Realism (Ch 9.7), Mind/Body Dualism (Ch 9.7c, 12.8), Metaphysics (Ch 9.1), Epistemology (Ch 9.1), Christian sources of knowledge (Ch 9.7a). Scripture anchor: John 1:1. See [lecture companion §Day 2 — Lens 2 Philosophy](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:29–0:41 · Lens 3 — Ethics [12 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:** *Agape — self-giving love, grounded in God's character.*

Read the Habermas quote aloud (it's in the reading packet, but worth saying out loud). Local example: Idaho Falls nonprofits. Hit vocabulary: Agape (Ch 10.8), Greatest commandment (Ch 10.8), Sermon on the Mount (Ch 10.8), Universal lawgiver (Ch 10.1). Scripture anchor: Romans 12:9. See [lecture companion §Day 2 — Lens 3 Ethics](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:41–0:46 · Synthesis check

*"Across these three lenses, what's the through-line?"* Listen for: *God's character is the source.*

### 0:46–0:50 · Bridge to Day 3

*"Tomorrow: Biology, Psychology, Sociology. We move from* who God is *to* who we are. *Read Part 2 §4–6 of the reading packet tonight."*

## Teacher Notes

- **The vocabulary is the bridge to the test.** Every term in **bold** in the lecture companion appears on the Day-13 test. Say each one in the publisher's exact wording at least once. Resist paraphrasing.
- **Pacing is tight.** 12 min × 3 lenses leaves 9 min for opening + synthesis + bridge. If a lens runs over, cut from synthesis, not from the lens.
- Watch for students who are *not* writing in their outlines. The outline is the unit's external memory; if they're not filling it in, they're falling behind.
