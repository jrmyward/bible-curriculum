## How to Read This Grid

- **Rows** are the 10 analytical disciplines — the lenses we use on every worldview.
- **Columns** are the 9 worldviews we'll study this year.
- **Cells** are the *dominant answer* that worldview gives to that discipline's core question.
- The **Christianity column** is the baseline. Everything else compares back to it.

When two cells share a phrase (e.g., "Special Creation" appears in multiple columns), the interesting work is in the *differences underneath the shared term*. The cell is the starting point of the conversation, not the end of it.

## What You're Going to Do With This Grid

- **Day 1 of every worldview unit:** Circle the column we're about to study. This is what we're filling in.
- **Last day of every worldview unit:** You should be able to speak to every cell in that column from memory.
- **In Unit 10 (Capstone):** You'll defend the Christianity column against the column you found most challenging.
- **In May:** You should walk out of this course able to draw this grid from memory and use it in a real conversation.

---

## One-Sentence Rule for the Whole Year

**Cells are starting points for conversations, not the end of them.** "Trinitarian Monotheism" is not the Christian view of God — it's the three-word pointer to the Christian view of God. Your job is to know what's behind the pointer.
