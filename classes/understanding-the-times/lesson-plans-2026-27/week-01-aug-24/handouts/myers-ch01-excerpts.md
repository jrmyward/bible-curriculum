**Suggested passage types** (look for these when selecting):

- **Passage A — The opening scene or anecdote.** Myers typically opens with a story or image that frames the chapter. This sets the emotional register.
- **Passage B — Myers' definition of "worldview."** Usually in the first 5 pages. Look for the clean, quotable definition.
- **Passage C — Why ideas have consequences.** Myers makes an argument that beliefs shape actions and actions shape culture. Pick the passage that states this most directly.
- **Passage D — The "battle of ideas" metaphor.** The chapter title. Look for the passage where Myers explains what's "battling" and what's at stake.

Keep passages short — 1 paragraph each, ~100 words. Students will read all 4 in about 15 minutes.
