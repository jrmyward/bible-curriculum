## Class Schedule (45 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Attendance + Brief Intro

Greet students. Take attendance using the roster.

Read aloud (or paraphrase):

> *"Good morning. Mr. Ward is out today. He's left you a full assignment — a short video and a reflection journal. This is a graded assignment. Take it seriously. There should be no talking during the video or during the journal writing. I'll collect journals at the end of class."*

### 0:05–0:20 · Watch the Video (15 min)

- Play the video Mr. Ward has queued. The link or file is in the sub folder.
- Students watch silently. No phones out, no side conversations.
- If a student asks what the video is about, say: *"Just watch. Your journal will help you think through it."*

**Backup plan if video won't play:**
- There is a printed handout in the folder labeled "Backup Reading — Fri Aug 28." Distribute copies. Students read the handout silently for the 15 minutes instead.

### 0:20–0:40 · Reflection Journal (20 min)

- Distribute reflection journal handouts (one per student).
- Read the instructions aloud:

> *"Respond to all three prompts on the handout. Use complete sentences. Your writing doesn't have to be polished — but it should be honest. This is graded on whether you actually engaged with the questions, not on whether you got 'right' answers. There are no right answers."*

**The three prompts** (printed on the handout):

1. *What worldview did you grow up in? What did it teach you without saying so?*
2. *Which of the 10 worldview questions (Theology, Philosophy, Ethics, Biology, Psychology, Sociology, Law, Politics, Economics, History) does your family most clearly answer? Which does it leave ambiguous?*
3. *One thing the video made you think about that you hadn't considered before.*

Students should have their names on the top of their page.

**Teacher move for the sub:**
- Silent writing. No conversation.
- If a student says they can't think of anything, say: *"Write 'I'm having trouble with this question' and then keep going. Explain why it's hard. Don't leave it blank."*
- If a student finishes early (before 20 minutes), say: *"If you're done, read back over what you wrote and add one more sentence to each answer."*

### 0:40–0:45 · Collect Journals + Dismiss

- Collect journals. Put them in the collection envelope labeled for today.
- Hand the envelope back to the main office or leave on Mr. Ward's desk.
- Announce: *"Have a good weekend. See you Tuesday — Monday is Labor Day."*

## If Something Goes Wrong

**If a student refuses to participate:**
- Don't argue. Mark them present, note it on the attendance sheet. Mr. Ward will follow up Monday.

**If a student asks about content or tries to debate the video:**
- Say: *"That's a great question for Mr. Ward. Write it in your journal — he'll respond Monday."*

**If students get unruly:**
- Call the main office. Ask for admin support.

**If a student is absent:**
- Mark them on the roster. Put extra journal handout in the sub folder with their name.

**If the video has technical issues:**
- Use the backup reading (in the folder). Same time allocation.

## Teacher-Facing Notes (Mr. Ward)

- Grade journals over the weekend. Use a 5-point scale:
  - 5 = thoughtful response to all 3 prompts; clearly engaged
  - 4 = complete but surface-level
  - 3 = partial (answered 1–2 prompts well, 1 poorly)
  - 2 = minimal effort
  - 1 = written but clearly not engaged
  - 0 = absent / missing
- Return journals Mon Sep 1 with brief written comment on each.
- Pick 1–2 strong journals to read aloud Monday (ask student permission first).
- If any journal shows real distress (a student naming a hard family situation, for example), check in privately with that student.
