# The Four Cohort Tools

**A student reference for how we'll work this year.**

*Condensed from the full tool specifications in [_shared/cohort-tools.md](../../../../../_shared/cohort-tools.md).*

---

## Why Cohort Method?

This class doesn't work like a traditional Bible class. You are not going to be lectured at for 45 minutes a day and then tested on what you memorized. You're going to read, write, argue, listen, and present.

The four cohort tools below are the **vocabulary of this class.** Each has its own rules. Each exists for a reason.

---

## Tool 1: Worldview Brief

*(A variant of "Discussion Brief" — specific to this course.)*

**When:** At the end of every worldview unit (roughly every 2 weeks).

**What:** A **1-page written response** in which you:

1. Summarize the worldview charitably — the way a thoughtful proponent of it would recognize as fair.
2. Identify the feature of that worldview you find **most compelling** — the place where it's at its strongest.
3. Identify the deepest point of **contradiction with Christianity** — where the two worldviews cannot coexist.
4. Sketch the **Gospel conversation** you would have with an adherent of this worldview.

**Graded on:** honesty, thoughtfulness, and charity. Not polish, not "right answers."

**Collected:** Last day of each worldview unit.

**What this builds:** The ability to represent other worldviews fairly, recognize their strengths, identify the real disagreements, and speak the Gospel into that specific context.

---

## Tool 2: Pair & Defend

**When:** Once per worldview unit, usually around Day 4 of the 10-day rhythm.

**What:** A paired debate with **assigned sides**.

**How it runs:**

| Phase | Time | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Prep | 1 min | Silent thinking. No notes, no phones. |
| Partner A | 3 min | Present your assigned side. |
| Partner B | 3 min | Present your assigned side. |
| A asks B | 2 min | One follow-up question, B answers. |
| B asks A | 2 min | One follow-up question, A answers. |
| Class debrief | 5 min | *"What was the strongest argument you heard from the side you disagreed with?"* |

**The rule you cannot break:** You may not refuse your assigned side. The point is to represent it faithfully, not to agree with it.

**Graded on:** participation, not "winning." Arguing your assigned side seriously = full credit. Refusing = no credit.

**What this builds:** Ability to steel-man opposing views. Rhetorical skill. Empathy.

---

## Tool 3: Case Study

**When:** Day 5 or 6 of the 10-day worldview rhythm.

**What:** A real-world scenario that the worldview being studied has to respond to. Small groups work through it together.

**Group structure:**
- 3–4 students per group
- Each group produces three things:
  1. A summary of the dilemma (what makes this hard?)
  2. Their recommended response (what should the worldview say to this?)
  3. The strongest counterargument to their own recommendation

**Timing:**
- 10 min group work
- 5 min per group presentation
- 5 min class vote + teacher debrief

**The vote:** Not about popularity. The question is *"Which response would you trust in a real situation?"*

**What this builds:** Application from abstract worldview claims to concrete human situations.

---

## Tool 4: Capstone

**When:** Unit 10 (the final two weeks of the course).

**What:** A **5-minute presentation** + **3-minute Q&A** + **2-page final brief** in which you:

1. Pick the worldview that most challenged your thinking this year.
2. Represent it charitably (steel-manned).
3. Identify the decisive differences with Christianity (2–3 disciplines from the 10-lens grid).
4. Present the Christian response.
5. Articulate the Gospel conversation you would have with a thoughtful proponent of that worldview.

**Graded on** (rubric details given separately in Unit 10):
- Charitable representation: 25 points
- 10-lens comparison: 20 points
- Christian response: 20 points
- Gospel conversation: 20 points
- Presentation & Q&A: 10 points
- Final Brief: 5 points

**What this builds:** Synthesis of everything the year has taught. The ability to do, under pressure, what the course has prepared you for.

## How the Tools Fit Together

The tools are not a menu — they work **in sequence**. Every worldview unit uses all four in this pattern:

1. **Read and prepare** (Days 1–3)
2. **Pair & Defend** (Day 4) — argue it for and against
3. **Case Study** (Days 5–6) — apply it to real scenarios
4. **Practice Gospel conversation** (Day 8) — for a proponent of this specific worldview
5. **Worldview Brief** (Days 9–10) — write the reflection

By the time we reach Unit 10 (Capstone), you'll have used each tool nine times.

> **Charitable engagement is the first ethical move. You learn by arguing fairly. You win by listening well.**
