## Class Schedule (45 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Attendance + Brief Intro

Greet students. Take attendance using the roster.

Read aloud (or paraphrase):

> *"Good morning. Mr. Ward is out today. You have an independent reading assignment and a response worksheet. This is a graded assignment. You should already be familiar with Myers Chapter 1 — today's task is to read it in full (or re-read it) and complete the worksheet. Silent work for 35 minutes. I'll collect worksheets at the end."*

### 0:05–0:40 · Silent Reading + Worksheet (35 min)

- Distribute response worksheets (one per student).
- If any student does not have the Myers textbook, hand them a printed Ch 1 copy from the folder.
- Students work silently. No phones. No conversation.
- The worksheet has three prompts:

**Worksheet prompts** (printed on the handout):

1. *Summarize Myers' argument in Chapter 1 in 2–3 sentences. What is he claiming, and what evidence does he use?*
2. *Which of the 10 worldview disciplines (Theology, Philosophy, Ethics, Biology, Psychology, Sociology, Law, Politics, Economics, History) do you think is most likely to shape how a person lives? Why?*
3. *One question you still have after reading Ch 1. (This can be about something you didn't understand, something you disagree with, or something you want to know more about.)*

Names on the top of the page.

**Teacher moves for the sub:**
- If a student says they already read Ch 1: they should re-read and look for things they missed the first time.
- If a student finishes very early (before 20 minutes): prompt them to add one more sentence to each answer, or to ask a second question in #3.
- If a student has read part of Ch 1 but not all: have them read what they haven't read first, then complete the worksheet.

### 0:40–0:45 · Collect Worksheets + Dismiss

- Collect worksheets. Put them in the collection envelope.
- Hand the envelope back to the main office or leave on Mr. Ward's desk.
- Announce: *"Have a good long weekend. See you Tuesday — tomorrow is a day off, Monday is Labor Day, and we're back Tuesday."*

## If Something Goes Wrong

**If a student refuses to participate:**
- Don't argue. Mark them present, note it on the attendance sheet. Mr. Ward will follow up Tuesday.

**If a student asks about content or tries to debate the reading:**
- Say: *"That's a great question for Mr. Ward. Write it in #3 on your worksheet — he'll respond Tuesday."*

**If students get unruly:**
- Call the main office. Ask for admin support.

**If a student doesn't have the textbook or a printed copy:**
- Give them a printed copy from the folder. If the folder is empty, have them sit with a classmate and share (silently — one person reading while the other follows along).

**If a student is absent:**
- Mark them on the roster. Set aside an extra worksheet marked "absent" in the folder.

## Teacher-Facing Notes (Mr. Ward)

- Grade worksheets over the long weekend. Use a 5-point scale:
  - 5 = all 3 prompts answered with depth; engaged honestly
  - 4 = complete; shows comprehension but not deep engagement
  - 3 = partial or surface-level
  - 2 = minimal effort
  - 1 = written but clearly not engaged
  - 0 = absent / missing
- Return worksheets Tue Sep 8 (Unit 0 Day 10) with brief written comments.
- Use worksheet responses to calibrate Day 10 synthesis — if half the class missed Myers' central argument, spend the first 10 minutes of Day 10 reframing before moving to the quiz.
- Note any student who wrote "I have no questions" for #3 — that's usually a signal they didn't engage. Private follow-up.
- Check in with any student whose #3 question is intellectually serious — invite a one-on-one conversation.
