# Day 8 — Wed Sep 2, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 0 — Foundations (Day 8 of 10)
**Topic:** Cohort tools walkthrough · mini Pair & Defend practice

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Name the four cohort tools (Discussion Brief / Worldview Brief, Pair & Defend, Case Study, Capstone) and explain what each is for
2. Participate in a full Pair & Defend cycle (3 min each + 2 min follow-up + debrief) on a low-stakes topic
3. Understand that assigned-sides debate is a skill-building exercise, not endorsement

## Class Schedule (45 min)

### 0:00–0:15 · The Four Cohort Tools — Walkthrough

Hand out the condensed cohort tools reference. Walk through each briefly.

**1. Worldview Brief** (variant of Discussion Brief for this course)
- 1-page written response at the end of every unit.
- Four parts: (a) charitable summary of the worldview, (b) most compelling feature, (c) deepest contradiction with Christianity, (d) Gospel conversation sketch.
- Collected at the end of each unit. Grade on thoughtfulness and honesty, not polish.

**2. Pair & Defend**
- Paired debate. Sides assigned, not chosen.
- 3 minutes each presenting, 2 minutes each follow-up Q&A, 5 minutes class debrief.
- Debrief question: *"What was the strongest argument you heard from the side you disagreed with?"*

**3. Case Study**
- Groups of 3–4. Real-world scenario. 10 minutes group work, 5 minutes presentations each, class vote, teacher debrief.
- Each group produces: summary of dilemma, recommended response, strongest counterargument to your own recommendation.

**4. Capstone** (Unit 10 — the course culminating assessment)
- 5-minute presentation + 3-minute Q&A + 2-page final brief.
- Pick the worldview that most challenged you; defend the Christian worldview against it, charitably.

These four tools rotate through every worldview unit. Today we practice Pair & Defend.

### 0:15–0:35 · Mini Pair & Defend Practice (20 min)

**Setup:**
- Random pairings (use cards, digital randomizer, whatever).
- Each pair draws a topic card. Partners are assigned sides.

**Suggested topic cards (school-related, not worldview-heavy):**
1. *"School uniforms are a good idea"* vs. *"School uniforms limit student expression"*
2. *"Phones should be allowed during lunch"* vs. *"Phones should be locked during school hours"*
3. *"Homework helps students learn"* vs. *"Homework is busywork that doesn't improve learning"*
4. *"Standardized tests fairly measure student ability"* vs. *"Standardized tests measure the wrong things"*
5. *"High school students should be able to choose their teachers"* vs. *"Teacher assignment should be the school's decision"*
6. *"Senior trips are a valuable experience"* vs. *"Senior trips are a distraction from graduation"*

**Run the full structure:**
- 1 minute prep (silent thinking — NO notes, NO phones)
- 3 minutes: Partner A presents assigned side
- 3 minutes: Partner B presents assigned side
- 2 minutes: Partner A asks one follow-up question; Partner B answers
- 2 minutes: Partner B asks one follow-up question; Partner A answers

Teacher keeps time, projects the timer on the board. Move briskly.

### 0:35–0:45 · Debrief (10 min)

- Whole class. The debrief question: *"What was the strongest argument you heard from the side you disagreed with?"*
- **Teacher move:** go around and ask 5–6 students this question specifically. Don't let them dodge into "my partner was good at arguing."
- Close with expectations for the year:
  - *"We do this every worldview unit. Assigned sides. Real arguments. Real listening."*
  - *"Refusing to argue your assigned side will cost you participation points. This is the skill you're building."*
  - *"Your grade on Pair & Defend is NOT based on which side wins. It's based on whether you argued your assigned side seriously. Half the time you'll argue the side you agree with. Half the time you won't. Both matter."*

## Teacher Notes

- This is the most procedural day of Unit 0 — the mini P&D is designed to teach the format so that when students encounter it in Unit 1 (Christianity), they already know the shape.
- **Watch for:** the student who refuses to argue. Private conversation after class. Not punitive — formative. *"The point isn't that you believe it. The point is you learn to represent it. This is a skill you'll use your whole life."*
- **Watch for:** the student who turns it into a performance — cracking jokes, arguing absurdly, making the classmate uncomfortable. Redirect in real time. *"Make your case seriously. This is a court of reason, not a comedy club."*
- Keep the topics light today. The first real Pair & Defend on a worldview topic happens in Unit 1 Day 6 (Wed Sep 16).
- If timing runs short, drop the 2-minute Q&A rounds and extend the debrief. The debrief is the pedagogical payload.
