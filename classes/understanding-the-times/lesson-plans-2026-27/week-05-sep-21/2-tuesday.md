# Day 9 — Tue Sep 22, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 9 of 13)
**Topic:** The Christian Family — Catholic · Eastern Orthodox · Protestant
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Recite the heart of the Nicene Creed (or recognize its claims when they read it)
2. Distinguish what all three traditions **share** (Trinitarian Monotheism, incarnation, bodily resurrection, the apostolic Church) from where they **diverge** (authority, salvation, sacraments)
3. Name the one-sentence summary of each tradition (Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant) accurately enough that a thoughtful adherent would recognize it as fair
4. Explain why traditions like Mormonism and Jehovah's Witnesses, despite using Christian vocabulary, sit on the worldview grid as **separate columns**, not inside the Christian column

### 0:05–0:15 · What all three share — the Nicene minimum [10 min]

Walk through the Nicene Creed (reading packet Part 3 Source 2). All three traditions confess every clause. Land six shared claims: Trinitarian Monotheism · Jesus fully God and fully man · the Holy Spirit · one holy catholic and apostolic Church · baptism for forgiveness · resurrection of the dead. *"If a tradition denies these, they've stepped off the historic Christian map. Nicaea is the MINIMUM, not the maximum, of Christianity."*

See [lecture companion §Day 9 — What all three share](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md).

### 0:15–0:40 · Where they diverge [25 min — ~8 min per tradition]

#### Roman Catholic [8 min]

Authority (Scripture + Sacred Tradition + Magisterium / Pope). Salvation (faith *formed by love*; Trent anathematized "faith alone"). Sacraments (7; transubstantiation). Mary (Theotokos, Immaculate Conception, Assumption). 1.4 billion. One-sentence: *the visible, hierarchical, sacramental Church preserves and teaches the deposit of faith.*

#### Eastern Orthodox [8 min]

Authority (Scripture + Holy Tradition + 7 ecumenical councils; no Pope). Salvation (*theosis*). Sacraments (7; mystery emphasis). Iconography (venerated). The Great Schism of 1054 (papal authority + *filioque*). 220 million. One-sentence: *the Church is the living continuation of the apostolic mystery; salvation is participation in God.*

#### Protestant [8 min]

Authority (*sola scriptura*). Salvation (*sola fide* — grace alone, faith alone, Christ alone). Sacraments (2 — baptism and the Lord's Supper, with internal disagreement). Origin (1517 — Luther's 95 Theses). 900 million; dominant in the US and at Watersprings. One-sentence: *Scripture alone is the final authority; salvation is by grace through faith in Christ alone.*

See [lecture companion §Day 9 — Where they diverge](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md) for the full breakdown for each.

### 0:40–0:48 · Where the line ends [8 min]

**WRITE ON BOARD:**

```text
INSIDE THE FAMILY        OUTSIDE
Catholic                  Mormonism (different Jesus, different God)
Orthodox                  Jehovah's Witnesses (no Trinity)
Protestant                Christian Science, Unity, etc.
```

The Nicene Creed test: any group that denies one of the central claims (Trinity, incarnation, bodily resurrection, apostolic Church) is a different worldview, not a Christian denomination. *"That doesn't mean their members aren't lovely people. It means the theology is categorically distinct. We spend two weeks on Mormonism in Unit 4."*

## Teacher Notes

- **Tone matters here.** Many students come from a single Christian tradition (mostly Protestant at Watersprings). Resist either condescending to the others ("Catholics are mostly Christian") or capitulating ("they're all basically the same"). Both moves fail. The historic Christian position: all three confess the Nicene Creed; they argue *inside* the family about authority, salvation, sacraments; the family has an edge.
- **The Mormonism preview matters.** Some students will have LDS friends and families. The point today is structural: vocabulary overlap with categorical difference. Unit 4 does the full treatment.
- **The "different Jesus, different God" language for LDS is the publisher's language**, not an invention. Use it as the publisher uses it: as a structural claim about Christology and theology proper, not a personal attack on LDS people.
