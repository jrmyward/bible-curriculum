# Day 12 — Fri Sep 25, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 12 of 13)
**Topic:** Worldview Brief writing — students write their own Christianity column in their own words
**Class Period:** 50 minutes · independent in-class writing

---

## Why This Day Matters

The Worldview Brief is the student's **reference document for the rest of the year**. They will return to it in Unit 10 (Capstone, May) and notice how their thinking has matured. They will also compare it side-by-side to nine other worldview briefs they'll write over the year. Make today count.

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should have produced:

1. A 2–3 page handwritten (or 600–900 typed word) brief giving Christianity's answer to each of the 10 disciplines
2. One Scripture anchor per discipline (chapter and verse — quoted or accurately paraphrased)
3. At least three of the **Eight Theological Anchors** named explicitly somewhere in the brief
4. Their own voice — not paraphrased Myers/Noebel

## Materials

- Printed Worldview Brief assignment (one per student) — full text in [facilitation guide §Day 12 — The assignment](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md)

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Opening + frame

*"Today you write. No discussion. No partner. Just you, your reading packet, your filled-in outline, and a pen. By the end of the period you will have a Christianity column you wrote yourself. We will come back to it nine times this year — every time we study a new worldview, you compare it back to this column. Put real thought in."*

### 0:05–0:08 · Distribute the assignment + rules

Read the rules aloud:

- One paragraph (3–5 sentences) per discipline
- One Scripture anchor per discipline
- Your own words — if a sentence sounds like Myers/Noebel wrote it, rewrite it
- At least three of the Eight Anchors named explicitly
- Length: 2–3 pages handwritten or 600–900 words typed
- May use: reading packet, learning outline, your Bible
- May NOT use: another student's brief, the internet, the lecture companion

### 0:08–0:48 · Independent writing [40 min]

Walk the room. Answer specific questions one-on-one. **Don't deliver mini-lectures.** If three students ask the same question, pause and address it briefly to the whole room.

Coaching scripts if students get stuck (full list in [facilitation guide §Day 12 — Trouble spots](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md)):

- *"I don't know how to start."* → *"Start with what you remember best. Pick any discipline. Write one sentence. Then write another."*
- *"I don't think I believe all of this."* → *"Your job today is to articulate the Christian position accurately, not to defend it personally. You can write the Christian column without being a Christian — and that distinction is, in fact, what this whole class is teaching you to do."*
- *"Can I just copy the learning outline?"* → *"No. You may use it as a reference. The brief must be in your voice."*

### 0:48–0:50 · Closing

Two reminders:

1. *"Monday — Day 13 — your Doug Letter is due at the start of class. After you hand it in, you take the unit test. Tonight: re-read your reading packet's Publisher Vocabulary section. Quiz yourself on every term. If you can define each in your own words AND match it to the publisher's exact phrase, you're ready."*
2. *"You will refer back to this brief many times. Don't lose it. Keep it in your packet."*

## Assessments Collected

- **Worldview Brief** — collected at end of period. Graded for:
  - Completion (all 10 disciplines + one Scripture each)
  - Accuracy (Christianity correctly represented)
  - Own-voice (not paraphrased Myers/Noebel)
  - At least three Eight Anchors named

Briefs that read as paraphrased Myers/Noebel are returned for revision. Return graded briefs by Day 14 (the first day of Unit 2 = Tue Sep 29).

---

## Teacher Notes

- **The biggest pitfall: paraphrased copying.** Watch for sentences that match the reading packet's phrasing too closely. The whole point is for students to articulate Christianity in *their* voice. Push back hard on first-week-of-school instinct to "give the teacher what she wants."
- **Most students will finish on time.** A few will sprint. A few will need to finish over the weekend. State the policy now: late briefs lose 1 letter grade per day (or whatever your standing policy is).
- **Some students will finish early and want to do something else.** Don't let them out the door early. *"Re-read your brief. Mark one sentence you want to strengthen. Strengthen it."*
