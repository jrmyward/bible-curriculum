## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Attendance + Brief Intro

Greet students. Take attendance using the roster.

Read aloud (or paraphrase):

> *"Good morning. Mr. Ward is out today. He's left you a video by a thoughtful Christian writer named [TIM KELLER / FRANCIS COLLINS / REBECCA McLAUGHLIN — whichever Mr. Ward picked]. After the video, you'll write down two things on a card: one thing that struck you, and one question it raised. I'll collect the cards at the end. This is graded. Take it seriously."*

If a student asks why we're watching a video by a Christian: *"This unit is on Christianity. We're studying how thoughtful Christians articulate their faith today. Just watch. Your card will help you think through it."*

### 0:05–0:08 · Frame the Resource

Read aloud:

> *"As you watch, take notes on two things. One — something that struck you. It could be something you agreed with, disagreed with, or had never heard before. Two — a question it raised. The question doesn't have to be a polite question. Honest is better than polite."*

### 0:08–0:43 · Watch the Video (~35 min — adjust to actual length)

- Play the video Mr. Ward has queued.
- Students watch silently. No phones out, no side conversations.
- If a student asks what the video is about, say: *"Just watch. Your card will help you think through it."*
- If a student asks for the bathroom, allow one student at a time, max 5 minutes.

**If the video runs shorter than 35 minutes:** use the remaining time for paired discussion. Have students turn to a neighbor: *"Each of you, take 2 minutes. Tell your partner one thing that struck you in the video. Then switch."*

**Backup plan if video won't play:**
- There is a printed handout in the folder labeled "Backup Reading — Mark Gospel." Distribute copies. Students read silently for the 35 minutes. The Mark passages are listed; they only need to read those.
- Same 3×5 card task at the end — *"one thing that struck me, one question."*

### 0:43–0:48 · Debrief (5 min)

- Distribute 3×5 cards (one per student).
- Read instructions aloud:

> *"On your card, write your name at the top. Then write two things. One — one thing from the video that struck you. Two — one question the video raised for you. Don't overthink it. Honest sentences are better than polished ones."*

- Students write silently for ~3 minutes.
- Then go around the room — every student says ONE of their two things aloud (their pick). Just one trip around the room; don't let anyone go twice.

**If a student says they have nothing to write:** *"Write 'I'm having trouble with this' and then keep going. Explain why it's hard. Don't leave it blank."*

### 0:48–0:50 · Collect Cards + Dismiss

- Collect cards. Put them in the collection envelope labeled for today.
- Hand the envelope to the main office or leave on Mr. Ward's desk.
- Announce: *"Tomorrow Mr. Ward is back. We're on Day 9 — the Christian Family. See you then."*

## If Something Goes Wrong

**If a student refuses to participate:**
- Don't argue. Mark them present, note it on the attendance sheet. Mr. Ward will follow up Tuesday.

**If a student says "I'm not a Christian, why do I have to watch this?":**
- Say: *"This class isn't about converting anyone. It's about understanding what people believe. You can take notes as a critic if you want — Mr. Ward would actually prefer that to fake agreement. Just engage."*

**If a student tries to debate the video's content with you:**
- Say: *"That's a great question for Mr. Ward. Write it on your card — he'll respond Tuesday."*

**If students get unruly:**
- Call the main office. Ask for admin support.

**If a student is absent:**
- Mark them on the roster. Put extra card in the sub folder with their name.

**If the video has technical issues:**
- Use the backup reading (in the folder). Same 50-min structure with reading replacing video.
