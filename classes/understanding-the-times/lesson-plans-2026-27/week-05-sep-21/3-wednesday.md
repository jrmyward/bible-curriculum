# Day 10 — Wed Sep 23, 2026 · THE MODEL EXERCISE

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 10 of 13)
**Topic:** Four-Test Self-Application — applying Myers's four tests to Christianity itself
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Why This Day Matters

This is **the model exercise of the year.** Every later worldview unit's Day 7 follows this template. Calibrate carefully — Units 2–9 will all reproduce the rhythm of: 10-min lecture frame → 35-min small-group worksheet → 5-min whole-class synthesis.

*(Originally scheduled for Day 8 Mon Sep 21; swapped with the Contemporary Resource day so the model exercise lands on a teacher day, not a sub day. See [teaching-map.md](../../teaching-map.md) § Unit 1 Day 8 entry for the swap rationale.)*

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Name and define each of the four tests (Reason · Outer World · Inner World · Real-World Consequences)
2. Apply at least **two** of the four tests to Christianity itself, identifying both:
   - (a) where Christianity *passes cleanly*
   - (b) where Christianity *raises a hard question* a thoughtful Christian must be ready to answer
3. Articulate how a thoughtful Christian responds to the hard question — without pretending it isn't there

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:10 · Lecture frame (the 10 min that holds the rest together)

Three sub-moves:

1. **Opening [3 min]** — *"We're going to apply the four tests to Christianity itself. Not to attack it. To test it. A worldview that can't survive its own tests probably shouldn't be defended."*
2. **The frame: charity AND honesty [4 min]** — both rules at once; what passes cleanly + where the hard question is.
3. **What "passes cleanly" and "hard question" look like [3 min]** — walk Test 1 (Reason) as the worked example. Christianity passes on the four-act story + objective morality + dignity-and-brokenness account. Hard question: Trinity and Incarnation are mysteries — are they contradictions? (Classical answer: no; explain *why* — same thing in same sense at same time, which they aren't.)

**WRITE ON BOARD:**

```text
1. REASON          — Is it internally coherent?
2. OUTER WORLD     — Does it match what we observe?
3. INNER WORLD     — Does it match our deepest intuitions?
4. REAL-WORLD CONSEQUENCES — When lived at scale, what fruit does it bear?
```

See [lecture companion §Day 10](../../lectures/unit-01-christianity-lecture-companion.md) for the full lecture-frame script.

### 0:10–0:13 · Group assignment + worksheet distribution

Groups of 3–4. Assign each group two of the four tests:

- Groups 1, 2: Reason + Outer World
- Groups 3, 4: Inner World + Real-World Consequences

(Adjust by group count. Every group gets two tests; at least two groups cover each test so the debrief has cross-perspectives.)

### 0:13–0:38 · Small-group work [25 min, ~12 min per test]

Students fill the worksheet for each of their two tests:

- **(a) Where Christianity passes cleanly** — 2 specific places, with publisher vocabulary
- **(b) Where Christianity raises a hard question** — name it; don't soften it
- **(c) How a thoughtful Christian responds** — 2–3 sentences

Walk the room. At each group, push: *"Where exactly? Be specific. The hard-question half is the whole point."*

See [facilitation guide §Day 10 — What to listen for at each table](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for the publisher-grade answers per test (passes + hard questions + responses).

### 0:38–0:45 · Whole-class synthesis [7 min]

Quick popcorn. For each of the four tests:

- *"What did your group identify as the strongest Christian pass?"* (1 group)
- *"What did your group identify as the hardest pressure point?"* (1 group)
- *"How would a thoughtful Christian respond?"* (1 group)

Move fast. Goal: every test gets briefly aired so all students see the model worked across all four.

### 0:45–0:50 · Closing + bridge

*"This is the exercise we'll repeat for every worldview. Today you applied the four tests to Christianity itself — and you found that it can survive its own tests, but only when you're honest about the hard questions. That honesty is what we'll demand of every later worldview too. Tomorrow: Gospel Conversation practice — the foundational exercise the rest of the year copies."*

---

## Teacher Notes

- **The hard-question discipline matters.** A group that refuses to name a hard question hasn't tested Christianity — they've praised it. Push them: *"Try again."*
- **If a student suggests a hard question that's actually a confusion** (e.g., "How can there be three Gods?" — which misunderstands the Trinity), use it: *"Tell me more — what specifically is the question?"* Usually clarification surfaces what they actually meant.
- **Don't pre-empt the answers.** Let groups generate their own pass/hard/response material. The publisher-grade list in the facilitation guide is for *you* to recognize good answers, not for you to dictate them.
- **This is calibration for the year.** If today goes well, every subsequent Day 7 is a known quantity. If today goes poorly, every subsequent Day 7 will be a re-invention.
