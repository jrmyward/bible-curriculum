**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 13 of 13) — the final day

**Class Period:** 50 minutes

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should:

1. Have demonstrated mastery of Unit 1 content on the Day-13 blended test (51 questions, 83 points)
2. Have submitted their Doug Letter (Christianity edition — the publisher's Ch 02 Writing Assignment)

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Doug Letter collection

Call alphabetically. Mark off on clipboard who handed in and who didn't. Accept partial letters with a note for late credit. No public shaming.

### 0:05–0:45 · Test administration [40 min]

Distribute the printed test. Standard administration:

> *"This is a single-class-period test. You have 40 minutes. Answer every question. The True/False section requires you to alter the underlined word if the statement is false — don't just write 'F.' The essay at the end (Q51 — the Four-Test exercise from Day 10) is worth 4 points; don't skip it.*
>
> *You may not use your reading packet, your outline, or your notes. You may not talk to a neighbor. Place your phone face-down on the corner of your desk.*
>
> *When you finish, turn your test over and sit quietly. If you have time, re-check your answers. Begin."*

**Walk the room.** Do not answer content questions. If a student asks *"what does this word mean?"* → *"You'll need to use your best judgment."* You may clarify phrasing (not content) if a student is genuinely stuck on a phrasing issue.

### 0:45–0:50 · Bridge to Unit 2 (Judaism)

After collecting tests:

> *"Two weeks ago we started with the question:* what is the Christian answer to each of the 10 disciplines? *You can now answer that question. From here on, every unit asks the comparison version of the same question:* how does this worldview answer differently, and why?
>
> *Tomorrow we open Unit 2 — Judaism. Not the Judaism of the Old Testament —* modern *Judaism. The tradition that, after the destruction of the Temple in AD 70, rebuilt itself around prayer, study, and acts of lovingkindness instead of sacrifice. The question we'll ask is:* what does Judaism do with the cessation of the sacrificial system? *That question is going to set up the Christian apologetic conversation that arrives explicitly on Day 8 of Unit 2.*
>
> *Read the first half of the Unit 2 reading packet for tomorrow. It'll be posted on the class portal. See you then."*

## Teacher Notes

- **The Four-Test essay (Q51) is built directly from Day 10's small-group exercise.** Students who engaged Day 10 well should do well on Q51. Students who phoned it in on Day 10 will struggle. Use Q51's distribution as a calibration signal for whether Day 10 needs adjustment in year 2.
- **The cross-comparative items (Q9, Q10, Q11, Q12, Q13)** were adapted to remove references to worldviews students haven't yet studied (Islam, New Spirituality, Postmodernism). See the test teacher version's footer for the adaptation notes.
- **If a student didn't finish the Doug Letter,** take what they have, partial credit, talk after class. Don't shame them in front of the room.
- **If a student didn't finish the test,** standard policy — score what's there.
- **If a student tries to cheat,** quietly remove the test, zero, talk after class, document.
- **The bridge to Unit 2 should land emotionally** — *"the tradition Christianity claims to fulfill"* is the key framing. Don't preempt the Day-8 apologetic move; just set up the question.
