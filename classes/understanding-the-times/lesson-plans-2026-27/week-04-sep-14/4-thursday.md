# Day 7 — Thu Sep 17, 2026

**Unit:** Unit 1 — Christianity (Day 7 of 13) · Case Study
**Topic:** Hannah & Sarah at the coffee shop — what does the Christian worldview offer a 22-year-old who just lost her father?
**Class Period:** 50 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of class, students should be able to:

1. Apply the 10 disciplines concretely to a real human moment (grief)
2. Name **at least one hard answer** the Christian worldview asks Hannah to hold — not soften, not flatten
3. Draft 3–4 honest sentences Hannah could say to Sarah — not a sermon, a real human answer
4. Distinguish a Christianity-shaped response from a generic-supportive-friend response

---

- Printed group worksheet (one per group — template is in the facilitation guide §Day 7)

## Class Schedule (50 min)

### 0:00–0:05 · Publisher supplemental quiz (Gospel of Mark)

Administer the publisher's Ch 02 supplemental-reading quiz (~5 min, closed-book, individual). Collect at end.

### 0:05–0:08 · Introduce the case

Read the Hannah/Sarah case aloud once. Then hand out the printed copy. See [facilitation guide §Day 7 — The case](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for the full case text.

### 0:08–0:18 · Group work [10 min]

Groups of 3–4 work through the printed worksheet. Three sections:

1. **What the Christian worldview offers** (across the 10 lenses) — pick 3 lenses, write one sentence each
2. **Where the Christian answer is hard** — identify one hard answer; don't soften
3. **What Hannah might actually say to Sarah** — 3–4 sentences, not a sermon

See [facilitation guide §Day 7 — Worksheet template](../../handouts/unit-01-christianity-facilitation-guide.md) for the full worksheet structure and what to listen for at each group.

### 0:18–0:33 · Group presentations [15 min, scale to group count]

Each group presents (2–3 min): which 3 lenses, the hard answer, Hannah's draft response. One class question per group.

### 0:33–0:43 · Class vote + debrief [10 min]

Vote: *"Which group's response to Sarah would you trust most in a real conversation?"* Brief teacher debrief — name what was strong, what was missing.

**Land the closing point:** *"You are going to have a version of this conversation in your actual life. Maybe sooner than you think. The Christian answer is not 'we know why.' It is 'we know who.'"*

### 0:43–0:48 · Individual response

Each student hands in a 3-sentence written response: *"What would YOU say to Sarah?"*

### 0:48–0:50 · Bridge to Day 8 + the long weekend

Per the facilitation guide bridge: *"Tomorrow is no school. Monday is a sub day — Day 8 will be a contemporary-resource video. We pick up Tuesday with Day 9 (Catholic / Orthodox / Protestant) and Wednesday is Day 10 — Four-Test self-application. Re-read Part 1 of the reading packet over the weekend, especially the Eight Anchors; the small-group work on Day 10 assumes you have them cold."*

## Teacher Notes

- **If a student in the room has actually lost a parent recently,** be aware in advance. Talk with that student privately before class — they can opt out, or participate, but they shouldn't be ambushed.
- **If a group writes a Hallmark response** that flattens the theology, push: *"Where in your answer does the actual Christian gospel show up? If it could have been written by any kind, non-Christian friend, you haven't reached Christianity yet."*
- **The "hard answer" section is the whole point.** A group that names only what Christianity offers and skips the hard part has missed the assignment. Mark accordingly.
- **Day 8 bridge:** remind students explicitly that Monday is a sub day — students need to know to take it seriously despite the absence.
