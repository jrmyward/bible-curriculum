Chapter 2 asks the big question behind the whole course: what IS the Bible, where did it come from, and can we trust it?

Questions we'll wrestle with this unit:
• How has the Bible shaped the world — and what are the four ways people read it?
• How is the Bible arranged, and what do the "map" and "compass" pictures teach us?
• What's the difference between general and special revelation?
• How did we get the 66 books — what is the canon, and how were the New Testament books chosen?
• What do inspiration and inerrancy mean, and why can we trust the Bible?
• If someone says "that's true for you, not for me," what's wrong with that — and why does truth matter?

By the end of this unit, you'll be able to:
• Name ways the Bible has influenced the world and the four ways people read it.
• Describe how the Bible is arranged (39 OT + 27 NT = 66) and explain the map & compass analogies.
• Tell general revelation from special revelation, with examples.
• Explain the canon and the three criteria for the New Testament (apostolicity, orthodoxy, pedigree).
• Define inspiration and inerrancy, and give reasons to trust the Bible.
• Explain why "there is no truth" refutes itself, and say why truth matters.
