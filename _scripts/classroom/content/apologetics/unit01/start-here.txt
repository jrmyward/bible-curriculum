Welcome to Apologetics. This first unit introduces the whole course: apologetics is the work of sharing, defending, and commending the Christian faith. It complements evangelism by helping us answer the hard questions people actually ask, and it can strengthen our own faith along the way.

Questions we'll wrestle with this unit:
• Why should Christians be prepared to explain and defend their faith?
• What is apologetics, and how is it different from evangelism?
• How is the Christian's mission grounded in the Bible's story and the Great Commission?
• What is the place of reason, and of doubt, in following Jesus?
• How can we share and defend the faith in a way that reflects the character of Christ?

By the end of this unit, you'll be able to:
• Say why it matters to be prepared to explain the faith, and describe the purpose of apologetics.
• Define a worldview and explain why finding the one that fits reality matters.
• Trace God's mission through the six acts of the Bible's story and name three lessons from the Great Commission.
• Tell evangelism (clear presentation, active demonstration, generous invitation) from apologetics (defending, commending, translating).
• Describe the weapons of the spiritual battle — chiefly prayer — and the roles of reason and doubt.
