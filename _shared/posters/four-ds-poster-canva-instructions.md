# How to Create the Four Ds Poster in Canva

**Time required:** 20–30 minutes  
**Skill level:** Beginner (no design experience needed)  
**Cost:** Free (Canva free account) or $13/month (Canva Pro for premium templates)

---

## Step 1: Set Up Your Canvas

1. Go to [canva.com](https://www.canva.com) and log in (or create free account)
2. Click **Create a design** → **Custom size**
3. Enter dimensions:
   - **Width:** 36 inches
   - **Height:** 24 inches
   - **Units:** Inches
4. Click **Create new design**

---

## Step 2: Set Background

1. In the left sidebar, click **Elements**
2. Search for "rectangle"
3. Drag a rectangle to cover the entire canvas
4. Click the rectangle, then click **Color** in the top toolbar
5. Choose a neutral background:
   - Light gray (#F5F5F5) — clean, professional
   - White (#FFFFFF) — classic
   - Cream (#FFF8E7) — warm, vintage

---

## Step 3: Create the Title Section

1. Click **Text** in the left sidebar → **Add a heading**
2. Type: **THE FOUR Ds**
3. Position at the top center of the canvas
4. Format:
   - Font: **Montserrat Bold** or **Bebas Neue** (bold, modern)
   - Size: **150–180 pt**
   - Color: Black or dark navy
   - Alignment: Center
5. Add subtitle below:
   - Click **Text** → **Add a subheading**
   - Type: **A Framework for Doctrinal Disagreement**
   - Font: **Montserrat Regular** or **Open Sans**
   - Size: **48–60 pt**
   - Color: Dark gray
6. Below that, add tagline:
   - Type: *"Not all disagreements are equal. Know which fight you're in."*
   - Font: Italic
   - Size: **36 pt**

---

## Step 4: Divide Canvas into Four Columns

### Option A: Manual Dividers
1. Click **Elements** → search "line"
2. Drag three vertical lines to divide the canvas into four equal sections
3. Make lines **6–8 pt thick**, dark gray or black
4. Use the ruler/guides (View → Show rulers) to ensure equal spacing

### Option B: Use Shapes
1. Click **Elements** → search "rectangle"
2. Drag four rectangles, one for each column
3. Size them equally (each ~8.5" wide, full height minus title space)
4. Add colored backgrounds:
   - Column 1: Light red (#FFEBEE)
   - Column 2: Light orange (#FFF3E0)
   - Column 3: Light yellow (#FFFDE7)
   - Column 4: Light green (#E8F5E9)
5. Adjust transparency if needed (20–30% opacity for subtle color)

---

## Step 5: Add Column 1 — DIE FOR

1. **Icon:**
   - Click **Elements** → search "shield icon" or "cross icon"
   - Drag to top of column 1
   - Resize to ~200 × 200 px
   - Color: Red (#D32F2F)

2. **Category name:**
   - Click **Text** → **Add a heading**
   - Type: **DIE FOR**
   - Font: **Montserrat Extra Bold**
   - Size: **72 pt**
   - Color: Red (#D32F2F)
   - Alignment: Center
   - Position below icon

3. **Definition:**
   - Add text box: *"Essential gospel truths. Denying these puts you outside Christian orthodoxy."*
   - Font: **Open Sans Regular**
   - Size: **28 pt**
   - Color: Black
   - Alignment: Center or left
   - Line spacing: 1.2

4. **Scripture:**
   - Add text box: *"Matters of first importance" (1 Cor 15:3–5)*
   - Font: Italic
   - Size: **24 pt**
   - Color: Dark gray

5. **Examples (bulleted list):**
   - Add text box:
     ```
     • Deity of Christ
     • Salvation by grace through faith
     • Bodily resurrection
     • Authority of Scripture
     ```
   - Font: **Open Sans Regular**
   - Size: **26 pt**
   - Color: Black
   - Line spacing: 1.5

6. **Engage instruction:**
   - Add text box: **DEFEND BOLDLY**
   - Font: **Montserrat Bold**
   - Size: **32 pt**
   - Color: Red
   - Background: White box with red border (optional)

7. **Interactive box (bottom of column):**
   - Add rectangle at bottom of column (~3" tall)
   - Outline: 4 pt red border
   - Fill: White or light gray
   - Add text inside: *"Place topic here"*

---

## Step 6: Repeat for Columns 2, 3, 4

Follow the same structure as Column 1, using content from the poster design specs:

### Column 2: DIVIDE FOR
- **Icon:** Church building or fork in road
- **Color scheme:** Orange (#FF6F00)
- **Definition:** *"Important secondary doctrines. Faithful Christians disagree; often determines denominational lines."*
- **Scripture:** *"Unity in essentials, liberty in non-essentials"*
- **Examples:**
  - Baptism (mode, subjects)
  - Church government
  - Calvinism vs. Arminianism
  - Spiritual gifts
- **Engage:** **STUDY DEEPLY, ARGUE RESPECTFULLY**

### Column 3: DEBATE FOR
- **Icon:** Speech bubbles or scales
- **Color scheme:** Yellow (#FBC02D)
- **Definition:** *"Issues worth discussing but not dividing over. Faithful Christians can disagree within the same church."*
- **Scripture:** *"Iron sharpens iron" (Prov 27:17)*
- **Examples:**
  - Eschatology details
  - Creation and science
  - Worship style
  - Bible translation philosophy
- **Engage:** **DISCUSS FREELY, HOLD HUMBLY**

### Column 4: DECIDE FOR YOURSELF
- **Icon:** Open hand or person silhouette
- **Color scheme:** Green (#388E3C)
- **Definition:** *"Personal conscience matters. Scripture gives principles, individuals decide before God."*
- **Scripture:** *"Each one should be fully convinced in his own mind" (Rom 14:5)*
- **Examples:**
  - Food and drink
  - Sabbath observance
  - Entertainment choices
  - Education methods
- **Engage:** **DON'T JUDGE, DON'T FLAUNT**

---

## Step 7: Add Interactive Section

Below the four columns, add a horizontal section:

1. **Heading:**
   - Text: **Before we debate, place the topic here →**
   - Font: **Montserrat Semi-Bold**
   - Size: **42 pt**
   - Centered above the four interactive boxes

2. **Four boxes** (aligned under each column):
   - Use rectangles with colored borders matching each column
   - Each box ~6" wide × 3" tall
   - White fill
   - Add small text inside: *"DIE FOR topic"* (gray, 18 pt)

---

## Step 8: Add Footer

1. **Quote (centered at bottom):**
   - Text: *"In essentials, unity; in non-essentials, liberty; in all things, charity."*
   - Font: Italic
   - Size: **32 pt**
   - Attribution below: *— Attributed to Rupert Meldenius (17th c.)*

2. **Source credit:**
   - Text: *Framework from Dr. Gerry Breshears (Western Seminary) & Mark Driscoll, Vintage Church*
   - Font: Regular
   - Size: **20 pt**
   - Color: Dark gray

---

## Step 9: Final Touches

1. **Alignment:**
   - Select all elements in one column
   - Click **Position** → **Tidy up** to align automatically
   - Repeat for each column

2. **Spacing:**
   - Ensure equal margins around all elements
   - Use Canva's smart guides (purple lines that appear when dragging elements)

3. **Review:**
   - Zoom out to see the full poster (Ctrl/Cmd + 0)
   - Check for typos, alignment issues, color consistency
   - Ask someone else to proofread

---

## Step 10: Download and Print

1. Click **Share** (top right) → **Download**
2. **File type:** PDF (Print) — this preserves quality for large printing
3. **Quality:** Best (300 DPI)
4. Click **Download**

### Printing Options:

#### **Option A: Local Print Shop**
- Take PDF file to FedEx Office, Staples, or local printer
- Request:
  - Size: 24" × 36"
  - Paper: Heavy cardstock (100 lb) or foam board
  - Finish: Matte lamination (for dry-erase use) or gloss (for durability)
- **Cost:** $20–$40

#### **Option B: Online Print Service**
- Upload PDF to Vistaprint, Canva Print, or PostersOnline
- Select poster size: 24" × 36"
- Choose material: Glossy photo paper, matte, or foam board
- Add lamination if available
- **Cost:** $15–$35 + shipping

#### **Option C: School Printer**
- If your school has a large-format printer (plotter), save as PDF and ask IT/admin to print
- **Cost:** Free or minimal

---

## Step 11: Lamination (Optional but Recommended)

**Why laminate?**
- Protects from wear and tear
- Allows use of dry-erase markers for temporary notes
- Easy to clean with sticky note residue remover

**How:**
- Many print shops offer lamination as an add-on
- DIY: Buy self-adhesive laminate sheets (Amazon, Office Depot) and apply at home

## Bonus: Create a Digital Version

1. In Canva, click **Share** → **Download** → **PNG** (for high quality)
2. Upload to:
   - Google Classroom (as a resource)
   - Class website or LMS
   - Print as 8.5" × 11" handouts (just scale down in print settings)

3. **Interactive digital version:**
   - Use Google Slides or Jamboard
   - Students can drag text boxes to categorize topics during remote learning

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Elements won't align  
**Solution:** Use Canva's **Position** tool (top right) → **Tidy up** or manually align using rulers/guides

**Problem:** Text is too small when printed  
**Solution:** Font sizes below 24 pt may be hard to read from across the room — increase to 28–32 pt minimum

**Problem:** Colors look different when printed  
**Solution:** Request a test print or ask the print shop to match Canva colors (provide hex codes)

**Problem:** Poster looks cluttered  
**Solution:** Reduce text, increase white space, use bullet points instead of paragraphs

## Time-Saving Tip

If you want a pre-made template, search Canva's template library:
1. Search "infographic poster 24x36"
2. Find a 4-column layout
3. Replace text/colors with Four Ds content
4. Saves 10–15 minutes of layout work
