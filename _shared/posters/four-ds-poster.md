# Four Ds Poster — Design Specs

**Purpose:** Classroom visual for categorizing doctrinal discussions before engaging emotionally. Students/teacher place a sticky note or magnet under the appropriate category at the start of each debate.

---

## Poster Layout (Landscape, 24" × 36" recommended)

### Title (Top Center)
```
THE FOUR Ds
A Framework for Doctrinal Disagreement
```

**Subtitle (smaller):**
"Not all disagreements are equal. Know which fight you're in."

---

## Four Columns (Left to Right)

Each column has:
1. **Category name** (large, bold)
2. **One-sentence definition**
3. **Key Scripture reference**
4. **3–4 examples** (bulleted)
5. **How to engage** (1–2 words)
6. **Color/icon** for visual distinction

---

### Column 1: **DIE FOR**

**Color:** Red (danger/urgency)  
**Icon:** Shield or cross

**Definition:**  
Essential gospel truths. Denying these puts you outside Christian orthodoxy.

**Scripture:**  
"Matters of first importance" (1 Cor 15:3–5)

**Examples:**
- Deity of Christ
- Salvation by grace through faith
- Bodily resurrection
- Authority of Scripture

**Engage:**  
**DEFEND BOLDLY**

---

### Column 2: **DIVIDE FOR**

**Color:** Orange (caution)  
**Icon:** Church building or road fork

**Definition:**  
Important secondary doctrines. Faithful Christians disagree; often determines denominational lines.

**Scripture:**  
"Unity in essentials, liberty in non-essentials"

**Examples:**
- Baptism (mode, subjects)
- Church government
- Calvinism vs. Arminianism
- Spiritual gifts (cessationist vs. continuationist)

**Engage:**  
**STUDY DEEPLY, ARGUE RESPECTFULLY**

---

### Column 3: **DEBATE FOR**

**Color:** Yellow (discussion)  
**Icon:** Speech bubbles or scales (balanced argument)

**Definition:**  
Issues worth discussing but not dividing over. Faithful Christians can disagree within the same church.

**Scripture:**  
"Iron sharpens iron" (Prov 27:17)

**Examples:**
- Eschatology details (pre/post/amil)
- Creation and science
- Worship style
- Bible translation philosophy

**Engage:**  
**DISCUSS FREELY, HOLD HUMBLY**

---

### Column 4: **DECIDE FOR YOURSELF**

**Color:** Green (freedom/go)  
**Icon:** Open hand or individual person

**Definition:**  
Personal conscience matters. Scripture gives principles, individuals decide before God.

**Scripture:**  
"Each one should be fully convinced in his own mind" (Rom 14:5)

**Examples:**
- Food and drink (alcohol, diet)
- Sabbath observance
- Entertainment choices
- Education methods

**Engage:**  
**DON'T JUDGE, DON'T FLAUNT**

---

## Interactive Element (Below Columns)

**Heading:**  
"Before we debate, place the topic here →"

**Four boxes** (aligned under each column) where students can place:
- Sticky notes (write the topic on the note)
- Magnets with dry-erase surface (write with marker)
- Velcro-backed cards (pre-printed topics)

**Instructions at bottom:**  
"Ask: Which D is this? Set your tone accordingly."

## Design Notes

### Typography
- **Title:** Bold sans-serif, 3–4 inches tall
- **Category names:** Bold, 2 inches tall, all caps
- **Definitions/examples:** Clean sans-serif, 0.5–0.75 inches
- **Scripture references:** Italicized

### Layout
- **Equal-width columns** with clear vertical dividers (thin black lines or color blocks)
- **Color gradients:** Red → Orange → Yellow → Green (visual spectrum from "hot" to "cool")
- **White space:** Don't overcrowd — this should be readable from across the room

### Materials
- Print on heavy cardstock or foam board (for durability)
- Laminate to allow dry-erase marker use
- Attach magnetic strips or Velcro dots in the interactive boxes

### Alternative Formats
- **Vertical (portrait):** Stack categories top to bottom instead of left to right
- **Digital version:** Google Slides or Canva template students can access remotely
- **Handout size:** 8.5" × 11" foldable tri-fold or single-page reference

## Print-Ready File

To create the actual poster, use:
- **Canva** (easiest for non-designers): Use "Poster" template (24" × 36"), drag-and-drop elements
- **Adobe InDesign** or **Illustrator** (if you have access): Full design control
- **Microsoft PowerPoint**: Set slide size to 24" × 36", design, export as PDF, send to print shop

**Print vendors:**
- Local print shop (FedEx Office, Staples)
- Online: Vistaprint, PostersOnline, Canva Print
- School printer (if large-format capable)

**Cost estimate:** $15–$30 for laminated foam board poster

## Classroom Use Protocol

### Step 1: Introduce the Framework
- Use the handout ([four-ds-doctrinal-framework.md](../handouts/four-ds-doctrinal-framework.md)) to teach the categories
- Walk through biblical basis and examples
- Practice categorizing 5–7 topics as a class

### Step 2: Install the Poster
- Hang in a visible location (front wall, side wall students face during discussions)
- Stock sticky notes or magnets nearby

### Step 3: Use Before Every Debate
- **Teacher or student** says, "Which D is this?"
- Write the topic on a sticky note
- Place it under the correct category
- **Brief discussion:** "Why is this a [Divide For] instead of [Die For]?"
- **Set tone:** "Okay, this is Debate For — so we're discussing freely, holding our views humbly, and staying unified. Let's go."

### Step 4: Debrief After Discussions
- "Did we engage appropriately for the category?"
- "Did anyone treat a Debate For issue like a Die For issue?"
- "Did we give freedom where Scripture gives freedom?"

### Step 5: Revisit and Refine
- If students consistently mis-categorize, re-teach the framework
- If a topic proves controversial (e.g., "Is gender identity Die For or Divide For?"), use that as a teaching moment on category boundaries
