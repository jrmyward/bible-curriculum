# Lesson Plan Quality Standards

Generated lesson plans should meet all of the following standards.

---

## Depth & Detail

- Each day is fully fleshed out (not just bullet points)
- Timing is specific (not vague "10-15 min")
- Teacher scripts are provided for key moments
- Transitions between activities are smooth and explicit

## Pedagogical Soundness

- Lessons follow a clear arc (hook → instruction → practice → synthesis)
- Activities match learning objectives
- Cognitive load is appropriate (don't overload with too many new concepts)
- Assessments align with what was taught

## Cohort Tool Integration

- Discussion Briefs, Pair & Defend, Case Studies are used correctly
- Cohort tools follow the rhythm: Reading → Brief → Discussion → Debate → Application
- Students are scaffolded into tools (Week 1 teaches *how* to write a brief; Week 10 assumes they know)

## Student Agency

- Students gradually take ownership (teacher-led → student-led over the year)
- Opportunities for student choice (e.g., student-generated Pair & Defend prompts)
- Reflection and metacognition are built in (not just content delivery)

## Substitute-Friendly Design

- Substitute days use non-cohort activities
- Instructions for subs are exhaustive (assume zero content knowledge)
- Students can work independently without teacher expertise

## Contextual Awareness

- Short weeks are lighter (no heavy new content on a 3-day week)
- Pre-break weeks are engaging but not assessment-heavy
- Post-break weeks reconnect to content (review, synthesis, Socratic seminar)
- Capstone weeks build in work days and flex time

## Follows Week 1 Exemplar

- Tone, structure, and level of detail match the Week 1 daily files (`week-01-aug-24/1-monday.md` through `5-friday.md`)
- Each day is its own file with a self-contained header (day, date, title, week/chapter context)
- Handouts are standalone files in the week's `handouts/` directory, not inlined in lesson plans
- Rubrics reference shared files in `assessments/` rather than being duplicated
- Teacher scripts are in the same style (warm, clear, not condescending)
