# Skill: Supplemental Content Integration for Teaching Maps

## Purpose

Guide the teaching map generator to incorporate supplemental units beyond the official textbook syllabus. This allows teachers to extend curricula with additional topics, perspectives, or content that addresses gaps in the published material.

## When to Use

- Official syllabus omits important topics (e.g., Buddhism/Hinduism/Mormonism in a worldview course)
- Teacher wants to add current events, case studies, or contemporary issues
- Curriculum needs to be adapted for specific school context or student needs
- Course includes multiple textbooks or custom content alongside a primary text

## Process

### Step 1: Identify Supplemental Content Needs

Ask the teacher:
1. What topics are missing from the official syllabus?
2. Why are these topics important to include?
3. What resources are available (textbooks, articles, videos, guest speakers)?
4. How much time should be allocated to supplemental content?

### Step 2: Determine Integration Approach

**Option A: Extend the Course**
- Add supplemental units at the end of the official syllabus
- Example: 24 weeks of core content + 8 weeks of supplemental = 32-week course
- Best when: You have extra instructional days and want comprehensive coverage

**Option B: Condense Core + Add Supplemental**
- Streamline the official syllabus to make room for supplemental content
- Example: Condense 36 weeks to 24 weeks + add 8 weeks of supplemental = 32-week course
- Best when: You're working within a fixed timeframe and supplemental content is essential

**Option C: Interleave Supplemental Content**
- Weave supplemental topics into the official syllabus where thematically appropriate
- Example: Add a Mormonism unit right after the Christianity unit in a worldview course
- Best when: Supplemental content directly relates to or contrasts with specific core topics

**Option D: Replace Sections**
- Swap out parts of the official syllabus with custom content
- Example: Replace 2 weeks of the official content with a deep dive on a more relevant topic
- Best when: Parts of the official syllabus are redundant, outdated, or less relevant

### Step 3: Plan Supplemental Units

For each supplemental unit, define:

1. **Topic/Title**: What worldview, issue, or content area?
2. **Duration**: How many weeks/days?
3. **Learning Objectives**: What should students know/be able to do?
4. **Resources**: 
   - Primary texts (books, articles, scripture passages)
   - Videos or documentaries
   - Guest speakers or field experiences
   - Case studies or scenarios
5. **Teaching Style**: Cohort, lecture-seminar, inductive, etc.
6. **Assessments**: How will mastery be measured?

### Step 4: Integrate into Teaching Map

#### For Extended Courses (Option A):
```markdown
## Week-by-Week Schedule

### Weeks 1-24: Core Content
(Official syllabus content)

---

### SUPPLEMENTAL CONTENT BEGINS

### Week 25: [Supplemental Topic]
**Topic**: Buddhism & Hinduism  
**Resources**: *The Universe Next Door* Ch 5, guest speaker
**Activities**:
- Mon: Introduction to Eastern thought
- Tue: Four Noble Truths and karma
- Wed: Hindu pantheon and Brahman
- Thu: Pair & Defend on suffering and reincarnation
- Fri: Quiz on Eastern religions

(Continue for all supplemental weeks)
```

#### For Condensed + Supplemental (Option B):
```markdown
## Course Structure

**Weeks 1-20**: Understanding the Times (condensed from 36 weeks)  
**Weeks 21-28**: Supplemental Worldviews (Buddhism, Hinduism, Mormonism)  
**Weeks 29-32**: Synthesis and Application

### Condensed Pacing Notes
- Official syllabus is streamlined by:
  - Combining overview days with content introduction
  - Reducing review days (integrated reviews instead of dedicated weeks)
  - Focusing on 4-5 key disciplines per worldview instead of all 10
  - Consolidating tests into instructional weeks

(Then provide week-by-week schedule showing both condensed and supplemental)
```

#### For Interleaved Content (Option C):
```markdown
### Week 8: Christianity — Deep Dive
(Official content)

---

### Week 9: Mormonism (LDS) — SUPPLEMENTAL
**Context**: Now that we've studied biblical Christianity, we examine a modern movement that claims to be Christian but departs from historic orthodoxy.
**Resources**: *Kingdom of the Cults*, Book of Mormon excerpts
**Activities**:
- Mon: Joseph Smith and LDS origins
- Tue: LDS theology vs. biblical theology
- Wed: Book of Mormon claims and archaeology
- Thu: Case Study — responding to LDS missionaries
- Fri: Quiz on Mormonism

---

### Week 10: Islam — Introduction
(Return to official content)
```

### Step 5: Create Custom Syllabus Document

If the teacher is using supplemental content extensively, create a **custom syllabus** file:
- Location: `classes/<class-name>/syllabus/custom-syllabus.md`
- Purpose: Comprehensive document showing official + supplemental content
- Include: rationale, resources, pacing, full outline

This supplements (doesn't replace) the official syllabus extraction.

### Step 6: Note Supplemental Content in Teaching Map

At the top of the teaching map, include a note:

```markdown
**Custom Content**: This teaching map includes supplemental units beyond the official textbook:
- Buddhism & Hinduism (Weeks 25-27)
- Mormonism / LDS (Weeks 28-30)
- Comparative Analysis (Weeks 31-32)

See `syllabus/custom-syllabus.md` for rationale and detailed content outline.
```

## Guidelines for Condensing Official Syllabi

When condensing official content to make room for supplemental units:

### What to Streamline
- **Repetitive review days**: Integrate review into instructional days
- **Redundant discussions**: Combine similar discussion topics
- **Lower-priority disciplines**: Focus on 4-5 key disciplines per worldview instead of all 10
- **Extended test prep**: 1 day of review is usually sufficient for high schoolers

### What NOT to Cut
- **Core concepts**: Don't sacrifice depth on essential doctrines or ideas
- **Primary texts**: Students still need to read the key passages/chapters
- **Critical thinking activities**: Pair & Defend, Case Studies, etc. are where learning happens
- **Major assessments**: Capstones and unit exams provide accountability

### Example: Condensing 36 Weeks to 24 Weeks
**Original pacing** (Understanding the Times):
- Week 1-7: Theology across 5 worldviews (1 week per worldview + 2 review weeks)
- Week 8-14: Philosophy across 5 worldviews
- (Similar for 8 more disciplines = 36 weeks total)

**Condensed pacing**:
- Week 1-4: Introduction + Christianity (theology, philosophy, ethics, biology)
- Week 5-7: Islam (theology, philosophy, ethics)
- Week 8-10: Secularism (theology, philosophy, ethics)
- Week 11-13: Marxism (theology, philosophy, ethics)
- Week 14-16: Postmodernism (theology, philosophy, ethics)
- Week 17-20: Cross-worldview synthesis (law, politics, economics, history)
- **Result**: 24 weeks covering core content, freeing up 12 weeks for supplemental

## Resources for Common Supplemental Topics

### Buddhism & Hinduism (Eastern Religions)
- *The Universe Next Door* by James Sire, Ch 5
- *Encountering World Religions* by Irving Hexham
- Websites: CARM.org, GotQuestions.org
- Videos: Ravi Zacharias, Nabeel Qureshi on Eastern thought

### Mormonism (LDS)
- *Kingdom of the Cults* by Walter Martin
- *Reasoning from the Scriptures with the Mormons* by Ron Rhodes
- *Letters to a Mormon Elder* by James R. White
- Websites: CARM.org, MRM.org (Mormon Research Ministry)
- Testimonies: Ex-Mormon videos, articles

### Jehovah's Witnesses
- *Kingdom of the Cults* by Walter Martin
- *Reasoning from the Scriptures with the Jehovah's Witnesses* by Ron Rhodes
- Websites: CARM.org, 4Witnesses.org

### New Age / New Spirituality
- *The Universe Next Door* by James Sire, Ch 7
- *Unmasking the New Age* by Douglas Groothuis
- Articles on Oprah spirituality, yoga/mindfulness in culture

### Contemporary Issues (Bioethics, Technology, Justice)
- Case studies from The Gospel Coalition, Desiring God
- *How to Find Truth in a Post-Truth World* by Summit Ministries
- Current event articles analyzed through worldview lenses

### Political Ideologies (Progressivism, Libertarianism, etc.)
- *Fault Lines* by Voddie Baucham (Critical Theory)
- *The Benedict Option* by Rod Dreher (cultural engagement)
- Primary documents from political movements

## Example: Worldview Course with Supplemental Content

**Official Syllabus**: Understanding the Times (36 weeks, 5 worldviews)  
**Supplemental Needs**: Buddhism, Hinduism, Mormonism (3 additional worldviews)

**Approach**: Condense core + add supplemental (Option B)

**Resulting Structure**:
- Weeks 1-24: Understanding the Times (condensed)
  - Focus on theology, philosophy, ethics for each worldview
  - Cross-worldview synthesis on 2-3 key disciplines (law, politics, history)
- Weeks 25-27: Buddhism & Hinduism
  - Introduction to Eastern thought
  - Four Noble Truths, karma, reincarnation
  - Hindu pantheon, caste, paths to liberation
- Weeks 28-30: Mormonism (LDS)
  - Joseph Smith, Book of Mormon, LDS claims
  - LDS theology vs. biblical Christianity
  - Evangelism and cultural engagement
- Weeks 31-32: Comparative analysis across all 8 worldviews
- Weeks 33-36: Final projects and assessment

**Total**: 36 weeks covering 8 worldviews instead of 5

## Integration with Teaching Map Generator

When running `/generate-map`, the system should:

1. Check for `custom-syllabus.md` in addition to `syllabus.md`
2. If custom syllabus exists, ask: "Use official syllabus or custom syllabus?"
3. If custom is selected, follow the structure defined in the custom document
4. Mark supplemental weeks clearly in the teaching map
5. Reference resources for supplemental content

---

## Summary

Supplemental content allows teachers to:
- Address gaps in published curricula
- Tailor courses to student/school context
- Integrate contemporary issues and real-world application
- Provide comprehensive coverage of important topics

The teaching map generator supports supplemental content through condensed pacing, extended courses, interleaved units, or section replacement. Always create a `custom-syllabus.md` to document the rationale and plan.
